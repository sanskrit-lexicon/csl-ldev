{#मूर्धन्#}¦ {%mūrdhan, ā,%} m. (in Uṇādi-s. 1. 158.
said to be fr. rt. {%murv,%} to bind), the forehead, brow,
skull; the head in general; a head, chief, leader,
superior, ({%sarveṣām bhūtānām mūrdhā rājā,%} the
king is the head of all creatures); the highest or
foremost or most prominent part, surface, top, point,
peak, summit, ({%parvatasya mūrdhā,%} the top or
peak of a mountain); ‘the summit,’ epithet of a
particular spiritual condition with Buddhists; (in
geometry) the base, (opposed to {%agra%}); {%mūrdhni%}
or {%mūrdhani,%} at the head or highest point, at the
beginning or commencement, in the front, ({%atiṣ-%}
{%ṭhan manujendrāṇām mūrdhni,%} he stood at the
head of the kings of men; {%saṅgrāma-mūrdhani,%}
in the front of the battle); before, above, over.
