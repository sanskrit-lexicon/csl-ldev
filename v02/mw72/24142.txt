{%nāma dhā%} or {%nāmnā kṛ%} or {%nāmnā vi-dhā,%} to give

{%tāṃ Duḥkha-labdhikāṃ nāmnā cakāra,%} he called

{%ḍitaḥ,%} a sage named Viṣṇu-śarman; {%nadī-nāmnī%}

{%dhavo nāma Brāhmaṇaḥ,%} a Brāhman, Mādhava by

will offer me something). {%Nāma%} as an emphatic
particle may have some of the above senses, but is
often untranslatable by any single equivalent English
word. It is frequently placed after interrogative
