{#ग्रीष्म#}¦ {%grīṣma, as, ā, am%} (said to be fr. rt.
{%gras%}), hot, warm; ({%as%}), m. the summer, the hot
season, consisting of the months Śuci and Śukra or
Jyeṣṭha and Āṣāḍha (from the middle of May to
the middle of July); heat, warmth; N. of a man;
({%ā%}), f., N. of a tree [cf. {%lodhra%}]; ({%ī%}), f. the tree
Jasminum Sambac; [cf. {%nava-mallikā;%} cf. also Hib.
<nsi>gris,</nsi> ‘fire;’ <nsi>griosgaim,</nsi> ‘I fry, boil;’ <nsi>griosach,</nsi>
‘burning embers;’ Lith. <nsi>karsztas</nsi> and <nsi>karsztis.</nsi>]
{%—Grīṣma-kāla, as,%} m. the hot season (see above).
{%—Grīṣmakālīna, as, ā, am,%} pertaining to the hot
season. {%—Grīṣma-ja, as, ā, am,%} produced by heat,
produced in the hot season; ({%ā%}), f. a kind of fruit-
tree, Anona Reticulata; [cf. {%lavanī.%}] {%—Grīṣma-%}
{%dhānya, am,%} n. summer corn. {%—Grīṣma-puṣpī,%}
f., N. of a plant, {%= karuṇī%}. {%—Grīṣma-bhava, as,%}
{%ā, am,%} produced by heat or in the hot season; ({%ā%}),
f. Jasminum Sambac. {%—Grīṣma-sundaraka, as,%}

m. a kind of pot-herb, Erythræa Centaureoides,

Mollugo. {%—Grīṣma-hāsa, as,%} m. the flocculent
