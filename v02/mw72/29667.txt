{#पूतना#}¦ {%pūtanā,%} f., N. of a female demon
(said to cause a particular disease in children; she
was sent by Kaṃsa to destroy the infant Kṛṣṇa,
and having assumed a pleasing form [according to
Hari-vaṃśa 3423, Śakunī-veśa-dhāriṇī] appeared to
the child and offered him her poisoned breast to
suck, which he seized and held till he had sucked
away her life; she is enumerated among the Mātṛs
attending upon Skanda, and associated with Śakuni
as a daughter of Bali; cf. {%andha-p°, ahi-p°, kaṭa-%}
{%p°, gandha-p°%}); a kind of disease, atrophy and
wasting in a child (ascribed to the demon Pūtanā);
yellow myrobalan, Terminalia Chebula or Citrina
({%= harītakī%}); a species of Valeriana ({%= gandha-%}
{%māṃsī%}). {%—Pūtanā-mokṣaṇa-prastāva, as,%} m.,
N. of a chapter of the Brahma-vaivarta-Purāṇa.
{%—Pūtanāri (°nā-ari°), is,%} m. ‘enemy of Pūtanā,’
an epithet of Kṛṣṇa. {%—Pūtanā-sūdana, as,%} m.
‘destroyer of Pūtanā,’ an epithet of Kṛṣṇa. {%—Pū-%}
{%tanā-han, ā,%} m. ‘slayer of Pūtanā,’ an epithet of
Kṛṣṇa.
