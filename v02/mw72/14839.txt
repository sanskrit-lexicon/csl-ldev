{#कीचक#}¦ {%kīcaka, as,%} m. (said to be fr. rt.
{%cīk%}), a hollow bamboo; a bamboo whistling or rattling
in the wind, Arundo Karka, a reed; a kind of tree;
N. of a people, a tribe of the Kekayas, (a Kīcaka is
chief of the army of king Virāṭa, and is conquered
by Bhīma-sena); N. of a Daitya or demon; a certain
Rākṣasa or goblin. {%—Kīcaka-jit, t,%} or {%kīcaka-%}
{%nisūdana, as,%} or {%kīcaka-bhid, t,%} m. an epithet of
Bhīma-sena, conqueror of Kīcaka. {%—Kīcaka-badha,%}
{%as,%} m. ‘the killing of Kīcaka,’ title of a poem.
