{#चाक्षुष#}¦ {%cākṣuṣa, as, ī, am%} (fr. {%cakṣus%}),
consisting in sight, depending on or produced from
it; belonging to the eye. visual, optical, perceptible by
the eye, visible, seen; relating to Manu Cākṣuṣa;
{%cākṣuṣī vidyā,%} that magical science which gives
the power of seeing anything; ({%as%}), m., N. of the
sixth Manu who with five other Manus is a son of
Manu Svāyambhuva, or according to other authorities
[Page0320-a+ 71]
a son of Viśva-karman and Ākṛti, or a son of
Cakṣus; N. of a son of Ripu and Bṛhatī, (according
to some he is called Cakṣuṣa, and is father of Manu
Cākṣuṣa); N. of a son of Kakṣeyu and brother
of Sabhānara; also of a son of Anu and brother of
Sabhānara; also of a son of Khanitra; ({%ās%}), m. pl., N.
of a class of deities in the fourteenth Manvantara;
({%am%}), n. knowledge which depends on vision.
{%—Cākṣuṣa-jñāna, am,%} n. ocular evidence. {%—Cā-%}
{%kṣuṣa-tva, am,%} n. perceptibility by sight.
