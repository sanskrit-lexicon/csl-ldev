{#उत#} 2¦. {%uta,%} ind. (as a particle of doubt or
<div n="lb"/>deliberation) and, also, even, or. Often used for the
<div n="lb"/>sake of emphasis, especially at the end of a line after
<div n="lb"/>{%iti%} or a verb (e. g. {%sarva-bhūtāni tam pārtha sadā%}
<div n="lb"/>{%paribhavanty uta,%} all creatures, O king, certainly
<div n="lb"/>always despise him).
<div n="P"/>(As an interrogative particle, generally at the begin-
<div n="lb"/>ning of the second or following part of a double in-
<div n="lb"/>terrogation) or, utrum-an (e. g. {%katham nirṇīyate%}
<div n="lb"/>{%kiṃ syān niṣkāraṇo bandhur uta viśvāsa-ghā- %}
[Page0147-c+ 73]
<div n="lb"/>{%takaḥ,%} how can it be decided whether he be a friend
<div n="lb"/>without a motive or a violator of confidence ?). In
<div n="lb"/>this sense it may be strengthened by {%āho%} (e. g. {%kaccit%}
<div n="lb"/>{%tvam asi mānuṣī utāho surāṅganā,%} art thou a
<div n="lb"/>mortal woman or divine ?), or by {%āho-svit%} (e. g.
<div n="lb"/>{%Śālihotraḥ kiṃ nu syād utāhosvid rājā Nalaḥ,%}
<div n="lb"/>can it be Śālihotra or king Nala ?). Rarely {%kim%} is
<div n="lb"/>repeated before {%uta%} used in this sense (e. g. {%kiṃ nu%}
<div n="lb"/>{%svargāt prāptā tasyā rūpeṇa kimuta anyā āgatā,%}
<div n="lb"/>has she arrived from heaven or has another come in
<div n="lb"/>her form ?).
<div n="P"/>(As a particle of wishing, especially at the beginning
<div n="lb"/>of a sentence followed by a potential) would that!
<div n="lb"/>utinam! (e. g. {%uta adhīyīta,%} would that he would
<div n="lb"/>read!).
<div n="P"/>({%Uta%} preceded by {%kim%}) on the contrary, how
<div n="lb"/>much more, how much less (e. g. {%samartho 'si sa-%}
<div n="lb"/>{%hasram api jetuṃ kimuta ekam,%} thou art able to
<div n="lb"/>conquer even a thousand, how much more one ?).
<div n="P"/>({%Uta%} preceded by {%prati%}) on the contrary, rather
<div n="lb"/>(e. g. {%eṣa pṛṣṭo 'smābhir na jalpati hanti%}
<div n="lb"/>{%pratyuta pāṣāṇaiḥ,%} this one questioned by us does
<div n="lb"/>not speak, but rather throws stones at us). {%Uta vā,%}
<div n="lb"/>or else, and (e. g. {%samudrād uta vā purīṣāt,%} from
<div n="lb"/>the sea or from the moisture in the air); {%vā-uta%}
<div n="lb"/>{%vā%} or {%utāho vāpi-vā,%} either — or; {%uta-uta,%} both — 
<div n="lb"/>and (e. g. {%uta balavān uta abalaḥ,%} both the strong
<div n="lb"/>and the weak); {%kim-uta vā,%} whether — or else.
