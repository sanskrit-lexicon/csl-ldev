{#तामस#}¦ {%tāmasa, as, ī, am%} (fr. {%tamas%}), dark,
of or belonging to darkness; affected by or apper-
taining to the third quality or that of darkness (vice,
ignorance, &c.); ignorant, vicious, malignant; relating
to Manu Tāmasa; ({%as%}), m. a malignant or mis-
chievous person, a villain, an incendiary; a snake;
an owl; N. of the fourth Manu; N. of one of the
attendants of Śiva; ({%ī%}), f. night; sleep; an epithet
of Durgā; N. of a river, {%= jaṭāmāṃsī%} (in this sense
a wrong reading for {%tāpasī%}); ({%am%}), n. darkness
[cf. {%andha-tāmasa%}]; {%tāmasī tanūs,%} the form as-
sumed by the Deity for the destruction of the world;
{%tāmasī śaktis,%} the faculty of Tamas. {%—Tāmasa-%}
{%kīlaka, as,%} m., N. of certain astronomical Ketus.
{%—Tāmasa-guṇa, as,%} m. the quality of darkness;
see {%guṇa%}. {%—Tāmasa-līna, as, ā, am,%} (in the
Sāṅkhya phil.) one of the forms of {%a-tuṣṭi,%} dissatis-
faction. {%—Tāmasa-vana, am,%} n. ‘the dark wood,’
N. of a forest.
