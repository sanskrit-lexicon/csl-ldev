<div n="P">Bhagavān (Nārāyaṇa) told the seven
<div n="lb">sages (Marīci, Atri and others 12. 322. 27)
<div n="lb">that the {%śāstra%} composed by them in a
<div n="lb">hundred thousand {%ślokas%} and from which the
<div n="lb">{%dharma%} proceeds is one which is approved
<div n="lb">by (i. e. is in accordance with) the Ṛks,
<div n="lb">Yajus and Sāmans, as also the Atharvāṅgi-
<div n="lb">rasas ({%kṛtaṃ śatasahasraṃ hi ślokānām%}
<div n="lb">{%idam uttamam/ … yasmād dharmaḥ%}
<div n="lb">{%pravartate// … ṛgyajuḥsāmabhir juṣṭam%}
<div n="lb">{%atharvāṅgirasais tathā%}) 12. 322. 36-37; when
<div n="lb">Kṛṣṇa asked Arjuna to insult Yudhiṣṭhira
<div n="lb">which was as good as ‘killing’ him, he told
<div n="lb">Arjuna that his instruction was according
<div n="lb">to the Atharvāṅgirasī Śruti which was the
