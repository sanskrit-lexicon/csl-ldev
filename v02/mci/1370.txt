<div n="lb">{@kuñjaḥ@} m.: Name of a {%tīrtha.%}
[Page377-a+ 40]
<div n="lb">{@A.@} Location: On the river Sarasvatī in
<div n="lb">the Kurukṣetra ({%tato naimiṣakuñjaṃ ca%}
<div n="lb">{%samāsādya … /ṛṣayaḥ … kurukṣetraṃ gatāḥ%}
<div n="lb">{%purā//tataḥ kuñjaḥ sarasvatyāṃ kṛto%}
<div n="lb">{%bharatasattama%}) 3. 81. 92-93; 9. 36. 48,
<div n="lb">50, 54. {@B.@} Origin: The sages once went
<div n="lb">to the Kurukṣetra to perform long sessions
<div n="lb">of sacrifice but were disappointed as there
<div n="lb">was no room for them; hence Sarasvatī
<div n="lb">created many Kuñjas there and returned;
<div n="lb">these Kuñjas together came to be known as the
<div n="lb">Naimiṣeya Kuñja ({%evaṃ sa kuñjo rājendra%}
<div n="lb">{%naimiṣeya iti smṛtaḥ%}) 9. 36. 48-51, 54; ({%tataḥ%}
<div n="lb">{%kuñjaḥ sarasvatyāṃ kṛto bharatasattama/%}
<div n="lb">{%ṛṣīṇām avakāśaḥ syād yathā … //%}) 3. 81.
<div n="lb">93. {@C.@} Description: Holy ({%puṇya%}) 3.
<div n="lb">82. 53; 13. 105. 45; 13. 151. 18; always
<div n="lb">holy ({%nityaṃ puṇyam%}) 3. 82. 57; ritually
<div n="lb">pure ({%medhya%}) 3. 82. 57; resorted to by
<div n="lb">the Siddhas ({%siddhaniṣevita%}) 3. 82. 53. {@D.@}
<div n="lb">Holy: (1) Naimiṣa looked upon as holy
<div n="lb">on the whole earth ({%pṛthivyāṃ naimiṣaṃ%}
<div n="lb">{%puṇyam%}) 3. 81. 173 (Nī., who takes
<div n="lb">{%naimiṣa%} to mean the religious merit acquired
<div n="lb">in the Naimiṣa forest (see the next entry),
<div n="lb">on Bom. Ed. 3. 83. 202: {%naimiṣaṃ%}
<div n="lb">{%naimiṣāraṇye kṛtam puṇyam pṛthivyām%}
<div n="lb">{%eva saṃtatisaṃpattisārvabhaumādipadaprā-%}
<div n="lb">{%ptihetur ity arthaḥ%}); (2) By taking a
<div n="lb">bath ({%abhiṣeka%}) in this {%tīrtha%} one who
<div n="lb">is controlled and lives on limited food
<div n="lb">secures the fruit of a Gavāmayana sacrifice
<div n="lb">and purifies his lineage up to the seventh
<div n="lb">generation 3. 82. 56; the bath {%(snātvā) in%}
<div n="lb">the {%tīrtha%} secures for the person the fruit of
<div n="lb">gifting a thousand cows 3. 81. 94; the bath
<div n="lb">({%upaspṛśya kṛtodakaḥ%}) secures for one who
<div n="lb">has controlled his sense organs the fruit of
<div n="lb">a Puruṣamedha 13. 26. 32; one who bathes
<div n="lb">({%vigāhya%}) in the ample waters of the Naimiṣa
[Page377-b+ 40]
<div n="lb">wakes up in the Vimāna praised by the
<div n="lb">Apsarases 9. 36. 35; (3) More than bathing
<div n="lb">in waters one should bathe in (i. e. practise)
<div n="lb">austerities at this {%tīrtha (?) (adbhyo%}
<div n="lb">{%bhūyaḥ plāvyaṃ tapasā vai śarīraṃ)%} 12.
<div n="lb">286. 37 (Nī., however, on Bom. Ed. 12.

<div n="lb">{%tatra āpaḥ (? apaḥ) tyaktvā%}); (4) The wise
<div n="lb">declare that one who abandons life at the
<div n="lb">Naimiṣa by fasting goes to heaven 3. 82. 57;
<div n="lb">(5) Those that are high-souled, have observed
<div n="lb">vows and who resort to this {%tīrtha%} become
<div n="lb">auspicious and reach the Goloka ({%? gavāṃ%}
<div n="lb">{%vimāne%} 13. 105. 44). 13. 105. 45 (for other
<div n="lb">details see {@Gayaśiras@}). {@E.@} Importance:
<div n="lb">(1) Brahman always lives there together
<div n="lb">with hosts of gods 3. 82. 53; (2) Finds

<div n="lb">18, 2. {@F.@} Past event: The sages living in
