{@Salilarājasya tīrtham@}¦ nt.: Name of a
{%tīrtha.%}
<div n="P">Situated at the place where the river
Sindhu meets the ocean ({%sāgarasya ca sindhoś%}
{%ca saṃgamaṃ prāpya%}); if one who has con-
trolled his mind ({%prayatamānasaḥ%}) bathes in
this {%tīrtha%} and gives offerings to manes,
gods and sages ({%tarpayitvā pitṝn devān%}
{%ṛṣīṃś ca%}) he shines with his own lustre
and goes to the world of Varuṇa ({%prāpnoti%}
{%vāruṇaṃ lokaṃ dīpyamānaḥ svatejasā%}); the
wise say ({%pravadanti manīṣiṇaḥ%}) that if one
worships the god Śaṅkukarṇeśvara there one
gets the reward of an Aśvamedha ten times
({%aśvamedhaṃ daśaguṇam%}) 3. 80. 85-87.
