{#अपि#}¦ {%<ab>ind.</ab>%} (Sometimes with the {#अ#}
dropped according to the opinion of
Bhāguri; {#वष्टि भागुरिरल्लोपमवाप्योरुपसर्गयोः#};
{#पिधा, पिधान#} &c.) {@1@} (Used with roots
and nouns in the sense of) Placing
near or over, taking towards, <lbinfo n="unit+ing"/>
uniting with; reaching or going up to,
proximity, nearness &c. [Cf. <ab>Gr.</ab>
{%epi%}, Zend {%api%}, <ab>Germ.</ab> and <ab>Eng.</ab> {%be%}].
(Note. {#अपि#}, as a prefix to roots, occurs
mostly in Veda, its place being taken
by {#अभि#} in classical literature). {@--2@} (As
a separable adverb or conjunction)
And, also, too, moreover, besides, in
addition, having a cumulative force
({#समुच्चय#}); {#अस्ति मे सोदरस्नेहोप्येतेषु#}; <ls>Ś. 1</ls>
on one's part, in one's turn;  <lbinfo n="vizRu#SarmaRApi"/>
{#विष्णुशर्मणापि राजपुत्राः पाटिताः#} <ls>Pt. 1</ls>;  <lbinfo n="rAjA#pi"/>
{#राजापि मुनिवाक्यमंगीकृत्यातिष्ठत्#} <ls>Dk. 2</ls>; {#अपि#}
{#-अपि#} or {#अपि च#} as well as, and also;
{#अपि स्तुहि, अपि सिंच#} <ls>P. I. 4. 96</ls> <ls>Sk.</ls>,
{#न नापि-न चैव, न वापि, नापि वा न चापि#}
neither-nor; {#न वापि काव्यं नवमित्यवद्यं#}
<ls>M. 1. 2</ls> nor; {#वापि#} or; {#अल्पोप्येवं महान्#}
{#वापि#} <ls>Ms. 3. 53</ls> whether small or great.
{@--3@} It is often used to express <lbinfo n="em+phasis"/>
emphasis in the sense of ‘too’, ‘even’
‘very’; {#विधुरपि विधियोगाद् ग्रस्यते#} <lbinfo n="rA#haRAsO"/>
{#राहणासौ#} <ls>H. 1</ls> the very moon;  <lbinfo n="yUyamapya#nena"/>
{#यूयमप्यनेन कर्मणा परिश्रांताः#} <ls>Ś. 1</ls> even you,
you also; {#अन्यदपि#} also another;
{#अद्यापि#} even, yet, still, even now;  <lbinfo n="idA#nImapi"/>
{#इदानीमपि#} even now; {#मुहूर्तमपि#} even
for a moment, for one moment at
least; {#नाद्यापि#} not yet; {#यद्यपि#} though,
although, even if; {#तथापि#} still,
nevertheless, notwithstanding, yet;
sometimes {#यद्यपि#} is understood,
{#तथापि#} only being used; as in  <lbinfo n="ls:Ki. 1.+ 28."/>
<ls>Ki. 1. 28.</ls> {@--4@} Though (<ab>oft.</ab> translateable
by ‘even’, ‘even if’); {#सरसिजमनुविद्धं#}
{#शैवलेनापि रम्यं#} <ls>Ś. 1. 20</ls> though <lbinfo n="over+spread"/>
overspread &c.; {#इयमधिकमनोज्ञा वल्कलेनापि#}
{#तन्वी#} {%<ab>ibid.</ab>%} though in her bark dress;
{#बलवदपि शिक्षितानाम्#} <ls n="Ś.">1. 2</ls> though ever
so learned. In this sense {#अपि#} is most
frequently used by writers to show
real or imaginary opposition ({#विरोध#});
{#कृष्णमपि असुदर्शनं, पुष्पवत्यपि पवित्रा#} &c. {@--5@}
But, however. {@--6@} Used at the <lbinfo n="be+ginning"/>
beginning of sentences {#अपि#} introduces
a question; {#अपि सन्निहितोऽत्र कुलपतिः#}
<ls>Ś. 1</ls>; {#अपि क्रियार्थं सुलभं समित्कुशं… अपि#}
{#स्वशक्त्या तपसि प्रवर्तसे#} <ls>Ku. 5. 33</ls>, <ls n="Ku. 5.">34</ls>,
<ls n="Ku. 5.">35</ls>; {#अप्यग्रणीर्मंत्रकृतामृषीणां#} <lbinfo n="kuSAgrabu#dDe"/>
{#कुशाग्रबुद्धे कुशली गुरुस्ते#} <ls>R. 5. 4.</ls> {@--7@} Hope, <lbinfo n="ex+pectation"/>
[Page0117-b+ 58]
expectation (usually with the potential
mood); {#कृतं रामसदृशं कर्म । अपि जीवेत्स#}
{#ब्राह्मणशेशुः#} <ls>U. 2.</ls> I hope the Brāhmaṇa
boy comes to life. Note-In this
sense {#अपि#} is frequently used with
{#नाम#} and has the sense of ({%a%}) ‘is it
likely’, ‘may it be’; ({%b%}) <lbinfo n="‘per+haps"/>
‘perhaps’, ‘in all probability’ or ({%c%})
‘would that’, ‘I wish or hope’
that’; {#अपि नाम#} <lbinfo n="kulapatoriyamasavarRa#kzetrasaMBavA"/>
{#कुलपतोरियमसवर्णक्षेत्रसंभवा स्यात्#} <ls>Ś. 1</ls>; <ls>Ś. 7</ls>; {#तदपि नाम#}
{#मनागवतीर्णोसि रतिरमणबाणगोचरं#} <ls>Māl. 1</ls>
perhaps, in all probability; {#अपि नाम#}
{#तयोः कल्याणिनोः अभिमतः पाणिग्रहः#} <lbinfo n="syA#t"/>
{#स्यात्#} {%<ab>ibid.</ab>%}; {#अपि नाम रामभद्रः पुनरपीदं#} <lbinfo n="vanamalaM#kuryAt"/>
{#वनमलंकुर्यात्#} <ls>U. 2</ls>; ‘is it likely’, ‘I wish’;
{#यथा वनज्योत्स्नानुरूपेण पादपेन संगता#}
{#अपि नाम एवमहमप्यात्मनोऽनुरूपं वरं#}
{#लभेयेति#} <ls>Ś. 1</ls> would that; {#अपि नामाहं#}
{#पुरूरवा भवेयं#} <ls>V. 2</ls> I wish I were P.
{@--8@} Affixed to interrogative words
{#अपि#} makes the sense indefinite,
‘any’, ‘some’; {#कोपि#} some one; {#किमपि#}
something; {#कुत्रापि#} somewhere; {#कदापि#}
at any time; {#कथमपि#} any how &c.
{#केऽपि एते प्रवयसः त्वां दिदृक्षवः#} <ls>U. 4</ls>
some people. It may often be
translated by ‘unknown’, <lbinfo n="‘indescrib+able"/>
‘indescribable,’ ‘inexpressible’ ({#अनिर्वाच्य#});
{#व्यतिषजति पदार्थानांतरः कोपि हेतुः#}  <lbinfo n="ls:U.+ 6. 12"/>
<ls>U. 6. 12</ls>; {#तत्तस्य किमपि द्रव्यं यो हि यस्य#}
{#प्रियो जनः#} <ls n="U.">2. 19</ls>; <ls>Mu. 3. 22</ls>; <ls>K. 143</ls>;
{#कोपि महिमा स्यात्#} <ls>U. 6</ls>, <ls n="U.">6. 11</ls>, <ls n="U.">7. 12</ls>;
<ls>Māl. 1. 26</ls>; <ls>R. 1. 46.</ls> {@--9@} After words
expressing number {#अपि#} has the
sense of ‘totality’, ‘all’; {#चतुर्णामपि#}
{#वर्णानां#} of all the 4 castes; {#सर्वैरपि#}
{#राज्ञां प्रयोजनं#} <ls>Pt. 1.</ls> {@--10@} It sometimes
expresses ‘doubt’ or ‘uncertainty,’
‘fear’ ({#शंका#}); {#अपि चोरो भवेत्#} <ls>G. M.</ls>
there is perhaps a thief. {@--11@} (with
pot. mood) It has the sense of {#संभावना#}
‘possibility’, ‘supposition’; <ls>P. I. 4. 96</ls>;
<ls n="P.">III. 3. 154</ls>; {#अपि स्तुयाद्विष्णुं, अपि#} <lbinfo n="stuyA#drAjAnaM"/>
{#स्तुयाद्राजानं, अपि गिरिं शिरसा भिंद्यात्#} <ls>Sk.</ls>;
{#सोयमपि सिंचेत्सहस्रं द्राक्षाणां क्षणेनैकेन#}
<ls>Dk. 127.</ls> {@--12@} Contempt, <lbinfo n="cen+sure"/>
censure, or reproof; <ls>P. I. 4. 96</ls>,  <lbinfo n="ls:III. 3.+142"/>
<ls n="P.">III. 3. 142</ls>; {#धिग्देवदत्तमपि स्तुयाद्वृषलं; धिग्जाल्मं#}
{#देवदत्तमपि सिंचेत् पलांडुं; अपि जायां#} <lbinfo n="tya#jasi"/>
{#त्यजसि जातु गणिकामाधत्से गर्हितमेतत्#} <ls>Sk.</ls>
shame to, &c. or fie upon, Devadatta
&c. {@--13@} It is also used with the <lbinfo n="Im+perative"/>
Imperative mood to mark ‘indifference
on the part of the speaker’, where
he permits another to do as he likes,
({#अन्ववसर्ग#} or {#कामचारानुज्ञा#}, the imperative
being softened;) {#अपि स्तुहि#} <ls>Sk.</ls> you

may praise (if you like); {#अपि स्तुह्यपि#}
{#सेधास्मांस्तथ्यमुक्तं नराशन#} <ls>Bk. 8. 92.</ls>
{@--14@} {#अपि#} is sometimes used as a <lbinfo n="parti+cle"/>
particle of exclamation, {@--15@} Rarely in the
sense of ‘therefore,’ ‘hence’ ({#अत एव#}).
{@--16@} Used as a separable preposition
with <ab>gen.</ab> it is said to express the
sense of a word understood ({#पदार्थ#}),
and is treated as a {#कर्मप्रवचनीय#}  <lbinfo n="ls:P. I.+4"/>
<ls>P. I. 4. 96</ls>; the example usually given is
{#सर्पिषोपि स्यात्#} where some word like
{#बिंदुरपि#} ‘a drop,’ ‘a little’ &c.
has to be understood, ‘there may
perhaps be a drop of ghee,’ ‘I <lbinfo n="pre+sume"/>
presume there may be at least a drop’
&c. {#अपि संभावनाप्रश्नशंकागर्हासमुच्चये ।#} <lbinfo n="taTAyu#ktapadArTezu"/>
{#तथायुक्तपदार्थेषु कामचारक्रियासु च ॥#} <ls>Viśva.</ls>; {#अपिः#}
{#पदार्थसंभावनान्ववसर्गगर्हासमुच्चयेषु#} <ls>P. I. 4. 96.</ls>
