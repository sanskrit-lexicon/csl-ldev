{#अन्योन्य#}¦ {%<ab>a.</ab>%} [{#अन्य-कर्मव्यतिहारे द्वित्वं#},
{#पूर्वपदे सुश्च#}] One another, each other,
mutual (treated like a pronoun).
In many cases the use of this word 
[Page0100-b+ 57]
corresponds to the use of the word
‘each other’ or ‘one another’ in
English; {#अन्योन्यं ताडयतः#} <ls>Mk. 9</ls>
they strike each other ({#अन्यः अन्यं#}
{#ताडयति#}). Thus {#अन्यः#} may be regarded
as the subject and {#अन्यं#} as the object
of the verb, as in English. The
second {#अन्य#} may, therefore, in many
cases stand in the <ab>instr.</ab>, <ab>gen.</ab>, or
<ab>loc.</ab> cases; {#अन्योन्यैराहताः संतः#} <lbinfo n="sasvanu#rBImaniHsvanAH"/>
{#सस्वनुर्भीमनिःस्वनाः#} <ls>Rām.</ls>; {#अन्योन्यस्य#} <lbinfo n="vyati#lunaMti"/>
{#व्यतिलुनंति#} <ls>P. I. 3. 16</ls> <ls>Sk.</ls> But there are
several instances, especially when
{#अन्योन्य#} enters into compound, in
which the first {#अन्य#} loses all its
nominative force and becomes a sort
of oblique case, or an irregular com
pound of {#अन्य#} and {#अन्य#}, see <lbinfo n="ls:P. VIII.+1"/>
<ls>P. VIII. 1. 12</ls> <ls>Sk.</ls>; {#अन्योन्यस्याव्यभीचारः#}  <lbinfo n="ls:Ms.+ 9. 101"/>
<ls>Ms. 9. 101</ls>; <ab>oft.</ab> in <ab>comp.</ab> and translated
by ‘mutual’, ‘reciprocal’, ‘mutually’;
{#°शोभाजननात्#} <ls>Ku. 1. 42</ls>; so {#°कलह#}, <lbinfo n="°darSa#na"/>
{#°दर्शन#}, &c. {#--न्यं#} {%<ab>ind.</ab>%} Mutually. {#--न्यं#} (In
<ab>Rhet.</ab>) A figure of speech, the <lbinfo n="‘Recipro+cal"/>
‘Reciprocal’, in which two things do the same
act to each other;  <lbinfo n="anyonyamuBayoreka#kriyAyAH"/>
{#अन्योन्यमुभयोरेकक्रियायाः करणं मिथः । त्वया सा शोभते तन्वी#}
{#तया त्वमपि शोभसे । रजन्या शोभते चंद्रश्चंद्रेणापि#}
{#निशीथिनी#} <ls>S. D. 724.</ls> {@--Comp.@} {#--अध्यासः#}
reciprocal attribution of identity ( <lbinfo n="a#nyonyatAdAtmyAropaH"/>
{#अन्योन्यतादात्म्यारोपः#}); {#जलव्योम्ना घटाकाशो यथा#}
{#सर्वस्तिरोहितः । तथा जीवे च कूटस्थः#} <lbinfo n="sonyo#nyADyAsa"/>
{#सोन्योन्याध्यास उच्यते ॥#} {#--अपहृत#} {%<ab>a.</ab>%} taken
from one another, taken secretly.
{#--अभावः#} mutual non-existence or
negation; one of the two main kinds
of {#अभाव#}; it is reciprocal negation of
identity, essence, or respective <lbinfo n="pecu+liarity"/>
peculiarity, and is equivalent to difference
({#भेद#}); {#तादात्म्यसंबंधावच्छिन्नप्रतियोगिकोऽ#} <lbinfo n="nyo#nyABAvaH"/>
{#न्योन्याभावः#}, as {#घटः पटो न भवति#}; it exists
between two notions which have no
property in common. {#--आश्रय#} {%<ab>a.</ab>%}
mutually dependent. ({#--यः#}) mutual or
reciprocal dependence, support, or
connection; reciprocal relation of
cause and effect (a term in Nyāya).
{#--उक्तिः#} {%<ab>f.</ab>%} conversation. {#--पक्षनयनं#}
transposition of numbers from one
side to another. {#--भेदः#} mutual <lbinfo n="dis+sension"/>
dissension or enmity; so {#°कलह#}. {#--मिथुनं#}
mutual union. {#--विभागः#} mutual
partition of an inheritance made
by the sharers (without the presence
of any other party). {#--वृत्तिः#} {%<ab>f.</ab>%} mutual
