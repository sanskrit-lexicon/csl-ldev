<L>8984<pc>0333-a<k1>उपसर्गः<k2>उपसर्गः
{#उपसर्गः#}¦ {@1@} Sickness, disease,
change occasioned by a disease; also
a disease superinduced on another;
{#क्षीणं हन्युश्चोपसर्गाः प्रभूताः#} <ls>Suśr.</ls>
{@--2@} Misfortune, trouble, calamity,
injury, harm; {#प्रशमिताशेषोपसर्गाः#}
{#प्रजाः#} <ls>Ratn. 1. 10</ls>; {#सोपसर्गं वो नक्षत्रं#}  <lbinfo n="ls:M.+4."/>
<ls>M. 4.</ls> {@--3@} Portent, natural phenomenon
foreboding evil. {@--4@} An eclipse.
{@--5@} An indication or symptom of
death. {@--6@} Addition. {@--7@} Possession
by an evil spirit. {@--8@} A preposition 
[Page0333-b+ 56]
prefixed to roots; {#निपाताश्चादयो ज्ञेयाः#}
{#प्रादयस्तूपसर्गकाः । द्योतकत्वात् क्रियायोगे#}
{#लोकादवगता इमे ॥#} {%Upasargas%} are 20
in number: {#--प्र, परा, अप, सम्#},
{#अनु, अव, निस्#} or {#निर्, दुस्#} or {#दुर्, वि, आ#}
{#(ङ्), नि, अधि, अपि, अति, सु, उत्, अभि#},
{#प्रति, परि, उप#}; or 22 if {#निस्-निर्#} and {#दुस्-दुर्#}
be taken as separate words. There
are two theories as to the character
of these prepositions. According to
one theory roots have various <lbinfo n="mean+ings"/>
meanings in themselves ({#अनेकार्था हि धातवः#});
when prepositions are prefixed to
them they simply bring to light
those meanings already existent but
hidden in them, but they do not
{%express%} them, being meaningless
themselves; <ab>cf.</ab> <ls>Śi. 10. 15</ls>: {#--संतमेव#}
{#चिरमप्रकृतत्वादप्रकाशितमदिद्युतदंगे । विभ्रमं#} <lbinfo n="ma#DumadaH"/>
{#मधुमदः प्रमदानां धातुलीनमुपसर्ग इवार्थम् ॥#}.
According to the other theory <lbinfo n="pre+positions"/>
prepositions {%express%} their own <lbinfo n="inde+pendent"/>
independent meanings; they modify,
intensify, and sometimes entirely
alter, the senses of roots; <ab>cf.</ab> <ls>Sk.</ls>:  <lbinfo n="--"/>
--{#उपसर्गेण धात्वर्थो बलादन्यत्र नीयते ।#} <lbinfo n="prahArAhArasaM#hAravihAraparihAravat"/>
{#प्रहाराहारसंहारविहारपरिहारवत् ॥#} <ab>cf.</ab> also {#धात्वर्थं बाधते#}
{#कश्चित्कश्चित्तमनुवर्तते । तमेव विशिनष्ट्यन्य#} <lbinfo n="upa#sargagatistriDA"/>
{#उपसर्गगतिस्त्रिधा ॥#} (The latter theory <lbinfo n="ap+pears"/>
appears to be more correct. For a fuller
exposition see Nirukta).
