{#वेदः#}¦ [{#विद्-अच् घञ् वा#}] {@1@} <lbinfo n="Know+ledge"/>
Knowledge. {@--2@} Sacred knowledge, holy
learning, the scriptures of the
Hindus. (Originally there were 
[Page1010-b+ 57]
only three {%Vedas%}: {#ऋग्वेद, यजुर्वेद#} and
{#सामवेद#}, which are collectively called
{#त्रयी#} ‘the sacred triad’; but a fourth,
the {#अथर्ववेद#}, was subsequently <lbinfo n="add+ed"/>
added to them. Each of the {%Vedas%} has
two distinct parts, the {%Mantra%} or
{%Saṃhitā%} and {%Brāhmaṇa%}. According
to the strict orthodox faith of the
Hindus the Vedas are {%a-paurusheya%},
‘not human compositions’, being
supposed to have been directly
revealed by the Supreme Being
Brahman, and are called ‘Śruti’ {%<ab>i. e.</ab>%}
‘what is heard or revealed’, as <lbinfo n="dis+tinguished"/>
distinguished from ‘Smṛti’, {%<ab>i. e.</ab>%} ‘what
is remembered or is the work of
human origin’; see {#श्रुति, स्मृति#} also;
and the several sages, to whom the
hymns of the Vedas are ascribed,
are, therefore, called {#द्रष्टारः#} ‘seers’,
and not {#कर्तारः#} or {#स्रष्टारः#} ‘composers’).
{@--3@} A bundle of Kuśa grass;  <lbinfo n="ls:Ms. 4.+ 36."/>
<ls>Ms. 4. 36.</ls> {@--4@} <ab>N.</ab> of Viṣṇu. {@--5@} A part of a
sacrifice ({#यज्ञांग#}). {@--6@} Exposition,
comment, gloss. {@--7@} A metre. {@--8@}
Acquisition, gain, wealth (<ab>Ved.</ab>).
