{#अप्सरस्#}¦ {%<ab>f.</ab>%} ({#--राः --रा#}). [{#अद्भ्यः#} <lbinfo n="sa#raMti"/>
{#सरंति उद्गच्छंति, सृ-असुन्#} <ls>Uṇ. 4. 236</ls>;
<ab>cf.</ab> <ls>Rām.</ls> {#अप्सु निर्मथनादवे#} <lbinfo n="rasAttasmAdvara#striyaH"/>
{#रसात्तस्माद्वरस्त्रियः । उत्पेतुर्मनुजश्रेष्ठ तस्मादप्सरसोऽभवन्#}]
{@1@} A class of female divinities or <lbinfo n="celes+tial"/>
celestial damsels who reside in the sky
and are regarded as the wives of the
Gandharvas. They are very fond of
bathing, can change their shapes,
and are endowed with superhuman
power ({#प्रभाव#}). They are called  <lbinfo n="sva#rveSyAH"/>
{#स्वर्वेश्याः#} and are usually described as
the servants of Indra, who, when
alarmed by the rigorous austerities
of some mighty sage, sends down
one of them to disturb his penance,
and her mission is generally <lbinfo n="success+ful"/>
successful; <ab>cf.</ab> {#या तपोविशेषपरिशंकितस्य#} <lbinfo n="suku#mAraM"/>
[Page0124-b+ 57]
{#सुकुमारं प्रहरणं महेंद्रस्य#} <ls>V. 1.</ls> They are also
said to covet heroes who die <lbinfo n="glori+ously"/>
gloriously on the battle-field; <ab>cf.</ab>  <lbinfo n="ls:R. 7.+ 53."/>
<ls>R. 7. 53.</ls> Bāṇa mentions 14 different <lbinfo n="fa+milies"/>
families of these nymphs (see <ls>K. 136</ls>).
The word is usually said to be in
<ab>pl.</ab> ({#स्त्रियां बहुष्वप्सरसः#}); but the singular,
as also the form {#अप्सरा#}, sometimes
occur; {#नियमविघ्नकारिणी मेनका नाम अप्सराः#}
{#प्रेषिता#} <ls>Ś. 1</ls>; {#एकाप्सरः#} &c. <ls>R. 7. 53</ls> and see
<ls>Malli.</ls> thereon; {#अनप्सरेव प्रतिभासि#} <ls>V. 1.</ls> {@--2@}
Direction or the intermediate point
of the compass ({#दिक् च उपदिक् च#}).
{@--Comp.@} {#--तीर्थं#} <ab>N.</ab> of a sacred pool
in which the Apsarasas bathe; <lbinfo n="pro+bably"/>
probably it is the name of a place, see
<ls>Ś. 6.</ls> {#--पतिः#} lord of the Apsarasas,
epithet of Indra.
