{#क#}] <ab>N.</ab> of the daughter of <lbinfo n="Viśvā+mitra"/>
Viśvāmitra by the nymph Menakā who
was sent down by Indra to <lbinfo n="dis+turb"/>
disturb the sage's austerities. [When
Menakā went up to the heaven she left
the child in a solitary forest where she
was taken care of by ‘Śakuntas’ or
birds, whence she was called <lbinfo n="“Śakun+talā"/>
“Śakuntalā”. She was afterwards found by
the sage Kaṇva and brought up as his 
[Page1028-b+ 57]
own daughter. When Duṣyanta in the
course of his hunting came to the sage's
hermitage, he was fascinated by her
charms and prevailed on her to become
his wife by the Gandharva form of <lbinfo n="mar+riage"/>
marriage; (see Duṣyanta). She bore to
him a son named Bharata, who became
a universal monarch, and gave his name
to India which came to be called <lbinfo n="Bhara+tavarsha"/>
Bharatavarsha].
