{%a secret apartment%} or {%chamber%}; acc. to Speyer Av ii.158.10
n., {%a subterranean room%}. Several times mss. (of Av, Divy)
present avaraka, which might be regarded as a case of
haplology or haplography; but twice, at least, Pali seems
to read (a)varaka instead of ovaraka: jāto (')varake Jāt.
i.391.21 and Vv comm. 158.14 (PTSD would understand
jāt' ovarake; both prose): KP 71.1 (prose) gṛhe vā layane
vā avavarake vā; Mmk 519.27 (prose) guhye pradeśe
avavarake vā; 534.10 (vs) prachanne…svagṛhe vāvava-
rake 'pi ca (meter corrupt); Av ii.54.5 avarakaṃ praviṣṭā
udbandhanahetoḥ (to hang herself); here text with ms.
avara°; 55.7 avavarakam (so ms., text avara°) avabhā-
samānā; 158.10 avavarakaṃ praviśa, and °kaṃ praviṣṭā;
Divy 471.8 apavarakaṃ (no v.l.) prāviśat; avavarake (or
avarake) strī prasūtā Divy 471.9 (text apav°, but no ms.
has -p-; they point to avarake or avavarake); 479.25
(text apav°, but all mss. avarake), 26--27 (text apav°
with 1 ms., 2 mss. (a)varake, actually varake after -e).
