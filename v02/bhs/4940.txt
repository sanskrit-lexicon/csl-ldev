{@kuṇāla@}¦, or {@kunāla@}; see also {@koṇāla@}; m., ({@1@}) (= Pali
kuṇāla; not in Pkt. or Skt. in this sense), a kind of bird,
in Pali apparently {%the Indian cuckoo%}, Skt. kokila: kuṇ°
Mvy 4880; LV 40.5; 286.13; 301.14; Av ii.201.2, 4; RP
26.15; Śikṣ 329.6; Gv 100.26; 194.12; kun° LV 162.19--20
(most mss. kuṇ°) and in a passage found only in ms. H
but confirmed by Tib. (ku na la; ms. H kunāra), see Crit.
App. on LV 11.3; RP 41.9; Divy 406.6 ff.; ({@2@}) (in this
sense not recorded in Pali or Skt., but AMg. Kuṇāla),
n. of a son of King Aśoka, so named because his eyes
were like those of the bird acc. to Av ii.201.4 ff. (Kuṇ°)
and Divy 406.14 ff.; other occurrences Divy 403.8 ff.;
405.14 (in Divy always Kun°); Kunālāvadāna, colophon
to Divy chap. 27, Divy 419.13.
