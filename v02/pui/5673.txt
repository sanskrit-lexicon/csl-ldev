{%Trivarga%}¦ — three ends of human endeavour, Dharma,
Artha and Kāma; was taught to Prahlāda by his tutors.
This did not appeal to him. It should be a means to realise
Hari, according to Prahlāda.<sup>1</sup> Even the householder, it is
said, should not exert himself too much for Trivarga. But
it is generally observed by householders.<sup>2</sup> A Bhikṣu is
expected to discard it.<sup>3</sup> To one devoid of Dharma, the other
two, Kāma and Artha, are of one use.<sup>4</sup>
