{%Nara%}¦ (I) {%(Nārāyaṇa)%} — an {%avatār%} of Viṣṇu, born of
Dharma and Mūrtī, a daughter of Dakṣa; Ādiśeṣa form of
Hari, distinguished for tapas;<sup>1</sup> a friend and associate of
Nārāyaṇa said to have performed tapas at Badarī;<sup>2</sup> seeing
the sage's penance Indra got afraid and sent the God of
Love and the Apsarasas to disturb his contemplation. Nara
invited him and was hospitable by creating a number of
beautiful women who served them; Nara asked them to
choose one among them as an ornament of Heaven. So
they took Ūrvaśi and narrated to Indra the superior powers
of the sage;<sup>3</sup> was seen with Nārāyaṇa by Mārkaṇḍeya and
was praised by him.<sup>4</sup>
