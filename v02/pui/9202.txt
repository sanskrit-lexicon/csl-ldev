{%Bṛhaspati-(Vedhas)%} (I)¦ — a son of Angiras by Sunīpā;
(also Prajāpati-{%Vā. P.%}); a Brahmiṣṭha famous in Svāro-
ciṣāntara; a sage of the Svārociṣa epoch,<sup>1</sup> born of Phalguni
in Cakṣuṣāntara; a Mantra-Brāhmaṇa; identified with Bha-
gavān,<sup>2</sup> lord of Viśravas and Angiras; sister, Yogasiddha,
wife of Prabhāsa; wife Tārā who was taken away by Soma;
she came back after Śiva's war with him and Brahmā's inter-
vention; father of Kaca and Bharadvāja;<sup>3</sup> had sexual
[Page2-501+ 52]
intercourse with Mamata (brother's wife), when she
was pregnant; cursed the baby in the womb for obstructing
the entry of his semen. Bharadvāja was born who was
brought up by the Maruts;<sup>4</sup> appointed Purohita of the Gods
and got back to life the Gods killed by the Asuras;<sup>5</sup> had faith
in {%Kriyāyoga;%}<sup>6</sup> served as calf for the sages (milked the cow-
earth-{%Vā. P.%}) to milk the Vedic lore from the earth and got
the essence of tapas; saciva of Indra; caused illusion for
<div n="P"/>^7 Ib. IV. 18. 14; M. 10. 17; Vā. 62. 138 and
177.
Rajo's sons and got back the place for Indra;<sup>8</sup> wielded over-
lordship over Indra and was the cause of his {%Yogakṣema;%}<sup>9</sup>
master of Mantras;<sup>10</sup> finding Śukra engaged for ten years
with Jayanti, Br. took the guise of Śukra and acted as Śukra
to the Asuras; when Śukra spoke of the deceit, they would
not listen to him and hence were cursed; Bṛhaspati threw
off his guise and went to his place;<sup>11</sup> appeared before the
Asuras in the guise of Kāvya;<sup>12</sup> fought with Uśanas or Śukra
in the Devāsura war;<sup>13</sup> advised Indra besieged by Bali, to
abandon the city and await better times<sup>14</sup> was not accorded
due welcome by Indra in his pride and hence Bṛhaspati
abandoned the Devas when the Asuras rose up in arms
against them; after consulting him on Tāraka's advance,
Indra resolved on war;<sup>15</sup> blessed Umā with a son; blessed
the Deva host in the Tārakāmaya.<sup>16</sup>
