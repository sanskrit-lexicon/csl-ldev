{%Pratimālakṣaṇa%}¦ — rules relating to the carving of
different sizes of images and of gods to be worshipped in
houses: relative proportions of a Navatāla image;<sup>1</sup> Speciali-
ties of feminine forms;<sup>2</sup> Images of other sizes including Rāma,
Varāha, Narasimha, of Śiva with ten arms, four or eight
arms: defective forms to be condemned;<sup>3</sup> Specialities of
Ardhanārīśvara or Umāmaheśvara;<sup>4</sup> of Śivanārāyaṇa;<sup>5</sup> of
Brahmā and Kārttikeya;<sup>6</sup> of Kātyāyanī;<sup>7</sup> of Indra;<sup>8</sup> images of
Mother goddesses.<sup>9</sup> Pedestals of different images — A pedestal
is divided into 16 parts; ten kinds of pedestals described:
made of stone, earth, wood or a mixture according as the
{%devata%} is made of;<sup>10</sup> The symbol of Śiva in the shape of a
phallus, the size depending on the size of the temple: made
of ruby, diamond, earth, wood, gold and stone according to
one's choice and means.<sup>11</sup>
