{%Śākalya (Devamitra)%}¦ — a son of Māṇḍukeya; divided the
Samhitā into five parts and transmitted them to five disciples
 — Vātsya, Mudgala, Śālīya, Gokhalya and Sīsira (Mudgala,
Golka, Khāliya, Matsya and Śośareya-{%Vā. P.%}).<sup>1</sup> A pupil of
Satyaśriya and a Śākapravartaka. During the sacrifice of
Janaka, there was a dispute as to who was the learned among
those present. The prize was won by Yājñavalkya but
Śākalya insulted him and was cursed.<sup>2</sup>
