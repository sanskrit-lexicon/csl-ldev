{%Sarasvatī%} (I)¦ — (Vāk Devī); Viśvarūpā, a mind-born
daughter of Brahmā who began to love her. Being criticised
by his sons for it, he gave up his body in shame. Presented
Pṛthu with a wreath, and was offered a wreath of pearls by
Viśvakarman. Gave away a string of beads to the Lord.
Gave Vīṇa to Kumāra; a Mother goddess.<sup>2</sup> The Goddess of
Learning;<sup>3</sup> enshrined in the faces of Brahmā,<sup>4</sup> Gāyatrā<sup>5</sup> in
the 23rd Kalpa; constitutes 32 akṣaras; is prakṛti and Gaulī,<sup>6</sup>
source of all worlds;<sup>7</sup> of four feet.<sup>8</sup> Presented to Skanda a
great vīra.<sup>9</sup>
