{%Lakṣmaṇā%} (I)¦ — a daughter of the king of the Madras;
heard of Kṛṣṇa's glory from Nārada and set her mind on
[Page3-110+ 43]
him. It was arranged that in a {%svayamvara%} he, who hit a
target (fish) correctly by looking at its reflection in the water
below was to wed her. All tried in vain but Kṛṣṇa succeeded
and married her; when she was taken in a chariot, other
kings attacked Kṛṣṇa; he vanquished all of them, and reached
Dvārakā. Addressed Draupadī at length on her marriage;<sup>1</sup>
sons, Gātravat and others.<sup>2</sup>
