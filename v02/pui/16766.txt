{%Soma%}¦ (II) (Candra s.v.) — a Lokapāla with his city
Vibhāvarī on the north of Meru;<sup>1</sup> Lord of stars, and one face
of Śiva: served as calf when sages and Pṛthu milked the
earth-cow;<sup>2</sup> his rays gave rise to certain Apsara clans:
Kaśyapa made him king of Brahmans;<sup>3</sup> a Śrāddhadeva:
Lord of Pitṛs: (1/8) {%amśa%} of Śiva coming out of the {%dhāma%} that
issued along with tears of Śiva: Nine women desired and
enjoyed his company;<sup>4</sup> one of the nine grahas with white
colour;<sup>5</sup> in the Devāsura wars, was vanquished by māyā of
Kālanemī, took part in the Tārakāmaya, helped Varuṇa at
the suggestion of Indra;<sup>6</sup> the dot in him is the earth's
shadow;<sup>7</sup> propitiated in the installation of an image and in
house building;<sup>8</sup> born from the ocean of milk;<sup>9</sup> world of;<sup>10</sup>
nectar of, drunk by Pitṛs and gods;<sup>11</sup> feeds rivers by causing
rain.<sup>12</sup>
