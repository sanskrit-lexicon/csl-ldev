{%Hāstināpura%}¦ — (also Hastināpura and Gajāhva);<sup>1</sup> visit-
ed by Kṛṣṇa often. Here Kṛṣṇa spent some months after
Bhīṣma's death and Yudhiṣṭhira's coronation. A Brahmana of
this city informed Kṛṣṇa of the banishment of the Pāṇḍavas
to forest;<sup>2</sup> visited by Akrūra,<sup>3</sup> and by Balarāma. The latter
got offended at the remark of the Kurus about his king and
threatened to lift up the city and throw it into the Ganges.<sup>4</sup>
Return of the Pāṇḍavas and others after visit to Syamanta
pañcaka; visited by Kṛtavarman<sup>5</sup> and by Satyabhāmā after
her father's death;<sup>6</sup> left by Vidura;<sup>7</sup> when this city was
washed away Kauśāmbī became capital;<sup>8</sup> capital of the Kurus
and Parīkṣit.<sup>9</sup>
