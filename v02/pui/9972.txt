{%Mathurā%} (I)¦ — founded by Śatrughna after killing
Lavaṇa;<sup>1</sup> capital of Subāhu and Śūrasena; here was celebrat-
ed the marriage of Vasudeva and Devakī when the couple
[Page2-604+ 46]
were the recipients of rich presents of elephants, horses, {%dāsis%}
and others; Kamsa joined in the festivities when a voice in
the sky said that her eighth son was to kill him;<sup>2</sup> visited by
Nanda to pay his annual tribute; left by Nanda;<sup>3</sup> capital of
the Yadus, besieged by Jarāsandha and his followers;
defended by the Yādavas on all the four sides; entered by
Kṛṣṇa after defeating Jarāsandha; second attempt of Jarā-
sandha on the city with Bāṇa's army; entered by Kṛṣṇa after
a third success over Jarāsandha;<sup>4</sup> Rājamārga and sabhā of;<sup>5</sup>
with a huge army Kṛṣṇa and Rāma returned to; besieged by
Yavana; abandoned by Kṛṣṇa in fear, for Dvārakā;<sup>6</sup> reached
by Rāma, Kṛṣṇa, Akrūra and others; Gopa's camp outside
the city; entered by the brothers and Gopas; the city describ-
ed; joy of the women at the sight of the brothers;<sup>7</sup> left by
Akrūra for Vraja by chariot, as also by Uddhava; Gopis'
enquiry about Kṛṣṇa. Uddhava's return to Mathurā from
Vraja.<sup>8</sup> reached by Nanda, Gopas and Gopis after Vasu-
deva's sacrifice at Syamantapañcaka;<sup>9</sup> Vajra anointed king
of, by Yudhiṣṭhira;<sup>10</sup> sometime ruled by seven Nāga kings;<sup>11</sup>
sacred to Devakī.<sup>12</sup> Vasudeva's residence in;<sup>13</sup> left by
Akrūra in a chariot as directed by Kamsa to fetch Kṛṣṇa
from Vraja; Kṛṣṇa's visit to, which travelling by chariot
took one day;<sup>14</sup> ruled by Kamsa and then Ugrasena;<sup>15</sup> evacuat-
ed for fear of Kālayavana and migrated to Dvārakā,<sup>16</sup>
temple of Hari at.<sup>17</sup>
