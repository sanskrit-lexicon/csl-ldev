{%Bharadvāja%} (II)¦ — a sage of the Vaivasvata epoch; a
{%yogi;%} son of Bṛhaspati arisen out of the sexual fluid at the
refusal of Mamatā to receive it; abandoned by his parents,
was brought up by the Maruts who gave him to Bharata who
wanted him; hence two castes Brahmanas and Vaiśyas sprang
from him; became a Kṣatriya;<sup>1</sup> residence at Govardhana
where he planted flowers and trees; lives with the sun for
part of the year;<sup>2</sup> a Ṛṣika; a Mantrakṛt;<sup>3</sup> a Pañcārṣeya; dva-
yāmuṣyāyana gotra;<sup>4</sup> no marital relations among the Bṛhas-
pati, Gārga and Bharadvāja lines.<sup>5</sup> Praised Śiva for burn-
ing Tripuram;<sup>6</sup> the 19th Veda-Vyāsa; Jaṭāmāli, the {%avatār%}
of the Lord.<sup>7</sup>
