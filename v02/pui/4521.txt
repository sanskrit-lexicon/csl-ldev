{%Cakravartins%}¦ — came into being in Treta and partook
the {%amśa%} of Hari to protect Dharma. Their seven {%ratnas%} — 
cakra, ratha, maṇi, sword, carma, Ketu, and nidhi (some
include wife, horse and elephant and leave out sword
and Ketu) and seven creatures: wife, purohita, senāni,
rathakrit, mantri, aśva and kalabha; had the attributes of
Viṣṇu; they enjoyed trivarga, fame and success, aiśvarya like
Aṇima and Prabhu śakti; learned and pure; entertained with
their prowess sages, gods, devils, men, etc.; bodily characte-
[Page1-573+ 29]
ristics of; legs with the symbol of wheel and fish, hands with
conch and lotus.<sup>1</sup> Reigned for a long time with daṇḍanīti.<sup>2</sup>
Vāli was a cakravartī. Arjuna (Kārtavīrya) was another.<sup>3</sup>
