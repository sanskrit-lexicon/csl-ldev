{%Agastya (Kumbhayoni)%} (III)¦ — born from a pitcher into
<div n="lb"/>which Mitra and Varuṇa dropped their {%vīrya%} at the sight
<div n="lb"/>of the charms of Urvaśī: a brother of Vasiṣṭha.<sup>1</sup> Dwarfed
<div n="lb"/>the Vindhyas, made a home in Laṅkā: seeing the universe
<div n="lb"/>troubled by Tāraka and other Asuras caused the ocean to
<div n="lb"/>dry up by drinking the waters and brought relief to the
<div n="lb"/>Devas: performed {%tapas%} seated on Mount Malaya with his
<div n="lb"/>wife Lopāmudrā: white in colour, with four hands, {%Akṣa%}
<div n="lb"/>{%mālā%} and {%Kamaṇḍalu,%} narrated the story of Ajāmila:<sup>2</sup> pre-
<div n="lb"/>sent at Rāma's abhiṣeka.<sup>2(a</sup> Visited by Paraśurāma, initiated
<div n="lb"/>him to the Kṛṣṇapremāmṛtam mantra which he heard
<div n="lb"/>from Śeṣa, the Nāga king. Spoke to him of the threefold
<div n="lb"/>{%bhakti%} and its characteristics.<sup>3</sup> A Brahmiṣṭha, versed in
<div n="lb"/>Vaikhānasa; a Devaṛṣi, versed in Vedic literature.<sup>4</sup>
<div n="lb"/>The guest of Ilvala who cooked Vātāpi for him.<sup>5</sup>
<div n="lb"/>Roaming about, he found all creatures engrossed in
<div n="lb"/>ignorance. Reaching Kāñcī, he prayed to Kāmākṣi and
<div n="lb"/>Ekāmraśiva. Here Hayagrīva Viṣṇu appeared before him
<div n="lb"/>and pointed out ways to redeem the ignorant millions. Was
<div n="lb"/>entertained with the story of Śakti by Hayagrīva.<sup>6</sup> Praised
<div n="lb"/>Śiva out to ruin Tripuram.<sup>7</sup> He who worships Agastya
<div n="lb"/>becomes lord of the seven worlds.<sup>8</sup>
