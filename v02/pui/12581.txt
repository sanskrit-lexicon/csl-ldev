<L>12581<pc>3-197<k1>वाराणसी<k2>वाराणसी
{%Vārāṇasī%}¦ — Kāśī: sacred to Hari;<sup>1</sup> fit for Śrāddha offer-
ing, and sacred to Lalitā;<sup>2</sup> the capital of Divodāsa devastated
by the Rākṣasa Kṣemaka, resulting in the change of capital
to Gomatī. The abode of Śiva and Umā after their marriage,
and hence the name Avimuktam Kṣetram;<sup>3</sup> the goddess
enshrined here is Viśālākṣī: a place of pilgrimage;<sup>4</sup> capital of
Rudraśṛeṇya of the Yadu race: city where Mārkaṇḍeya
lived.<sup>5</sup> Lord became Kṣetrapāla: the Yakṣa Harikeśa per-
formed penance here;<sup>6</sup> the place of Yogis and Siddhas: death
here is release from rebirth;<sup>7</sup> a siddha kṣetra where Śiva
and His consort live all the three yugas and make Avi-
muktam gṛham in Kaliyuga;<sup>8</sup> {%avatār%} of Lāngali at, in the
22nd dvāpara;<sup>9</sup> cursed by Nikumbha to become deserted for
1,000 years;<sup>10</sup> burnt down by Kṛṣṇa.<sup>11</sup>
