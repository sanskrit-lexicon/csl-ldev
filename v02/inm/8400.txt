etc.; the tīrtha {%Plakṣāvataraṇa(d%}). “Having taken his bath

{%Yudhiṣṭhira%} beheld all the worlds and {%Arjuna (Śvetavāhana%})
(III, {@129@}).—§ 410: {%Dakṣa%}, when engaged in a sacrifice
there, had blessed the spot, saying: “Those men that die on
this spot shall win a place in heaven.” {%Lomaśa%} pointed out to
{%Yudhiṣṭhira%} the {%Sarasvatī%}, the {%Oghavatī, Vinaśana (b), Cama-%}
{%sodbheda (c%}), the tīrtha of {%Sindhu (d), Prabhāsa (e), Viṣṇupada%},
{%Vipāśā (f), Kāśmīramaṇḍala (g%}), the gate of {%Mānasa%}, a gap
(? see Nīl.) opened by {%Rāma%} in the midst “of this mountain”.

{%Kuśavat%} with a lotus in the kuśa grass, and the hermitage of
