[{@Suvarṇaṣṭhīvi-sambhavopākhyāna(ṃ)@}]¦ (“the
<div n="lb">episode relating to the origin of Suvarṇaṣṭhīvin”). § 634{%b%}
<div n="lb">(Rājadh.): {%Parvata,%} after having taken counsel with {%Nārada,%}
<div n="lb">granted a boon to {%Sṛñjaya%} in the form of a son {%(Suvarṇa-%}
<div n="lb">{%ṣṭhīvin),%} but {%Indra%} would not let him be long-lived, and
<div n="lb">{%Parvata%} would not destine otherwise from partiality to {%Indra,%}
<div n="lb">but {%Nārada%} told {%Sṛñjaya%} to think of him. {%Nārada%} and
<div n="lb">{%Parvata%} left {%Sṛñjaya's%} presence. {%Indra%} slew {%Suvarṇaṣṭhīvin,%}
<div n="lb">causing his {%vajra%} to assume the form of a tiger, on the shores
<div n="lb">of the {%Bhāgīrathī. Sṛñjaya,%} in his distress, thought of
<div n="lb">{%Nārada,%} who recited to him the Ṣoḍaśarājop. (§ 632{%b%}), and
<div n="lb">brought the child back to life, with {%Indra's%} permission;
<div n="lb">{%Suvarṇaṣṭhīvin%} ruled for 1100 years, gratifying D. and P.,
<div n="lb">and procreated many sons ere he died (XII, {@31@}).
