<div n="lb">§ 543: Having slain {%Rāvaṇa, Rāma%} was worshipped by
<div n="lb">D., Ṛ., and G. uttering {%jaya;%} he gave {%Laṅkā%} to {%Vibhīṣaṇa,%}
<div n="lb">{%Avindhya%} came with {%Sītā,%} whom {%Rāma%} repudiated. {%Lakṣ-%}
<div n="lb">{%maṇa%} and the monkeys were sorrowful; {%Brahmán%} appeared
<div n="lb">on his chariot, etc. (<lang n="greek"></lang>); {%Sītā%} swore by {%Mātariśvan,%} etc.
<div n="lb">(<lang n="greek"></lang>), that she was innocent. A celestial voice was heard,
<div n="lb">{%Vāyu,%} etc. (<lang n="greek"></lang>) attested her innocence. {%Brahmán%} said that
<div n="lb">it was by his grace that {%Rāvaṇa%} (the enemy of D., G., N.,
<div n="lb">Y., Dā., M.-ṛ.) had been unslayable; but {%Sītā%} had been
<div n="lb">protected by the curse of {%Nalakūbara. Daśaratha%} commanded
<div n="lb">him to take back his wife and rule his kingdom at {%Ayodhyā,%}
<div n="lb">his exile of fourteen years was over. {%Rāma%} gave boons to
<div n="lb">{%Avindhya%} and {%Trijaṭā. Brahmán%} and the gods, headed by
<div n="lb">{%Indra,%} granted {%Rāma%} adherence to virtue and invincibility,
<div n="lb">and the monkeys were restored to life. {%Sītā%} granted to
<div n="lb">{%Hanūmat,%} that his life might last as long as the fame of
<div n="lb">{%Rāma,%} and gave him celestial wines and drinks. {%Mātali%}
<div n="lb">said: “Thou hast dispelled the sorrows of D., G., Y., As.,
<div n="lb">N., and men, and therefore all creatures with D., As., G.,
<div n="lb">Y., Rā., and Pn. will always speak of thee.” Having made
<div n="lb">arrangements for the protection of {%Laṅkā, Rāma,%} etc. (<lang n="greek"></lang>)
<div n="lb">recrossed the bridge riding on {%Puṣpaka,%} at the seashore he

<div n="lb">Then he entered {%Nandigrāma%} and saw {%Bharata%} and {%Śatrughna,%}

<div n="lb">{%Vāmadeva%} installed him in the sovereignty under the {%nakṣatra%}

<div n="lb">horse-sacrifices on the {%Gomatī%} (III, {@291@}).
