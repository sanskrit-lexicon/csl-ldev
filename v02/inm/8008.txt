<div n="lb">§ 190: When {%Gāndhārī%} had been with child a full year
<div n="lb">{%Kuntī%} invoked {%Dharma,%} who came seated on his chariot.
<div n="lb">She brought forth {%Yudhiṣṭhira (b).%} Similarly the birth of
<div n="lb">{%Bhīmasena (c)%} and {%Arjuna (d)%} are related in full (cf. § 157)—
<div n="lb">§ 191: Then {%Kuntī%} refused to have any more sons (I, {@123@}).
<div n="lb">—§ 192: Birth of {%Nakula%} and {%Sahadeva%} (cf. § 157); celestial
<div n="lb">voice. The {%ṛṣis%} on {%Śataśṛṅga%} gave the sons of {%Pāṇḍu%}
<div n="lb">names. They were born at intervals of one year from one
<div n="lb">another. They became great favourites of the {%ṛṣis%} and
<div n="lb">their wives, dwelling on {%Śataśrṅga%} (cf. v. 4864: {%Haimavate%}
<div n="lb">{%girau%}). {%Kuntī%} refused to help {%Madrī%} to get more children, as
<div n="lb">she had been deceived by her having two sons at once (I, {@124@}).
