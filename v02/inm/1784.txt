[{@Brahma-Rudra-saṃvāda(ḥ)@}],¦ (“the discourse
between Brahmán and Rudra”). § 717{%d%} (Nārāyaṇīya):
{%Vaiśampāyana%} said: In the midst of the ocean of milk there
is a mountain of golden splendour, named {%Vaijayanta%}, where
{%Brahmán%} (leaving his abode inhabited by D., As., Ṛ., G., and
Aps.) often used to sit thinking of {%Adhyātma%}; once, in days
of yore, his son {%Śiva%} (endued with high {%yoga%}), who had sprung
from his forehead, when proceeding along the sky saw him and
dropped down before him, and was instructed by him about
the many {%Puruṣas%} created by him (Brahmán), and the one
{%Puruṣa%}, who transcends all {%Puruṣas%} and is invisible, etc.
(XII, {@351@}). {%Brahmán%} discoursed on the indications of
{%Puruṣa (Mahāpuruṣa%}) (XII, {@352@}).
