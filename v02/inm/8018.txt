{@[Pāṇḍu-digvijaya(ḥ)]@}¦ (“subjection of the earth by
<div n="lb">Pāṇḍu”). § 177 (Sambhavap.): After some time {%Bhīṣma%}
<div n="lb">went to the capital of the {%Madra%} king {%Śalya%} (“the bull of
<div n="lb">the {%Bāhlīkas%}”), and according to the custom observed in
<div n="lb">that family and ordained by {%Svayambhū%} himself, he obtained
<div n="lb">his sister {%Mādrī%} for {%Pāṇḍu%} by giving great riches, and took
<div n="lb">her to {%Hāstinapura,%} where the nuptials were celebrated on an
<div n="lb">auspicious day and {%muhūrta.%} After thirty days {%Pāṇḍu%} started
<div n="lb">on his conquest of the world, and conquered the {%Daśārṇas%}
<div n="lb">{%(āgaskṛtaḥ),%} slew the {%Magadha%} king {%Dīrgha%} (who had offended
<div n="lb">against numerous kings) in {%Rājagṛha;%} then going into {%Mithilā%}
<div n="lb">he defeated the {%Videhas;%} then the {%Kāśis, Suhmas,%} and {%Puṇḍras,%}
<div n="lb">etc., and after having made all kings the vassals of the {%Kurus,%}
<div n="lb">he returned laden with much wealth to {%Hāstinapura,%} where
<div n="lb">people said: “They who before robbed the {%Kurus%} of both
<div n="lb">territory and wealth, have been made to pay tribute” by
<div n="lb">{%Pāṇḍu%} (I, {@113@}).
