{@Varāhāvatāra(ḥ)@}¦ (“Viṣṇu's incarnation as a boar”).
<div n="lb">§ 422 bis (Gandhamādanapr.). Asked by {%Yudhiṣṭhira%}
<div n="lb">{%Lomaśa%} related: In the {%Kṛta yuga,%} when the primeval deity
<div n="lb">{%(Ādidevaḥ purātanaḥ)%} assumed the function of {%Yama,%} the
<div n="lb">creatures did not die, and multiplied to such a degree that
<div n="lb">the {%Earth%} sank 100 {%yojanas.%} She applied to {%Nārāyaṇa%}
<div n="lb">(= Viṣṇu), who, in the shape of a boar with a single tusk
<div n="lb">{%(ekaśṛṅga),%} lifted her up by means of that. The gods,
<div n="lb">with the {%ṛṣis,%} became afraid of the agitation produced, but
<div n="lb">learnt the matter from {%Brahmán,%} and with him they went to
<div n="lb">{%Nandana,%} there {%Viṣṇu%} was sitting in the shape of a boar
<div n="lb">with {%Śrīvatsa%} on his breast, together with {%Suparṇa,%} and
<div n="lb">having seen him they returned home (III, {@142@}).
