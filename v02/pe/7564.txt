<div n="lb"/>the Pāṇḍavas beggars by defeating them in a false game
<div n="lb"/>of dice. Dhṛtarā ṭra stood as an accomplice to that.
<div n="lb"/>To play the game Dhṛtarāṣṭra constructed a palace
<div n="lb"/>and that palace was called Toraṇasph ṭika.
<div n="NI"/>That diamond-studded palace had a thousand pillars and
<div n="lb"/>a hundred entrances. It had crystal fortresses and
<div n="lb"/>golden thrones in all the chambers. This palace was
<div n="lb"/>called Dyūtabhavana also. (Chapter 56, Sabhā Parva).
