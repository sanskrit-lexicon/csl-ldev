MADRA¦ (M). An ancient place of habitation in Bhārata.
<div n="lb"/>This is situated near the river Jhelum. Mādrī,
<div n="lb"/>the wife of Pāṇḍu was a princess of this place. Bhīṣma
<div n="lb"/>went to Madra and brought Mādrī for Pāṇḍu. (Chapter
<div n="lb"/>112, Ādi Parva). At the time of Arjuna's birth there
<div n="lb"/>was a voice from heaven which said, “This child will
<div n="lb"/>grow up and capture many countries like Madra”.
<div n="lb"/>Aśvapati, father of Sāvitrī, was a king of Madra. Karṇa
<div n="lb"/>condemned Madra and Vāhīka as countries which had
<div n="lb"/>fallen low in virtue. (Chapter 44, Karṇa Parva).
