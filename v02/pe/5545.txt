PUṢKARA IV¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} This is a holy place situated
<div n="lb"/>36 Kilometres to the north of Ajmer. Brahmā once did
<div n="lb"/>penance there. There is a temple of Brahmā there.
<div n="lb"/>Padma Purāṇa gives a story about the origin of this
<div n="lb"/>tīrtha:—
<div n="NI"/>Once Brahmā came to a place holding a lotus. Then
<div n="lb"/>Brahmā saw Vajranābha who was engaged in penance
<div n="lb"/>for the destruction of the devas. Suddenly the lotus in
<div n="lb"/>the hands of Brahmā fell down making a thundering
<div n="lb"/>noise. The sound was so loud that its vibrations killed
<div n="lb"/>Vajranābha. From that day onwards the place was
<div n="lb"/>known as Puṣkara.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Arjuna spent the period after his pilgrimage in this
<div n="lb"/>tīrtha. (Śloka 14, Chapter 220, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Once sage Pulastya praised the greatness of
<div n="lb"/>Puṣkara. (Śloka 20, Chapter 82, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Sage Dhaumya praised the greatness of Puṣkara-
<div n="lb"/>tīrtha. (Śloka 16, Chapter 89, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Once the god of death came and did penance at
<div n="lb"/>this place. (Śloka 26, Chapter 54, Droṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Once Brahmā performed a Yāga at this place and
<div n="lb"/>then Sarasvatī rose up from the place in the name of
<div n="lb"/>Suprabhā. (Śloka 5, Chapter 38, Śalya Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) If one performs penance at this place peacefully
<div n="lb"/>one can attain mokṣa. (Chapter 297, Śānti Parva).
