AŚOKAVANIKĀ¦. A famous park in Laṅkā. It was in
<div n="lb"/>this park that Rāvaṇa kept Sītā having stolen her from
<div n="lb"/>Śrī Rāma. It is described in Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa,
<div n="lb"/>Sundarakāṇḍa, Sarga 14 and Araṇyakāṇḍa, Sarga 56
<div n="lb"/>and in Mahābhārata also:—
<div n="NI"/>“Thinking of her husband alone, clad in the garments
<div n="lb"/>of a hermit woman, eating only roots and fruits, in
<div n="lb"/>prayer and fast, in sorrow and sadness, she of the wide
<div n="lb"/>eyes lived in the Aśoka park which seemed as a hermit-
<div n="lb"/>age.” (Bhāṣā Bhārata, Araṇya Parva Chapter 280,
<div n="lb"/>Stanzas 42 and 43).
