BHŪMANYU I¦. A king who was the grandson of King
<div n="lb"/>Duṣyanta and the son of Bharata. From Mahābhārata
<div n="lb"/>Ādi Parva, Chapter 94, Verse 19, we see that he was
<div n="lb"/>born by the blessing of Bharadvāja. His mother was
<div n="lb"/>Sunandā, daughter of Sarvasena, King of Kāśi. When
<div n="lb"/>Bhūmanyu grew up, his father Bharata entrusted him
<div n="lb"/>with the affairs of the Kingdom. Bhūmanyu had six
<div n="lb"/>sons by his wife Puṣkariṇī; they were Diviratha, Suhotra,
<div n="lb"/>Suhotā, Sukavi, Suyaju, and Ṛcīka. In Mahābhārata,
<div n="lb"/>Ādi Parva, Chapter 95, Verse 33, we see that he had
<div n="lb"/>another son Suhotra by a woman Vijayā, a Dāśārha
<div n="lb"/>maid.
