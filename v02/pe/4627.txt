MŪNNU¦ (THREE). The importance of the number
<div n="lb"/>three is indicated below:
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Agnitraya (Three agnis).%} The three agnis are
<div n="lb"/>Dakṣiṇa, Gārhapatya and Āhavanīya. Of these the
<div n="lb"/>first is in the shape of a semi-circle, the second in the
<div n="lb"/>shape of a full circle and the third, a square.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Adhamatraya.%} (Three kinds of low people). The
[Page510-a+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>three classes of people who are considered to have a
<div n="lb"/>very low position in society are the wifeless, sonless and
<div n="lb"/>servantless ones.
<div n="NI"/>3) {%Abhijātavihitatraya.%} (Three dealings with men of
<div n="lb"/>equal nobility). The three important dealings which
<div n="lb"/>should be had with only people of equal nobility are
<div n="lb"/>alliance, marriage and litigation.
<div n="NI"/>4) {%Abhinayatraya.%} (Three kinds of acts). The three
<div n="lb"/>kinds of acts are the Hastābhinaya (actions with the
<div n="lb"/>hand), Āṅgyābhinaya (actions with gestures) and
<div n="lb"/>Rasābhinaya (actions with expressions of sentiment).
<div n="NI"/>5) {%Avasthātraya.%} Man lives always in any one of the
<div n="lb"/>following states: Wakefulness, Dreaming and Sleep.
<div n="NI"/>6) {%Avasthābhedatraya.%} The three different forms in
<div n="lb"/>which matter exists are as Ghana, (solid) Drava (liquid)
<div n="lb"/>and Vātaka (gas).
<div n="NI"/>7) {%Āsanatraya.%} Three kinds of important postures are
<div n="lb"/>Vīrāsana, Padmāsana. and Svastikāsana.
<div n="NI"/>8) {%Itivṛttatraya.%} The three kinds of plots in a story
<div n="lb"/>are Prakhyāta, Utpādya and Miśra.
<div n="NI"/>9) {%Ṛṇatraya.%} The three different kinds of debts of
<div n="lb"/>man are Deva-ṛṇa (debts to gods), Pitṛ-ṛṇa (debts to
<div n="lb"/>the Manes) and Ṛṣi-ṛṇa (debts to ṛṣis).
<div n="NI"/>10) {%Eṣaṇātraya.%} Eṣaṇā means desire. The three kinds
<div n="lb"/>of eṣaṇās are Putraiṣaṇā (desire for children), Vittai-
<div n="lb"/>ṣaṇā (desire for wealth) and Dāraiṣaṇā (desire for
<div n="lb"/>wife).
<div n="NI"/>11) {%Karaṇatraya.%} The three instruments of action are
<div n="lb"/>mind, speech and body.
<div n="NI"/>12) {%Karmatraya.%} The three actions are Sṛṣṭi (creation),
<div n="lb"/>Sthiti (maintenance) and Saṃhāra (destruction). Yāga,
<div n="lb"/>Vedapaṭhana and Dharma also form one Karmatraya.
<div n="NI"/>13) {%Karmakāraṇatraya.%} The three kinds of causes of
<div n="lb"/>action are knowledge, what is to be known and what
<div n="lb"/>has already been known (Jñāna, Jñeya and Jñātā).
<div n="NI"/>14) {%Kālatraya.%} Past, present and future are the three
<div n="lb"/>divisions of time.
<div n="NI"/>15) {%Kāvyatraya.%} Gadya (prose), Padya (verse) and
<div n="lb"/>Miśra (combination of gadya and padya) are the
<div n="lb"/>three different constituents of literature.
<div n="NI"/>16) {%Kāvyaguṇatraya.%} The three chief qualities of poetic
<div n="lb"/>compositions are Prasāda (clearness of style), Mādhurya
<div n="lb"/>(sweetness of style) and Ojas (force of expression).
<div n="NI"/>17) {%Kāvyopādhitraya.%} The three main attributes of
<div n="lb"/>poetic compositions are Vastu (plot), Rīti (diction)
<div n="lb"/>and Rasa (sentiment).
<div n="NI"/>18) {%Gandharvadharmatraya.%} The three duties assigned
<div n="lb"/>to a Gandharva are Pūjyasevā (serving venerable
<div n="lb"/>people worthy of worship), Nṛtyagītavādyaparijñāna
<div n="lb"/>(study of dance, music and instrumental music) and
<div n="lb"/>remaining devoted to Sarasvatī (goddess of learning).
<div n="NI"/>19) {%Guṇatraya.%} The three distinguishing properties of
<div n="lb"/>nature (guṇas) belonging to all created beings are
<div n="lb"/>Sattvaguṇa, Rajoguṇa and Tamoguṇa The three good
<div n="lb"/>qualities (guṇas) of Man are Satya (truth), Sadācāra
<div n="lb"/>(good conduct) and Lajjā (modesty). The three results
<div n="lb"/>or benefits (guṇas) to which man aspires are Dhana
<div n="lb"/>(wealth), Kīrti (fame) and Svarga (heaven).
<div n="NI"/>20) {%Gurutraya.%} The three gurus are Mātā (mother)
<div n="lb"/>Pitā (father) and Ācārya (preceptor). Guru, Parama-
<div n="lb"/>guru and Parameṣṭhiguru are also three gurus.
<div n="NI"/>21) {%Tāpatrayas%} The three kinds of miseries (which
<div n="lb"/>human beings have to suffer in this world are Ādhyāt-
<div n="lb"/>mika (of the body and mind), Ādhibhautika (inflicted
<div n="lb"/>by animals) and Ādhidaivika (by fate). The other
[Page510-b+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>three tāpas (agonies) are Anakṣarajñasambhāṣaṇa
<div n="lb"/>(conversing with illiterate persons), Duṣprabhusevana
<div n="lb"/>(serving arrogant masters) and Lambapayodharāliṅ-
<div n="lb"/>gana (embracing women with hanging breasts).
<div n="NI"/>22) {%Tauryatrika.%} The three constituents of triple
<div n="lb"/>symphony are Dance, Music and Instrumental music.
<div n="NI"/>23) {%Jātitraya.%} The three distinguishing types of all
<div n="lb"/>created beings are Uttama (best), Madhyama
<div n="lb"/>(mediocre) and Adhama (worst).
<div n="NI"/>24) {%Doṣatraya.%} The disorders of the three humours of
<div n="lb"/>the body are Vāta (wind), Pitta (Bile) and Kapha
<div n="lb"/>(phlegm). Avyāpti, Ativyāpti and Asambhava are
<div n="lb"/>also three doṣas (fallacies).
<div n="NI"/>25) {%Tripuṭikās.%} Jñātā (knower), Jñāna (knowledge)
<div n="lb"/>and Jñeya (the object of knowledge) are the three
<div n="lb"/>puṭikās. Subject, predicate and object are also three
<div n="lb"/>puṭikās.
<div n="NI"/>26) {%Tṛipuras.%} The three demons who always formed
<div n="lb"/>a group are Tārakākṣa, Kamalākṣa and Vidyunmālī.
<div n="NI"/>27) {%Tribhuvana.%} Svarga (heaven), Bhūmi, (earth) and
<div n="lb"/>Pātāla (Netherworld) are the three bhuvanas. (worlds).
<div n="NI"/>28) {%Trimadhura.%} The three sweet things are honey,
<div n="lb"/>sugar and Kadalī (plantain fruit).
<div n="NI"/>29) {%Trimūrtis.%} The three Mūrtis (deities) are Brahmā,
<div n="lb"/>Viṣṇu and Maheśvara.
<div n="NI"/>30) {%Triliṅga.%} The three liṅgas (genders) are Pulliṅga
<div n="lb"/>(masculine), Strīliṅga (feminine) and Napuṃsaka
<div n="lb"/>(neuter).
<div n="NI"/>31) {%Triloka.%} The three worlds are Manuṣyaloka
<div n="lb"/>(world of men), Pitṛloka (world of the Manes) and
<div n="lb"/>Devaloka (world of the gods).
<div n="NI"/>32) {%Trivarga.%} The following groups of three are
<div n="lb"/>classed as Trivargas (a) Dharma (Virtue), Artha
<div n="lb"/>(wealth) and Kāma (desire). (b) Vṛddhi (increase)
<div n="lb"/>Sthāna (same position) and Kṣaya (decrease). (c)
<div n="lb"/>Sattva, Rajas and Tamas.
<div n="NI"/>33) {%Pralayatraya.%} The three pralayas (floods) are
<div n="lb"/>Naimittika (floods due to rains in and out of season)
<div n="lb"/>Prākṛtapralaya (floods arising out of saṃvarttāgni)
<div n="lb"/>and Ātyantikapralaya (floods due to heavy rains).
<div n="NI"/>34) {%Trividhayajñas.%} Yajñas are of three kinds.
<div n="lb"/>Karmayajña, Upāsanayajña and Jñānayajña. There
<div n="lb"/>are six divisions in Karmayajña namely, Nityakarma,
<div n="lb"/>Naimittikakarma, Kāmyakarma, Ādhyātmikakarma,
<div n="lb"/>Ādhidaivikakarma and Adhibhautikakarma. For
<div n="lb"/>Upāsanayajña there are nine divisions namely, Nir-
<div n="lb"/>guṇopāsana, Saguṇopāsana, Bhūtapretopāsana,
<div n="lb"/>Mantrayogavidhi, Aṣṭāṅgayogavidhi, Layayogavidhi,
<div n="lb"/>Rājayogavidhi, Avatāropāsana and Maharṣidevopā-
<div n="lb"/>sana. Jñānayajña has got three divisions namely,
<div n="lb"/>Manana, Nididhyāsana and Śravaṇa.
<div n="NI"/>35) {%Trivṛtti.%} Vaidarbhī, Pāñcālī and Gauḍī are the
<div n="lb"/>three Kāvyavṛttis.
<div n="NI"/>36) {%Triveda.%} The three Vedas are Ṛgveda, Yajurveda
<div n="lb"/>and Sāmaveda.
<div n="NI"/>37) {%Triśakti.%} The three kinds of powers are, power of
<div n="lb"/>wealth and position, power of endeavour and power
<div n="lb"/>of good counsel (Prabhuśakti, Utsāhaśakti and Mantra-
<div n="lb"/>śakti).
<div n="NI"/>38) {%Triśarīras.%} The three bodies or physical adjuncts
<div n="lb"/>of a soul are Sthūla, Sūkṣma and Kāraṇa and the three
<div n="lb"/>bodies of Paramātman are Īśa, Sūtra and Virāṭ.
<div n="NI"/>39) {%Trisandhyās.%} Prabhāta (morning), Madhyāhna
<div n="lb"/>(midday) and Sāyāhna (evening) are the three
<div n="lb"/>Sandhyās (union of two divisions of time).
[Page511-a+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>40) {%Dūṣaṇatraya.%} Vāta, Pitta and Kapha are the
<div n="lb"/>three dūṣaṇas (disorder of the humours in a body).
<div n="NI"/>41) {%Nāḍītraya.%} The three nāḍīs of the body are Iḍā,
<div n="lb"/>Piṅgalā and Suṣumnā.
<div n="NI"/>42) {%Puruṣatraya.%} The three classes of men are Uttama
<div n="lb"/>(best), Madhyama (mediocre) and Adhama (lowest).
<div n="NI"/>43) {%Pramāṇatraya.%} The three kinds of means of valid
<div n="lb"/>knowledge are Pratyakṣa (what can be seen), Anumāna
<div n="lb"/>(what can be logically inferred) and Āgama (Verbal
<div n="lb"/>testimony.)
<div n="NI"/>44) {%Brahmalakṣaṇatraya.%} Śuddha, Śiva and Śānta are
<div n="lb"/>the three Brahmalakṣaṇas.
<div n="NI"/>45) {%Munitraya.%} The celebrated trio of sages are Pāṇini,
<div n="lb"/>Patañjali and Kātyāyana.
<div n="NI"/>46) {%Yogatraya.%} The three kinds of yogas are: Jñāna-
<div n="lb"/>yoga, Bhaktiyoga and Karmayoga.
<div n="NI"/>47) {%Rītitraya.%} The three rītis are Vaidarbhī, Pāñcālī
<div n="lb"/>and Gauḍī.
<div n="NI"/>48) {%Rūpakatraya.%} Nāṭya, Nṛtya and Nṛtta are the
<div n="lb"/>three rūpakas.
<div n="NI"/>49) {%Vaiśyavṛttitraya.%} The three duties of a Vaisya are
<div n="lb"/>Kṛṣ (agriculture), Paśupālana (Breeding of cattle) and
<div n="lb"/>Vāṇijya (trade).
<div n="NI"/>50) {%Saraṇatraya.%} Buddha, Dharma and Saṅgha are
<div n="lb"/>the three Śaraṇas.
<div n="NI"/>51) {%Siddhitraya.%} The following groups of three are
<div n="lb"/>considered to be Siddhitrayas. (a) Karmasiddhi,
<div n="lb"/>Yogasiddhi and Jñānasiddhi. (b) Aiśvaryasiddhi,
<div n="lb"/>Jñānasiddhi and Vairāgyasiddhi. (c) Śraddhā, Vitta
<div n="lb"/>and Bhāgya. (d) Maṇi, Mantra and Auṣadha. (Siddhi
<div n="lb"/>means attainment, accomplishment).
<div n="NI"/>52) {%Svaratraya.%} The different kinds of vowel are three
<div n="lb"/>in number namely Hrasva (short), Dīrgha (long) and
<div n="lb"/>Pluta (prolated). Udātta, Anudātta and Svarita are
<div n="lb"/>also three different kinds of accent, i.e. the acute grave
<div n="lb"/>and circumflexed).
