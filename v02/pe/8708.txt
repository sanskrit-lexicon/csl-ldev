YAKṢA¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} A class of Semi-gods. There are chiefly
<div n="lb"/>three classes of inhabitants in Heaven:—Devas, Gaṇa-
<div n="lb"/>devas and Upadevas. Gaṇadevas consist of the 12
<div n="lb"/>Ādityas, 10 Viśvadevas, 8 Vasus, 36 Tuṣitas, 64 Ābhās-
<div n="lb"/>varas, 49 Anilas 220 Mahārājikas, 12 Sādhyas and 11
<div n="lb"/>Rudras.
<div n="NI"/>Among the Upadevas there are 10 subdivisions. They
<div n="lb"/>are, Vidyādharas, Apsaras, Yakṣas, Rākṣasas, Gandhar-
<div n="lb"/>vas, Kinnaras, Piśācas, Guhyakas, Siddhas and Bhūtas.
[Page893-a+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Origin.%} There are different views relating to the
<div n="lb"/>origin of the Yakṣas. In Mahābhārata, Ādi Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter l we find that the Yakṣas took birth after
<div n="lb"/>Brahmā's birth from “Virāṭ Puruṣa's” aṇḍa. According
<div n="lb"/>to a statement in Agni Purāṇa, Chapter 19, Yakṣas and
<div n="lb"/>Rākṣasas were born from Munī, the grand daughter of
<div n="lb"/>Kaśyapaprajāpati. Thus Yakṣas and Rākṣasas are relat-
<div n="lb"/>ed as brothers. In Mahābhārata, Ādi Parva, Chapter 66,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 7, there is another passage which says that Yakṣas
<div n="lb"/>are the progeny of the sage Pulastya.
<div n="NI"/>3) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>i) Once Śukadeva sang the story of Mahābhārata to the
<div n="lb"/>Yakṣas. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 1, Verse 108).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Lakhs of Yakṣas remain in Kubera's assembly,
<div n="lb"/>worshipping him. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 10,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 18).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) There are Yakṣas in Brahmā's assembly also.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 11, Verse 56).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Kubera is the King of Yakṣas. (M.B. Vana Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 111, Verse 10).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Bhīmasena once drove away Yakṣas and Rākṣasas.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter 16, Verse 57).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) On another occasion, Sunda and Upasunda defeated
<div n="lb"/>and persecuted the Yakṣas. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chap-
<div n="lb"/>ter 208, verse 7).
