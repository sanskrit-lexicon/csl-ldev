DHAUMYA I¦. A hermit.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} This hermit was the younger
<div n="lb"/>brother of Devala, a hermit. The Pāṇḍavas, who
<div n="lb"/>escaped from burning in the Lākṣā house, reached the
<div n="lb"/>banks of the Ganges when this hermit was performing
<div n="lb"/>penance in the holy tīrtha of Utkoca. Arjuna defeated
<div n="lb"/>Citraratha, a Gandharva. After that Citraratha and
<div n="lb"/>Arjuna became friends. The gandharva advised him
<div n="lb"/>that a priest was unavoidable and that the Pāṇḍavas
<div n="lb"/>should accept the hermit Dhaumya who was doing
<div n="lb"/>penance in the Utkocatīrtha as their priest. Accordingly
<div n="lb"/>the Pāṇḍavas accepted Dhaumya as their priest. From
<div n="lb"/>that day onwards in everything the Pāṇḍavas did,
<div n="lb"/>Dhaumya was their priest. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>182).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%} (1) After the Svayaṃvara of Pāñcālī,
<div n="lb"/>Dhaumya performed the marriage ceremony for each
<div n="lb"/>of the Pāṇḍavas from Dharmaputra to Sahadeva
<div n="lb"/>separately with Pāñcālī. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>197).
<div n="NI"/>(2) When sons were born to the Pāṇḍavas, Dhaumya
<div n="lb"/>performed the rites of investiture etc. with the Brahma
<div n="lb"/>string etc. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 220, Stanza 87).
<div n="NI"/>(3) Dhaumya was the chief priest who performed the
<div n="lb"/>rites of sacrifice at the Rājasūya of Yudhiṣṭhira. He
<div n="lb"/>anointed Yudhiṣṭhira as King. (M.B. Sabhā Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 53, Stanza 10).
<div n="NI"/>(4) When the Pāṇḍavas started for forest life, Dhaumya
<div n="lb"/>walked in front of them with Kuśa grass in his hands,
<div n="lb"/>singing Yamasāma and Rudrasāma songs. (M.B. Sabhā
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 80, Stanza 8).
<div n="NI"/>(5) Once Dhaumya talked about the attributes of the
<div n="lb"/>Sun and advised Dharmaputra to worship the Sun.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter 3).
<div n="NI"/>(6) In the forest Dhaumya rendered powerless the
<div n="lb"/>illusive and magical arts of Kirmīra, an asura (demon).
<div n="lb"/>(Mahābhārata, Vana parva, Chapter 11, Stanza 20).
<div n="NI"/>(7) Dhaumya described to Dharmaputra the importance
<div n="lb"/>of several holy tīrthas or Baths. (M.B. Vana Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapters 87 to 90).
<div n="NI"/>(8) On another occasion Dhaumya described to
<div n="lb"/>Dharmaputra the motions of the Sun and the Moon
[Page233-a+ 64]
<div n="lb"/>and the positions of Viṣṇu and Brahmā. (M.B. Vana
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 163).
<div n="NI"/>(9) When Jayadratha had stolen Pāñcālī, Dhaumya
<div n="lb"/>blamed him and tried to recover Pāñcālī. (M.B. Vana
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 238, Stanza 26).
<div n="NI"/>(10) Dhaumya advised the Pāṇḍavas how to preserve
<div n="lb"/>pseudonymity in the capital of Virāṭa. (M.B. Virāṭa
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 4).
<div n="NI"/>(11) When the Pāṇḍavas started their life incognito
<div n="lb"/>Dhaumya performed the rite of Agniṣṭoma and uttered
<div n="lb"/>the Veda mantras for their prosperity, recovery of
<div n="lb"/>kingdom and victory in the world etc. When they
<div n="lb"/>started Dhaumya took the fire with oblations and went
<div n="lb"/>to the country of Pāñcāla. (M.B. Virāṭa Parva, Chap-
<div n="lb"/>ter 4, Stanza 54).
<div n="NI"/>(12) After the bhārata battle, Dhaumya performed
<div n="lb"/>the funeral ceremonies, offerings etc. of the relatives
<div n="lb"/>of the Pāṇḍavas. (M.B. Strī Parva, Chapter 24).
<div n="NI"/>(13) After Dharmaputra was anointed King, Dhaumya
<div n="lb"/>disclosed to him the secrets of righteousness. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 127, Stanza 15).
