SAPTASĀRASVATA¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} A holy place situated within
<div n="lb"/>Kurukṣetra. This is a holy bath dedicated to Sarasvatī.
<div n="lb"/>Mention is made in Mahābhārata, Śalya Parva, Chap-
<div n="lb"/>ter 37, Verse 61, that during his pilgrimage Balabhadra-
<div n="lb"/>rāma visited this holy bath.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Origin.%} A story occurs in Mahābhārata, Śalya
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 38 about the origin of this tīrtha. It is
<div n="lb"/>given below:—
<div n="NI"/>Once Brahmā performed a sacrifice on the island of
<div n="lb"/>Puṣkara. All the devas (gods and goddesses) attended
<div n="lb"/>the sacrifice. Among the rivers, Sarasvatī alone did
[Page692-a+ 64]
<div n="lb"/>not come at first. Then Brahmā remembered
<div n="lb"/>Sarasvatī and she appeared under the name Suprabhā.
<div n="lb"/>Thus she appeared before the hermits who were doing
<div n="lb"/>penance in that place assuming seven different figures.
<div n="lb"/>From that day onwards the place became famous under
<div n="lb"/>the name Saptasārasvata. (See under Sarasvatī).
