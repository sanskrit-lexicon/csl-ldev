PAÑCĀYATANA¦. An idol of Śiva in Kāśī. This idol is
<div n="lb"/>called Oṃkāra also. Bhagavān Parameśvara who gives
<div n="lb"/>mokṣa to men dwells in this idol in the form of Pañcā-
<div n="lb"/>yatana. The five souls of Śiva are the five āyatanas.
<div n="lb"/>They are Śānti (tranquillity), Atītaśānti (passed beyond
<div n="lb"/>tranquillity), Parāparavidyā (Greater and smaller know-
<div n="lb"/>ledge), Pratiṣṭhā (celebrity) and Nivṛtti (Recession).
<div n="lb"/>Because these five āyatanas dwell in the idol of Śiva
<div n="lb"/>at Kāśī it got the name Pañcāyatana. (Chapter 34,
<div n="lb"/>Padma Purāṇa).
