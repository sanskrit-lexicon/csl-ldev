SYŪMARAŚMI¦. An ancient hermit. It is stated in
Mahābhārata, Śānti Parva, Chapter 238, that this
hermit got into the stomach of a cow and sitting there,
talked with the hermit Kapila about the ways of Karma
[Page782-b+ 62]
and renunciation This hermit Syūmaraśmi is as ancient
as the Ṛgveda. A statement as follows, occurs in Ṛg-
veda, Maṇḍala 1, Anuvāka 16, Sūkta 112. “Ye Aśvins
who are the Leaders. Come here also with those pro-
tective weapons by which you gave protection to Atri,
Śaṃyu and Manu, and the arrows by shooting which
you saved Syūmaraśmi.”
