<div n="lb"/>Mahābhārata, Śānti Parva, Chapter 238, that this
<div n="lb"/>hermit got into the stomach of a cow and sitting there,
<div n="lb"/>talked with the hermit Kapila about the ways of Karma
[Page782-b+ 62]
<div n="lb"/>and renunciation This hermit Syūmaraśmi is as ancient
<div n="lb"/>as the Ṛgveda. A statement as follows, occurs in Ṛg-
<div n="lb"/>veda, Maṇḍala 1, Anuvāka 16, Sūkta 112. “Ye Aśvins
<div n="lb"/>who are the Leaders. Come here also with those pro-
<div n="lb"/>tective weapons by which you gave protection to Atri,
<div n="lb"/>Śaṃyu and Manu, and the arrows by shooting which
<div n="lb"/>you saved Syūmaraśmi.”
