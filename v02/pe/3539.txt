<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} A King called Agnīdhra was born in the
dynasty of Priyavrata, son of Manu. He became lord
of the Jambū island, and married an apsarā woman
named Pūrvacitti. To them were born nine sons called
Nābhi, Kimpuruṣa, Hari, Ilāvṛta, Ramyaka, Hiraṇ-
maya, Kalva, Bhadrāśva and Ketumāla. Agnīdhra
partitioned the kingdom among the nine sons. The
land Kimpuruṣa ruled over was known as Kimpuruṣa
or Kimpuruṣavarṣa. It lay to the south of Hemakūṭa
mountain. It was here that Hanūmān spent his last
days worshipping Śrī Rāma.
<div n="NI"/>“The son of the wind God (Hanūmān) lives in the
country called Kimpuruṣavarṣa in the worship of Śrī
Rāma.” (Bhāgavata, 5th Skandha).
<div n="NI"/>According to the Sabhā Parva (Chapter 23, Verses 1, 2)
Kimpuruṣavarṣa guarded by the sons of Druma is
situated to the north of the Himālayas facing the
Dhavala mountain, and this country was once conquer-
ed by Arjuna.

<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other information.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Kimpuruṣas were the sons of Pulaha Prajāpati.
(Ādi Parva, Chapter 66, Verse 8).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) They witnessed Agastya drinking up the ocean
dry. (Vana Parva, Chapter 104, Verse 21).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) They guard the lotus ponds kept by Kubera to
sport with his beloved ones. (Vana Parva, Chapter
15, Verse 9).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) In his conflict with Rāvaṇa, Kubera left behind
him Laṅkā and his Puṣpaka Vimāna, escaped towards
the north and settled down on mount Gandhamādana
with the help of the Kimpuruṣas. (Vana Parva, Chapter
275, Verse 33).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Yakṣa women were their mothers. (Śānti Parva,
Chapter 207, Verse 25).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) They were present at the aśvamedha of Yudhi-
ṣṭhira. (Aśvamedha Parva, Chapter 88, Verse 37).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) Śuka Brahmarṣi reached Bhārata, crossing
Kimpuruṣavarṣa. (Śānti Parva, Chapter 325).
