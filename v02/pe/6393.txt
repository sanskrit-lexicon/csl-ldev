ŚATĀNĪKA V¦. A brother of Virāṭa, the King of
Matsya. He was called Sūryadatta too. Further he
was the commander-in-chief of the army of Virāṭa.
When Virāṭa's cows were lifted by the Kauravas,
Śatānīka went to war against them along with the
Trigartas, who wore golden helmets on their heads.
(Virāta Parva, Chapter 31). He was an ally of the
Pāṇḍavas in the great war. He was wounded in the
war by Bhīṣma and killed by Śalya. (Droṇa Parva,
Chapter 167, Verse 30; Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 118,
Verse 27).
