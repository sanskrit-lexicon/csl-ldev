TRIGARTA¦. A powerful kingdom of ancient Bhārata.
There are several references in the Mahābhārata to the
Kings of Trigarta. The following are a few of them
worth noticing:—
<div n="NI"/>(i) The Pāṇḍavas passed through the country of
Trigarta while they were wandering in the forests after
the fire accident at the wax palace. (Śloka 2, Chapter
155, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Arjuna during his victory march to the north
defeated the King of Trigarta. (Śloka 18, Chapter 27,
Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Nakula once conquered the country of Trigarta.
(Śloka 7, Chapter 32, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) The Kings of Trigarta used to pay tribute to
Dharmaputra. (Sabhā Parva, Chapter 52, Śloka 14).
<div n="NI"/>(v) A King of Trigarta once killed the horses tied to
the chariot of Dharmaputra and then committed
suicide. (Śloka 12, Chapter 271, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) A King of Trigarta named Suratha was slain by
Nakula. (Śloka 18, Chapter 271, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) Suśarmā, King of Trigarta with his army joined
the Kaurava side and carried away the cattle of the
King of Virāṭa and also fought with him. Arjuna
during his stay incognito at the palace of the King of
Virāṭa killed many Trigartas. (Chapter 32, Virāṭa
Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) Suśarmā, King of Trigarta, took the King of
Virāṭa as a captive and enraged at this Bhīmasena was
about to kill Suśarmā when Arjuna intervened and
stopped him from that. (Chapter 23, Virāṭa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) Satyaratha, King of Trigarta, had four brothers
and the Pāṇḍavas fought against all the five. (Śloka 9,
Chapter 166, Udyoga Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(x) In the Garuḍa-vyūha constructed by Bhīṣma
(battle array in the shape of a vulture) five Trigarta
princes stood at the head of the formation. (Chapter
56, Bhīṣma Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(xi) Arjuna used the Vāyavyāstra against the Trigartas
in the great battle. (Chapter 102, Bhīṣma Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(xii) Śrī Kṛṣṇa once defeated the Trigartas. (Chapter
11, Droṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(xiii) Once Paraśurāma killed many Trigartas. (Chap-
ter 70, Droṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(xiv) There was a fight between Sātyaki and the
Trigartas once. (Chapter 181, Droṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(xv) Arjuna who led the sacrificial horse of the
Aśvamedhayajña of Dharmaputra created great havoc
to the Trigartas. (Chapter 74, Aśvamedha Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(xvi) The Kings of Trigarta live in the court of Yama.
(Śloka 20, Chapter 8, Sabhā Parva).
