TRIGARTA¦. A powerful kingdom of ancient Bhārata.
<div n="lb"/>There are several references in the Mahābhārata to the
<div n="lb"/>Kings of Trigarta. The following are a few of them
<div n="lb"/>worth noticing:—
<div n="NI"/>(i) The Pāṇḍavas passed through the country of
<div n="lb"/>Trigarta while they were wandering in the forests after
<div n="lb"/>the fire accident at the wax palace. (Śloka 2, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>155, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Arjuna during his victory march to the north
<div n="lb"/>defeated the King of Trigarta. (Śloka 18, Chapter 27,
<div n="lb"/>Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Nakula once conquered the country of Trigarta.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 7, Chapter 32, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) The Kings of Trigarta used to pay tribute to
<div n="lb"/>Dharmaputra. (Sabhā Parva, Chapter 52, Śloka 14).
<div n="NI"/>(v) A King of Trigarta once killed the horses tied to
<div n="lb"/>the chariot of Dharmaputra and then committed
<div n="lb"/>suicide. (Śloka 12, Chapter 271, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) A King of Trigarta named Suratha was slain by
<div n="lb"/>Nakula. (Śloka 18, Chapter 271, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) Suśarmā, King of Trigarta with his army joined
<div n="lb"/>the Kaurava side and carried away the cattle of the
<div n="lb"/>King of Virāṭa and also fought with him. Arjuna
<div n="lb"/>during his stay incognito at the palace of the King of
<div n="lb"/>Virāṭa killed many Trigartas. (Chapter 32, Virāṭa
<div n="lb"/>Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) Suśarmā, King of Trigarta, took the King of
<div n="lb"/>Virāṭa as a captive and enraged at this Bhīmasena was
<div n="lb"/>about to kill Suśarmā when Arjuna intervened and
<div n="lb"/>stopped him from that. (Chapter 23, Virāṭa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) Satyaratha, King of Trigarta, had four brothers
<div n="lb"/>and the Pāṇḍavas fought against all the five. (Śloka 9,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 166, Udyoga Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(x) In the Garuḍa-vyūha constructed by Bhīṣma
<div n="lb"/>(battle array in the shape of a vulture) five Trigarta
<div n="lb"/>princes stood at the head of the formation. (Chapter
<div n="lb"/>56, Bhīṣma Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(xi) Arjuna used the Vāyavyāstra against the Trigartas
<div n="lb"/>in the great battle. (Chapter 102, Bhīṣma Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(xii) Śrī Kṛṣṇa once defeated the Trigartas. (Chapter
<div n="lb"/>11, Droṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(xiii) Once Paraśurāma killed many Trigartas. (Chap-
<div n="lb"/>ter 70, Droṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(xiv) There was a fight between Sātyaki and the
<div n="lb"/>Trigartas once. (Chapter 181, Droṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(xv) Arjuna who led the sacrificial horse of the
<div n="lb"/>Aśvamedhayajña of Dharmaputra created great havoc
<div n="lb"/>to the Trigartas. (Chapter 74, Aśvamedha Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(xvi) The Kings of Trigarta live in the court of Yama.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 20, Chapter 8, Sabhā Parva).
