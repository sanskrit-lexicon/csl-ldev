ŚĀṆḌILYA I¦. A great maharṣi. Some details about
him are given hereunder.
<div n="NI"/>(1) King Śatānīka and his wife Viṣṇumatī, who had
no issues approached the maharṣi in great sorrow. As
a result of eating rice given by the maharṣi Viṣṇumatī
became the mother of a son, who became in later years
famous as emperor Sahasrānīka. (Kathāsaritsāgara,
Kathāmukhalambaka, Taraṅga 1).
<div n="NI"/>(2) A member of Yudhiṣṭhira's court. (Sabhā Parva,
Chapter 4, Verse 17).
<div n="NI"/>(3) He had an ascetic daughter. (Śalya Parva, Chapter
54, Verse 5).
<div n="NI"/>(4) He once opined that the gift of a mere ox-cart was
equal to the gift of water in a golden pot. (Anuśāsana
Parva, Chapter 65, Verse 19).
<div n="NI"/>(5) He visited Bhīṣma on his bed of arrows. (Śānti
Parva, Chapter 47, Verse 6).
<div n="NI"/>(6) King Sumanyu once gave the maharṣi plenty of
food materials. (Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 173, Verse
22).
