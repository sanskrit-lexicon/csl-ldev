ŚĀṆḌILYA I¦. A great maharṣi. Some details about
<div n="lb"/>him are given hereunder.
<div n="NI"/>(1) King Śatānīka and his wife Viṣṇumatī, who had
<div n="lb"/>no issues approached the maharṣi in great sorrow. As
<div n="lb"/>a result of eating rice given by the maharṣi Viṣṇumatī
<div n="lb"/>became the mother of a son, who became in later years
<div n="lb"/>famous as emperor Sahasrānīka. (Kathāsaritsāgara,
<div n="lb"/>Kathāmukhalambaka, Taraṅga 1).
<div n="NI"/>(2) A member of Yudhiṣṭhira's court. (Sabhā Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 4, Verse 17).
<div n="NI"/>(3) He had an ascetic daughter. (Śalya Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>54, Verse 5).
<div n="NI"/>(4) He once opined that the gift of a mere ox-cart was
<div n="lb"/>equal to the gift of water in a golden pot. (Anuśāsana
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 65, Verse 19).
<div n="NI"/>(5) He visited Bhīṣma on his bed of arrows. (Śānti
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 47, Verse 6).
<div n="NI"/>(6) King Sumanyu once gave the maharṣi plenty of
<div n="lb"/>food materials. (Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 173, Verse
<div n="lb"/>22).
