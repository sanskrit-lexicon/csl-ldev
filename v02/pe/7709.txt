ULŪKA I¦. The son of Śakuni. (M.B. Udyoga Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 57, Stanza 25). It is stated in Mahābhārata, Ādi
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 182, Stanza 22, that Ulūka was present
<div n="lb"/>at the Svayaṃvara (the Bride choosing a husband) of
<div n="lb"/>Draupadī. In the Bhārata Battle Ulūka was sent as a
<div n="lb"/>messenger to the camp of the Pāṇḍavas by Duryodhana.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Udyoga Parva, Chapter 161). After that he
<div n="lb"/>returned to Duryodhana with the message of the Pāṇḍa-
<div n="lb"/>vas. (M.B. Udyoga Parva, Chapter 163). He combated
<div n="lb"/>with the King of Cedi on the first day of the battle.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 45). After that Sahadeva
<div n="lb"/>attacked Ulūka. (M.B. Bhīsma Parva, Chapter 72,
<div n="lb"/>Stanza 5). Arjuna defeated Ulūka. (M.B. Droṇa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 171, Stanza 40). After the death of the teacher
<div n="lb"/>Droṇa, Ulūka fled from the battle-field. (M.B. Droṇa
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 193, Stanza 14). It is mentioned in
[Page806-a+ 64]
<div n="lb"/>Mahābhārata, Karṇa Parva, Chapter 25, Stanzas 9 to
<div n="lb"/>11, that Ulūka defeated Yuyutsu. Next fight was bet-
<div n="lb"/>ween Sahadeva and Ulūka in which Sahadeva killed
<div n="lb"/>Ulūka. (M.B. Śalya Parva, Chapter 28, Stanzas 32 and
<div n="lb"/>33). The following are the synonyms of Ulūka, given
<div n="lb"/>in the Mahābhārata:—Śākuni, Kaitaka, Saubalyasuta
<div n="lb"/>and Kaitavya.
