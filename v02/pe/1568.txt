CITRAKŪṬA¦. A mountain.
<div n="NI"/>Renowned in the purāṇas, this mountain is on the banks
<div n="lb"/>of the river, Mandākinī. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>85). It was here, at Citrakūṭa that Śrī Rāma, Sītā
<div n="lb"/>and others lived for a period of time. It is said that
<div n="lb"/>Rājalakṣmī (royal wealth and welfare) will embrace
<div n="lb"/>those who fast on the Citrakūṭa after a bath in the
<div n="lb"/>Mandākinī. (M.B. Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 26,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 29). This mountain is in Bāndā Zilla of the U.P.
<div n="lb"/>in modern India.
<div n="NI"/>Citrakūṭa is described in Cantos 56 and 94 of Vālmīki
<div n="lb"/>Rāmāyaṇa.
