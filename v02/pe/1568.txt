CITRAKŪṬA¦. A mountain.
<div n="NI"/>Renowned in the purāṇas, this mountain is on the banks
of the river, Mandākinī. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter
85). It was here, at Citrakūṭa that Śrī Rāma, Sītā
and others lived for a period of time. It is said that
Rājalakṣmī (royal wealth and welfare) will embrace
those who fast on the Citrakūṭa after a bath in the
Mandākinī. (M.B. Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 26,
Verse 29). This mountain is in Bāndā Zilla of the U.P.
in modern India.
<div n="NI"/>Citrakūṭa is described in Cantos 56 and 94 of Vālmīki
Rāmāyaṇa.
