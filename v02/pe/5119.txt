PAUṆḌRA II¦. A resident of the state of Pauṇḍra. This
<div n="lb"/>state was in existence during the Mahābhārata period.
<div n="lb"/>The king of the state was also called Pauṇḍra. Pauṇḍra
<div n="lb"/>was present for the marriage of Pāñcālī. (Chapter 186,
<div n="lb"/>Ādi Parva). Pauṇḍras were also present for the Rāja-
[Page586-b+ 64]
<div n="lb"/>sūya conducted by Dharmaputra. During the big battle
<div n="lb"/>the Pauṇḍras fought against the Kauravas constructing
<div n="lb"/>a Krauñcavyūha (a battle array in the shape of a
<div n="lb"/>stork). (Chapter 50, Bhīṣma Parva). Kṛṣṇa and Karṇa
<div n="lb"/>on two different occasions conquered this country.
<div n="lb"/>(Śānti Parva, M.B.) The Pauṇḍras were Kṣatriyas
<div n="lb"/>once. But the brahmins cursed them and changed them
<div n="lb"/>into Śūdras. (Chapter 85, Anuśāsana Parva).
