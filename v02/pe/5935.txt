<div n="lb"/>also known as “Śailūsas”. Some references found in the
<div n="lb"/>Purāṇas concerning Śailūṣas are given below:—
<div n="NI"/>(i) Śrī Rāma sent Bharata and completely destroyed
<div n="lb"/>the class of Gandharvas called Śailūṣas who were

<div n="NI"/>(ii) During the reign of Śrī Rāma, as ordered by him,

<div n="lb"/>Gandharva named Śailūṣa and his three crores of
<div n="lb"/>sons who lived on the banks of the river Sindhu. (Agni
<div n="lb"/>Purāṇa, Chapter 11).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Rāvaṇa's brother, Vibhīṣaṇa had married Saramā
<div n="lb"/>the daughter of a Śailūṣa Gandharva. (Uttara
<div n="lb"/>Rāmāyaṇa).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) The Gandharva named Śailūṣa serves Kubera and
