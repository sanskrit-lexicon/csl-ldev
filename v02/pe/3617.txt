KRATU¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} One of the six mental sons of
<div n="lb"/>Brahmā. Marīci, Aṅgiras, Atri, Pulastya, Pulaha and
<div n="lb"/>Kratu were the mental sons of Brahmā. (M.B. Ādi
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 65). Kratu is described as one of the
<div n="lb"/>21 Prajāpatis (lords of emanation).
[Page417-a+ 64]
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Some details.%} (1) It is stated in Mahābhārata, Ādi
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 65, Stanza 9, that the hermits called
<div n="lb"/>Bālakhilyas were the sons of Kratu.
<div n="NI"/>(2) Kratu was present at the birth-celebration of
<div n="lb"/>Arjuna. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 122, Stanza 52).
<div n="NI"/>(3) Kratu came to save the Rākṣasas from the Rākṣasa
<div n="lb"/>sattra, (A great sacrificial fire meant for the Rākṣasas
<div n="lb"/>(giants) to jump into and die by themselves) performed
<div n="lb"/>by the hermit Parāśara. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>189, Stanza 9).
<div n="NI"/>(4) Kratu was a luminary in the councils of Brahmā
<div n="lb"/>and Indra. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 7, Stanza 17).
<div n="NI"/>(5) Kratu was present at the Birth celebration of
<div n="lb"/>Skandadeva. (M.B. Śalya Parva, Chapter 45, Stanza 10).
<div n="NI"/>(6) There is a group of hermits called ‘Citraśikhaṇḍins,
<div n="lb"/>of which Kratu is a member. (M.B. Śānti Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 335, Stanza 27).
<div n="NI"/>(7) By the blessings of Śiva, Kratu got a thousand sons.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 14, Stanza 87).
<div n="NI"/>(8) Kratu went to visit Bhīṣma who was lying on the
<div n="lb"/>bed of arrows awaiting death in the beginning of
<div n="lb"/>Uttarāyaṇa of the year. (M.B. Anuśāsana Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 26, Stanza 4).
