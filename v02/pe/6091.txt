SAMPĀTI I¦. A bird. The elder brother of Jaṭāyu.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Birth.%} Aruṇa and Garuḍa were the sons born to
Prajāpati Kaśyapa by his wife Vinatā. Two sons named
Sampāti and Jaṭāyu were born to Aruṇa. (For further
details see under Jaṭāyu, para 1).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Sampāti in the Rāmāyaṇa.%} Once Sampāti and his
younger brother Jaṭāyu flew to the Sun. To protect his
younger brother Jaṭāyu who had neared the Sun, Sampāti
opened his wings which were burnt and he fell on the
shore of the salt sea. At this time an army of the
monkeys, with Hanūmān at their head came there, in
search of Sītā. Sampāti gave them directions of the path
they were to follow. (Detailed story is given under
Rāma and Mālī
