AṂŚA¦. A sage of the family of Marīci.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Genealogy.%} He was descended from Viṣṇu through
<div n="lb"/>Brahmā, Marīci and Kaśyapa.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Birth.%} Marīci was one of the six sons, all ṛṣis, of
<div n="lb"/>Brahmā. These six sons, born parthenogenetically of
<div n="lb"/>Brahmā, were: Marīci, Aṅgiras, Atri, Pulastya, Pulaha
<div n="lb"/>and Kratu. Marīci had a son named Kaśyapa. Kaśyapa
<div n="lb"/>married the thirteen daughters of Dakṣa. The eldest of
<div n="lb"/>them, Aditi, gave birth to twelve sons Dhātā, Aryaman,
<div n="lb"/>Mitra, Śakra, Varuṇa, Aṃśa, Bhaga, Vivasvān, Pūṣan,
<div n="lb"/>Savitā, Tvaṣṭā and Viṣṇu. Aṃśa is one of these
<div n="lb"/>twelve sons who have been called the twelve Ādityas.
<div n="lb"/>(See Chapter 65 of Ādi Parva of the Mahābhārata).
<div n="NI"/>3) {%Events.%} The Mahābhārata says (Śloka 66, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>123) that Aṃśa was present at the time of Arjuna's birth
<div n="lb"/>when several devas had come there to see the infant.
<div n="lb"/>In Śloka 34, Chapter 45 of Śalya Parva it is said that
<div n="lb"/>Aṃśa was present on the occasion of the Abhiṣeka of
<div n="lb"/>Skandadeva. Aṃśa is said to have presented to Skanda-
<div n="lb"/>deva five good warriors: Parigha, Vaṭa, Bhīma,
<div n="lb"/>Dahana and Dahati.
