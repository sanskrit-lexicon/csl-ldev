DHṚṢṬAKETU III¦. The son of Śiśupāla, the King of
Cedi. The details obtained from Mahābhārata about
this King are given below.
<div n="NI"/>(1) Dhṛṣṭaketu was the rebirth of Anuhlāda, the son of
Hiraṇyakaśipu. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 67,
Stanza 7).
<div n="NI"/>(2) Dhṛṣṭaketu was anointed as King after the death
of Śiśupāla. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 45,
Stanza 36).
<div n="NI"/>(3) After the death of his father, Dhṛṣṭaketu became
a tributary King of the Pāṇḍavas (M.B. Vana Parva,
Chapter 12, Stanza 2).
<div n="NI"/>(4) Dhṛṣṭaketu had a sister named Kareṇumatī. (M.B.
[Page235-b+ 64]
Vana Parva, Chapter 22, Stanza 40).
<div n="NI"/>(5) During the Bhārata-battle, Dhṛṣṭaketu supplied
the Pāṇḍavas with an akṣauhiṇī (21870 elephants,
21870 chariots, 65610 horses and 109350 infantry).
(M.B. Udyoga Parva, Chapter 19, Stanza 7).
<div n="NI"/>(6) Dhṛṣṭaketu was appointed as one of the seven
commanders-in-chief of Yudhiṣṭhira. (M.B. Udyoga
Parva, Chapter 157, Stanza 11).
<div n="NI"/>(7) On the first day of the battle of Bhārata,
Dhṛṣṭaketu, confronted Bāhlika. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva,
Chapter 45)
<div n="NI"/>(8) He fought with Bhūriśravas. (M.B. Bhīṣma
Parva, Chapter 84).
<div n="NI"/>(9) Dhṛṣṭaketu fought with Paurava. (M.B. Bhīṣma
Parva, Chapter 116, Stanza 13).
<div n="NI"/>(10) Dhṛṣṭaketu fought with the teacher Kṛpa. (M.B.
Droṇa Parva, Chapter 14, Stanza 33).
<div n="NI"/>(11) He fought with Ambaṣṭha. (M.B. Droṇa Parva,
Chapter 25).
<div n="NI"/>(12) He killed Vīradhanvā. (M.B. Droṇa Parva,
Chapter 107, Stanza 17).
<div n="NI"/>(13) He fought with the teacher Droṇa and was killed
in the fight. (M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter 125,
Stanza 23).
<div n="NI"/>(14) Among the spirits of those relatives of the
Kauravas, who died in the Bhārata-battle, which were
evoked to the surface of the Ganges, by Vyāsa, the
spirit of Dhṛṣṭaketu also appeared. (M.B. Āśrama-
vāsika Parva, Chapter 33, Stanza 11).
<div n="NI"/>(15) After his death Dhṛṣṭaketu became a Viśvadeva
in heaven. (M.B. Svargārohaṇa Parva, Chapter 5, Stanza
15).
<div n="NI"/>(16) Vyāsa has used the following names in his
Bhārata for Dhṛṣtaketu: Caidya, Cedija, Cedipati, Cedi-
puṅgava, Cedirāṭ, Śaiśupāli, Śiśupālātmaja.
