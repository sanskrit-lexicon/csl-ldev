DĀNAM¦. Gift. In ancient India a spiritual significance
<div n="lb"/>was attached to dānam. (offering of a free gift). One
<div n="lb"/>who gave water was said to achieve contentment; one
<div n="lb"/>who gave food, eternal happiness; one who gave land,
<div n="lb"/>government of the country; one who gave gold would
<div n="lb"/>attain longevity; one who gave a house would get
<div n="lb"/>domestic felicity; one who gave silver would get physical
<div n="lb"/>beauty; one who gave clothes would reach candraloka;
<div n="lb"/>one who gave a horse would attain the world of the
<div n="lb"/>Aśvinīdevas; one who gave bulls would get prosperity;
<div n="lb"/>one who gave cows would reach sūryaloka; according to
<div n="lb"/>Manusmṛti.
