<L>3203<pc>381-b<k1>काम्बोज<k2>काम्बोज<h>II
KĀMBOJA II¦. This kingdom was situated in the north
<div n="lb"/>western part of India. It is the modern Kabul. We get
<div n="lb"/>the following information from Mahābhārata.
<div n="NI"/>(1) From Mahābhārata, Sabhā Parva, Chapter 27,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 23, we see that Arjuna had subdued this
<div n="lb"/>Kingdom.
<div n="NI"/>(2) The horses which were tied to Yudhiṣṭhira's chariot
<div n="lb"/>were brought from Kāmboja. (M.B. Sabhā Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 52, Verse 5).
<div n="NI"/>(3) The Mlecchas (a tribe of low-class people) of
<div n="lb"/>Kāmboja will become Kings in Kaliyuga. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Vana Parva, Chapter 188, Verse 36).
<div n="NI"/>(4) There were Kāmbojas in Duryodhana's army.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Udyoga Parva, Chapter 160, Verse 130).
<div n="NI"/>(5) At the time of Mahābhārata the King of Kāmboja
<div n="lb"/>was the brave and heroic Sudakṣiṇa. (M.B. Udyoga
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 166, Verses 1-3).
<div n="NI"/>(6) In the battle between Kauravas and Pāṇḍavas, the
<div n="lb"/>Kāmbojas took their position in some places in the
<div n="lb"/>“Garuḍa Vyūha”, a phalanx in the shape of an eagle
<div n="lb"/>made by Bhīṣma. (M.B. Bhīṣma parva, Chapter 56,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 7).
<div n="NI"/>(7) The horses of Kāmboja were beautiful in ap-
<div n="lb"/>pearance and of the colour of parrots. The horses which
<div n="lb"/>were tied to Nakula's chariot, were of this kind. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Droṇa Parva, Chapter 23, Verse 7).
<div n="NI"/>(8) When the horses of Kāmboja ran, their tails and
<div n="lb"/>ears remained motionless. (M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>36, Verse 36).
