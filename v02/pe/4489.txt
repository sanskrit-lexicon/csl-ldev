MEDHĀTITHI II¦. A Rajarṣi of the Pūru dynasty.
<div n="NI"/>{%Genealogy.%} Pūru—Janamejaya Prācīnvān—Manasyu—
<div n="lb"/>Vītabhaya—Śuṇḍu—Bahuvidha—Saṃyāti—Rahovādī—
<div n="lb"/>Bhadrāśva—Matināra—Pratiratha—Kaṇva—Medhā-
<div n="lb"/>tithi.
<div n="NI"/>This Medhātithi got two sons named Duṣyanta and
<div n="lb"/>Pravīra. It was this Duṣyanta who married Śakuntalā.
<div n="lb"/>(Chapter 278, Agni Purāṇa).
<div n="NI"/>Medhātithi was a celebrated sage of Bhārata. The
<div n="lb"/>maharṣi referred to in Śūkta 12, Anuvāka 4, Maṇḍala
<div n="lb"/>1 of Ṛgveda is Medhātithi son of Kaṇva. Once
<div n="lb"/>Indra coming in the form of a goat drank the soma of
<div n="lb"/>Medhātithi and the latter called him ‘goat’ (Meṣa)
<div n="lb"/>and thenceforth Indra got the name Meṣa. (Sūkta 51,
<div n="lb"/>Anuvāka 10, Maṇḍala 1, Ṛgveda).
<div n="NI"/>We get the following details about Medhātithi from the
<div n="lb"/>Mahābhārata.
<div n="NI"/>(1) He was a King in the court of Indra.
<div n="NI"/>(2) Medhātithi had a son named Kaṇvamuni who was
<div n="lb"/>popular on the east coast of Bhārata. (Śloka 23,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 107, Śānti Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(3) Medhātithi observed Vānaprastha and attained
<div n="lb"/>Svarga. (Śloka 7, Chapter 336, Śānti Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(4) Medhātithi was considered to be a sage deserving
<div n="lb"/>worship and when he went to see Bhīṣma once Dharma-
<div n="lb"/>putra received him with respect and worshipped him.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 3, Chapter 26, Anuśāsana Parva).
