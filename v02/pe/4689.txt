NAKṢATRAKALPA¦. A part of the Atharvaveda. The
<div n="lb"/>great hermit Muñjakeśa had divided the Atharvaveda
<div n="lb"/>into five parts called Nakṣatrakalpa, Vedakalpa,
<div n="lb"/>Saṃhitākalpa, Āṅgirasakalpa and Śānti Kalpa.
<div n="lb"/>Nakṣatra Kalpa deals with the order of worshipping the
<div n="lb"/>Nakṣatras(stars), Veda Kalpa deals with the activities of
<div n="lb"/>Brahmā as a Ṛtvik (family priest), the Saṃhitā Kalpa,
<div n="lb"/>the mantras (spells and incantations). In Āṅgirasa Kalpa
<div n="lb"/>sorcery and enchantment and in Śāntikalpa, Śāntis
<div n="lb"/>(Alleviation or cure) of horse, elephant etc. are given.
<div n="lb"/>(Viṣṇu Purāṇa, Aṃśa 3, Chapter 6).
