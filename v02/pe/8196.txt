VEṆĀ¦. A river famous in the Purāṇas. Information
<div n="lb"/>about this river, taken from Mahābhārata, is given
<div n="lb"/>below:
<div n="NI"/>(i) Veṇā-river stays in the palace of Varuṇa serving
<div n="lb"/>him. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 9, Stanza 18).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Sahadeva defeated the ruler of the country at the
<div n="lb"/>basin of river Veṇā, during his conquest of the regions
<div n="lb"/>of the south. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 31, Stanza
<div n="lb"/>12).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Those who fast on the basin of this river for three
<div n="lb"/>days will go to heaven in an aerial chariot yoked with
<div n="lb"/>pea-cocks and swans. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter 85,
<div n="lb"/>Stanza 32).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Among the rivers which originate fire, this river
<div n="lb"/>also is included. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter 224,
<div n="lb"/>Stanza 24).
<div n="NI"/>(v) It is mentioned in Mahābhārata, Anuśāsana Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 165, Stanza 20, that this is a river worthy to
<div n="lb"/>be remembered every morning and evening.
