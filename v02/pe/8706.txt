<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} The Caturvedas are:—Ṛgveda, Yajurveda
<div n="lb"/>Sāmaveda and Atharvaveda. Viṣṇu Purāṇa, Part 3,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 5, mentions that Vaiśampāyana, the disciple of
<div n="lb"/>Vedavyāsa, divided Yajurveda into twentyseven branch-
<div n="lb"/>es and taught them to his disciples. Among those disci-
<div n="lb"/>ples there was Yājñavalkya, the son of Brahmarāta. It
<div n="lb"/>was Vyāsa who divided the Vedas into four parts. After
<div n="lb"/>dīviding them, the sage Vyāsa taught Ṛgveda to Paila,
<div n="lb"/>Yajurveda to Vaiśampāyana, Sāmaveda to Jaimini and
<div n="lb"/>Atharvaveda to Sumantu.
<div n="NI"/>The rules for Japa, Homa etc. of Yajurveda were taught
<div n="lb"/>to Vyāsa by Agnideva. If all the rules of Yajurveda
<div n="lb"/>are correctly observed, all desires will be fulfilled.
<div n="lb"/>There are special rules for the observance of homa for
<div n="lb"/>the fulfilment of particular desires.
