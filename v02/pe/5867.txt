ŚABARA¦. A mleccha—low caste. The Mahābhārata
has the following about Śabaras.
<div n="NI"/>(i) Śabaras were born from the dung and urine of
Nandinī, the cow of Vasiṣṭha. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 174,
Verse 16).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) When Sātyaki annihilated the Kauravas the dead
bodies of thousands of Śabaras were heaped on the
battle-field. (Droṇa Parva, Chapter 119, Verse 46).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) In early days the Śabaras lived in the kingdom
of Māndhātā, their profession being murder and loot-
ing. (Śānti Parva, Chapter 65, Verse 13).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Śiva had once taken the form of forest-dwellers
and Śabaras. (Anuśāsana Parva. Chapter 65, Verse
17).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Many Kṣatriyas lived for many years hidden in
caves for fear of Paraśurāma, and as they had no
association with kṣatriyas during the period, they
became Śabaras. (Aśvamedhika Parva, Chapter 29,
Verse 15).
