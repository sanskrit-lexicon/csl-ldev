ILILA¦. A King of Pūruvaṃśa; father of King Duṣyanta.
(M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 71). Rathantarī was Ilila's
wife. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 74). Some Purāṇas call
Ilila by the name Īlina and Rathantarī as Rathaṃdarī
also.
<div n="NI"/>Matināra, the most righteous of Kings had four sons
endowed with great prowess named Taṃsu, Mahān,
Atiratha and Druhyu. Taṃsu was responsible for the
growth of the Pūru dynasty. He conquered the whole
of earth and won fame. Īlina was Taṃsu's son. He also
conquered the whole world, and he had five sons. (M.B.
Ādi Parva, Chapter 94, Verses 14-18).
