PULAHA¦. One of the Prajāpatis. The references about
<div n="lb"/>him in the Purāṇas are the following:
<div n="NI"/>(1) Pulaha was one of the spiritual sons of Brahmā.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 12, Chapter 204, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(2) Kṣamā, wife of Pulaha, delivered three sons named
<div n="lb"/>Kardama, Urvarīvān and Sahiṣṇu. (Chapter 10, Aṃśa 1,
<div n="lb"/>Viṣṇu Purāṇa).
<div n="NI"/>(3) Pulaha got of his wife Kṣamā another son named
<div n="lb"/>Karmaśreṣtha. (Chapter 20, Agni Purāṇa).
<div n="NI"/>(4) Pulaha is included in the group of six powerful
<div n="lb"/>sages. (Śloka 4, Chapter 66, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(5) From Pulaha were born the butterflies, lions, tigers,
<div n="lb"/>lambs, wolves and Kimpuruṣas. (Śloka 3, Chapter 66,
<div n="lb"/>Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(6) Pulaha took part in the Janmotsava of Arjuna.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 52, Chapter 122, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(7) Pulaha was also among the sages who dissuaded
<div n="lb"/>Parāśara from conducting a yāga to kill all the rākṣasas.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 9, Chapter 180, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(8) Pulaha was a member of the court of Indra. (Śloka
<div n="lb"/>17, Chapter 7, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(9) Pulaha was a worshipper of Brahmā. (Śloka 18,
<div n="lb"/>(Chapter 11, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(10) Pulaha did penance at a place on the shores of
<div n="lb"/>Alakanandā, a tributary of river Gaṅgā. (Śloka 6,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 142, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(11) He took part in the Janmotsava of Subrahmaṇya.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 9, Chapter 45, Śalya Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(12) Pulaha is included in the twenty one Prajāpatis.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 35, Chapter 334, Śānti Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(13) Pulaha is one among the group of Saptarṣis
<div n="lb"/>called Citraśikhaṇḍins. (Śloka 29, Chapter 335, Śānti
<div n="lb"/>Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(14) Pulaha is also one of the Aṣṭaprakṛtis. (Chapter
<div n="lb"/>340, Śānti Parva).
