VINDHYA¦. One of the seven chief mountain chains.
<div n="lb"/>This mountain which separates South India from
<div n="lb"/>North India is famous in various ways in the Purāṇas.
<div n="NI"/>(i) Agastya kicked Vindhya down. (See under Agastya).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Sunda and Upasunda, two asuras, did penance on
<div n="lb"/>Vindhya and got boons. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>208, Stanza 7).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Because of the fierce penance of Sunda, the
<div n="lb"/>mountain Vindhya became hot and from that day
<div n="lb"/>onwards smoke comes out from Vindhya. (M.B. Ādi
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 208, Stanza 10).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) The deity of Vindhya stays in the palace of Kubera
<div n="lb"/>serving him. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 10, Stanza
<div n="lb"/>31).
<div n="NI"/>(v) On this mountain there is the eternal abode of
<div n="lb"/>Devī Durgā. (M.B. Virāṭa Parva, Chapter 6, Stanza
<div n="lb"/>17).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) Vindhya is one of the seven chief mountain
<div n="lb"/>chains. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 9, Stanza 11).
[Page857-b+ 64]
<div n="NI"/>(vii) In Tripuradahana (the burning of Tripuras)
<div n="lb"/>Vindhya appeared behind the chariot of Śiva as the
<div n="lb"/>emblem on his flag. (M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter 202,
<div n="lb"/>Stanza 71);
<div n="NI"/>(viii) Vindhya once became the axle of the chariot of
<div n="lb"/>Śiva. (M.B. Karṇa Parva, Chapter 34, Stanza 22).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) This mountain gave Subrahmaṇya two attendants
<div n="lb"/>named Ucchṛṅga and Atiśṛṅga. (M.B. Śalya Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 45, Stanza 49).
<div n="NI"/>(x) Mention is made in Mahābhārata, Anuśāsana
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 25, Stanza 49, that he who does pe-
<div n="lb"/>nance on the Vindhya, for a month without killing any
<div n="lb"/>living creature, could obtain all attainments.
