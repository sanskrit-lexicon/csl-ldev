VINDHYA¦. One of the seven chief mountain chains.
This mountain which separates South India from
North India is famous in various ways in the Purāṇas.
<div n="NI"/>(i) Agastya kicked Vindhya down. (See under Agastya).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Sunda and Upasunda, two asuras, did penance on
Vindhya and got boons. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter
208, Stanza 7).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Because of the fierce penance of Sunda, the
mountain Vindhya became hot and from that day
onwards smoke comes out from Vindhya. (M.B. Ādi
Parva, Chapter 208, Stanza 10).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) The deity of Vindhya stays in the palace of Kubera
serving him. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 10, Stanza
31).
<div n="NI"/>(v) On this mountain there is the eternal abode of
Devī Durgā. (M.B. Virāṭa Parva, Chapter 6, Stanza
17).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) Vindhya is one of the seven chief mountain
chains. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 9, Stanza 11).
[Page857-b+ 64]
<div n="NI"/>(vii) In Tripuradahana (the burning of Tripuras)
Vindhya appeared behind the chariot of Śiva as the
emblem on his flag. (M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter 202,
Stanza 71);
<div n="NI"/>(viii) Vindhya once became the axle of the chariot of
Śiva. (M.B. Karṇa Parva, Chapter 34, Stanza 22).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) This mountain gave Subrahmaṇya two attendants
named Ucchṛṅga and Atiśṛṅga. (M.B. Śalya Parva,
Chapter 45, Stanza 49).
<div n="NI"/>(x) Mention is made in Mahābhārata, Anuśāsana
Parva, Chapter 25, Stanza 49, that he who does pe-
nance on the Vindhya, for a month without killing any
living creature, could obtain all attainments.
