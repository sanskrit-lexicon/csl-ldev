RAṆṬU¦ (The Malayālam word for ‘two’).
<div n="NI"/>1. {%Ayanas two.%}
<div n="NI"/>A. Uttarāyaṇa, from July-August to December-January
(Dhanu). B. Dakṣiṇāyana, from January-February
(Makaram) to June-July (Mithunam). For the Devas
Uttarāyaṇa is day and Dakṣiṇāyana night. Religious-
minded Hindus believe that Uttarāyaṇa is an auspici-
ous period for death.
<div n="NI"/>2. {%Arthadūṣaṇas%} 2. (Misuse of wealth)
<div n="NI"/>(i) Spending money for bad purposes and not spending
money for good purposes.
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Stealing money or wealth and not giving money to
poor deserving people.
<div n="NI"/>3. {%Alaṅkāras%} 2. (Figures of speech). Arthālaṅkāra and
Śabdālaṅkāra.
<div n="NI"/>4. {%Auṣadhas%} 2. (Medicines). Curatives like medicated
oils and ghees, honey etc. and purgatives like vasti
(enema), recanam (purgation) and vamanam
(vomiting).
<div n="NI"/>5. {%Aids for health%} 2. Cleanliness and sexual continence.
<div n="NI"/>6. {%Gurus%} 2. (Preceptors). Actual preceptor or precep-
tor in effect and casual preceptor).
<div n="NI"/>7. {%Kaṭudvayam.%} Pippalī (long pepper) and Marica
(Pepper)
<div n="NI"/>8. {%Kalahakāraṇam%} 2. (Causes for quarrel). Gold and
woman (Kanaka and Kāminī).
<div n="NI"/>9. {%Kāyadvayam (Two bodies).%} Mind and body.
<div n="NI"/>10. {%Kāraṇadvayam (Two causes).%} Upādānam (ultimate
cause) and Nimittam (immediate cause).
<div n="NI"/>11. {%Kṣāradvayam (Two salts).%} Alkaline salt (Saltpetre),
Sodium Carbonate.
<div n="NI"/>12. {%Capaladvayam (Fickle ones 2).%} Women and fools.
<div n="NI"/>13. {%Jñānadvayam%} (Knowledges. 2). Superficial knowledge
and deep knowledge. (Mandajñāna and Dṛḍhajñāna).

<div n="NI"/>14. {%Dharmadvayam%} ({%Duties%} 2). Action and renunciation.
(Pravṛtti and Nivṛtti).
<div n="NI"/>15. {%Niśādvayam.%} Turmeric and Amonum Xanthorrhizon
<div n="NI"/>16. {%Niṣṭhādvayam.%} Sādhya (knowledge) and Sādhana
(action).
<div n="NI"/>17. {%Pakṣadvayam.%} Kṛṣṇapakṣa (dark fortnight) and
Śuklapakṣa moon-lit fortnight.
<div n="NI"/>18. {%Padyakāvyadvayam%} (Poetry 2). Lyric and Mahā-
kāvyam (epic).
<div n="NI"/>19. {%Pāpadvayam%} ({%Sins%} 2). Original sin and acquired.
(Janmapāpa and Karmapāpa).
<div n="NI"/>20. {%Mokṣakāraṇadvayam.%} Causes for salvation, 2). Yoga
and death in war.
<div n="NI"/>21. {%Yogadvayam.%} Karma (action) and Jñāna (know-
ledge).
<div n="NI"/>22. {%Rāgadvayam%} (colour). Pūrṇam (unmixed) and
Janyam (mixed).
<div n="NI"/>23. {%Rogadvayam (Diseases 2).%} (a) Physical and mental
(Kāyika and Mānasika). (b) Congenital and acquir-
ed. (Sahaja and Āgantuka).
<div n="NI"/>24. {%Vīryadvayam.%} Heat and cold.
<div n="NI"/>25. {%Śṛṅgāradvayam.%} Love or the erotic sentiment 2). Love
in union and that in separation. (Sambhoga and
Vipralambha).
<div n="NI"/>26. {%Śrutidharmadvayam (Musical cadence 2).%} High pitch
and low pitch (Ucca and nīca).
<div n="NI"/>27. {%Samādhānakāraṇadvayam (Two causes for peace).%} Pati-
ence or forbearance and generosity. (Kṣamā and
dānaśīla).
