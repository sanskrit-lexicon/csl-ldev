VIDHĀTĀ¦. A son born to Bhṛgu. By his wife Khyāti
<div n="lb"/>two sons Dhātā and Vidhātā and a daughter named
<div n="lb"/>Lakṣmī were born to Bhṛgu. Lakṣmī was given in
<div n="lb"/>marriage to Viṣṇu. The daughter of Meru, Āyati, was
<div n="lb"/>married by Dhātā and Niyati by Vidhātā. The son
<div n="lb"/>Mṛkaṇḍu was born to Vidhātā by Niyati. This Mṛkaṇḍu
<div n="lb"/>was the father of Mārkaṇḍeya. (Viṣṇu Purāṇa, Aṃśa
<div n="lb"/>1, Chapter 10).
<div n="NI"/>The following statements occur about Dhātā and
<div n="lb"/>Vidhātā in Mahābhārata.
<div n="NI"/>(i) At Nākaloka (heaven) Dhātā and Vidhātā took
<div n="lb"/>the form of women and allowed hermit Uttaṅka to
<div n="lb"/>see them. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 3, Stanza 166).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Dhātā and Vidhātā stood above the city of
<div n="lb"/>Virāṭa to see the battle between Arjuna and Kṛpa.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Virāṭa Parva, Chapter 56, Stanza 11).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Dhātā and Vidhātā lived with Manu. (M.B. Ādi
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 65, Stanza 42).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Dhātā and Vidhātā gave to Subrahmaṇya two
<div n="lb"/>followers named Suvrata and Sukarmā. (M.B. Śalya
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 45, Stanza 42).
