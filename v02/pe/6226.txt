ŚĀNTANU¦ (ŚANTANU). Son of King Pratīpa of the
<div n="lb"/>lunar dynasty.
<div n="NI"/>(i) For previous life and birth as Śantanu see under
<div n="lb"/>Bhīṣma, Para II.
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Married life. (See under Bhīṣma, Paras 2, 3).
<div n="NI"/>3) {%Other information.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) He was the second son of Pratīpa. His elder brother
<div n="lb"/>was called Devāpi, younger one Bālhīka and his mother
<div n="lb"/>Sunandā. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 94, Verse 61; Chapter
<div n="lb"/>95, Verse 45).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) He was called Śantanu as things touched by both
<div n="lb"/>his hands used to become youthful.
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Śantanu became King as his elder brother Devāpi
<div n="lb"/>had, as an infant, renounced the throne and left for the
[Page689-b+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>forest. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 94, Verse 62; Chapter 95,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 45).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) It was he, who brought to the palace and brought
<div n="lb"/>up Kṛpa and Kṛpī, who were found in the forest as
<div n="lb"/>orphans. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 95, Verse 46).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Śantanu worships Yama in his court. (Sabhā
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 8, Verse 25).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) He attained heaven by doing tapas on Mount
<div n="lb"/>Ārcika. (Vana Parva, Chapter 125, Verse 19).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) He is one of the Kings to be remembered both
<div n="lb"/>at dawn and dusk. (Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 165,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 58).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) He was absolutely wedded to truth and he
<div n="lb"/>possessed great prowess. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 96, Verse
<div n="lb"/>1).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) He conducted thousand aśvamedhas and hundred
<div n="lb"/>rājasūyas. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 96, Verse 2).
<div n="NI"/>(x) Synonyms used of him in the Mahābhārata:—
<div n="lb"/>Bharata, Bhāratagoptā, Bharatasattama, Kauravya,
<div n="lb"/>Kurusattama, Prātīpa etc.
