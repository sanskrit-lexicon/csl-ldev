DHŪMRĀKṢA II¦. A minister of Rāvaṇa.
[Page240-a+ 63]
<div n="lb"/>{%Genealogy.%} Brahmā created the Rākṣasa (giant) Heti.
<div n="lb"/>The son Vidyutkeśa was born to him of his wife Bhayā.
<div n="lb"/>Sukeśa was born to Vidyutkeśa by his wife Sālakaṭaṅkā.
<div n="lb"/>Sukeśa married Devavatī. Three sons, Mālyavān,
<div n="lb"/>Sumālī and Mālī were born to the couple. To Sumālī,
<div n="lb"/>by his wife Ketumatī, fourteen children were born:
<div n="lb"/>Prahasta, Akaṃpana, Vikaṭa, Kālakāmukha, Dhūmrā-
<div n="lb"/>kṣa, Daṇḍa, Supārśvā, Saṃhrāda, Prākvāda, Bhāsa-
<div n="lb"/>karṇa, Veka, Puṣpotkaṭa, Kaikasi, and Kumbhīnasi;
<div n="lb"/>most of them were ministers of Rāvaṇa. (Uttara
<div n="lb"/>Rāmāyaṇa).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Work and death.%} Dhūmrākṣa was one who worked in
<div n="lb"/>all the branches of administration of the state. In all
<div n="lb"/>the battles fought by the Rākṣasas Dhūmrākṣa was
<div n="lb"/>present. He was also called Dhūmralocana. He was
<div n="lb"/>killed in the battle of Rāma and Rāvaṇa at the hands
<div n="lb"/>of Hanūmān. (Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa, Yuddha Kāṇḍa,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 51).
