VṚṢṆI¦. A famous King of the Yadu dynasty.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Genealogy.%} Descended from Viṣṇu in the following
<div n="lb"/>order: Brahmā-Atri-Candra-Budha-Purūravas-Āyus-
<div n="lb"/>Nahuṣa-Yayāti-Yadu-Sahasrajit-Śatajit-Hehaya-
<div n="lb"/>Dharma-Kuṇi-Bhadrasena-Dhanaka-Kṛtavīrya-Kārta-
<div n="lb"/>vīryārjuna-Madhu-Vṛṣṇi.
[Page883-b+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) The birth of Śrī Kṛṣṇa was in the family of Vṛṣṇi.
<div n="lb"/>(See the genealogy of Śrī Kṛṣṇa).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) The line of Kings beginning with Vṛṣṇi is called
<div n="lb"/>the dynasty of Vṛṣṇi. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 217,
<div n="lb"/>Stanza 18).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Thinking that the jewel Syamantaka had been
<div n="lb"/>stolen by Kṛṣṇa himself, Vṛṣṇi took a hostile mentality
<div n="lb"/>towards Kṛṣṇa. (Brahmāṇḍa Purāṇa, 3: 71: 1).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Vṛṣṇi had two wives named Gāndhārī and Mādrī.
<div n="lb"/>Five sons were born to him by Mādrī. (Vāyu: 94:
<div n="lb"/>14; Mahābhārata, Ādi Parva, Chapter 211; 1-2; 5: 8).
