SARASVATĪ II¦. River Sarasvatī, flowing through north
India is, according to Purāṇic conception, Sarasvatī
devī who has assumed the form of a river. (See under
Sarasvatī I). The following information about river
Sarasvatī famed in the Purāṇas is from the Mahā-
bhārata.
<div n="NI"/>(i) King Matināra once performed a yajña on the
banks of river Sarasvatī. At the close of the yajña
Sarasvatīdevī appeared and chose the King as her hus-
band, and a son called Taṃsu was born to the couple.
(Ādi Parva, Chapter 95, Verse 26).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) River Sarasvatī is one of the seven tributaries of
river Gaṅgā and its source is under the Plaksa tree. One
[Page696-b+ 64]
who drinks its water will become free from sin. (Ādi
Parva, Chapter 16, Verse 19).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Sarasvatī worships Varuṇa in his court. (Sabhā
Parva, Chapter 9, Verse 19).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) The Pāṇḍavas, while travelling in the forest,
crossed the river. (Vana Parva, Chapter 5, Verse 2).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Śrī Kṛṣṇa conducted a yajña in the plains of river
Sarasvatī. (Vana Parva, Chapter 12, Verse 14).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) Kāmyaka forest is on the banks of Sarasvatī.
(Vana Parva, Chapter 36, Verse 41).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) It is a holy river. If one bathes in it and worships
one's ancestors one will attain Sārasvataloka. (Vana
Parva, Chapter 84, Verse 66)
<div n="NI"/>(viii) Dadhīca had his āśrama on the banks of the
river. (Vana Parva, Chapter 100, Verse 13).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) Muni Lomaśa once extolled the greatness of river
Sarasvatī. (Vana Parva, Chapter 129, Verse 20).
<div n="NI"/>(x) The river disappears in Vināśanatīrtha and
reappears at Camasodbheda. (Vana Parva, Chapter 130,
Verse 3).
<div n="NI"/>(xi) It is the source of Agni. (Vana Parva, Chapter
222, Verse 22).
<div n="NI"/>(xii) There are many holy places on the plains of the
river. They are described in Chapters 35-54 of Salya
Parva.
<div n="NI"/>(xiii) River Sarasvatī once carried Vasiṣṭha in its flow.
(See under Vasiṣṭha).
<div n="NI"/>(xiv) Viśvāmitra cursed Sarasvatī. (See under
Vasiṣṭha).
<div n="NI"/>(xv) Sarasvatī returned to Dadhīci maharṣi the son
born to her by him and he blessed her. (Śalya Parva,
Chapter 51).
<div n="NI"/>(xvi) Balabhadrarāma once extolled the greatness
of Sarasvatī. (Śalya Parva, Chapter 54, Verse 33).
<div n="NI"/>(xvii) Arjuna appointed the son of Sātyaki as the
master of a particular region on the banks of the river.
(Mausala Parva, Chapter 8, Verse 71).
<div n="NI"/>(xviii) Following the death of Śrī Kṛṣṇa his 16008
wives drowned themselves to death in Sarasvatī.
(Svargārohaṇa Parva, Chapter 5, Verse 25).
