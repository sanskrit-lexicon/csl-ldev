DURDAMA¦. Son of a Gandharva called Viśvāvasu.
<div n="lb"/>The following story about him is told in the Skanda
<div n="lb"/>Purāṇa. Chapters 1, 3 and 4).
<div n="NI"/>While maharṣis like Vasiṣṭha and Atri were once
<div n="lb"/>engaged in the worship of Lord Śiva at Mount Kailāsa,
<div n="lb"/>Durdama, with his thousands of wives came there. In
<div n="lb"/>the Hālāsyatīrtha near-by they got engaged in water
<div n="lb"/>sports with their bodies stark naked. On seeing their
<div n="lb"/>naked sports Vasiṣṭha cursed Durdama to be turned
<div n="lb"/>into a Rākṣasa. When his wives begged of Vasiṣṭha for
<div n="lb"/>redemption from the curse he told them that seven-
<div n="lb"/>teen years thence Durdama would regain his old form.
<div n="lb"/>Durdama thus turned Rākṣasa tried once to devour
<div n="lb"/>sage Gālava when the Sudarśana Cakra of Viṣṇu killed
<div n="lb"/>him. He regained his old form and returned to
<div n="lb"/>Gandharvaloka.
