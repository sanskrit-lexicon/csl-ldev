MĀLAVA¦. An ancient country on the west coast of India.
<div n="lb"/>Mahābhārata contains the following statements regard-
<div n="lb"/>ing Mālava:
<div n="NI"/>(i) The people of Mālava participated in the Rājasūya
<div n="lb"/>of Yudhiṣṭhira. (Śloka 11, Chapter 34, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Armed young Kṣatriyas of Mālava presented
<div n="lb"/>Yudhiṣṭhira with great wealth. (Śloka 15, Chapter 52,
<div n="lb"/>Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Karṇa conquered Mālavadeśa. (Śloka 20, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>254, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Mālava was one of the prominent and renowned
<div n="lb"/>countries of ancient India (Śloka 60, Chapter 9, Bhīṣma
<div n="lb"/>Parva).
[Page471-b+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>(v) Obeying the order of Bhīṣma the people of Mālava
<div n="lb"/>attacked Arjuna. (Śloka 76, Chapter 59, Bhīṣma
<div n="lb"/>Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) Śrī Kṛṣṇa once conquered Mālavadeśa. (Śloka 16,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 19, Droṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) Paraśurāma exterminated the Kṣatriyas of Mālava-
<div n="lb"/>deśa. (Śloka 11, Chapter 70, Droṇa Parva).
