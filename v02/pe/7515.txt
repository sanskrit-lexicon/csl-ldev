TĀRA¦. A monkey who was a devotee of Śrī Rāma.
<div n="lb"/>This big monkey was the son of Bṛhaspati. Bṛhaspati
<div n="lb"/>made this monkey greater in size and intellect than
<div n="lb"/>all other monkeys. This Tāra was the minister of Bāli.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 10, Sarga 17, Bāla Kāṇḍa, Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa
<div n="lb"/>and Uttara Rāmāyaṇa). In the Rāma-Rāvaṇa battle
<div n="lb"/>this monkey fought against the demon Nikharvaṭa.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 9, Chapter 285, Vana Parva).
