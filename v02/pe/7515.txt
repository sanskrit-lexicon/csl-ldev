TĀRA¦. A monkey who was a devotee of Śrī Rāma.
This big monkey was the son of Bṛhaspati. Bṛhaspati
made this monkey greater in size and intellect than
all other monkeys. This Tāra was the minister of Bāli.
(Śloka 10, Sarga 17, Bāla Kāṇḍa, Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa
and Uttara Rāmāyaṇa). In the Rāma-Rāvaṇa battle
this monkey fought against the demon Nikharvaṭa.
(Śloka 9, Chapter 285, Vana Parva).
