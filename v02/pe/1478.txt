CARMAṆVATĪ¦. A river in north India, now known
as river Campā.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} King Śaśabindu, who ruled northern
India in olden days once performed a yajña. The skins
(carmans) of animals killed in the yajña lay there in a
heap like a hill. When rain fell there flowed from the
‘skin-hill’ a river and it was called Carmaṇvatī. (Devī-
bhāgavata, Prathama Skandha).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%} (1) River Carmaṇvatī serves Varuṇa
in his assembly. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 9, Verse
21).
<div n="NI"/>(2) Once on the bank of this river Sahadeva defeated
the son of Jaṃbhaka in fight. (M.B. Sabhā Parva,
Chapter 31, Verse 7).
<div n="NI"/>(3) He who bathes in this river will get the same result
as from the Agniṣṭoma yajña. (M.B. Vana Parva,
Chapter 82, Verse 54).
<div n="NI"/>(4) Carmaṇvatī is one of the rivers responsible for the
origin of Agni. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 222,
Verse 23).
