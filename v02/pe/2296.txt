GADA I¦. A brother (Yādava) of Balabhadrarāma.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} Vasudeva had, besides Devakī, seven wives
<div n="lb"/>called Rohiṇī, Bhadrā, Mālinī, Madirā, Ilā, Rocanā
<div n="lb"/>and Pauravī, and out of the seven Rohiṇī had six sons
<div n="lb"/>called Balabhadrarāma, Gada, Sāraṇa, Durdama,
<div n="lb"/>Vikala and Kṛta. (Bhāgavata, Navama Skandha).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(1) Gada was present at the svayaṃvara of Draupadī.
<div n="lb"/>(Ādi Parva, Chapter 185, Verse 32).
<div n="NI"/>(2) On the marriage of Arjuna with Subhadrā,
<div n="lb"/>it was Gada who brought the dowry of Subhadrā from
<div n="lb"/>Dvārakā to Indraprastha. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 220,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 32).
<div n="NI"/>(3) When Śrī Kṛṣṇa came to Dvārakā Gada received
<div n="lb"/>him with embraces. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 2,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 35).
<div n="NI"/>(4) Gada also was present on the occasion when the
<div n="lb"/>Pāṇḍavas first stepped into the beautiful palace built
<div n="lb"/>for them by Maya at Indraprastha. (M.B. Sabhā
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 4, Verse 39).
<div n="NI"/>(5) Along with the other Yādavas Gada too was
<div n="lb"/>present at the rājasūya yajña of Yudhiṣṭhira. (M.B.
<div n="lb"/>Sabhā Parva, Chapter 34, Verse 16).

<div n="NI"/>(6) Gada held a prominent position among the Yāda-
<div n="lb"/>vas deputed to guard Dvārakā against the onslaught
<div n="lb"/>of King Sālva. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter 15,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 9).
<div n="NI"/>(7) Gada also accompanied Śrī Kṛṣṇa to the Aśva-
<div n="lb"/>medha yajña performed by Yudhiṣṭhira. (Aśvamedha
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 86, Verse 9).
<div n="NI"/>(8) Śrī Kṛṣṇa felt uncontrollably angry when Gada
<div n="lb"/>was attacked during the mutual fighting with iron rods
<div n="lb"/>by the Yādavas which climaxed in their total annihila-
<div n="lb"/>tion. (Mausala Parva, Chapter 3, Verse 45).
