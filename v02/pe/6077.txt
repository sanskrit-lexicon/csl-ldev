ŚAMĪKA I¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} A muni. He remained with herds of cattle
<div n="lb"/>and performed tapas feeding himself on the foam from
<div n="lb"/>the mouth of calves drinking their mother's milk. He was
<div n="lb"/>the father of Śṛṅgī, who cursed king Parīkṣit, who once
<div n="lb"/>threw a dead snake on the neck of Śamīka. The curse
<div n="lb"/>was that he would die within seven days of the incident
<div n="lb"/>by snake-bite. For details see under Parīkṣit, Para 3).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other information.%}
<div n="NI"/>i) Śamīka worships Indra in his court. (Sabhā Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 7, Verse 16).
<div n="NI"/>ii) Śamīka too was present on the occasion when Vyāsa
<div n="lb"/>called up and showed Janamejaya the souls of dead
<div n="lb"/>kings. (Aśvamedhika Parva, Chapter 35, Verse 8).
