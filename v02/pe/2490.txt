GOTAMA¦. A sage named Gotama, son of Rahūgaṇa,
<div n="lb"/>is found everywhere in Ṛgveda. The seventy-fourth
<div n="lb"/>sūkta in the thirteenth anuvāka of the first maṇḍala of
<div n="lb"/>Ṛgveda is composed by this sage. There are many other
<div n="lb"/>sūktas also in his name. This sage is not the Gautama,
<div n="lb"/>husband of Ahalyā, who made Sūkta 58, Anuvaka 11,
<div n="lb"/>Maṇḍala 1 of Ṛgveda.
<div n="NI"/>Once this Gotama tired of thirst asked the Maruts for
<div n="lb"/>some water. The Maruts took a huge well to his side
<div n="lb"/>and poured water into a big pot. (Sūktas 86, 87,
<div n="lb"/>Anuvāka 14, Maṇḍala 1, Ṛgveda).
<div n="NI"/>It was Aśvinīdevas who took the well to Gotama.
<div n="lb"/>(Sūkta 116, Anuvaka 17, Maṇḍala 1, Ṛgveda).
