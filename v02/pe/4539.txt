MITRA¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} One of the twelve Sūryas. The
twelve Sūryas born to Aditi of Kaśyapaprajāpati are
Viṣṇu, Śakra, Aryamā, Dhātā, Tvaṣṭā, Pūṣā, Vivasvān,
Savitā, Mitra, Varuṇa, Bhaga and Aṃśa. (Chapter 15,
Aṃśa 1, Viṣṇu Purāṇa).
<div n="NI"/>Mitrāvaruṇas are two devatās of great intimacy. They
are always found together. If you pray to Mitrāvaruṇas
you will get plenty of rains. (Śūkta 2, Anuvāka 1,
Maṇḍala 1, Ṛgveda).
<div n="NI"/>See para 2 under Agastya to understand the story of
how Mitrāvaruṇas became the father of Agastya.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Mitra came and stood in the sky at the time of the
birth of Arjuna. (Śloka 66, Chapter 122, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Mitra was one among those sent by Indra to fight
against Kṛṣṇa and Arjuna at the time of the burning
of the forest Khāṇḍava. (Śloka 36, Chapter 226 Ādi
Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Mitra was a member of the Indra sabhā. (Śloka
21, Chapter 7, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) He presented two Pārṣadas named Suvrata and
Satyasandha to Subrahmaṇya. (Śloka 41, Chapter 45,
Śalya Parva).
