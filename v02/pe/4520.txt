MERUDĀNA¦. Merudānas (gifts of heaps like Meru)
<div n="lb"/>are all very effective and productive of good results.
<div n="lb"/>The Merus for the dānas are the following:
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Dhānyameru.%} A dhānyameru or a thousand droṇas
<div n="lb"/>of dhānya (grain) is considered the best; a dāna of
<div n="lb"/>half that quantity (i.e. 500) was fair and half of the
<div n="lb"/>latter, the worst.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Lavaṇameru.%} A dāna of sixteen droṇas of lavaṇa
<div n="lb"/>(salt) is considered to be Uttama (best); with half
<div n="lb"/>of that as Madhyama (fair) and with half of the latter
<div n="lb"/>as Adhama (worst).
<div n="NI"/>3) {%Guḍameru.%} One with ten bhāras of guḍa (jaggery)
<div n="lb"/>is considered Uttama; with half of that as Madhyama
<div n="lb"/>and with half of the latter as adhama.
<div n="NI"/>4) {%Svarṇameru.%} A svarṇameru with a thousand palas
<div n="lb"/>of Svarṇa (gold) is considered Uttama; with half of
<div n="lb"/>that as madhyama and with half of the latter as
<div n="lb"/>adhama.
<div n="NI"/>5) {%Tilameru.%} One with ten droṇas of tila (gingelly) is
<div n="lb"/>considered Uttama; with half of that as madhyama and
<div n="lb"/>with half of the latter as adhama.
<div n="NI"/>6) {%Kārpāsameru.%} A Meru with twenty bhāras of Kārpāsa
<div n="lb"/>(cotton) is considered Uttama; with ten as madhyama
<div n="lb"/>and with five as adhama.
<div n="NI"/>7) {%Ghṛtameru.%} One with twenty jars of ghṛta (ghee)
<div n="lb"/>is Uttama; with ten, madhyama and five, adhama.
<div n="NI"/>8) {%Rajatameru.%} One with ten thousand palas of silver
<div n="lb"/>is deemed Uttama; with half of that as madhyama
<div n="lb"/>and with half of the latter as adhama.
<div n="NI"/>9) {%Śarkarāmeru.%} One with eight bhāras of sugar is
<div n="lb"/>deemed Uttama; with half of that as madhyama and
[Page502-b+ 64]
<div n="lb"/>with half of the latter as adhama. (Chapter 210, Agni
<div n="lb"/>Purāṇa).
