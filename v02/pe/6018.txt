ŚALYA¦. A king who gave advice and leadership to the
<div n="lb"/>Kauravas. He ruled over the Madra or Bālhīka kingdom.
<div n="lb"/>Mādrī, wife of Pāṇḍu was Śalya's sister. Yet, in the
<div n="lb"/>great war he stood firmly on the Kaurava side and died
<div n="lb"/>at Kurukṣetra. The following role he played in the
<div n="lb"/>Bhārata story.
<div n="NI"/>(1) At the instance of Bhīṣma he gave his sister Mādrī
<div n="lb"/>in marriage to Pāṇḍu. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 112).
<div n="NI"/>(2) He was present at the wedding of Draupadī in the
<div n="lb"/>company of his sons Rukmāṅgada and Rukmaratha.
<div n="lb"/>(Ādi Parva, Chapter 185, Verse 18).
<div n="NI"/>(3) At the Śvayaṃvara of Draupadī he withdrew from
<div n="lb"/>stringing the bow, owning defeat. (Ādi Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>186, Verse 28).
<div n="NI"/>(4) Bhīma defeated him at the wedding of Draupadī.
<div n="lb"/>(Ādi Parva, Chapter 189, Verse 23)
<div n="NI"/>(5) During his triumphal tour of the west, Nakula be-
[Page675-a+ 64]
<div n="lb"/>friended his uncle Śalya, and entertained him. (Sabhā
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 32, Verse 14).
<div n="NI"/>(6) He participated in Yudhiṣṭhira's Rājasūya yajña.
<div n="lb"/>(Sabhā Parva, Chapter 34, Verse 7).
<div n="NI"/>(7) Śiśupāla once opined that Śalya was nobler than
<div n="lb"/>Śrī Kṛṣṇa. (Sabhā Parva, Chapter 37, Verse 14).
<div n="NI"/>(8) He presented a good sword and a golden jar to
<div n="lb"/>Yudhiṣṭhira on the occasion of his coronation. (Sabhā
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 53, Verse 9).
<div n="NI"/>(9) When Dharmaputra went to Hastināpura to play
<div n="lb"/>the game of dice, Śalya was found to have arrived there
<div n="lb"/>earlier. (Sabhā Parva, Chapter 58, Verse 24).
<div n="NI"/>(10) Before the great war began he visited the Pāṇḍavas
<div n="lb"/>and blessed them. Moreover he agreed to act as Karṇa's
<div n="lb"/>charioteer and render him impotent so that the Pāṇḍavas
<div n="lb"/>could become victorious. (Udyoga Parva, Chapter 8).
<div n="NI"/>(11) Śalya joined the Kauravas in the great war with
<div n="lb"/>an akṣauhiṇī (a particular division of soldiers). (Udyoga
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 19, Verse 16).
<div n="NI"/>(12) Śalya wished that Yudhiṣṭhira won the war.
<div n="lb"/>(Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 43, Verse 79).
<div n="NI"/>(13) On the first day of the war he fought a duel with
<div n="lb"/>Yudhiṣṭhira. (Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 45, Verse 28).
<div n="NI"/>(14) Śalya killed Uttara, the Virāṭa prince. (Bhīṣma
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 47, Verse 35).
<div n="NI"/>(15) He defeated Śaṅkha, another Virāṭa prince. (Bhīṣma
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 49, Verse 35).
<div n="NI"/>(16) He fought with Dhṛṣṭadyumna. (Bhīṣma Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 62).
<div n="NI"/>(17) Bhīmasena defeated Śalya. (Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>64, Verse 27).
<div n="NI"/>(18) He fought with Yudhiṣṭhira again. (Bhīṣma Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 71, Verse 20).
<div n="NI"/>(19) Nakula and Sahadeva attacked Śalya. (Bhīṣma
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 81, Verse 26).
<div n="NI"/>(20) Sahadeva defeated Śalya. (Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>88, Verse 51).
<div n="NI"/>(21) Śalya attacked Śikhaṇḍī. (Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>85, Verse 27).
<div n="NI"/>(22) In the battle that followed he attacked the whole
<div n="lb"/>lot of Pāṇḍavas and wounded Yudhiṣṭhira. (Bhīṣma
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 105, Verse 30).
<div n="NI"/>(23) Bhīma and Arjuna fought with Śalya. (Bhīṣma
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapters 113 and 114).
<div n="NI"/>(24) Śalya fought a duel with Yudhiṣṭhira for the third
<div n="lb"/>time. (Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 116, Verse 40).
<div n="NI"/>(25) He fought with Nakula. (Droṇa Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>14, Verse 31).
<div n="NI"/>(26) Abhimanyu and Śalya fought with each other.
<div n="lb"/>(Droṇa Parva, Chapter 14, Verse 78).
<div n="NI"/>(27) Afterwards in a club-fight with Bhīma, Śalya got
<div n="lb"/>defeated. (Droṇa Parva, Chapter 15).
<div n="NI"/>(28) He fought again with Yudhiṣṭhira. (Droṇa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 25, Verse 15).
<div n="NI"/>(29) In the fight with Abhimanyu he fell down uncons-
<div n="lb"/>cious. (Droṇa Parva, Chapter 37, Verse 38).
<div n="NI"/>(30) Abhimanyu defeated him again. (Droṇa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 48, Verse 14).
<div n="NI"/>(31) He fought for the fifth time with Yudhiṣṭhira.
<div n="lb"/>(Droṇa Parva, Chapter 26, Verse 99).
<div n="NI"/>(32) Afterwards he clashed with Arjuna as the protector
<div n="lb"/>of Jayadratha. (Droṇa Parva, Chapter 145, Verse 9).
<div n="NI"/>(33) He killed Śatānīka, brother of Virāṭa. (Droṇa
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 137, Verse 30).

<div n="NI"/>(34) When Droṇa was killed Śalya ran away from
<div n="lb"/>the battlefield. (Droṇa Parva, Chapter 193, Verse 11).
<div n="NI"/>(35) Śrutakīrti attacked Śalya. (Karṇa Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>13, Verse 10).
<div n="NI"/>(36) Duryodhana insisted upon Śalya acting as
<div n="lb"/>charioteer of Karṇa. At first Śalya got angry about
<div n="lb"/>the suggestion, but finally he took up the charioteer-
<div n="lb"/>ship. (Karṇa Parva, Chapter 32).
<div n="NI"/>(37) As the charioteer of Karṇa Śalya cried down the
<div n="lb"/>former and praised Arjuna. (Karṇa Parva, Chapter 37,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 33).
<div n="NI"/>(38) Karṇa threatened to kill Śalya. (Karṇa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 40).
<div n="NI"/>(39) Śalya cried down Karṇa by relating to him the
<div n="lb"/>story of the swan and the crow. (Karṇa Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>41).
<div n="NI"/>(40) When Bhīma wanted to cut off Karṇa's tongue
<div n="lb"/>Śalya reminded him of Arjuna's vow and thus made
<div n="lb"/>Bhīma retract from his plan. (Karṇa Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>50; Mahābhārata, Dākṣiṇātyapāṭha).
<div n="NI"/>(41) When Karṇa attempted to kill Nakula, Sahadeva
<div n="lb"/>and Yudhiṣṭhira, Śalya prevented him from doing so.
<div n="lb"/>(Karṇa Parva, Chapter 63, Verse 21).
<div n="NI"/>(42) He consoled Karṇa, who got alarmed by the
<div n="lb"/>prowess of Bhīma. (Karṇa Parva, Chapter 84, Verse
<div n="lb"/>8).
<div n="NI"/>(43) Śalya comforted Duryodhana, who grieved over
<div n="lb"/>the death of Karṇa. (Karṇa Parva, Chapter 92, Verse
<div n="lb"/>10).
<div n="NI"/>(44) As desired by Duryodhana, he assumed charge
<div n="lb"/>over the army as its commander-in-chief. (Śalya
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 6, Verse 28).
<div n="NI"/>(45) He fought a fierce battle with Bhīma and Dharma-
<div n="lb"/>putra. (Śalya Parva, Chapters 11 and 12).
<div n="NI"/>(46) Dharmaputra defeated Śalya. (Śalya Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 16, Verse 63).
<div n="NI"/>(47) In the battle that followed, Śalya was killed by
<div n="lb"/>Yudhiṣṭhira. (Śalya Parva, Chapter 17, Verse 52).
<div n="NI"/>(48) Among the souls of those killed in battle beckon-
<div n="lb"/>ed to the surface of the Gaṅgā, Śalya's soul also was
<div n="lb"/>present. (Āśramavāsika Parva, Chapter 32, Verse 10).
<div n="NI"/>(49) Saṃhlāda, brother of Prahlāda and son of
<div n="lb"/>Hiraṇyakaśipu was another birth of Śalya. (Ādi
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 67, Verse 6).
<div n="NI"/>(50) Synonyms used in the Mahābhārata for Śalya:
<div n="lb"/>Ārtāyani, Bālhīkapuṅgava, Madrādhipa, Madraja,
<div n="lb"/>Madrajanādhipa, Mādraka, Madrarāṭ, Sauvīra etc.
