SĀTYAKI¦ (YUYUDHĀNA). A Yādava, who was a
<div n="lb"/>warrior of the Vṛṣṇi dynasty and a friend of Śrī Kṛṣṇa.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Genealogy.%} Descended from Viṣṇu thus: Brahmā-
<div n="lb"/>Atri-Candra-Budha-Purūravas-Āyus-Nahuṣa-
<div n="lb"/>Yayāti-Yadu-Sahasrajit-Śatajit-Hehaya-Dharma-
<div n="lb"/>Kuni-Bhadrasena-Dhanaka-Kṛtavīrya-Kārttavīr-
<div n="lb"/>yārjuna-Madhu-Vṛṣṇi-Yudhājit-Śini-Satyaka-
<div n="lb"/>Sātyaki.
<div n="NI"/>Śini was the younger son of Yudhājit. Sātyaki alias Yuyu-
<div n="lb"/>dhāna, a companion of Śrī Kṛṣṇa and a great friend of
<div n="lb"/>the Pāṇḍavas, was Śini's grandson.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%His relation to other prominent Yādavas.%} The dynasty of
<div n="lb"/>Anamitra, first son of Yudhājit, became famous through
<div n="lb"/>Prasena, Satrājit, Śvaphalka and Akrūra. Of the four
<div n="lb"/>sons of Andhaka, predecessor of Kṛṣṇa, Kukura and
<div n="lb"/>Bhajamāna deserve special mention. Āhuka, in the
<div n="lb"/>dynasty of Kukura had two sons named Devaka and
<div n="lb"/>Ugrasena. Devakī, daughter of Devaka, became wife
<div n="lb"/>of Vasudeva and mother of Kṛṣṇa. Kaṃsa was the son
<div n="lb"/>of Ugrasena. Śūra, seventh in the line of Bhajamāna,
<div n="lb"/>son of Andhaka had twelve children. His first son
[Page706-b+ 64]
<div n="lb"/>Vasudeva was Kṛṣṇa's father. Śrutakīrti, Śrutaśravas,
<div n="lb"/>Śrutadevī and Rājādhidevī the four daughters of
<div n="lb"/>Śūra were married respectively by Dhṛṣṭaketu,
<div n="lb"/>king of Kekaya, Damaghoṣa, king of Cedi, Vṛddha-
<div n="lb"/>śarman, king of Karūṣa and Jayasena, king of
<div n="lb"/>Avantī. Dhṛṣṭaketu was a great ally of the Pāṇḍavas.
<div n="lb"/>Śiśupāla was the son of Damaghoṣa, and Dantavaktra
<div n="lb"/>the son of Vṛddhaśarman. Śiśupāla and Dantavaktra,
<div n="lb"/>the third human incarnation of Jaya and Vijaya were
<div n="lb"/>war-crazy and haughty persons. Vinda and Anuvinda,
<div n="lb"/>sons of Jayasena, were great friends of the Pāṇḍavas. As
<div n="lb"/>Kuntī, daughter of Śūra became the adopted daughter
<div n="lb"/>of Ugrasena, she became also the niece of Vasudeva and
<div n="lb"/>Kṛṣṇa's father's sister. Kuntī married Pāṇḍu and she
<div n="lb"/>delivered four sons, Karṇa, Yudhiṣṭhira, Bhīmasena and
<div n="lb"/>Arjuna.
