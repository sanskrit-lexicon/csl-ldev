MĀHIṢMATĪ¦. An ancient city on the banks of river
Narmadā. There is reference to this place in many
places in the Purāṇas and a few of them are given
below:
<div n="NI"/>(i) King Nīla was reigning in a country with Māhiṣ-
matī as its capital. Sahadeva, one of the Pāṇḍavas
during his victorious march to the south conquered
this country. (Chapter 31, Sabhā Parva).
[Page468-a+ 64]
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Daśāśva, tenth son of Ikṣvāku, had made this
city his capital. (Śloka 6, Chapter 2, Anuśāsana
Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Kārtavīryārjuna who had made Māhiṣmatī the
capital of his kingdom had subdued many kings.
(Śloka 3, Chapter 152, Anuśāsana Parva). (See under
Kārtavīryārjuna.)
