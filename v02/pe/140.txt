AKAMPANA¦. (A mighty warrior among the demons).
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Genealogy.%} Descended from Viṣṇu in the following
[Page021-b+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>order: Viṣṇu-Brahmā-Heti-Vidyukeśa-Sukeśa
<div n="lb"/>Sumāli-Akampana.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%} Sumāli married Ketumatī and got
<div n="lb"/>fourteen children. They were 1) Prahasta 2) Akampana
<div n="lb"/>3) Vikaṭa 4) Kālakāmukha 5) Dhūmrāksa 6) Daṇḍa
<div n="lb"/>7) Supārśvā 8) Sāṃhrāda 9) Prākvāta 10) Bhāsakarṇa
<div n="lb"/>11) Vekā 12) Puṣpotkaṭā 13) Kaikasī and 14) Kumbhī-
<div n="lb"/>nadī. Of these the last four are daughters. Prahasta was
<div n="lb"/>one of the ministers of Rāvaṇa. The thirteenth child
<div n="lb"/>Kaikasī was married to Viśravas, son of Pulastya.
<div n="lb"/>Viśravas got three sons and a daughter. They were
<div n="lb"/>Rāvaṇa, Kumbhakarṇa and Vibhīṣaṇa and Śūrpaṇakhā.
<div n="lb"/>It was Akampana who informed Rāvaṇa that Rāma and
<div n="lb"/>Lakṣmaṇa had killed his three allies, Khara, Dūṣaṇa
<div n="lb"/>and Triśiras (Araṇya Kāṇḍa, Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa).
<div n="NI"/>3) {%Death.%} Akampana fought a fierce battle with
<div n="lb"/>Hanūmān and in the end Hanūmān plucked a big tree
<div n="lb"/>and hit Akampana on the head with it and killed him.
<div n="lb"/>(Sarga 56, Yuddha Kāṇḍa of Rāmāyaṇa)
