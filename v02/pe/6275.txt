SARAMĀ I¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} Bitch of the Devas. Śyāma and Śabala,
<div n="lb"/>sons of Saramā, were two prominent messengers of
<div n="lb"/>Yama and they possessed four eyes each. The off-
<div n="lb"/>springs of these dogs are called Sārameyas. The Ṛgveda
<div n="lb"/>and Mahābhārata contain a story about Saramā cursing
<div n="lb"/>Janamejaya. (For details see under Śrutaśravas III).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other information.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Saramā worships Brahmā in his court. (Sabhā
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 11, Verse 40).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Saramā is a graha (Evil spirit) of Subrahmaṇya
<div n="lb"/>which enters the womb of pregnant women and steals
<div n="lb"/>the babies. (Vana Parva, Chapter 230, Verse 34).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Saramā after having once drunk milk from
<div n="lb"/>dasyus lied about it to Indra, and he punished her.
<div n="lb"/>(Varāha Purāṇa).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Indra once deputed Saramā to find out the place
<div n="lb"/>where the Paṇis had hidden the cows on condition that
<div n="lb"/>he would feed her children. Saramā found out the
<div n="lb"/>place and informed Indra about it thus earning for her
<div n="lb"/>children their livelihood. (Ṛgveda, Maṇḍala 1, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>5).
