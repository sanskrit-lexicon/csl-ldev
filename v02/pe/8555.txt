VIŚVĀVASU II¦. A Gandharva King. The following
information about this King is taken from the Purāṇas.
<div n="NI"/>(i) The father of this Devagandharva was Prajāpati
Kaśyapa and his mother was Pradhā. (M.B. Ādi Parva,
Chapter 25, Stanza 47).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Pramadvarā was born to Viśvāvasu by the celestial
maid Menakā. (See under Pramadvarā).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Viśvāvasu was present at the Birth-festival of
Arjuna. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 122, Stanza 22).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) This Viśvāvasu learned from Soma, Cākṣuṣīvidyā
(the art of seeing all) and taught Citraratha, a
Gandharva the same art. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 169,
Stanza 43).
<div n="NI"/>(v) He was present at the Svayaṃvara (marriage) of
Draupadī. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 186, Stanza 7).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) Viśvāvasu stays in the palace of Indra glorifying
him. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 7, Stanza 22).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) It is mentioned in Mahābhārata, Sabhā Parva,
Chapter 10, Stanza 25, that he stays in the palace of
Kubera praising him.
<div n="NI"/>(viii) This Gandharva recited a poem at the sacrifice
performed by Jamadagni. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter
90, Stanza 18).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) Kabandha, the asura, who stopped Rāma and
[Page877-b+ 65]
Lakṣmaṇa, was the changed form of Viśvāvasu by a
curse (See under Kabandha).
<div n="NI"/>(x) It was this Viśvāvasu who played the lute in the
sacrifice performed by emperor Dilīpa. (M.B. Droṇa
Parva, Chapter 61, Stanza 7).
<div n="NI"/>(xi) Once Viśvāvasu asked hermit Yājñavalkya twenty-
four questions. When he got satisfactory answers, the
Gandharva returned to heaven. (M.B. Śānti Parva,
Chapter 318, Stanza 26).
<div n="NI"/>(xii) It was Viśvāvasu and some other Gandharvas who
took away Urvaśī from Purūravas. (See under Purūra-
vas).
<div n="NI"/>(xiii) At the time of emperor Pṛthu, when the earth was
milked, the thing the Gandharvas got were those befitt-
ing them, In the course of the milking Viśvāvasu stood
as the calf. (Bhāgavata, Skandha 4).
