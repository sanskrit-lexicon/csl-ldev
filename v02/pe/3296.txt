KĀPOTA¦. A sage. Citrāṅgadā, the daughter of
Kakutstha and Urvaśī, was his wife. She had two sons
[Page389-b+ 65]
named Tumburu and Suvarcas. Kāpota received much
wealth from Kubera and gave it to his sons. Once
Kāpota cursed Tārāvatī, the queen of Candraśekhara,
that she would bring forth two sons with monkey-faces.
(Kālikā Purāṇa, Chapter 56).
