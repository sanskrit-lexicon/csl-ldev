DAṆḌAKĀRAṆYA¦. A forest in the Indo-Gangetic
<div n="lb"/>Valley. For the story of how this place became a great
<div n="lb"/>forest see under Arā.
<div n="NI"/>{%Other information.%} (1) This is a holy place. He who
<div n="lb"/>takes bath in this place would get the fruits of giving a
<div n="lb"/>thousand cows as gift. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter 85,
<div n="lb"/>Stanza 41).
<div n="NI"/>(2) During the time of the forest life of Śrī Rāma he made
<div n="lb"/>his cottage in this forest and stayed there for a while.
<div n="lb"/>It was in this forest that a part of the body of Śūrpaṇakhā
<div n="lb"/>was cut off and the infantry of fourteen thousand giants
<div n="lb"/>who came under the leadership of Khara, Dūṣaṇa and
<div n="lb"/>Triśira was completely destroyed. Sītā was stolen,
<div n="lb"/>Mārīca was killed by Śrī Rāma, and Jaṭāyu died by
<div n="lb"/>the sword of Rāvaṇa in this forest of Daṇḍakāraṇya.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Vana Parva, Chapters 277 to 279.)
