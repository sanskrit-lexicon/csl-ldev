BHAGADATTA¦. Bhagadatta was King of Prāgjyotiṣa-
pura. Mahābhārata furnishes the following information
about him.
<div n="NI"/>1) Bhagadatta was born from a limb of the asura
called Bāṣkala. (M.B., Ādi Parva, Chapter 67, Verse
9).
<div n="NI"/>2) He was present at the wedding of Pāñcālī. (Ādi
Parva, Chapter 185, Verse 12).
<div n="NI"/>3) Known also as lord of the Yavanas (Yavanādhipa).
Bhagadatta was a friend of Pāṇḍu, and that friendship
was transferred to Yudhiṣṭhira too. (Sabhā Parva,
Chapter 14, Verse 14).
<div n="NI"/>4) The triumphal tour of Arjuna in connection with
the Rājasūya yajña performed by Yudhiṣṭhira evoked
feelings of jealousy in the heart of Bhagadatta, and he
fought against Arjuna. The courage and prowess Arjuna
exhibited in the fight astonished Bhagadatta, who
congratulated Arjuna on his supreme courage and
admitting defeat yielded to Arjuna. (Sabhā Parva,
Chapter 26, Verse 7).
<div n="NI"/>5) Bhagadatta, along with the Yavanas was present at
the Rājasūya of Yudhiṣṭhira. He also made presents of
horses of very high pedigree and very costly gems etc.
(Sabhā Parva, Chapter 51, Verse 14).
<div n="NI"/>6) Karṇa once defeated Bhagadatta in fight. (Vana
Parva, Chapter 254, Verse 5).
<div n="NI"/>7) In the great war he, with his army, fought on the
side of Duryodhana. (Udyoga Parva, Chapter 19,
Verse 15).
<div n="NI"/>8) During the first day of the war Bhagadatta fought
a duel with the King of Virāṭa. (Bhīṣma Parva, Chap-
ter 45, Verse 49).
<div n="NI"/>9) He was defeated in his fight with Ghaṭotkaca.
(Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 64, Verse 59).
<div n="NI"/>10) In the fight which took place after the above de-
feat Bhagadatta caused Bhīmasena to faint in the field.
(Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 64, Verse 13).
<div n="NI"/>11) And after that he defeated Ghaṭotkaca in fight.
(Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 83, Verse 40).
<div n="NI"/>12) Next ensued the combat with King Daśārṇa in
which the King was defeated. (Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter
95, Verse 48).
<div n="NI"/>13) It was not long after this that Bhagadatta cut off
the hands of the Kṣatradeva. (Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter
95, Verse 73).
<div n="NI"/>14) Viśoka, the charioteer of Bhīma-sena, hit by the
arrows of Bhagadatta fell down and fainted. (Bhīṣma
Parva, Chapter 95, Verse 76).
<div n="NI"/>15) Bhagadatta and Sātyaki fought against each other.
(Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 111, Verse 7).
<div n="NI"/>16) Chapters 113 and 114 of the Bhīṣma Parva des-
cribe the combats fought by Bhagadatta with Bhīma-
sena and Arjuna.
<div n="NI"/>17) Bhagadatta and Drupada fought with each other.
(Droṇa Parva, Chapter 14, Verse 40).
<div n="NI"/>18) Bhagadatta killed king Daśārṇa. (Droṇa Parva,
Chapter 26, Verse 38).
[Page112-b+ 64]
<div n="NI"/>19) Bhagadatta killed Ruciparvan. (Droṇa Parva,
Chapter 26, Verse 52).
<div n="NI"/>20) He shot the Vaiṣṇavāstra (arrow given by Viṣṇu)
on Arjuna. (Droṇa Parva, Chapter 29, Verse 17).
<div n="NI"/>21) Arjuna killed Bhagadatta. (Droṇa Parva, Chapter
29, Verse 48).
<div n="NI"/>22) After his death, his son, Vajradatta became King
of Prāgjyotiṣa. Arjuna killed Vajradatta also. (Aśva-
medha Parva, Chapter 76).
<div n="NI"/>23) King Śailālaya, who was Bhagadatta's grandfather,
attained Indraloka on account of the greatness of his
tapas. (Āśramavāsika Parva, Chapter 20, Verse 10).
