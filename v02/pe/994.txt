<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} One of the twelve Ādityas born as sons of
<div n="lb"/>Kaśyapa prajāpati by his wife, Aditi. Viṣṇu, Śakra,
<div n="lb"/>Aryaman, Dhātā, Tvaṣṭā, Pūṣā, Vivasvān, Savitā,
<div n="lb"/>Mitra, Varuṇa, Aṃśu and Bhaga—these are the Dvā-
<div n="lb"/>daśādityas, and they were Devas famous as Tuṣitas in
<div n="lb"/>the last Cākṣuṣamanvantara.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other information.%} (1) Bhaga married Siddhi, and the
<div n="lb"/>couple begot three sons called Mahiman, Vibhu and
<div n="lb"/>Prabhu and three daughters called Suvratā, Varārohā,
<div n="lb"/>and Āśīs.
<div n="NI"/>(2) Bhaga participated in the birthday celebrations of
<div n="lb"/>Arjuna. (M.B., Ādi Parva, Chapter 122, Verse 66).
<div n="NI"/>(3) At the time of Khāṇḍavadāha (burning of the
<div n="lb"/>Khāṇḍava forest) Bhaga, as a supporter of Indra, who
<div n="lb"/>was fighting Arjuna and Śrī Kṛṣṇa, sprang upon the
<div n="lb"/>enemies with sword in hand. (M.B., Ādi Parva, Chap-
<div n="lb"/>ter 236, Verse 36).
<div n="NI"/>(4) Bhaga shines forth in Indra's assembly. (M.B.,
<div n="lb"/>Sabhā Parva, Chapter 7, Verse 22).
<div n="NI"/>(5) Bhaga was also present at the installation of Subrah-
<div n="lb"/>maṇya as commander of the fighting forces. (M.B.,
<div n="lb"/>Śalya Parva, Chapter 45).
<div n="NI"/>(6) After Devayuga (Deva age), the Devas assembled
<div n="lb"/>together and decided upon the share of yajñas due to
<div n="lb"/>each of them, and in thus fixing shares they left out
<div n="lb"/>Rudra. Enraged at this neglect Rudra made a bow
<div n="lb"/>and fought against the Devas. During the fight Rudra,
<div n="lb"/>with the point of his bow, extracted the hands of
<div n="lb"/>Savitā, the eyes of Bhaga and the teeth of Pūṣā. Ulti-
<div n="lb"/>mately the Devas satisfied and pleased. Rudra, who
<div n="lb"/>returned to Bhaga and others the eyes etc. which had
<div n="lb"/>been extracted. (M.B., Sauptika Parva).
