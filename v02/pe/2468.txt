GOMATĪ¦. (KAUŚIKĪ). A celebrated river of Purāṇic
<div n="lb"/>fame. This is worshipped as a goddess.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%The curse.%} Kaṃpa Rāmāyaṇa states that this river
<div n="lb"/>was Kauśikī, sister of Viśvāmitra. She became a river
<div n="lb"/>by a curse.
<div n="NI"/>Kauśikī was married to a sage called Ṛcīka. Once
<div n="lb"/>Ṛcīka went to devaloka to see Brahmā. Kauśikī unable
<div n="lb"/>to bear the separation followed her husband by her
<div n="lb"/>power of chastity. On the way Ṛcīka saw her and
<div n="lb"/>cursed her and made her into a river. From that day
<div n="lb"/>onwards she started running as a river named Kauśikī.
<div n="lb"/>See under Kauśikī. (Bāla Kāṇḍa, Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details from the Purāṇas regarding Gomatī.%}
<div n="NI"/>i) Śrī Rāma conducted the Aśvamedha yāga at Naimi-
<div n="lb"/>śāraṇya on the banks of this river. (Uttara Rāmāyaṇa).
<div n="NI"/>ii) The sins of those who drink the water of this river
<div n="lb"/>are washed away. (Śloka 20, Chapter 169, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>iii) The devī of Gomatī river lives in the court of
<div n="lb"/>Varuṇa worshipping him. (Śloka 23, Chapter 9, Sabhā
<div n="lb"/>Parva).
<div n="NI"/>iv) Dharmaputra came to this river during his pilgri-
<div n="lb"/>mage. (Śloka 2, Chapter 95, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>v) Gomatī is the wife of Agnideva called Viśvabhuk.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 19, Chapter 219, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>vi) This is one of the most important rivers of Bhārata-
<div n="lb"/>varṣa. (Śloka 18, Chapter 9, Bhīṣma Parva).
<div n="NI"/>vii) The land of Divodāsa, King of Ayodhyā, extended
<div n="lb"/>from the shores of Gaṅgā to the base of Gomatī. (Brah-
<div n="lb"/>māṇḍa Purāṇa, Chapter 2).
<div n="NI"/>GOMATĪMANTRA. A mantra for obtaining blessings
<div n="lb"/>from cows. If one recites this mantra standing in the
<div n="lb"/>midst of cows one would get many children and great
<div n="lb"/>wealth and if it is recited by a woman she would get
<div n="lb"/>the affection and love of her husband. (Śloka 42, Chap-
