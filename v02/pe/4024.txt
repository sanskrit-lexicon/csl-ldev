LOMAŚA¦ I (ROMAŚA) I. A sage, who was a great
story-teller. Many of the stories found as episodes in the
Purāṇas were told by this sage. Mahābhārata gives the
following details about him.
<div n="NI"/>(1) Lomaśa was very virtuous and longlived. (Śloka
18, Chapter 31, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(2) Once Lomaśa entered the court of Indra and spoke
to Indra about Arjuna sitting sharing a half of his seat
before him. (Śloka 1, Chapter 47, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(3) Lomaśa returned to Kāmyakavana from the court
of Indra and gave Yudhiṣṭhira the messages from Indra
and Arjuna. (Śloka 33, Chapter 47, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(4) He prophesied to Arjuna that he would get divine
weapons from Śiva. (Śloka 10, Chapter 91, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(5) Lomaśa consoled Yudhiṣṭhira when he came to the
[Page458-a+ 65]
forests in exile leaving all his happy surroundings in the
country. (Śloka 17, Chapter 94, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(6) He told Dharmaputra the story of Agastya.
(Chapter 96, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(7) Lomaśa told Dharmaputra the stories of Śrī
Rāma and Paraśurāma. (Śloka 40, Chapter 99, Vana
Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(8) Asuras defeated all the Devas and the latter were
perplexed. It was Lomaśa who then advised them to
make weapons out of the bones of the sage Dadhīca.
(Chapter 100, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(9) Lomaśa mentioned to the Devas a trick to kill
Vṛtrāsura. (Chapter 101, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(10) The story of Bhagīratha, the episode of Ṛṣyasṛṅga
etc. were all told by Lomaśa. (Chapters 103 to 113,
Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(11) Lomaśa told Yudhiṣṭhira the story of the Yāga of
King Gaya, the story of the river Payoṣṇī, the glory of
the mountain of Vaiḍūrya and the river Narmadā, and
the story of Cyavana the great sage. (Chapter 121,
Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(12) He told the Pāṇḍavas the story of Māndhātā.
(Chapter 126, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(13) Lomaśa told Yudhiṣṭhira the story of Somaka and
Jantu. (Chapter 127, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(14) He praised the glory of the holy place Yamunā-
tīrtha, known as Plakṣaprasravaṇa also, near Kuru-
kṣetra. Chapter 129, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(15) It was Lomaśa who told the story of the emperor
Śibi who gave his own flesh to protect a dove which
went to him for refuge. (Chapter 130, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(16) Lomaśa told the story of the sage Aṣṭāvakra to the
Pāṇḍavas. (Chapter 133, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(17) He described the story of Yavakrīta and Medhāvī
to the Pāṇḍavas. (Chapter 135, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(18) Dharmaputra heard the stories of Bharadvāja,
Raibhya, Arvāvasu and Parāvasu from Lomaśa.
(Chapter 137, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(19) Lomaśa told Dharmaputra the story of Narakā-
sura and the incarnation of Viṣṇu as Varāha. (Chapter
142, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(20) Lomaśa visited Bhīṣma lying on his bed of arrows.
(Śānti Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(21) Lomaśa praised the glory of giving rice as charity,
(Śloka 10, Chapter 67, Anuśāsana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(22) Lomaśa was a sage from the north. (Śloka 46,
Chapter 165, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(23) Lomaśa relieved many nymphs like Pramohinī
from their curses. (See under Pramohinī).
