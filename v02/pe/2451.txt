GODĀVARĪ¦. A river of South India. This river has
<div n="lb"/>been glorified much in the Purāṇas.
<div n="NI"/>(1) Godāvarī is a member of the court of Varuṇa.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 20, Chapter 9, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(2) This river originates from Brahmagiri, situated
<div n="lb"/>near Tryambakajyotirliṅga of Nasik district in South
<div n="lb"/>India. The river is very deep and is a giver of pros-
<div n="lb"/>perity to those who worship her. Many sages worshipped
<div n="lb"/>this river. (Śloka 2, Chapter 88, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(3) If one bathes in this river one will get the benefit
<div n="lb"/>of conducting a Gomedha yajña. Not only that, after
<div n="lb"/>his death he will go to the land of Vāsuki. (Śloka 33,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 85, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(4) The origin of Agni is from Godāvarī. (Śloka, 24,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 222, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(5) Śrī Rāma, Lakṣmaṇa and Sītā stayed for a long
<div n="lb"/>time during their exile in Pañcavaṭī on the shores of
<div n="lb"/>this river Godāvarī. (Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa, Araṇya
<div n="lb"/>Kāṇḍa, Sarga 16).
<div n="NI"/>(6) Godāvarī is one of the most important rivers of
<div n="lb"/>India. (Śloka 14, Chapter 9, Bhīṣma Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(7) He who bathes in Godāvarī will be prosperous.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 29, Chapter 25, Anuśāsana Parva).
