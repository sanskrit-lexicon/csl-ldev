KUÑJALA II¦. A wise parrot which lived on the banks
of the river Narmadā. The Padma Purāṇa (Chapter
85, Bhūmikhaṇḍa) has the following to say about the
parrot.
<div n="NI"/>Kuñjala, the wise parrot, had a wife and four sons
called Ujjvala, Samujjvala, Vijvala and Kapiñjala.
These brothers used to feed themselves on sweet fruits
etc. on the planes, mountains and other places. They
also used to carry tasty fruits to their parents. The four
brothers thus led a happy life in every way. (See under
Subāhu).
