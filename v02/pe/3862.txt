KUÑJALA II¦. A wise parrot which lived on the banks
<div n="lb"/>of the river Narmadā. The Padma Purāṇa (Chapter
<div n="lb"/>85, Bhūmikhaṇḍa) has the following to say about the
<div n="lb"/>parrot.
<div n="NI"/>Kuñjala, the wise parrot, had a wife and four sons
<div n="lb"/>called Ujjvala, Samujjvala, Vijvala and Kapiñjala.
<div n="lb"/>These brothers used to feed themselves on sweet fruits
<div n="lb"/>etc. on the planes, mountains and other places. They
<div n="lb"/>also used to carry tasty fruits to their parents. The four
<div n="lb"/>brothers thus led a happy life in every way. (See under
<div n="lb"/>Subāhu).
