KETU I¦. (KETUMĀN). A Dānava. He was the son
<div n="lb"/>of Kaśyapa (grandson of Brahmā and son of Marīci)
<div n="lb"/>by his wife Danu. This Asura, who exists in the shape
<div n="lb"/>of a planet had thirtythree brothers, i.e. Vipracitti,
<div n="lb"/>Śambara, Namuci, Pulomā, Asilomā, Keśī, Durjaya,
<div n="lb"/>Ayaśśiras, Aśvaśiras, Aśva, Śaṅku, Mahābala, Garga,
<div n="lb"/>Amūrdhan, Vegavān, Mānavān, Svarbhānu, Aśvapati,
<div n="lb"/>Vṛṣaparvan, Ajaka, Aśvagrīva, Sūkṣma, Tuhuṇḍa,
<div n="lb"/>Ekapāt, Ekacakra, Virūpākṣa, Harāhara, Nikumbha,
<div n="lb"/>Kapaṭa, Śarabha, Śalabha, Sūrya and Candramas.
<div n="lb"/>(Ādi Parva, Chapter 65).
<div n="NI"/>But Ketu maintained closer relationship with Rāhu, a
<div n="lb"/>step-brother of his, being the son of Kaśyapa by ano-
<div n="lb"/>ther wife called Siṃhikā. Rāhu and Ketu are even
<div n="lb"/>today considered as inauspicious planets. Rāhu wears
<div n="lb"/>a half-moon and Ketu holds in his hands a sword and
<div n="lb"/>lamp. Amitaujas was Ketu reborn. (Ādi Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>67, Verse 11).
