COLA¦(Ṃ). The three celebrated kingdoms of South-
<div n="lb"/>India of old were Cola, Pāṇḍya and Cera. References
<div n="lb"/>to these will be found in many places in the Purāṇas
<div n="lb"/>and a few are given below:—
<div n="NI"/>1) Among those present for the svayaṃvara of Rukmiṇī
<div n="lb"/>were the Kings of Cola, Pāṇḍya and Kerala.
<div n="NI"/>2) Descending in order from Turvasu were Varga—
<div n="lb"/>Gobhānu—Traiśāni—Karandhama—Marutta—
<div n="lb"/>Duṣyanta—Varūtha—Gāṇḍīra. From Gāṇḍīra were
<div n="lb"/>born the people of Kerala, Pāṇḍya and Cola.
<div n="lb"/>(Chapter 277, Agni Purāṇa).
<div n="NI"/>3) Arjuna conquered the army of the King of Cola.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 21, Chapter 27, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>4) The King of Cola gave gifts to Dharmaputra.
<div n="lb"/>(Sabhā Parva, Chapter 52, Śloka 35).
<div n="NI"/>5) When during the great battle Dhṛṣṭadyumna formed
<div n="lb"/>the Krauñcavyūha the soldiers from Cola guarded the
<div n="lb"/>southern wing. (Śloka 60, Chapter 9, Bhīṣma Parva).
<div n="NI"/>6) Śrī Kṛṣṇa once conquered the land of Cola. (Śloka
<div n="lb"/>17, Chapter 11, Droṇa Parva).
