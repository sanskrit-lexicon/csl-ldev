VAKA¦ DĀLBHYA (BAKA DĀLBHYA). A hermit of
Ancient India. The information obtained from Mahā-
bhārata about this hermit is given below.
<div n="NI"/>(He was a member of the council of Yudhiṣṭhira. (M.B.
Sabhā Parva, Chapter 4, Stanza 11).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Once Vaka Dālbhya lectured to Yudhiṣṭhira about
the greatness of Brāhmaṇas. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter
26, Stanza 6).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) On another occasion he described the welfare of
eternal beings to Indra. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter
193).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Once he stopped Śrī Kṛṣṇa, who was going to
Hastināpura, and conversed with him on the way. (M.B.
Udyoga Parva, Chapter 83, Stanza 65).
<div n="NI"/>(v) The hermit Baka Dālbhya once talked about how
the kingdom of Dhṛtarāṣṭra would be made burnt offering
to fire. (M.B. Śalya Parva, Chapter 41, Stanza 5).
