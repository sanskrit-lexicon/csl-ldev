ŚŪRA VI¦. A Yādava King. He was the father of Vasu-
<div n="lb"/>deva and grandfather of Śrī Kṛṣṇa. By his first wife,
<div n="lb"/>Māriṣā, he had ten sons and four daughters. The sons
<div n="lb"/>were, Vasudeva, Devabhāga, Devaśravas, Ānaka,
<div n="lb"/>Sṛñjaya, Śyāmaka, Kaṅka, Śamīka, Vatsaka and Vṛka.
<div n="lb"/>The names of the daughters were, Pṛthā, Śrutadevā
<div n="lb"/>(Śrutavedā), Śrutaśravā and Rājādhidevī. The eldest
<div n="lb"/>of these, Pṛthā was given as an adopted daughter to
<div n="lb"/>Kuntibhoja. (Harivaṃśa, 2-34; 17-28; M.B. Ādi Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 43; Verse 3. Chapter 104. Verse 1; Bhāgavata,
<div n="lb"/>9th Skandha). In Vāyu Purāṇa it is stated that he had

<div n="lb"/>and mortals were born. (See under Śūrasena I).
