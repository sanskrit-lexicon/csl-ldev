MUCUKUNDA¦. A celebrated King of the Solar
<div n="lb"/>dynasty.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Genealogy.%} Descending in order from Viṣṇu—
<div n="lb"/>Brahmā—Marīci—Kaśyapa—Vivasvān—Vaivasvata
<div n="lb"/>Manu—Ikṣvāku—Śaśāda—Purañjaya (Kakutstha)—
<div n="lb"/>Anenas—Pṛthulāśva—Prasenajit—Yuvanāśva—
<div n="lb"/>Māndhātā—Mucukunda.
<div n="NI"/>Māndhātā got three sons named Ambarīṣa, Mucu-
<div n="lb"/>kunda and Purukutsa and fifty daughters and all the
<div n="lb"/>fifty were married to a sage named Saubhari.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%How Mucukunda attained salvation.%} (For the story
<div n="lb"/>see para 72, under Indra).
<div n="NI"/>3) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Mucukunda had a daughter named Candramukhī.
<div n="lb"/>She was married to Śobhana, son of Candrasena.
<div n="lb"/>Once, when Śobhana came to his wife's house it was
<div n="lb"/>an Ekādaśī day and all were observing fasting and so
<div n="lb"/>Śobhana also did so. The next day Śobhana was seen
<div n="lb"/>lying dead. The soul of Śobhana went to the valley of
<div n="lb"/>the mountain of Mandara and started enjoying heaven-
<div n="lb"/>ly bliss. One day a pilgrim from the country of
<div n="lb"/>Mucukunda named Somaśarmā met the soul of
<div n="lb"/>Śobhana and coming to know that it was the son-in-
<div n="lb"/>law of their King he started making enquiries about
<div n="lb"/>him. Śobhana then said that all his happiness was
[Page507-a+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>due to his observing the Ekādaśī Vrata. On hearing the
<div n="lb"/>gladdening news about her husband Candramukhī
<div n="lb"/>became happy. (Chapter 61, Bhāga 1, Padma
<div n="lb"/>Purāṇa).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) This Rājarṣi shines in the court of Yama. (Śloka
<div n="lb"/>21, Chapter 8, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Once being greatly pleased with him. Kubera
<div n="lb"/>offered him all the worlds as gift. But Mucukunda
<div n="lb"/>refused to accept it saying that he would enjoy only
<div n="lb"/>what was earned by him by his own efforts. (Śloka
<div n="lb"/>97, Chapter 132, Udyoga Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Once Mucukunda just to try the might of
<div n="lb"/>Kubera attacked him. The demons sent by Kubera
<div n="lb"/>defeated him. He then meditated on Vasiṣṭha and he
<div n="lb"/>was able to defeat the army of asuras of Kubera.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 4, Chapter 74, Śānti Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Once Paraśurāma gave dharmopadeśa (Instruct-
<div n="lb"/>ion on morality, piety etc) to Mucukunda. (Śloka 7,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 143, Śānti Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) Once the King of Kāmboja gave him a sword
<div n="lb"/>and he gave that sword to Marutta. (Śloka 77,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 166, Śānti Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) He believed in making gifts of cows (Śloka 25,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 76, Anuśāsana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) Mucukunda hated eating flesh. (Śloka 61,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 115, Anuśāsana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) Mucukunda is listed among those kings who are
<div n="lb"/>worthy of being remembered morning and evening.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 54, Chapter 165, Anuśāsana Parva).
