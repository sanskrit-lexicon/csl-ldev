PUṢKALA II¦. The youngest son of Bharata, son of
Daśaratha. Māṇḍavī was the mother of Puṣkala.
(Chapter 88, Vāyu Purāṇa; Chapter 6, Brahmāṇḍa
Purāṇa; Chapter 4, Viṣṇu Purāṇa; Chapter 11, Agni
Purāṇa).
<div n="NI"/>Details available about Puṣkala from Padma Purāṇa
and Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa are the following:
<div n="NI"/>(1) Puṣkala was with Śatrughna when the latter served
as the guardian of the horse in all the three aśvamedha-
yāgas conducted by Śrī Rāma. (Padma Purāṇa, Pātāla
Khaṇḍa, Chapters 1 and 22).
<div n="NI"/>(2) He defeated Damana, son of Subāhu, while he was
following the sacrificial horse. (Padma Purāṇa, Pātāla
Khaṇḍa, Chapter 34).
<div n="NI"/>(3) He fought fiercely against the demons, Vidyun-
mālī and Ugradaṃṣṭra. (Padma Purāṇa. Pātāla
Khaṇḍa, Chapter 34).
<div n="NI"/>(4) He fought against Rukmāṅgada and Vīramaṇi.
(Padma Purāṇa, Pātāla Khaṇḍa, Chapter 41).
<div n="NI"/>(5) He was defeated by Lava who checked the progress
of the sacrificial horse. (Padma Purāṇa, Pātāla Khaṇḍa,
Chapter 61).
<div n="NI"/>(6) Puṣkala conquered the country of Gāndhāra and
built there a city called Puṣkalāvatī alias Puṣkalāvata
and made it his capital city. (Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa,
Uttara Kāṇḍa).
<div n="NI"/>(7) His wife's name was Kāntimatī. (Padma Purāṇa,
Pātāla Khaṇḍa, Chapter 67).
