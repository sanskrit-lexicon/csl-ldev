PUṢKALA II¦. The youngest son of Bharata, son of
<div n="lb"/>Daśaratha. Māṇḍavī was the mother of Puṣkala.
<div n="lb"/>(Chapter 88, Vāyu Purāṇa; Chapter 6, Brahmāṇḍa
<div n="lb"/>Purāṇa; Chapter 4, Viṣṇu Purāṇa; Chapter 11, Agni
<div n="lb"/>Purāṇa).
<div n="NI"/>Details available about Puṣkala from Padma Purāṇa
<div n="lb"/>and Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa are the following:
<div n="NI"/>(1) Puṣkala was with Śatrughna when the latter served
<div n="lb"/>as the guardian of the horse in all the three aśvamedha-
<div n="lb"/>yāgas conducted by Śrī Rāma. (Padma Purāṇa, Pātāla
<div n="lb"/>Khaṇḍa, Chapters 1 and 22).
<div n="NI"/>(2) He defeated Damana, son of Subāhu, while he was
<div n="lb"/>following the sacrificial horse. (Padma Purāṇa, Pātāla
<div n="lb"/>Khaṇḍa, Chapter 34).
<div n="NI"/>(3) He fought fiercely against the demons, Vidyun-
<div n="lb"/>mālī and Ugradaṃṣṭra. (Padma Purāṇa. Pātāla
<div n="lb"/>Khaṇḍa, Chapter 34).
<div n="NI"/>(4) He fought against Rukmāṅgada and Vīramaṇi.
<div n="lb"/>(Padma Purāṇa, Pātāla Khaṇḍa, Chapter 41).
<div n="NI"/>(5) He was defeated by Lava who checked the progress
<div n="lb"/>of the sacrificial horse. (Padma Purāṇa, Pātāla Khaṇḍa,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 61).
<div n="NI"/>(6) Puṣkala conquered the country of Gāndhāra and
<div n="lb"/>built there a city called Puṣkalāvatī alias Puṣkalāvata
<div n="lb"/>and made it his capital city. (Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa,
<div n="lb"/>Uttara Kāṇḍa).
<div n="NI"/>(7) His wife's name was Kāntimatī. (Padma Purāṇa,
<div n="lb"/>Pātāla Khaṇḍa, Chapter 67).
