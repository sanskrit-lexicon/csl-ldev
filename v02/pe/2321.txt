GANDHAMĀDANA¦(M). A mountain famed in the
<div n="lb"/>Purāṇas and lying to the east of the Himālayas. (Bhā-
<div n="lb"/>gavata, Pañcama Skandha). The purānic importance of
<div n="lb"/>the mountain may be summarised as follows:—
<div n="NI"/>(1) Kaśyapaprajāpati did tapas at this mountain. (Ādi
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 30, Verse 10).
<div n="NI"/>(2) Ananta (Ādiśeṣa) had once done tapas here. (Ādi
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 36, Verse 3).
<div n="NI"/>(3) Once Pāṇḍu went to the Śataśṛṅga mountain
<div n="lb"/>along with his wives Kuntī and Mādrī for doing tapas,
<div n="lb"/>and on that occasion they visited Gandhamādana also.
<div n="lb"/>(Ādi Parva, Chapter 118, Verse 48).
<div n="NI"/>(4) This mountain assumes the form of a divine per-
<div n="lb"/>son. attends Kubera's assembly and worships him.
<div n="lb"/>(Sabhā Parva, Chapter 10, Verse 32).
<div n="NI"/>(5) Śrī Kṛṣṇa in the company of Nārāyaṇarṣi spent
<div n="lb"/>10,000 years on this mountain as an anchorite. (Vana
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 12, Verse 11).
<div n="NI"/>(6) Arjuna on his way to Mount Kailāsa to do tapas
<div n="lb"/>crossed the Gandhamādana mountain. (Vana Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 37, Verse 41).
<div n="NI"/>(7) Only those who possess power got by tapas will
[Page274-b+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>be able to climb this mountain. (Vana Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>140, Verse 22).
<div n="NI"/>(8) There is the Badari tree and under it there is an
<div n="lb"/>āśrama of Naranārāyaṇas on the heights of this moun-
<div n="lb"/>tain, and Yakṣas stay there always. (Vana Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 141, Verse 22).
<div n="NI"/>(9) Once the Pāṇḍavas, with the aid of Ghaṭotkaca
<div n="lb"/>mounted this mountain. (Vana Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>145).
<div n="NI"/>(10) It was here, at this mountain that Bhīma killed
<div n="lb"/>Maṇimān, a friend of Kubera. (Vana Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>160, Verse 76).
<div n="NI"/>(11) Kubera, who was driven out of Laṅkā stayed at
<div n="lb"/>this mountain during the initial periods. (Vana Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 275, Verse 33).
