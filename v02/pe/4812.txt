NĪLA II¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} A King born in the Hehaya dynasty. His
<div n="lb"/>was a rebirth of the asura called Krodhavaśa. Nīla
<div n="lb"/>was called Duryodhana also. Māhiṣmatī was the capital
<div n="lb"/>of his kingdom. He attended the Svayaṃvara of Drau-
<div n="lb"/>padī. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 67, Verse 61).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other Information%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Once he fought a fierce battle with Sahadeva, but
<div n="lb"/>ultimately yielded to the latter at the instance of
<div n="lb"/>Agnideva. (See under Agni, Para 8).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) He gave his daughter Sudarśanā in marriage to
<div n="lb"/>Agnideva. (See under Agni, Para 8).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) During his triumphal tour, Karṇa defeated Nīla.
<div n="lb"/>(Vana Parva, Chapter 254, Verse 15).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) In the battle of Kurukṣetra he fought on the side
<div n="lb"/>of the Kauravas. (Udyoga Parva, Chapter 19, Verse 23).
<div n="NI"/>(v) He was reckoned as one of the mahārathis on the
<div n="lb"/>side of Duryodhana. (Udyoga Parva, Chapter 164,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 4).

<div n="NI"/>(vi) Sudarśanā was a daughter born to him of his
<div n="lb"/>wife Narmadā. (Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 2).
