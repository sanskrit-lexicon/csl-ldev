NĪLAKAṆṬHA¦ DĪKṢITAR. A Sanskrit poet who
<div n="lb"/>flourished in the 17th century A.D. He was the court
<div n="lb"/>poet of King Tirumala-Nāyaka. He has written works
<div n="lb"/>in all the branches of literature. His more important
<div n="lb"/>works are Śivalīlārṇava, Gaṅgāvataraṇa. Nīlakaṇṭhavi-
<div n="lb"/>jaya Campū, and Kaliviḍambana. Śivalīlārṇava contains
<div n="lb"/>twentytwo cantos. Its theme is the sixtyfour līlās (Divine
<div n="lb"/>sports) of Śiva installed at Madura. (South India)
<div n="lb"/>Gaṅgāvataraṇa is a poem in eight cantos which descri-
<div n="lb"/>bes the story of Bhagīratha bringing Gaṅgā down to
<div n="lb"/>the earth. In the first canto of Nīlakaṇthavijaya, A.D.
<div n="lb"/>1638-39 is referred to as the period of its composition,
<div n="lb"/>and this reference helps us to determine the date of the
<div n="lb"/>poet.
