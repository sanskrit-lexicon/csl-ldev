ŚRUTĀYUDHA¦. A king of the Kaliṅga land. He was
<div n="lb"/>the son of Varuṇa by Parṇāśā. (M.B. Sabhā Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 4, Verse 23; Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 16, Verse
<div n="lb"/>34).
<div n="NI"/>In Bhārata battle he took his stand on the Kaurava side
<div n="lb"/>and at first clashed with Bhīmasena. Even at the begin-
<div n="lb"/>ning of the battle, Bhīma killed Satya and Satyadeva,
<div n="lb"/>two of the assistants of Śrutāyudha. (M.B. Bhīṣma
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 50, Verse 69). In the end, he used his
<div n="lb"/>cudgel—which was given to him by Varuṇa—against Śrī
<div n="lb"/>Kṛṣṇa who did not take part in the battle at all. Śrutā-
<div n="lb"/>yudha died by his own Cudgel. (M.B. Droṇa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter, 67, Verses 43-48). (See also under Śrutāyus
<div n="lb"/>II).
