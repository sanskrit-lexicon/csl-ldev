NĪLA III¦. A monkey-chief, who was a dependant of Śrī
Rāma. He was Agni's son. ‘Nīla, son of Pāvaka (fire)
shone forth like agni (fire). He stood foremost among
the monkeys in the matter of effulgence, reputation and
prowess’. (Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa, Bālakāṇḍa, Canto 17).
<div n="NI"/>This monkey-chief was also included in the set of
monkeys deputed by Śrī Rāma to search for Sītā. In
the Rāma-Rāvaṇa war Nīla killed Pramāthī, the younger
brother of the Rākṣasa called Dūṣaṇa. (Vana Parva,
Chapter 287, Verse 27).
