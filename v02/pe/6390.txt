ŚATĀNĪKA II¦. Son of Nakula. The Mahābhārata
<div n="lb"/>contains the following information about this Śatānīka.
<div n="NI"/>(i) To Nakula was born of Draupadī a son called
<div n="lb"/>Śatānīka. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 63, Verse 123).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) This Śatānīka was born from an aspect of
<div n="lb"/>Viśvadeva. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 67, Verse 127).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Once upon a time there was in the Kaurava
<div n="lb"/>dynasty a Rājarṣi called Śatānīka and it was to
<div n="lb"/>perpetuate that name that Nakula named his son
<div n="lb"/>‘Śatānīka’. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 220, Verse 84).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) This Śatānīka defeated in the great war Jayatsena,
<div n="lb"/>Duṣkarṇa and Citrasena. (Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 79,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 42; Chapter 79, Verse 46 and Droṇa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 168, Verse 12).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Śatānīka fought with Vṛṣasena, Śrutakarmā, the
<div n="lb"/>son of Dhṛtarāṣṭra and Aśvatthāmā. (Droṇa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 167, Verse 7; Karṇa Parva, Chapter 25, Verse
<div n="lb"/>13 and Chapter 85, Verse 14).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) He killed Bhūtakarman and the prince of Kaliṅga
<div n="lb"/>in the great war. (Droṇa Parva, Chapter 25, Verse 23;
<div n="lb"/>Karṇa Parva, Chapter 85, Verse 21).
[Page701-b+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>(vii) He died in the great war hit by the arrow of
<div n="lb"/>Aśvatthāmā. (Sauptika Parva, Chapter 8, Verse 57).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) He is referred to by the following names also
<div n="lb"/>in the Mahābhārata, viz. Nakulaputra, Nakuladāyāda,
<div n="lb"/>Nākuli.
