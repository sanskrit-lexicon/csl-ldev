AṂŚĀVATĀRA¦. The incarnation of God on earth is
<div n="lb"/>called avatāra. When the incarnation is only partial,
<div n="lb"/>that is, when only some of the divine elements incarnate,
<div n="lb"/>it is called aṃśāvatāra (Aṃśa=part). Chapters 54 to
<div n="lb"/>64 of Ādi Parva of the Mahābhārata give us a list of
<div n="lb"/>the gods who have incarnated partially. The following
<div n="lb"/>list of devas and their aṃśāvatāras is based upon the
<div n="lb"/>account given in the fourth Skandha of Śrī Mahādevī
<div n="lb"/>Bhāgavata.
