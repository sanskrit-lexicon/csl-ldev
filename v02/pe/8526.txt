VIŚVABHUK II¦. The fourth son of Bṛhaspati. It is
mentioned in Mahābhārata, Vana Parva, Chapter 219,
Stanza 17, that it is this Viśvabhuk, who sits in the
stomach of all living things and digests food. This Agni
(fire) is particularly worshipped in sacrifices. The
Purāṇas say that the river Gomatī is the wife of this
Agni (fire).
