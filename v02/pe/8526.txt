VIŚVABHUK II¦. The fourth son of Bṛhaspati. It is
<div n="lb"/>mentioned in Mahābhārata, Vana Parva, Chapter 219,
<div n="lb"/>Stanza 17, that it is this Viśvabhuk, who sits in the
<div n="lb"/>stomach of all living things and digests food. This Agni
<div n="lb"/>(fire) is particularly worshipped in sacrifices. The
<div n="lb"/>Purāṇas say that the river Gomatī is the wife of this
<div n="lb"/>Agni (fire).
