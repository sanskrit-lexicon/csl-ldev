VIṢṆUVRATA¦. A Vrata (fast or vow) taken thinking
<div n="lb"/>of Viṣṇu in mind. By taking this vow, one could get
<div n="lb"/>what one desires for.
<div n="NI"/>Worship Viṣṇu, after bathing his image on the first day
<div n="lb"/>of the vow of four days, beginning with the second day
<div n="lb"/>of the bright lunar fortnight in the month of Pauṣa,
<div n="lb"/>with mustard; on the next day with gingelly seeds; on
<div n="lb"/>the third day with Orris root and on the fourth day
<div n="lb"/>with all the medicinal herbs. ‘All the medicinal herbs’,
<div n="lb"/>means medicine such as Māñcī, Oris root, Costus,
<div n="lb"/>Bitumen (stone juice), Saffron, Curcuma, bulb of
<div n="lb"/>Kaccūri, Flower of Michelia Champaca and bulb of
<div n="lb"/>Cyperus grass. After bathing the image as given above,
<div n="lb"/>Viṣṇu must be exalted on the first day by the name
<div n="lb"/>Kṛṣṇa, on the second day by the name Acyuta, on the
<div n="lb"/>third day, by the name Ananta and on the fourth day,
<div n="lb"/>by the name Hṛṣīkeśa. The worship should be perform-
<div n="lb"/>ed by offering flower, on the feet on the first day, on
<div n="lb"/>the navel on the second day, on the eyes on the third
<div n="lb"/>day and on the head on the fourth day, and Candra
<div n="lb"/>should be given oblations and exalted by names such
<div n="lb"/>as Śaśī on the first day, Candra on the second
<div n="lb"/>day, Śaśāṅka on the third day and Indu on the fourth
<div n="lb"/>day. It is ordained in Chapter 177, of Agni Purāṇa,
<div n="lb"/>that the worship of Viṣṇuvrata should be conducted in
<div n="lb"/>this way. This is a form of worship observed by Kings,
<div n="lb"/>women and Devas (gods).
