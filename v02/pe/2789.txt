IRĀVĀN¦. A son born to Arjuna of the serpent damsel
<div n="lb"/>(Nāgakanyā) named Ulūpī. (For genealogy see under
<div n="lb"/>Arjuna).
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Birth.%} According to the practice laid down Pāñcālī
<div n="lb"/>used to live with each husband for one year by turn.
<div n="lb"/>When she was living with one husband the other four
<div n="lb"/>husbands had no admission there. When once Arjuna
<div n="lb"/>wanted his arms to redeem the cows of a brahmin
<div n="lb"/>stolen by thieves he entered the apartment of Dharma-
<div n="lb"/>putra where the arms had been kept and took them.
<div n="lb"/>For thus having entered, against rules, the apartment
<div n="lb"/>then kept by Dharmaputra and Pāñcālī, Arjuna had to
[Page332-a+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>go on a pilgrimage for one year. Numerous brahmins
<div n="lb"/>accompanied Arjuna and while spending the night on
<div n="lb"/>the banks of the Gaṅgā, Arjuna entered its waters for
<div n="lb"/>bathing. There he saw the Nāga damsel, Ulūpī. They
<div n="lb"/>fell in love with each other and got married. And, a
<div n="lb"/>son named Irāvān was born to them. Ulūpī granted
<div n="lb"/>Arjuna the boon that all water-animals would obey
<div n="lb"/>him and that in water he would be invincible. Leaving
<div n="lb"/>Ulūpī and Irāvān behind, Arjuna continued his stay in
<div n="lb"/>the forest.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Further Information.%} (1) In the great war Irāvān
<div n="lb"/>fought on the side of the Pāṇḍavas. During the first
<div n="lb"/>day's fighting Irāvān and Śrutāyu fought a duel.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 45, Verses 69-71).
<div n="NI"/>(2) Irāvān defeated Vinda and Anuvinda in fight.
<div n="lb"/>(Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 83, Verses 18-22).
<div n="NI"/>(3) Irāvān killed five brothers of Śakuni. (Bhīṣma
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 90, Verses 27-46).
<div n="NI"/>(4) Irāvān fought against Alambuṣa and was killed
<div n="lb"/>by the latter. (Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 90, Verses 56-
<div n="lb"/>76).
