SOMADATTA III¦. A King of the Kuru dynasty.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} This King was the son of
<div n="lb"/>Bālhīka and the grandson of King Pradīpa. Three sons
<div n="lb"/>named Bhūri, Bhūriśravas and Śala were born to
<div n="lb"/>Somadatta.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Somadatta and his sons were present at the
<div n="lb"/>Svayaṃvara marriage of Draupadī. (M.B. Ādi Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 185, Verse 14).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Somadatta had participated in the royal consecra-
<div n="lb"/>tion sacrifice of Yudhiṣṭhira. (M.B. Chapter 34, Verse
<div n="lb"/>8).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) This Somadatta was present at the Svayaṃvara
<div n="lb"/>of Devakī. At that time there occurred a hand to
<div n="lb"/>hand fight between Śini and Somadatta. (M.B. Droṇa
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 114, Verse 14).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) The defeat from Śini being unbearable, Somadatta
<div n="lb"/>did penance to get power to vanquish him and Śiva
<div n="lb"/>appeared before him. Somadatta made the following
<div n="lb"/>request:- “God! May I be granted a son who would
<div n="lb"/>be powerful enough to kick down the son of Śini in his
<div n="lb"/>palace.” Śiva granted his wish. Bhūriśravas was the
<div n="lb"/>son born in conformity with this boon. He grew up
<div n="lb"/>and defeated the son of Śini and kicked him in the
<div n="lb"/>palace of the King. This story occurs in Mahābhārata,
<div n="lb"/>Droṇa Parva, Chapter 144.
<div n="NI"/>(v) Somadatta fought against Sātyaki and Bhīmasena
<div n="lb"/>in the battle of Bhārata and fell down unconscious.
<div n="lb"/>(Droṇa Parva, Chapter 157, Verse 10).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) In the battle which followed this, Sātyaki killed
<div n="lb"/>Somadatta. (M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter 162, Verse
<div n="lb"/>33).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) It is mentioned in Mahābhārata, Āśramavāsika
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 11, Verse 17, that Dhṛtarāṣṭra perform-
<div n="lb"/>ed the sacrifice of giving offerings to the spirit of
<div n="lb"/>Somadatta.
<div n="NI"/>(viii) Somadatta was one of the spirits which appeared
[Page734-a+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>on the surface of the Ganges when invoked by Vyāsa.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Āśramavāsika Parva, Chapter 32, Verse 12).
