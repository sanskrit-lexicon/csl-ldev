MADHUVILĀ¦ (MADHUBILĀ). A river which flows in
<div n="lb"/>the neighbourhood of Kardamila kṣetra. This river has
<div n="lb"/>another name Samaṅga. This is a very sacred tīrtha.
<div n="lb"/>Indra who became a Brahmahatyā-sinner by killing
<div n="lb"/>Vṛtrāsura was absolved of his sins by taking a bath in
<div n="lb"/>this river. Aṣṭāvakra, the sage with eight bends in his
<div n="lb"/>body, got himself perfect by taking a bath in this river
<div n="lb"/>obeying the orders of his father Kahoḍaka. (Chapter 135,
<div n="lb"/>Vana Parva).
