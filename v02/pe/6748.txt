<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} A mountain. It is situated to the north of the
<div n="lb"/>region called “Ilāvṛtta”. Devī Bhāgavata, 8th Skandha
<div n="lb"/>refers to three mountains—Nīlagiri, Śvetagiri, and Śṛṅga-
<div n="lb"/>vān—which are the sources of many rivers and which
<div n="lb"/>stand at intervals of 2,000 miles.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Arjuna crossed this mountain and entered Kuru-
<div n="lb"/>varṣa in the course of his triumphal campaign over the
<div n="lb"/>northern land.
<div n="NI"/>(ii) This mountain is rich in minerals, is of outstand-
<div n="lb"/>ing brilliance, and is the abode of Siddhas and Cāraṇas.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 6, Verse 5).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Sañjaya spoke at length about this mountain to
<div n="lb"/>Dhṛtarāṣṭra. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 8, Verse 8).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) This mountain is glorious enough to be remembered
<div n="lb"/>with reverence at dawn and dusk. (M.B. Anuśāsana
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 165, Verse 32).
