VĪRAVARMĀ II¦. A King who ruled over the city of
<div n="lb"/>Sārasvata. His chief queen was Mālinī, who was the
<div n="lb"/>daughter of a Yakṣa. Vīravarmā stopped the sacrificial
<div n="lb"/>horse of the Pāṇḍavas. When Kṛṣṇa and Arjuna got
<div n="lb"/>ready to fight with Vīravarmā to recover the horse,
<div n="lb"/>Vīravarmā, with the help of Yama fought with them.
<div n="lb"/>At last Kṛṣṇa entered into a treaty with Vīravarmā
<div n="lb"/>and got the horse back.
<div n="NI"/>Vīravarmā had five sons named Subhāla, Sulabha,
<div n="lb"/>Lola, Kuvala and Sarasa. (Jaimini, Aśva: 47: 49).
