RĀJAŚEKHARA¦. A Sanskrit dramatist who lived in
<div n="lb"/>India in 7th century A.D. Bālabhārata or Prakāṇḍa-
<div n="lb"/>pāṇḍava, Bālarāmāyaṇa, Viddhaśālabhañjikā and Kar-
<div n="lb"/>pūramañjarī are his more famous dramatic works.
<div n="lb"/>Karpūramañjarī refers to him as the preceptor of a
<div n="lb"/>king of Kanauj. Rājaśekhara was known by the name
<div n="lb"/>Kavirāja also.
<div n="NI"/>Bālarāmāyaṇa, called also Mahānāṭaka, is a drama in
<div n="lb"/>ten Acts. Bālabhārata contains only two Acts. Its theme
<div n="lb"/>is the wedding of Draupadī and the ruin caused by
<div n="lb"/>the game of dice. Viddhaśālabhañjikā is a drama in
<div n="lb"/>four Acts. He has a further work to his credit, Kāvya-
<div n="lb"/>mīmāṃsā in eighteen chapters.
<div n="NI"/>Some scholars hold the view that Rājaśekhara lived in
<div n="lb"/>the 10th century A.D.
