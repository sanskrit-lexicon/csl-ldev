SRĀVAŚAUCA¦. Defilement attached to the flux due to
<div n="lb"/>abortion. It is ordered in Agni Purāṇa, Chapter 258,
<div n="lb"/>how to observe this defilement. It is said that women
<div n="lb"/>should observe defilement for as many nights as the
<div n="lb"/>number of the months of pregnancy, till the flux. If the
<div n="lb"/>flux took place in the fourth month, defilement should
<div n="lb"/>be observed for three nights. If it was in the fifth
<div n="lb"/>month that the flux had occurred, pollution for ten
<div n="lb"/>days would have to be observed. This is meant for
<div n="lb"/>Brahmins. In the case of a Kṣatriya woman if the
<div n="lb"/>flux occurred in the fifth month, she has to observe
<div n="lb"/>defilement for four days; and five days for a Vaiśya
<div n="lb"/>woman, and eight days for a Śūdra woman. In the
<div n="lb"/>case of a Śūdra woman, if the flux is after five months,
<div n="lb"/>she has to observe pollution for twelve days. In this
<div n="lb"/>stage the father will be purified by a bath.
