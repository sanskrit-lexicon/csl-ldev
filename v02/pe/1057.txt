BHĀRADVĀJA III¦. A grammarian. According to the
<div n="lb"/>Ṛktantra, prātiśākhya of Sāmaveda, it was Brahmā,
<div n="lb"/>who first composed the science of grammar. This
<div n="lb"/>science was taught by Brahmā to others in the following
<div n="lb"/>order: Brahmā to Bṛhaspati, he to Indra, Indra to
<div n="lb"/>Bhāradvāja and he to his disciples.
<div n="NI"/>Pāṇini has discussed the grammatical concepts of Bhāra-
<div n="lb"/>dvāja. Ṛkprātiśākhya and Taittirīya have quoted the
<div n="lb"/>opinions of this grammarian.
