MALLARĀṢṬRA¦. A country in ancient India. The
<div n="lb"/>capital of Mallarāṣṭra was the present city of Kuśī. At
<div n="lb"/>the time of Mahābhārata, this country was ruled by a
<div n="lb"/>King named Pārthiva. There is a statement in the
<div n="lb"/>Mahābhārata that this King was defeated by Bhīmasena.
<div n="lb"/>When the Pāṇḍavas discussed the countries in which
<div n="lb"/>they were to live “incognito”, the name of Mallarāṣṭra
<div n="lb"/>was also mentioned. (Mahābhārata, Sabhā Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 30, Verse 3; Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 9, Verse
<div n="lb"/>44; Virāṭa Parva, Chapter 1, Verse 13).
