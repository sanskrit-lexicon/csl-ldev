<div n="lb"/>commercial business once was killed by thieves. As a
<div n="lb"/>result of sins committed in previous births he was turned
<div n="lb"/>into a Piśāca. After his death his son, who led quite a
<div n="lb"/>moral life went to Kāśī (Banares) to perform his father's
<div n="lb"/>obsequies, and at the particular spot where his father
<div n="lb"/>was living as Piśāca the son recited Chapter 3 of the
<div n="lb"/>Gītā, on hearing which Jaḍa got released from his state
<div n="lb"/>as Piśāca. (Padma Purāṇa, Uttarakhaṇḍa and Mār-
<div n="lb"/>kaṇḍeya Purāṇa).
