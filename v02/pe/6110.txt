SAṂVARAṆA¦. A king of the lunar dynasty.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Genealogy.%} Descended from Viṣṇu thus: Brahmā—
<div n="lb"/>Atri-Candra-Budha-Purūravas-Āyus-Nahuṣa-
<div n="lb"/>Yayāti-Pūru-Janamejaya-Prācinvān-Manasyu-
<div n="lb"/>Vītabhaya-Śuṇḍu-Bahuvidha-Saṃyāti-Rahovādī-
<div n="lb"/>Bhadrāśva-Matināra-Santurodha-Duṣyanta-
<div n="lb"/>Bharata-Bṛhatkṣatra-Hasti-Ajamīḍha-Ṛkṣa-
<div n="lb"/>Saṃvaraṇa.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Marriage.%} Saṃvaraṇa married Tapatī, daughter of
<div n="lb"/>Sūrya. (For details see under Tapatī).
<div n="NI"/>3) {%Other information.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) King Pāñcāla once attacked and subjugated him.
<div n="lb"/>(Ādi Parva, Chapter 93, Verse 37).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) He gave up the throne in fear of the enemy and
<div n="lb"/>went and lived on the banks of the river Sindhu. (Ādi
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 9, Verse 39).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) To regain his kingdom he appointed Vasiṣṭha as
<div n="lb"/>his priest. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 94, Verse 42).
[Page681-b+ 64]
<div n="NI"/>(iv) He got back the kingdom by Vasiṣṭha's help and
<div n="lb"/>then he performed a Yajña with the latter as high
<div n="lb"/>priest. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 94, Verse 45).
<div n="NI"/>(v) A son called Kuru was born to him by Tapatī
<div n="lb"/>daughter of Sūrya. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 94, Verse 48).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) He was a devotee of Sūrya. (Ādi Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>170, Verse 12).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) No other king more handsome than he had yet
<div n="lb"/>been born. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 170, Verse 15).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) Once there was no rainfall for twelve years in
<div n="lb"/>his kingdom. (Ādi Parva, Chapter 172, Verse 38).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) He is one of the kings to be remembered at dawn
<div n="lb"/>and at dusk. (Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter 165, Verse 54).
<div n="NI"/>(x) Words like Ājamīḍha, Ārkṣa, Paurava, Paura-
<div n="lb"/>vanandana and Ṛkṣaputra have been used in Mahā-
<div n="lb"/>bhārata as synonyms of Saṃvaraṇa.
