CĀṆŪRA II¦. An asura, one of the attendants of Kaṃsa.
<div n="lb"/>Kaṃsa had employed as his body-guards many
<div n="lb"/>pugilists—Pralaṃbaka, Cāṇūra, Tṛṇāvarta, Muṣṭika,
<div n="lb"/>Ariṣṭaka, Keśi, Dhenuka, Agha and Vivida and these
<div n="lb"/>pugilists were sent with Pūtanā to Gokula to kill Śrī
<div n="lb"/>Kṛṣṇa. On the death of Pūtanā they returned to
<div n="lb"/>Mathurā. When Kaṃsa invited Kṛṣṇa to Mathurā
<div n="lb"/>Cāṇūra and Muṣṭika were the chief pugilists entrusted
<div n="lb"/>with the duty of killing Kṛṣṇa. Kṛṣṇa fought with
<div n="lb"/>Cāṇūra and Balabhadra with Muṣṭika. Both Cāṇūra
<div n="lb"/>and Muṣṭika were killed. (Bhāgavata Daśama Skandha).
