GĀNDHĀRA¦. A stretch of land of ancient Bhārata. It
<div n="lb"/>is believed that this land stretched from the shores of
<div n="lb"/>river Sindhu to Kābul. Subala was a mighty ruler of
<div n="lb"/>this country. His daughter Gāndhārī was the wife of
<div n="lb"/>Dhṛtarāṣṭra. (Śloka 11, Chapter 111, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>Agni Purāṇa points out a relationship between the
<div n="lb"/>Gāndhāras and the Drāviḍas. Descending in order from
<div n="lb"/>Viṣṇu were Candra—Budha—Purūravas—Āyus—Nahuṣa
<div n="lb"/>-Yayāti—Turvasu. In order from Turvasu were Varga—
<div n="lb"/>Gobhānu—Traiśāni—Karandhama—Marutta—Duṣya-
<div n="lb"/>nta—Varūtha—Gāṇḍīra—Gāndhāra. From Gāndhāra
<div n="lb"/>arose the five different provincialists: Gāndhāras,
<div n="lb"/>Keralas, Colas, Pāṇḍyas and Kolas. (Chapter 277,
<div n="lb"/>Agni Purāṇa).
