<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} Son of sage A giras. (M.B. Ādi Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 66, Verse 5). He gave advice on subjects of

<div n="NI"/>2) {%Utathya drank up the sea dry.%} Varuṇa deva had an
<div n="lb"/>eye on Soma's daughter Bhadrā when Utathya married
<div n="lb"/>her. Incensed at the marriage, Varuṇa carried Bhadrā
<div n="lb"/>off to the sea one day when Utathya was not present.
<div n="lb"/>Nārada informed Utathya that it was Varuṇa who
<div n="lb"/>stole his wife. Though Nārada, at the request of
<div n="lb"/>Utathya, asked Varuṇa to return Bhadrā to the former
<div n="lb"/>he did not oblige. Enraged at this Utathya drank up
<div n="lb"/>the sea dry. Yet, Varuṇa did not come round. Then
<div n="lb"/>Utathya rendered all the lakes of Varuṇa dry. Trem-
<div n="lb"/>bling at this Varuṇa returned Bhadrā to Utathya and
<div n="lb"/>prostrated at his feet. He pardoned Varuṇa and gave
<div n="lb"/>back the sea to him. (M.B. Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>154).
