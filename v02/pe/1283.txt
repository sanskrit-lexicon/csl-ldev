BRAHMĀVARTTA¦. A holy place situated in Kuruk-
<div n="lb"/>ṣetra. It is mentioned in Mahābhārata, Vana Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 83, Stanza 53, that he who takes a bath in
<div n="lb"/>this holy place will go to the world of Brahmā. Manu-
<div n="lb"/>smṛti, Chapter 2, Stanza 17, refers to this Brahmāvartta.
<div n="lb"/>Manu says as follows:
<Poem>“Sarasvatīdṛṣadvatyor-
<div n="lb"/>devanadyoryadantaram /
<div n="lb"/>Taddevanirmitaṃ deśaṃ
<div n="lb"/>Brahmāvarttaṃ pracakṣate. //</Poem>
<div n="NI"/>“The place between the two rivers Sarasvatī and Dṛṣad-
<div n="lb"/>vatī is called Brahmāvartta, which was made by the
