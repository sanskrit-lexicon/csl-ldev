VIRĀṬA¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} The King of Matsya country.
During the pseudonymity of the Pāṇḍavas this King
sheltered them. At the end of the pseudonymity of one
[Page861-a+ 65]
year, the Kauravas had stolen the cows of Virāṭa. In
the fight which ensued Arjuna entered the battlefield
with Uttara, the son of King Virāṭa, and defeated the
Kauravas and proclaimed that the life of pseudonymity
was over. After that Abhimanyu married Uttarā the
daughter of Virāṭa. (M.B. Virāṭa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) This Virāṭa, the King of Matsya was born from a
portion of the Marudgaṇas. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter
67, Stanza 82).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) King Virāṭa had two sons named Uttara and
Śaṅkha. It was with these sons that he attended the
Svayaṃvara marriage of Draupadī. (M.B. Ādi Parva,
Chapter 185, Stanza 8).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) In the regional conquest conducted by Sahadeva,
before the Imperial consecration-sacrifice of the Pāṇḍa-
vas, Virāṭa fought with him and was defeated. (M.B.
Sabhā Parva, Chapter 31, Stanza 2).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) King Virāṭa attended the Rājasūya (sacrifice of
imperial consecration) of Yudhiṣṭhira. (M.B. Sabhā
Parva, Chapter 44, Stanza 20).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Virāṭa gave as a gift to Yudhiṣṭhira two thousand
tuskers adorned with gold chains. (M.B. Sabhā Parva,
Chapter 52, Stanza 26).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) The name of the wife of King Virāṭa was Sudeṣṇā.
(M.B. Virāṭa Parva, Chapter 9, Stanza 6).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) During the incognito-life of the Pāṇḍavas, King
Virāṭa sheltered them in his palace. He took Yudhi-
ṣṭhira as a court-favourite, Bhīmasena as the over-seer
of his dining hall, Arjuna as the dancing master, Nakula
as the head of the stables, and Sahadeva as the head of
the cow-herds. (M.B. Virāṭa Parva, Chapters 7, 8, 10,
11 and 12).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) The first wife of King Virāṭa was Surathā, the
princess of Kosala. A son named Śveta was born to
Virāṭa by Surathā. After the death of Surathā, he
married Sudeṣṇā the daughter of Sūta, King of Kekaya.
To Sudeṣṇā two sons named Śaṅkha and Uttara and as
the youngest, a daughter named Uttarā were born.
(M.B. Virāṭa Parva, Dākṣiṇātya Pāṭha, Chapter 16).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) It is stated in Mahābhārata, Virāṭa Parva,
Dākṣiṇātyapāṭha, Chapter 26, that King Virāṭa had
ten brothers.
<div n="NI"/>(x) Virāṭa had two brothers named Śatānīka and
Madirākṣa. Sūryadatta is another name of Śatānīka.
He was the commander of the army of Virāṭa. Madirā-
kṣa was also called Viśālākṣa. (M.B. Virāṭa Parva,
Chapters 31 and 32).
<div n="NI"/>(xi) At the time of the theft of the cows, King Virāṭa
engaged in combat with Suśarmā. (M.B. Virāṭa Parva,
Chapter 32, Stanza 28).
<div n="NI"/>(xii) Suśarmā caught hold of Virāṭa alive. (M.B.
Virāṭa Parva, Chapter 33, Stanza 7).
<div n="NI"/>(xiii) Virāṭa was one of the seven prominent command-
ers of the army of Yudhiṣṭhira. (M.B. Udyoga Parva,
Chapter 157, Stanza 11).
<div n="NI"/>(xiv) On the first day of the battle of Bhārata there
was a combat between Virāṭa and Bhagadatta. (M.B.
Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 45, Stanza 49).
<div n="NI"/>(xv) Virāṭa attacked Bhīṣma. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva,
Chapter 73, Stanza 1).
<div n="NI"/>(xvi) In the fight between Virāṭa and Droṇa, Śaṅkha
was killed. With that Virāṭa ran away. (M.B. Bhīṣma
Parva, Chapter 82, Stanza 14).
<div n="NI"/>(xvii) There was a combat between Virāṭa and Aśvat-
[Page861-b+ 64]
thāmā. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 110, Stanza 16).
<div n="NI"/>(xviii) Virāṭa combatted with Jayadratha. (M.B.
Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 116, Stanza 42).
<div n="NI"/>(xix) Virāṭa fought with Vinda and Anuvinda. (M.B.
Droṇa Parva, Chapter 25, Stanza 20).
<div n="NI"/>(xx) In the fight with Śalya, Virāṭa fell down uncons-
cious. (M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter 167, Stanza 34).
<div n="NI"/>(xxi) In the battle which followed, Droṇācārya killed
Virāṭa. (M.B. Karṇa Parva, Chapter 6, Stanza 6).
<div n="NI"/>(xxii) Mention is made in Mahābhārata, Strī Parva,
Chapter 26, Stanza 33, that the funeral ceremony of
Virāṭa was conducted in a befitting manner and in
Mahābhārata, Śānti Parva, Chapter 42, Stanza 4, that
Yudhiṣṭhira performed offering to the Manes for him.
<div n="NI"/>(xxiii) After death, Virāṭa entered heaven and joined
the Marudgaṇas. (M.B. Svargārohaṇa Parva, Chapter
5, Stanza 15).
<div n="NI"/>(xxiv) The synonyms used in Mahābhārata for Virāṭa
are, Matsya, Matsyapati, Matsyarāṭ, Matsyarāja etc.
