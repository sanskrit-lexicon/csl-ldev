AṢṬAVIDHANĀYIKĀ¦(S). Eight kinds of heroines of
the stage:
<div n="NI"/>(1) {%Svādhīnapatikā.%} Fit to be a counterpart in a love
scene and play the role of one who is always fondled by
her husband.
<div n="NI"/>(2) {%Vāsakasajjikā (Vāsakasajjā).%} Adorning herself well
and waiting for her lover in a well decorated bedroom.
<div n="NI"/>(3) {%Virahotkaṇṭhitā.%} Exhausted by the sorrow of separa-
tion from her lover.
<div n="NI"/>(4) {%Vipralabdhā.%} Cheated by her lover after fixing a
date and a rendezvous.
<div n="NI"/>(5) {%Khaṇḍitā.%} Angered at the arrival of the husband in
the early hours of the morning with suspicion about his
character.
<div n="NI"/>(6) {%Kalahāntaritā.%} Repenting after having arrogantly
disobeyed her husband.
<div n="NI"/>(7) {%Proṣitabhartṛkā.%} One who has gone weak and moody
thinking about her husband in a foreign place.
<div n="NI"/>(8) {%Abhisārikā.%} One who approaches her husband with
great passion. (Nāṭyakrama).
