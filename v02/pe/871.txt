<div n="lb"/>son of King Purujāti. Five sons were born to Bāhyāś-
<div n="lb"/>van called Mukula, Sṛñjaya, Bṛhadiṣṭha, Yavīnara and
<div n="lb"/>Kṛmila. These five sons became famous as Pāñcālas.
<div n="lb"/>Of these, the family of Mukula became the Mauku-
<div n="lb"/>lyas, who were having temples and were of two classes.
<div n="lb"/>A son named Pañcāśva was born to Mukula. A son
<div n="lb"/>named Divodāsa and a daughter named Ahalyā were
<div n="lb"/>born to Pañcāśva. Ahalyā got with child from the
<div n="lb"/>hermit Śāradvata and gave birth to a son named Śatā-
