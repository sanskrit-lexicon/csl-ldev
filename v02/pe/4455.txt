MATSYA II¦. An ancient country of Purāṇic fame. The
<div n="lb"/>people of this country are called Matsyas. The details
<div n="lb"/>available regarding Matsyadeśa from the Mahābhārata
<div n="lb"/>are given below:
<div n="NI"/>(1) The Pāṇḍavas wandering through the forests came
<div n="lb"/>to Matsyadeśa. (Śloka 2, Chapter 155, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(2) The people of Matsyadeśa in fear of Jarāsandha
<div n="lb"/>migrated from the north to the south. (Śloka 28,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 14, Sabhā Parva).
[Page494-a+ 65]
<div n="NI"/>(3) Bhīmasena during his victory march to the east
<div n="lb"/>conquered this country. (Śloka 8, Chapter 30, Sabhā
<div n="lb"/>Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(4) Sahadeva during his victory march to the south
<div n="lb"/>conquered the people of Matsyadeśa. (Śloka 4, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>31, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(5) Matsyadeśa was one of the countries suggested by
<div n="lb"/>Arjuna to spend their life incognito. (Śloka 12,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 1, Virāṭa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(6) Virāṭa was the chief of Matsyadeśa during the
<div n="lb"/>time of Mahābhārata. (Śloka 17, Chapter 1, Virāṭa
<div n="lb"/>Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(7) In the Kurukṣetra battle Virāṭa, King of Matsya-
<div n="lb"/>deśa, came to the help of Yudhiṣṭhira with an Akṣau-
<div n="lb"/>hiṇī (army). (Śloka 12, Chapter 18, Udyoga Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(8) The Pāṇḍavas spent a year of their life incognito
<div n="lb"/>at the palace of Virāṭa, king of Matsyadeśa. (Chapter
<div n="lb"/>7, Virāṭa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(9) Matsyarājya was one of the prominent kingdoms of
<div n="lb"/>ancient Bhārata. (Śloka 40, Chapter 9, Bhīṣma Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(10) Bhīṣma slaughtered many soldiers from Matsya-
<div n="lb"/>deśa.
<div n="NI"/>(11) Droṇācārya killed at a stretch five hundred
<div n="lb"/>Matsyas in the great battle. (Śloka 31 Chapter 190,
<div n="lb"/>Droṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(12) Karṇa once conquered Matsyadeśa. (Śloka 18,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 8, Karṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(13) The people of Matsyadeśa were honest and charit-
<div n="lb"/>able. (Chapter 45, Śloka 28, Karṇa Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(14) Those who were left out among the Matsyas in
<div n="lb"/>the great battle were killed by Aśvatthāmā. (Śloka 158,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 8, Sauptika Parva).
