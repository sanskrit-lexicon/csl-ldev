UDVAHA II¦. A part of air. It is believed that air or
<div n="lb"/>wind is the breath of Mahāviṣṇu. In the Purāṇas
<div n="lb"/>mention is made about 49 types of Maruts or winds.
<div n="lb"/>Seven breaths are important among them. They are: (1)
<div n="lb"/>Pravaha (2) Āvaha (3) Udvaha (4) Saṃvaha (5)
<div n="lb"/>Vivaha (6) Parīvaha and (7) Parāvaha. About Udvaha
<div n="lb"/>mention is made in Bhāṣā Bhārata, Śānti Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 329 thus:
<div n="NI"/>“Which is the wind that takes water from the four
<div n="lb"/>oceans and gives it to the clouds? That ever-blowing
<div n="lb"/>great wind is Udvaha.”
