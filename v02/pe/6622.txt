SINĪVĀLĪ I¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Birth.%} A daughter of Aṅgiras. The wife of Aṅgiras
<div n="lb"/>was Smṛti. She gave birth to four daughters named
<div n="lb"/>Kuhū, Rākā, Anumati and Sinīvālī. (Viṣṇu Purāṇa,
<div n="lb"/>Aṃśa 1, Chapter 10).
<div n="NI"/>(It is mentioned in Bhāgavata, Skandha 6, that
<div n="lb"/>Sinīvālī was the third daughter of Aṅgiras by his wife
<div n="lb"/>Śraddhā.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Dṛśyādṛśyā is another name of Sinīvālī. Her body
<div n="lb"/>is small. So sometimes she can be seen and sometimes
<div n="lb"/>she cannot be seen. It is in this meaning that she
<div n="lb"/>gets the name Dṛśyādṛśyā (seen and unseen). Śiva
<div n="lb"/>bears her in his forehead. So she has another name
<div n="lb"/>Rudrasutā. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter 218, Verse 5).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Śiva used Sinīvālī as the yoke of the horses of his
<div n="lb"/>chariot in the battle with Tripuras. (M.B. Karṇa
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 34, Verse 32).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) At the time of his birth Sinīvālī had come to see
<div n="lb"/>the child Subrahmaṇya. (M.B. Śalya Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>45, Verse 13).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) It is mentioned in Atharva Veda that Sinīvālī is a
<div n="lb"/>wife of Mahāviṣṇu.
