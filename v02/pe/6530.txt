SAVARṆĀ¦. Wife of the muni called Prācīnabarhis.
Savarṇā was the daughter of Samudra (ocean). (Viṣṇu
Purāṇa, Part 1, Chapter 14). The couple had ten
sons called the Pracetases, and they were very efficient
in Dhanurveda. All of them engaged themselves in
observing the same dharma (duty) and did tapas for
10,000 years under the sea. (For details see under
Prācīnabarhis).
