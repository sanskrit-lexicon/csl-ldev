<F>*) Ariṣṭhā, a wife of Kaśyapa, delivered thirteen Apsaras. They were: Alambuṣā, Miśrakeśī, Vidyutparṇā, Tilottamā, Rakṣitā,
<div n="lb"/>Rambhā, Manoramā, Keśinī, Subāhu, Surajā, Suratā and Supriyā. Ariṣṭhā gave birth also to four Gandharvas, Hāhā, Hūhū, Atibāhu
<div n="lb"/>and Tumburu as mentioned in the following verse.
<Poem>Ariṣṭāsūta Subhagādevī devarṣitaḥ purā
<div n="lb"/>Alambuṣā miśrakeśī Vidyutparṇā Tilottamā
<div n="lb"/>Aruṇā rakṣitā caiva rambhā tadvat manoramā
<div n="lb"/>Keśinī ca subāhuśca vikhyatau ca hahāhuhū
<div n="lb"/>Tumburuśceti catvāraḥ smṛtāḥ gandharvasattamāḥ.</Poem></F> An Apsaras is a nymph (devastrī). These
<div n="lb"/>apsarā women were born at the churning of the ocean
<div n="lb"/>of Milk. (Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa, Bālakāṇḍa, Chapter 45,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 32 and Viṣṇu Purāṇa, Part I, Chapter 9 and
<div n="lb"/>Agni Purāṇa, Chapter 3). There are thousands of
<div n="lb"/>Apsaras. (Details given against the names of Apsaras).
