ŚEṢA¦ (ĀDIŚEṢA). Ananta. (q. v.)
<div n="NI"/>{%Additional information%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Ananta is the reposing bed of Viṣṇu. It is this
<div n="lb"/>Ananta who dislodged Mandara mountain at the time
<div n="lb"/>of Kṣīrābdhi mathana (churning of the ocean of milk)
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 18, Verse 8).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Ananta is the noblest of the nāgas. (M.B. Ādi
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 35, Verse 2).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) When there were mutual dissensions among the
<div n="lb"/>nāgas, Ananta visited holy places such as Puṣkara.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 36, Verse 3).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Ananta obtained from Brahmā the boon to be
<div n="lb"/>able to stand firmly on Dharma. (M.B. Ādi Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 36, Verse 17).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Ananta carries and supports the earth at the behest
<div n="lb"/>of Brahmā. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 36, Verse 18).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) Ananta's father is Kaśyapa and his mother is
<div n="lb"/>Kadrū. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 65, Verse 41).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) Balabhadra Rāma was born from a portion of
<div n="lb"/>Ananta. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 67, Verse 152).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) At the time of Tripuradahana, Ananta served as
<div n="lb"/>the axle of Śiva's chariot. (M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>202, Verse 72).
