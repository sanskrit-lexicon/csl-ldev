ŚŪRASENA III¦. “Śūrasena was the old name for the
region now known as Mathurāmaṇḍala or Vrajamaṇḍala.
The natives of this place were called “Śūrasenas”. The
following pieces of information are given about the Śūra-
senas in the Mahābhārata:—
<div n="NI"/>(i) The Śūrasenas who were afraid of Jarāsandha, fled
to the southern lands. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 14,
Verse 26).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) In the course of his southern campaign, Sahadeva
conquered the Śūrasenas. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter
31, Verse 1).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) The Śūrasenas offered presents at Yudhiṣṭhira's
Rājasūya. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 52, Verse 13).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) It was between the two countries, South Yakṛllo-
mam and Śūrasenam that the Pāṇḍavas travelled from
the land of Pāñcāla to Matsyadeśa. (M.B. Virāṭa Parva
Chapter 5, Verse 4).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Śūrasenas were the body-guards of Bhīṣma during
the Bhārata Yuddha. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 18,
Verse 12).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) The Śūrasena forces once stopped Arjuna on
the way. (M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter 91, Verse 37).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) Śūrasena attacked Arjuna and Sātyaki in Bhārata
battle. (M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter 141, Verse 9).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) Yudhiṣṭhira soaked the earth with blood by
killing the Śūrasenas at Kurukṣetra. (M.B. Droṇa Parva
Chapter 157, Verse 29).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) Bhīmasena butchered the Kṣatriyas of Śūrasena
by hundreds. (M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter 169, Verse
4).
<div n="NI"/>(x) Kṛpācārya, Kṛtavarmā and Śakuni from the
Pāṇḍava side fought against the Śūrasenas. (M.B.
Karṇa Parva, Chapter 47, Verse 16).
