<div n="lb"/>region now known as Mathurāmaṇḍala or Vrajamaṇḍala.
<div n="lb"/>The natives of this place were called “Śūrasenas”. The
<div n="lb"/>following pieces of information are given about the Śūra-
<div n="lb"/>senas in the Mahābhārata:—
<div n="NI"/>(i) The Śūrasenas who were afraid of Jarāsandha, fled
<div n="lb"/>to the southern lands. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 14,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 26).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) In the course of his southern campaign, Sahadeva
<div n="lb"/>conquered the Śūrasenas. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>31, Verse 1).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) The Śūrasenas offered presents at Yudhiṣṭhira's
<div n="lb"/>Rājasūya. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 52, Verse 13).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) It was between the two countries, South Yakṛllo-

<div n="lb"/>Chapter 5, Verse 4).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Śūrasenas were the body-guards of Bhīṣma during
<div n="lb"/>the Bhārata Yuddha. (M.B. Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 18,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 12).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) The Śūrasena forces once stopped Arjuna on

<div n="NI"/>(viii) Yudhiṣṭhira soaked the earth with blood by

<div n="NI"/>(ix) Bhīmasena butchered the Kṣatriyas of Śūrasena

<div n="lb"/>4).
<div n="NI"/>(x) Kṛpācārya, Kṛtavarmā and Śakuni from the

<div n="lb"/>Karṇa Parva, Chapter 47, Verse 16).
