<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} There are many references to Yavanas and
the land of Yavanas in the Purāṇas. There is a statement
in Mahābhārata, Ādi Parva, Chapter 85, Verse 34, that
the Yavana rase takes its source from Turvasu, the son
of Yayāti. There is another version in Mahābhārata,
Ādi Parva, Chapter 174, Verse 36, that Yavanas were
born from the womb and the sides of Nandinī.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Arjuna's brother Sahadeva once conquered the Yavana
land. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 31, Verse 73).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Mahābhārata, Sabhā Parva, Chapter 32, Verse 17,
mentions that on another occasion, Nakula defeated the
Yavanas.
<div n="NI"/>(iii) According to Mahābhārata, Vana Parva, Chapter
188, Verse 35, the world will be filled with Yavanas
and other Mleccha (low class) kings.
<div n="NI"/>(iv) In the course of his conquests, Karṇa once subdued
the Yavanas. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter 254, Verse 8).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Mahābhārata, Udyoga Parva, Chapter 19, Verse 21,
mentions that king Sudakṣiṇa of Kāmboja approached
Duryodhana with an “Akṣauhiṇī” along with the
Yavanas, to take part in Bhārata Yuddha.
<div n="NI"/>(vi) Mahābhārata, Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 9, Verse 65
states that at the time of Mahābhārata, Yavana land
was part of India.
<div n="NI"/>(vii) Originally Yavanas were Kṣatriyas. But they be-
came Śūdras by the curse of Brāhmaṇas. (M.B. Anuśā-
sana Parva, Chapter 35, Verse 18).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) Once there was a duel between a Yavana king
and Mucukunda. (For further details, see under Mucu-
kunda).
