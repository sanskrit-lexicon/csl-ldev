<div n="NI"/>1) {%General.%} There are many references to Yavanas and
<div n="lb"/>the land of Yavanas in the Purāṇas. There is a statement
<div n="lb"/>in Mahābhārata, Ādi Parva, Chapter 85, Verse 34, that
<div n="lb"/>the Yavana rase takes its source from Turvasu, the son
<div n="lb"/>of Yayāti. There is another version in Mahābhārata,
<div n="lb"/>Ādi Parva, Chapter 174, Verse 36, that Yavanas were
<div n="lb"/>born from the womb and the sides of Nandinī.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Arjuna's brother Sahadeva once conquered the Yavana
<div n="lb"/>land. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 31, Verse 73).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Mahābhārata, Sabhā Parva, Chapter 32, Verse 17,
<div n="lb"/>mentions that on another occasion, Nakula defeated the
<div n="lb"/>Yavanas.
<div n="NI"/>(iii) According to Mahābhārata, Vana Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>188, Verse 35, the world will be filled with Yavanas
<div n="lb"/>and other Mleccha (low class) kings.
<div n="NI"/>(iv) In the course of his conquests, Karṇa once subdued
<div n="lb"/>the Yavanas. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter 254, Verse 8).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Mahābhārata, Udyoga Parva, Chapter 19, Verse 21,
<div n="lb"/>mentions that king Sudakṣiṇa of Kāmboja approached
<div n="lb"/>Duryodhana with an “Akṣauhiṇī” along with the
<div n="lb"/>Yavanas, to take part in Bhārata Yuddha.
<div n="NI"/>(vi) Mahābhārata, Bhīṣma Parva, Chapter 9, Verse 65
<div n="lb"/>states that at the time of Mahābhārata, Yavana land
<div n="lb"/>was part of India.
<div n="NI"/>(vii) Originally Yavanas were Kṣatriyas. But they be-
<div n="lb"/>came Śūdras by the curse of Brāhmaṇas. (M.B. Anuśā-
<div n="lb"/>sana Parva, Chapter 35, Verse 18).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) Once there was a duel between a Yavana king
<div n="lb"/>and Mucukunda. (For further details, see under Mucu-
<div n="lb"/>kunda).
