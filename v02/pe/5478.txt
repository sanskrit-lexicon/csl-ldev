PUṆḌRA II¦. An ancient region of Bhārata. The
<div n="lb"/>region comprised then the present district of Mālada,
<div n="lb"/>certain portions on the east coast of river Kosī and
<div n="lb"/>certain portions of Dinājpur. King Pāṇḍu conquered
<div n="lb"/>Puṇḍra. (Chapter 112, Ādi Parva). The people of
<div n="lb"/>Puṇḍra came to the Rājasūya of Yudhiṣṭhira with
<div n="lb"/>presents. The Pauṇḍrakavāsudeva mentioned in the
<div n="lb"/>Bhāgavata was the King of Puṇḍra. Karṇa and
<div n="lb"/>Arjuna conquered this country at different times.
<div n="lb"/>(Chapter 52, Sabhā Parva; Chapter 8, Karṇa Parva;
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 82, Āśvamedhika Parva).
