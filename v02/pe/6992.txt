SUHOTRA III¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} A king of the Candra dynasty.
<div n="lb"/>It is mentioned in Mahābhārata, Ādi Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>94, Verse 24, that he was the grandson of emperor
<div n="lb"/>Bharata and the son of the elder brother of Bhumanyu.
<div n="lb"/>Puṣkariṇī was the mother of this Suhotra.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) This Suhotra got suzerainty over the entire earth.
<div n="lb"/>After this he conducted horse-sacrifice and several
<div n="lb"/>other sacrifices. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 94, Verse
<div n="lb"/>25).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Suhotra married Suvarṇā, a princess of the Ikṣvāku
<div n="lb"/>dynasty. Three sons, Ajamīḍha, Sumīḍha and Puru-
<div n="lb"/>mīḍha were born to them. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>94, Verse 30).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Suhotra was a generous king. (M.B. Droṇa-
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 56).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Being pleased at his hospitality, Indra showered
<div n="lb"/>a rain of gold in the country for a year. Rivers were
<div n="lb"/>full of golden water. Indra showered golden fishes also
<div n="lb"/>in these rivers. But the king divided all this gold
<div n="lb"/>among Brahmins. (M.B. Śānti Parva, Chapter 29,
<div n="lb"/>Verse 25).
