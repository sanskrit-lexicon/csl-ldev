SUHOTRA III¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} A king of the Candra dynasty.
It is mentioned in Mahābhārata, Ādi Parva, Chapter
94, Verse 24, that he was the grandson of emperor
Bharata and the son of the elder brother of Bhumanyu.
Puṣkariṇī was the mother of this Suhotra.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) This Suhotra got suzerainty over the entire earth.
After this he conducted horse-sacrifice and several
other sacrifices. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 94, Verse
25).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Suhotra married Suvarṇā, a princess of the Ikṣvāku
dynasty. Three sons, Ajamīḍha, Sumīḍha and Puru-
mīḍha were born to them. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter
94, Verse 30).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Suhotra was a generous king. (M.B. Droṇa-
Parva, Chapter 56).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Being pleased at his hospitality, Indra showered
a rain of gold in the country for a year. Rivers were
full of golden water. Indra showered golden fishes also
in these rivers. But the king divided all this gold
among Brahmins. (M.B. Śānti Parva, Chapter 29,
Verse 25).
