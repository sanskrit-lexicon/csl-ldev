<div n="NI"/>1) {%Genealogy.%} Descending in order from Viṣṇu:—Brahmā-
<div n="lb"/>Atri—Candra—Budha—Purūravas—Āyus—Nahuṣa—

<div n="lb"/>Bhadru—Kṛta—Uśika—Cedi. The Cedi dynasty begins

<div n="lb"/>known as Cedirājya.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details regarding King Cedi.%}
<div n="NI"/>1) Once Uparicaravasu conquered his land. (Śloka 2,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 63, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>2) Kareṇumatī, wife of Nakula, was a princess of
<div n="lb"/>Cedi rājya. (Śloka 79, Chapter 95, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>3) Once Śiśupāla reigned over this country. After his
<div n="lb"/>death his son Dhṛṣṭaketu was crowned king. (Śloka 36,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 45, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>4) During the period of Nala, Cedirājya was ruled
<div n="lb"/>by king Subāhu. Damayantī lived in his palace very
<div n="lb"/>comfortably. (Śloka 44, Chapter 65, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>5) Dhṛṣṭaketu, a king of Cedi, came to the help of the
<div n="lb"/>Pāṇḍavas with an akṣauhiṇī (an army of 21870 chariots,
<div n="lb"/>equal number of elephants, 65610 horses and 10930
<div n="lb"/>infantry men.). (Śloka 7, Chapter 19, Udyoga Parva).
<div n="NI"/>6) The Kṣatriya warriors of this state helped Śrī
<div n="lb"/>Kṛṣṇa in many ways. (Śloka 11, Chapter 28, Udyoga
<div n="lb"/>Parva).
<div n="NI"/>7) Cedi was counted as one of the prominent kingdoms
<div n="lb"/>of ancient Bhārata. (Śloka 40, Chapter 9, Bhīṣma
<div n="lb"/>Parva).
