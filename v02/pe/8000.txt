VAṄGA¦. An important state in ancient India. The
present name of this country is Bengal. Several state-
ments occur in the Purāṇas about Vaṅga.
<div n="NI"/>The following are the statements about Vaṅga given
in the Mahābhārata.
<div n="NI"/>(i) Arjuna visited this country during his pilgrimage.
(M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 214, Stanza 9).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Bhīmasena attacked Vaṅga. (M.B. Sabhā Parva,
Chapter 30, Stanza 23).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) The Kings of Vaṅga visited Yudhiṣṭhira with
presents. (M.B. Sabhā Parva, Chapter 52, Stanza 18).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Karṇa conquered this country during his regional
conquest. (M.B. Vana Parva, Chapter 254, Stanza 8).
<div n="NI"/>(v) In the Bhārata battle the King of Vaṅga confront-
ed Ghaṭotkaca and was defeated. (M.B. Bhīṣma
Parva, Chapter 92, Stanza 6).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) Once Śrī Kṛṣṇa conquered the country of Vaṅga.
(M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter 11, Stanza 15).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) Paraśurāma exterminated the Kṣatriyas of Vaṅga.
(M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter 70, Stanza 12).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) The low castes of Vaṅga attacked the sacrificial
horse led by Arjuna who killed every one of them.
(M.B. Aśvamedha Parva, Chapter 82, Stanza 29).
<div n="NI"/>VAṄGṚDA. An asura. It is mentioned in Ṛgveda,
Maṇḍala 1, Anuvāka 1, Sūkta 10, that the Kings Ati-
thigva and Ṛjiśvā, with their armies surrounded the
cities of the asuras Karañja, Parṇaya and Vaṅgṛda and
that Indra helped the two Kings.
