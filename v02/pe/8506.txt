VIṢṆUPŪJĀ¦. (Form of worshipping Viṣṇu). There are
<div n="lb"/>conventions regarding the form of worship of Viṣṇu
<div n="lb"/>and his satellite gods. The general form of that worship
<div n="lb"/>is given below:
<div n="NI"/>Lakṣmī, Gaṅgā, Dhātā, Vidhātā, Yamunā, Navanidhis
<div n="lb"/>(nine treasures), Vāstupuruṣa, Śakti, Kūrma (turtle)
<div n="lb"/>Ananta (serpent) and Pṛthivī (Earth) are the satellites
<div n="lb"/>of Viṣṇu. First you must bow before Acyuta, (Viṣṇu)
<div n="lb"/>with all his satellites. Then worship Dharma (Virtue)
<div n="lb"/>Wisdom, self-renunciation, Supernatural power and
<div n="lb"/>Lawlessness, Ignorance, Want of Self-renunciation and
<div n="lb"/>want of supernatural powers, and then Bulb, Stalk,
<div n="lb"/>Lotus, Filament and pericarp of the lotus and, the four
<div n="lb"/>Vedas, four Yugas (ages) and the three attributes,
<div n="lb"/>Sattva (purity) Rajas (passion) and Tamas (darkness).
<div n="lb"/>After this the three Maṇdalas (regions) of Arka (the Sun)
<div n="lb"/>Soma (the Moon) and Vahni (fire). After this the nine
<div n="lb"/>powers should be worshipped. Vimalā, Utkarṣiṇī, Jñāna,
<div n="lb"/>Kriyā, Yoga, Prahva, Satya, Kānti and Īśa are the nine
<div n="lb"/>powers. In the same way, Durgā, Sarasvatī, Gaṇapati
<div n="lb"/>and Kṣetrapāla also should be worshipped. As the next
<div n="lb"/>step heart, head, lock of hair, armour, eyes and the
<div n="lb"/>weapons should be worshipped. Then worship the
<div n="lb"/>conch, the Discus the club, the lotus, the mark Śrīvatsa,
<div n="lb"/>the Kaustubha, the Vanamālā, the Earth, Guru and
<div n="lb"/>Garuḍa. With prayer and oblations offered to Indra,
<div n="lb"/>Agni Yama, Nirṛti, Varuṇa, Vāyu, Kubera, Īśa,
<div n="lb"/>Ananta and Brahmā, who are the guardians of the ten
<div n="lb"/>directions and the weapons and conveyance and
<div n="lb"/>Kumuda and the others (the four mountains standing
<div n="lb"/>near the Mahā Meru) and Viṣvaksena (Viṣṇu) Viṣṇu-
<div n="lb"/>pūjā will be finished. By performing this worship of
[Page868-b+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>Viṣṇu and his satellites, one could attain all one's wish-
<div n="lb"/>es. (Agni Purāṇa, Chapter 21)
