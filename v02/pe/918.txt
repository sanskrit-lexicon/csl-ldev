BĀLHĪKA IV¦. A son of Pratīpa, a King of the Kuru
<div n="lb"/>dynasty. He had two brothers, Devāpi and Śantanu. It
<div n="lb"/>is said in Mahābhārata, Ādi Parva, Chapter 94 that
<div n="lb"/>Sunandā, the princess of the country of Śibi was their
<div n="lb"/>mother. Mention is made in Bhāgavata, Skandha 9,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 22, Stanza 18 that this king Bālhīka had a son
<div n="lb"/>named Somadatta. Bālhīka was a friend of the Kauravas
<div n="lb"/>and the Pāṇḍavas. Bālhīka exhorted them strongly, not
<div n="lb"/>to engage in a battle. Still, when the battle was begun,
<div n="lb"/>Bālhīka sided with the Kauravas. Bālhīka was once
<div n="lb"/>elected as the general of eleven divisions of the army
<div n="lb"/>of Duryodhana.
<div n="NI"/>The achievements of Bālhīka in the battle of Kurukṣetra
<div n="lb"/>are given below:—
<div n="NI"/>1) There was a combat on the first day of the battle
<div n="lb"/>between Bālhīka and Dhṛṣtaketu. (M.B., Bhīṣma Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 45, Stanza 38).
<div n="NI"/>2) Bhīmasena defeated Bālhīka. (M.B., Bhīṣma Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 104, Stanza 26).
<div n="NI"/>3) Fought with Drupada. (Mahābhārata, Droṇa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 25, Stanza 18).
<div n="NI"/>4) Bālhīka fought with Śikhaṇḍī. (Mahābhārata, Droṇa
<div n="lb"/>Parva, Chapter 96, Stanza 7).
<div n="NI"/>5) Bhīmasena killed Bālhīka. (Droṇa Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>157, Stanza 15).
