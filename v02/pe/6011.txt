SĀLVA I¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} The lover of Ambā (q.v) the
<div n="lb"/>daughter of the King of Kāśī. It is mentioned in
<div n="lb"/>Mahābhārata, Ādi Parva, Chapter 67, Stanza 16 that
<div n="lb"/>this Kṣatriya King Sālva was born from a portion of
<div n="lb"/>Ajaka the younger brother of Vṛṣaparvā.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Sālva was the ruler of the city named Saubha.
<div n="lb"/>Mahābhārata, Ādi Parva, Chapter 102, Verse 61).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) He was present at the Svayaṃvara marriage of
<div n="lb"/>Draupadī. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 186 Verse 15).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) He was present at the sacrifice of Royal consecra-
<div n="lb"/>tion (Rājasūya) of Yudhiṣṭhira. (M.B. Sabhā Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 34, Verse 9).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) At the death of Śiśupāla, King Sālva became
<div n="lb"/>angry and besieged Dvārakā. In the battle which
<div n="lb"/>followed Śrī Kṛṣṇa killed Sālva. (M.B. Vana Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapters 15 to 22).
[Page674-b+ 64]
<div n="NI"/>(v) Sālva had several friends such as Damaghoṣa,
<div n="lb"/>Aṅga, Kaliṅga, Mālava, Kekaya, Vaṅga, Magadha,
<div n="lb"/>Kosala, Koṅkaṇa, Hehaya, Cola, Pāṇḍya, Kerala and
<div n="lb"/>others. (Bhāgavata, Skandha 10).
