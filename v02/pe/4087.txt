MĀGHA¦. A month (February). This is so called be-
<div n="lb"/>cause it is closely associated with the constellation
<div n="lb"/>Maghā. This month is between the months of Pauṣa and
<div n="lb"/>Phālguna. Mahābhārata makes some statements about
<div n="lb"/>the importance of the month of Māgha.
<div n="NI"/>(i) He who bathes at Prayāga during this month will
<div n="lb"/>be free from all sins. (Śloka 37, Chapter 25, Anuśāsana
<div n="lb"/>Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) He who gives gingelly as gift to Brahmins during
<div n="lb"/>this month will never go to hell. (Śloka 8, Chapter 66,
<div n="lb"/>Anuśāsana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) If one takes food only once a day during the whole
<div n="lb"/>of this month one will be born very rich in the next
<div n="lb"/>birth. (Śloka 31, Chapter 106, Anuśāsana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) If one worships Śrī Kṛṣṇa fasting on the Dvādaśī
<div n="lb"/>day of Māgha one will get the benefit of conducting a
<div n="lb"/>Rājasūya yajña. (Śloka 5, Chapter 109, Anuśāsana
<div n="lb"/>Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Bhīṣma expressed his desire to Kṛṣṇa to end his
<div n="lb"/>life on the aṣṭamī day falling in the bright fortnight
<div n="lb"/>of the month of Māgha. (Anuśāsana Parva, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>167, Śloka 28).
