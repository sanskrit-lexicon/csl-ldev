PRABHĀSA¦ II The sacred place called Prabhāsatīrtha.
<div n="lb"/>This place of Purāṇic importance is situated in Saurā-
<div n="lb"/>ṣṭra on the west coast. This is called Somatīrtha also.
<div n="lb"/>Some facts about this tīrtha from the Purāṇas are
<div n="lb"/>given below:
<div n="NI"/>(i) During the pilgrimage of Arjuna he did penance
<div n="lb"/>for a thousand divine years standing on one foot at Pra-
<div n="lb"/>bhāsa, Śloka 15, Chapter 12, Vana Parva).
[Page592-b+ 64]
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Agnideva resides in this place. One who bathes in
<div n="lb"/>the holy pond there would get the benefit of doing
<div n="lb"/>Agniṣṭomayajña. (Śloka 58, Chapter 82, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iii) Once Yudhiṣṭhira along with his brothers came
<div n="lb"/>and did penance here. (Śloka 15, Chapter 118, Vana
<div n="lb"/>Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(iv) Prabhāsatīrtha is dear to Indra. (Śloka 7, Chapter
<div n="lb"/>130, Vana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(v) Those who bathe in the holy pond in Prabhāsa are
<div n="lb"/>taken to heaven in Vimānas and they then by songs of
<div n="lb"/>praise wake up the celestial maidens there. (Śloka 9,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 25, Anuśāsana Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(vi) It was at this Prabhāsatīrtha that the Yādavas
<div n="lb"/>fought against one another and destroyed themselves.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 10, Chapter 3, Mausala Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(vii) Śrī Kṛṣṇa and Balarāma merged with the Supreme
<div n="lb"/>spirit at this place. (Chapter 4, Mausala Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(viii) The son of sage Sāndīpani was kidnapped by
<div n="lb"/>Pañcajana while the former was bathing at this tīrtha.
<div n="lb"/>(10th Skandha, Bhāgavata).
<div n="NI"/>(ix) It was while standing on the shore of the Prabhāsa
<div n="lb"/>pond that Arjuna first heard about Subhadrā. (10th
<div n="lb"/>Skandha, Bhāgavata).
