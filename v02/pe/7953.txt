VALA¦ (BALA). An asura. It is stated in Padma Purāṇa,
<div n="lb"/>Bhūmikhaṇḍa, how Indra killed this asura.
<div n="NI"/>One day Vala went to the sea for his evening worship.
<div n="lb"/>Devendra saw the asura, shining with the radiance
<div n="lb"/>of celibacy and the divine rod and deer-hide, praying
<div n="lb"/>on the sea-shore. Instantly Indra cut him into two with
<div n="lb"/>his weapon, the thunderbolt. Vala fell down motionless.
<div n="lb"/>Mention is made about this asura in Ṛgveda, Maṇḍala
<div n="lb"/>1, Anuvāka 4. (For further details see under Bala).
