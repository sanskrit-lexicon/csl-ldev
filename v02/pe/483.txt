ASITA I¦. One of the Ṛṭviks of the Sarpasatra of Jana-
mejaya.
<div n="NI"/>{%Some details.%} (1) The prominent Ṛtviks were Bhārgava,
Kautsa, Jaimini, Sārṅgarava, Piṅgala, Vyāsa, Uddālaka
Pramattaka, Śvetaketu, Asita, Devala, Nārada, Parvata,
Ātreya, Kuṇḍajāra, Kālaghāṭs, Vātsya, Kohala, Deva-
śarmā, Maudgalya, Samasaurabha. (Chapter 53, Ādi
Parva, M.B.)
<div n="NI"/>(2) Asita, Devala, Vaiśampāyana, Sumantu and Jaimini
were disciples of Vyāsa. (Prathama Skandha, Devī
Bhāgavata).
<div n="NI"/>(3) Asita, the sage, got by the blessing of Śiva a son
named Devala. (Brahmavaivarta Purāṇa).
<div n="NI"/>(4) Once Asita muni explained to King Janaka the
philosophy of rebirth. (Brahmāṇḍa Purāṇa, Chapter 47).
