KAKṢASENA I¦.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%General information.%} (i) A King who was the grandson
[Page366-b+ 65]
<div n="lb"/>of Avīkṣit and son of Parīkṣit. For genealogy see
<div n="lb"/>‘Parīkṣit’.
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Ugrasena, Citrasena, Indrasena, Suṣeṇa and
<div n="lb"/>Bhīmasena were his brothers. (Chapter 94, Ādi Parva).
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other details.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) Kakṣasena was a member of the court of Yama.
<div n="lb"/>(Chapter 8, Sabhā Parva).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Kakṣasena was considered to be one among the
<div n="lb"/>pious souls, like Śibi, Pratardana, Rantideva, Amba-
<div n="lb"/>rīṣa, Janamejaya, Vṛṣādarbhi, Paraśurāma, Śrī Rāma,
<div n="lb"/>Karandhama and Mitrasaha who had attained salva-
<div n="lb"/>tion by their charity and righteousness. Mahābhārata
<div n="lb"/>states that Kakṣasena the saintly king attained
<div n="lb"/>salvation by presenting gifts to the great Vasiṣṭha.
<div n="lb"/>(Śloka 14, Chapter 137, Anuśāsana Parva).
