SĀLVA II¦. There is a story in Mahābhārata, Ādi Parva,
<div n="lb"/>stating that three Sālvas and four Madras were born
<div n="lb"/>to Bhadrā from the dead body of her husband Vyuṣi-
<div n="lb"/>tāśva. (See under Bhadrā II).
<div n="NI"/>Here, ‘Sālvas’ means the rulers of Sālva. It is stated in
<div n="lb"/>Mahābhārata, Sabhā Parva, Chapter 14, Stanza 26 that
<div n="lb"/>the people of Sālva fled to the south in fear of Jarā-
<div n="lb"/>sandha. Satyavān, the husband of Sāvitrī, was a native
<div n="lb"/>of Sālva. It is also mentioned that the soldiers of Sālva
<div n="lb"/>who joined the army of the Kauravas fought against
<div n="lb"/>the Pāṇḍavas. There were Sālva on the side of the
<div n="lb"/>Pāṇḍavas also. Mention is made in Droṇa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 154, that the Sālvas on the side of the Pāṇḍavas,
<div n="lb"/>attacked Droṇa, the teacher.
