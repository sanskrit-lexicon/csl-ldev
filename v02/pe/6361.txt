ŚĀSTĀ¦. The presiding deity (idol installed) in the
<div n="lb"/>Śabarimala temple.
<div n="NI"/>1) {%Birth.%} Śiva fell in love with Mahāviṣṇu in his
<div n="lb"/>assumed form as Mohinī and Śāstā was the result of
<div n="lb"/>their union. (Kambarāmāyaṇa, Bālakāṇḍa). This story
<div n="lb"/>occurs in the 8th Skandha of Bhāgavata and the Asura
<div n="lb"/>kāṇḍa of Skanda Purāṇa, but only the Skanda
<div n="lb"/>Purāṇa refers to the child by name Śāstā.
<div n="NI"/>2) {%Other information.%}
<div n="NI"/>(i) In the battle between Indra and the asura called
<div n="lb"/>Śūrapadma the former deputed Śāstā for the protection
<div n="lb"/>of Śacīdevī. (Skanda Purāṇa, Asura Kāṇḍa).
<div n="NI"/>(ii) Śāstā is supposed to have two wives called Purāṇā
<div n="lb"/>and Puṣkalā and a son called Satyaka. (Aṣṭottaraśa-
<div n="lb"/>takam about Śāstā; also see under Śabarimala).
