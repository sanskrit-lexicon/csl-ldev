SATYAJIT II¦. A brother of Drupada, the King of
<div n="lb"/>Pāñcāla. He appears in the story of Mahābhārata for
<div n="lb"/>the first time at the battle between Drupada and Arjuna.
<div n="lb"/>When the education of the Kauravas and the Pāṇḍavas
<div n="lb"/>was over, Droṇa asked his pupils to bring Drupada tied
<div n="lb"/>as teacher's gift. An attempt was made to bring
<div n="lb"/>Drupada before Droṇa, in which attempt Drupada and
<div n="lb"/>Arjuna confronted each other. In this fight it was
<div n="lb"/>Satyajit who stood as the right hand of his brother
<div n="lb"/>Drupada. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 137, Verse 42).
<div n="lb"/>Arjuna defeated this Satyajit. Immediately Satyajit ran
<div n="lb"/>away from the battle-field.
<div n="NI"/>After the marriage of Pāñcālī, Pāṇḍavas and Drupada
<div n="lb"/>became relatives. With this Satyajit also came to be on
<div n="lb"/>good relation with the Pāṇḍavas. In the battle of
<div n="lb"/>Bhārata this Satyajit was the bodyguard of Yudhiṣṭhira.
<div n="lb"/>(M.B. Droṇa Parva, Chapter 17). In the battle which
<div n="lb"/>followed Droṇa killed Satyajit. (M.B. Karṇa Parva,
<div n="lb"/>Chapter 6, Verse 4).
