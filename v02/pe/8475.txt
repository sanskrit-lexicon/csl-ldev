VIŚĀLA¦. A son of Ikṣvāku. Mention is made in Vālmīki
<div n="lb"/>Rāmāyaṇa, Bālakāṇḍa, Sarga 47, that a son named
<div n="lb"/>Viśāla was born to Ikṣvāku, by the celestial maid
<div n="lb"/>Alambuṣā and that Viśāla built a city named Viśālā.
<div n="lb"/>This Viśāla had a son named Hemacandra. It was
<div n="lb"/>near the city Viśālā, that Ahalyā, the wife of Gautama,
[Page863-b+ 64]
<div n="lb"/>stood as stone, because of a curse. (See under Viśāla-
<div n="lb"/>purī).
