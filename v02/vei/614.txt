<L>614<pc>1-157<k1>किरात<k2>किरात<h>1
1. {@Kirāta@}¦ is a name applied to a people living in the caves
<div n="lb"/>of the mountains, as appears clearly from the dedication of the
<div n="lb"/>Kirāta to the caves ({%guhā%}) in the Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā,<sup>1</sup> and
<div n="lb"/>from the reference in the Atharvaveda<sup>2</sup> to a Kirāta girl ({%kairā-%}
<div n="lb"/>{%tikā%}), who digs a remedy on the ridges of the mountains.
[Page1-158+ 54]
<div n="lb"/>Later<sup>3</sup> the people called Kirātas were located in Eastern Nepal,
<div n="lb"/>but the name seems to have been applied to any hill folk, no
<div n="lb"/>doubt aborigines, though the Mānava Dharma Śāstra<sup>4</sup> regards
<div n="lb"/>them as degraded Kṣatriyas.
