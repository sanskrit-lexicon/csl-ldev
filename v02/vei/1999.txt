<L>1999<pc>2-033<k1>प्रतीपप्रातिसत्वन<k2>प्रतीपप्रातिसत्वन
{@Pratīpa Prātisatvana,@}¦<sup>1</sup> or {@Prātisutvana,@}<sup>2</sup> is the name of a
<div n="lb"/>man mentioned in a hymn of the Atharvaveda.<sup>2</sup> Zimmer,<sup>3</sup> with
<div n="lb"/>great ingenuity, compares the fact that {@Parikṣit@} is mentioned
<div n="lb"/>as a {@Kuru@} king in the Atharvaveda,<sup>4</sup> and that, according to the
<div n="lb"/>Epic genealogies, his grandson was Pratiśravas, with which
<div n="lb"/>name Prātisutvana, as very possibly a Prākritized version of
<div n="lb"/>Prātiśrutvana may be compared, and his great-grandson was
<div n="lb"/>Pratīpa. The identification cannot, however, be regarded as at
<div n="lb"/>all certain, and while the Epic may have derived its genealogy
<div n="lb"/>from the Atharvaveda, it may have preserved an independent
<div n="lb"/>tradition. Böhtlingk<sup>5</sup> renders {%prātisatvanam%} as ‘in the direction
<div n="lb"/>opposed to the Satvans’, and this may be right.
