<L>583<pc>1-149<k1>काम्पील<k2>काम्पील
{@Kāmpīla.@}¦ — In one passage of the Yajurveda Saṃhitās<sup>1</sup> the
<div n="lb"/>epithet Kāmpīla-vāsinī is applied to a woman, perhaps the
<div n="lb"/>king's Mahiṣī or chief wife, whose duty it was to sleep beside
<div n="lb"/>the slaughtered animal at the horse sacrifice (Aśvamedha).
<div n="lb"/>The exact interpretation of the passage is very uncertain, but
<div n="lb"/>both Weber<sup>2</sup> and Zimmer<sup>3</sup> agree in regarding Kāmpīla as the
<div n="lb"/>name of the town known as Kāmpīlya in the later literature,
<div n="lb"/>and the capital of Pañcāla in Madhyadeśa.
