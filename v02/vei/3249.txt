{@Śārṅga.@}¦ — The Anukramaṇī<sup>1</sup> (Index) of the Rigveda ascribes
a hymn of that text<sup>2</sup> to the Śārṅgas, Jaritṛ, Droṇa, Sārisṛkva,
and Stambamitra. The Mahābhārata<sup>3</sup> contains a tale describing
how the four Śārṅgas, sons of the Ṛṣi Mandapāla, were saved
from the great fire in the Khāṇḍava forest by means of
prayers. Sieg<sup>4</sup> has attempted to use this tale for the elucida-
tion of the hymn in question, but without substantial success.
As Oldenberg<sup>5</sup> says, the tale is based on the hymn rather than
{%vice versa.%}
