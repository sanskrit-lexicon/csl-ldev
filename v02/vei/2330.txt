{@Madugha,@}¦ ‘honey-plant,’<sup>1</sup> is the name of a sweet herb in the
Atharvaveda.<sup>2</sup> The spelling is somewhat uncertain, since
many manuscripts read Madhugha.<sup>3</sup>
<F>1) The literal meaning is probably
‘yielding honey,’ the word being, ac-

<F>2) i. 34, 4; vi. 102, 3. {%Cf.%} Weber,
{%Indische Studien,%} 5, 386, n.; 404;
Whitney, Translation of the Atharva-
veda, 34, 35, 355; Bloomfield, {%Hymns%}
{%of the Atharvaveda,%} 275; Zimmer, {%Altin-%}
{%disches Leben,%} 69.</F>
<F>3) These two forms probably stand
by haplology for {%ma[dhu]-dugha%} and
{%madhu-[du]gha. Cf.%} Macdonell, {%Vedic%}
