<L>3515<pc>2-439<k1>सलिलवात<k2>सलिलवात
{@Salila-vāta@}¦ occurs in the Yajurveda Saṃhitās<sup>1</sup> as an adjec-
tive meaning ‘favoured with a wind from the water.’<sup>2</sup> It
probably refers to the wind from the ocean, the south-west
monsoon.<sup>3</sup>
<F>1) Taittirīya Saṃhitā, iv. 4, 12, 3;
Kāṭhaka Saṃhitā, xxiv. 4; Maitrāyaṇī
Saṃhitā, iii. 16, 4.</F>
<F>2) Or, according to the commentator,
{%salilākhyena vāta-viśeṣeṇa anugṛhītaḥ,%}
‘favoured by a kind of wind called
Salila.’</F>
<F>3) {%Indian Empire,%} 1, 110. The mon-
soon is little noted in the Vedic texts,
except in so far as the Marut hymns
may be deemed to be a description of
the monsoon. See Rv. i. 19, 7; 37,
6 {%et seq.;%} 38, 8; 64, 8; 88, 5; v. 83,
1 {%et seq.;%} 85, 4; Zimmer, {%Altindisches%}
{%Leben,%} 42-44.</F>
