<L>612<pc>1-157<k1>किंपुरुष<k2>किंपुरुष
{@Kiṃ-puruṣa,@}¦ lit. ‘what sort of man,’ appears in the Brāh-
maṇas<sup>1</sup> to designate the ‘ape,’ which is a mimic man. Possibly
the same sense should be seen in the passage of the Vāja-
saneyi Saṃhitā,<sup>2</sup> where it occurs, and where Roth<sup>3</sup> assumes it
to refer to a contemptible man. Max Müller<sup>4</sup> renders it
‘savage.’
