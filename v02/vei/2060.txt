{@Prācya@}¦ denotes in the plural ‘dwellers in the east.’ They
<div n="lb"/>are mentioned in the list of peoples in the Aitareya Brāhmaṇa.<sup>1</sup>
<div n="lb"/>It is very probable that the {@Kāśis, Kosalas, Videhas,@} and
<div n="lb"/>perhaps {@Magadhas,@} are meant, as Oldenberg<sup>2</sup> supposes. In
<div n="lb"/>the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa<sup>3</sup> the Easterns are said to call Agni
<div n="lb"/>by the name of Śarva, and their mode of making tombs is
<div n="lb"/>there<sup>4</sup> referred to with disapproval. The Lāṭyāyana Śrauta
<div n="lb"/>Sūtra<sup>5</sup> explains the {@Vipatha,@} ‘rough car,’ of the Pañcaviṃśa
<div n="lb"/>Brāhmaṇa<sup>6</sup> as a car of the Easterns ({%prācya-ratha%}). In the
<div n="lb"/>Saṃhitopaniṣad Brāhmaṇa<sup>7</sup> reference is made to the Prācya-
<div n="lb"/>Pāñcālas.
