{@Tura Kāvaṣeya@}¦ is mentioned in the Vaṃśa (list of teachers)
<div n="lb"/>at the end of the tenth book of the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa<sup>1</sup> as
<div n="lb"/>the source of the doctrine set forth in that book, and as
<div n="lb"/>separated, in the succession of teachers, from {@Śāṇḍilya@} by
<div n="lb"/>{@Yajñavacas@} and {@Kuśri.@} In the same Brāhmaṇa<sup>2</sup> he is quoted
<div n="lb"/>by Śāṇḍilya as having erected a fire-altar on the {@Kārotī.@} In
<div n="lb"/>the Aitareya Brāhmaṇa<sup>3</sup> he appears as a Purohita, or ‘domestic
<div n="lb"/>priest,’ of {@Janamejaya Pārikṣita,@} whom he consecrated king.
<div n="lb"/>In the Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upaniṣad<sup>4</sup> and a Khila<sup>5</sup> he appears as
