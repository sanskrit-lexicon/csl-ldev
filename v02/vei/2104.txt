<L>2104<pc>2-055<k1>प्लक्षप्रास्रवण<k2>प्लक्षप्रास्रवण<h>3
3. {@Plakṣa Prā-sravaṇa@}¦ is the name of a locality, forty-four
<div n="lb"/>days’ journey from the spot where the {@Sarasvatī@} disappears.
<div n="lb"/>It is mentioned in the Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa<sup>1</sup> and the
<div n="lb"/>Jaiminīya Upaniṣad Brāhmaṇa.<sup>2</sup> In the latter text it is said
<div n="lb"/>that the middle of the earth is only a span ({@Prādeśa@}) to the
<div n="lb"/>north of it. In the Rigveda Sūtras<sup>3</sup> the locality is called
<div n="lb"/>Plākṣa Prasravaṇa, and is apparently meant to designate the
<div n="lb"/>source of the Sarasvatī rather than the place of its reappearance.

<div n="lb"/>Sūtra, xxiv. 6, 7; Lāṭyāyana Śrauta
<div n="lb"/>Sūtra, x. 17, 12. 14.</F>
<F>2) iv. 26, 12.</F>
<F>3) Āśvalāyana Śrauta Sūtra, xii. 6,
<div n="lb"/>1; Śāṅkhāyana Śrauta Sūtra, xiii. 29,
