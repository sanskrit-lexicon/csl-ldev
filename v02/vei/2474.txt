<L>2474<pc>2-156<k1>माष<k2>माष
{@Māṣa@}¦ is the name of a kind of bean ({%Phaseolus radiatus%}) in
the Atharvaveda<sup>1</sup> and later.<sup>2</sup> It is still one of the most valuable
of similar plants in India. The seeds<sup>3</sup> were pounded ({%piṣṭa%})
according to the Atharvaveda.<sup>4</sup> These beans ripened in the
winter ({%hemanta%}).<sup>5</sup> In the ritual the human head for the
sacrifice is bought for twenty-one Māṣas<sup>6</sup> : it does not appear
that the word here means a weight of metal, as it often does
later.<sup>7</sup> A taboo on beans is found in the Yajurveda Saṃhitās.<sup>8</sup>
