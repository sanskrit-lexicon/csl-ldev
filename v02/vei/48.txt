{@Atri.@}¦ — Neither Atri himself nor the Atris can claim any
<div n="lb"/>historical reality,<sup>1</sup> beyond the fact that Maṇḍala V. of the Rigveda
<div n="lb"/>is attributed, no doubt correctly, to the family of the Atris.<sup>2</sup>
<div n="lb"/>The Atris as a family probably stood in close relations with the
<div n="lb"/>{@Priyamedhas@}<sup>3</sup> and {@Kaṇvas,@}<sup>4</sup> perhaps also with the {@Gotamas@}<sup>5</sup>
<div n="lb"/>and {@Kākṣīvatas.@}<sup>6</sup> The mention of both the {@Paruṣṇī@} and the
<div n="lb"/>{@Yamunā@} in one hymn<sup>7</sup> of the fifth Maṇḍala seems to justify the
<div n="lb"/>presumption that the family was spread over a wide extent of
<div n="lb"/>territory.
