{@Aśva@}¦ is the commonest word for ‘horse’ in the Vedic
<div n="lb"/>literature. The horse is also called ‘the runner’ ({%atya%}), ‘the
<div n="lb"/>swift’ ({%arvant%}), ‘the strong,’ for pulling ({%vājin%}), ‘the runner’
<div n="lb"/>({%sapti%}), and ‘the speeding’ ({%haya%}). The mare is termed {%aśvā,%}
<div n="lb"/>{%atyā, arvatī, vaḍavā,%} etc. Horses of various colour were known,
<div n="lb"/>dun ({%harita, hari%}), ruddy ({%aruṇa, aruṣa, piśaṅga, rohita%}), dark
<div n="lb"/>brown ({%śyāva%}), white ({%śveta%}), etc. A white horse with black
<div n="lb"/>ears is mentioned in the Atharvaveda as of special value.<sup>1</sup>
<div n="lb"/>Horses were highly prized,<sup>2</sup> and were not rare, as Roth<sup>3</sup>
<div n="lb"/>thought, for as many as four hundred mares are mentioned in
<div n="lb"/>one Dānastuti (‘Praise of Gifts’).<sup>4</sup> They were on occasion
<div n="lb"/>ornamented with pearls and gold.<sup>5</sup>
<div n="P"/>Mares were preferred for drawing chariots because of their
<div n="lb"/>swiftness and sureness.<sup>6</sup> They were also used for drawing
<div n="lb"/>carts, but were not ordinarily so employed.<sup>7</sup> No mention is
<div n="lb"/>made of riding in battle, but for other purposes it was not
<div n="lb"/>unknown.<sup>8</sup>
<div n="P"/>Horses were often kept in stalls,<sup>9</sup> and fed there.<sup>10</sup> But they
<div n="lb"/>were also allowed to go out to grass,<sup>11</sup> and were then hobbled.<sup>12</sup>
<div n="lb"/>They were watered to cool them after racing.<sup>13</sup> Their attendants
[Page1-043+ 59]
<div n="lb"/>are frequently referred to ({%aśva-pāla,%}<sup>14</sup> {%aśva-pa,%}<sup>15</sup> {%aśva-pati%}).<sup>16</sup>
<div n="lb"/>Stallions were frequently castrated ({%vadhri%}).<sup>17</sup>
<div n="P"/>Besides reins ({%raśmayaḥ%}), reference is made to halters
<div n="lb"/>({%aśvābhidhānī%}),<sup>18</sup> and whips ({%aśvājani%}).<sup>19</sup> See also {@Ratha.@}
<div n="P"/>Horses from the Indus were of special value,<sup>20</sup> as also horses
<div n="lb"/>from the {@Sarasvatī.@}
