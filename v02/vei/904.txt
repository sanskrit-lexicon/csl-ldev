<L>904<pc>1-238<k1>गोमती<k2>गोमती
{@Go-matī,@}¦ ‘possessing cows,’ is mentioned as a river in the
<div n="lb"/>Nadī-stuti, or ‘Praise of Rivers,’ in the tenth Maṇḍala of the
<div n="lb"/>Rigveda.<sup>1</sup> In that hymn a river flowing into the Indus must
<div n="lb"/>be meant, and its identification<sup>2</sup> with the Gomal, a western
<div n="lb"/>tributary of the Indus, cannot be doubted. In one other passage
<div n="lb"/>of the Rigveda<sup>3</sup> the accentuation of Gomati shows that a river is
<div n="lb"/>meant. It is possible that in a third passage<sup>4</sup> the reading
<div n="lb"/>should be changed to {%gomatír%} from {%gómatīr.%} Geldner<sup>5</sup> suggests
<div n="lb"/>that in the two last passages the Gumti, or rather its four
<div n="lb"/>upper arms (hence the use of the plural) is meant: this accords
<div n="lb"/>well with the later use of the name and with the general
<div n="lb"/>probability of the river here intended being in {@Kurukṣetra,@} as
<div n="lb"/>the centre of Vedic civilization.<sup>6</sup>
