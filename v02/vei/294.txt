<L>294<pc>1-072<k1>आसन्दीवन्त्<k2>आसन्दीवन्त्
{@Āsandī-vant,@}¦ ‘possessing the throne,’ is the title of the royal
city of {@Janamejaya Pārikṣita,@} in which the horse, for his famous
Aśvamedha, was bound. The authorities both cite a Gāthā for
the fact, but they differ as to the priest who celebrated the rite.
In the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa<sup>1</sup> he is stated to have been Indrota
Daivāpa Śaunaka, but in the Aitareya<sup>2</sup> {@Tura Kāvaṣeya.@}
<F>1) xiii. 5, 4, 2.</F>
<F>2) viii. 21. {%Cf.%} Śāṅkhāyana Śrauta
Sūtra, xvi. 9, 1. Pāṇini, viii. 2, 12,
knows the name.</F>
