<L>3026<pc>2-319<k1>वृचीवन्त्<k2>वृचीवन्त्
{@Vṛcīvant@}¦ is the name of a tribe referred to once in the
<div n="lb"/>Rigveda,<sup>1</sup> where it is clearly stated that the {@Sṛñjaya@} king,
<div n="lb"/>{@Daivavāta,@} conquered the {@Turvaśa@} king and the Vṛcīvants.
<div n="lb"/>Zimmer<sup>2</sup> thinks that the Vṛcīvants and the Turvaśa people
<div n="lb"/>should be identified, but this is both unnecessary and improb-
<div n="lb"/>able; it is adequate to assume that they were allied against<sup>3</sup>
<div n="lb"/>the Sṛñjayas. The Vṛcīvants appear again only in the strange
<div n="lb"/>legend in the Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa,<sup>4</sup> according to which the
<div n="lb"/>{@Jahnus@} and the Vṛcīvants contended for sovereignty, {@Viśvā-@}
<div n="lb"/>{@mitra,@} the Jahnu king, winning it by his knowledge of a
<div n="lb"/>certain rite. See also {@Hariyūpīyā.@}
