<L>1002<pc>1-268<k1>जङ्गिड<k2>जङ्गिड
{@Jaṅgiḍa@}¦ is the name of a healing plant mentioned in the
hymns of the Atharvaveda.<sup>1</sup> It was used as an amulet against
the diseases, or symptoms of disease, {@Takman, Balāsa,@}
{@Āśarīka, Viśarīka, Pṛṣṭyāmaya,@}<sup>2</sup> fevers and rheumatic pains,
{@Viṣkandha@} and {@Saṃskandha,@}<sup>3</sup> {@Jambha,@} and so on. But it is
also regarded as a specific against all diseases, and as the best
of healing powers.<sup>4</sup> It is said to be produced from the juices
({%rasa%}) of ploughing ({%kṛṣi%}),<sup>5</sup> but this need only mean that it grew
in cultivated land, not that it was itself cultivated. What plant
the name designates is quite uncertain, for it disappears in the
later literature. Caland<sup>6</sup> takes it in the Kauśika Sūtra to be
the {%Terminalia arjuneya.%}
