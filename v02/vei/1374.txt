<L>1374<pc>1-377<k1>देवातिथिकाण्व<k2>देवातिथिकाण्व
{@Devātithi Kāṇva@}¦ (‘descendant of {@Kaṇva@}’) is mentioned in
the Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa<sup>1</sup> as the seer of a Sāman (chant) by
which he turned pumpkins into cows for himself and his son
when they were starving in the desert, whither they had been
driven by rivals. He is also the reputed author of a hymn
of the Rigveda.<sup>2</sup>
