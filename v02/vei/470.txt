{@Aukṣa-gandhi@}¦ (‘having the smell of bull's grease’) appears
<div n="lb"/>in the Atharvaveda<sup>1</sup> as the name of an Apsaras, beside other
<div n="lb"/>names, of which {@Guggulū@} and {@Naladī@} clearly indicate plants.
<div n="lb"/>This name, therefore, presumably also denotes some sort of
<div n="lb"/>fragrant plant. Aukṣa in the same Saṃhitā<sup>2</sup> means ‘bull's
<div n="lb"/>grease’ (from {%akṣan,%} ‘bull’).
<F>1) iv. 37. 3.</F>
<F>2) ii. 36. 7.
