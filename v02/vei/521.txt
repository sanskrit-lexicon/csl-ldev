{@Kamboja. — Yāska,@}¦ in the Nirukta,<sup>1</sup> refers to the speech of
<div n="lb"/>the Kambojas as differing from that of the other Āryas. The
<div n="lb"/>Kambojas were later settled to the north-west of the Indus, and
<div n="lb"/>are known as Kambujiya in the old Persian inscriptions. A
<div n="lb"/>teacher, {@Kāmboja Aupamanyava,@} pupil of {@Madragāra,@} is
<div n="lb"/>mentioned in the Vaṃśa Brāhmaṇa.<sup>2</sup> This points to a possible
<div n="lb"/>connexion of the Madras, or more probably the {@Uttara Madras,@}
<div n="lb"/>with the Kambojas, who probably had Iranian as well as Indian
<div n="lb"/>affinities.
