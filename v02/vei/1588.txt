{@Ni-ṣaṅgin@}¦ is found three times in the Rigveda,<sup>1</sup> where,
<div n="lb"/>owing to its following in one passage<sup>2</sup> the words {%sudhanvāna%}
<div n="lb"/>{%iṣumanto,%} ‘having good bows and arrows,’ the sense of
<div n="lb"/>{%niṣaṅginaḥ%} as ‘having quivers’ seems quite certain. In the
<div n="lb"/>Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā<sup>3</sup> it is taken by the commentator Mahīdhara
<div n="lb"/>to mean ‘having a sword,’ a sense which is quite possible both
<div n="lb"/>there and in the other passages<sup>4</sup> where it occurs. But the
<div n="lb"/>word far more probably means ‘having a quiver,’ for the bow
<div n="lb"/>was the Vedic weapon, not the sword ({@Asi@}).
