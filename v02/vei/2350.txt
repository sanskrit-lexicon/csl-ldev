{@Manu@}¦ in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> and later<sup>2</sup> has no claim to historical
<div n="lb"/>reality. He is simply the first man, father of the race, and its
<div n="lb"/>guide in all matters, sacrificial and other. Hence the views of
<div n="lb"/>the texts on inheritance are foisted on Manu and his youngest
<div n="lb"/>son, {@Nābhānediṣṭha.@}<sup>3</sup> He also plays the part of the hero in the
<div n="lb"/>Vedic legend of the flood.<sup>4</sup>
