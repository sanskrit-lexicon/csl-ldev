<L>303<pc>1-074<k1>आस्राव<k2>आस्राव
{@Ā-srāva@}¦ (‘discharge’) denotes a disease, thrice referred to in
the Atharvaveda,<sup>1</sup> the precise nature of which is uncertain.
The Scholiast<sup>2</sup> in one place interprets it as painful urination
({%mūtrātisāra%}), while Lanman<sup>3</sup> suggests diabetes. Bloomfield<sup>4</sup>
takes it to be diarrhœa, and Zimmer<sup>5</sup> argues that as the remedy
is called ‘wound healer’ ({%arus-srāṇa%}), the sense is ‘the flux from
unhealed wounds.’ Whitney<sup>6</sup> renders it ‘flux,’ and questions
Bloomfield's rendering. Ludwig<sup>7</sup> translates it vaguely by ‘sick-
ness’ and ‘cold.’
