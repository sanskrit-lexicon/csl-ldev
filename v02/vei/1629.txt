<L>1629<pc>1-463<k1>न्यङ्कु<k2>न्यङ्कु
{@Nyaṅku@}¦ is the name of an animal in the list of victims at the
Aśvamedha, or ‘horse sacrifice,’ in the Yajurveda Saṃhitās.<sup>1</sup>
It seems clear that a kind a ‘gazelle’ is meant, but the com-
mentary on the Taittirīya Saṃhitā<sup>1</sup> suggests ‘bear’ ({%ṛkṣa%}) as
a variant rendering.
<F>1) Taittirīya Saṃhitā, v. 5, 17, 1;
Maitrāyaṇī Saṃhitā, iii. 14, 9; Vāja-
saneyi Saṃhitā, xxiv. 27. 32. {%Cf.%}
Zimmer, {%Altindisches Leben,%} 83. The
metre {%nyaṅku-sāriṇī,%} ‘with a gazelle's
gait,’ is already mentioned in the
Ṛgveda Prātiśākhya, xvi. 31; Chandas,
5; Nidāna Sūtra, i. 2.</F>
