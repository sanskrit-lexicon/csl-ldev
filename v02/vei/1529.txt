1. {@Nāka@}¦ denotes the ‘firmament’ in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> and later.<sup>2</sup>
<div n="lb"/>It is often used with the epithet ‘highest’ ({%uttama%})<sup>3</sup> or ‘third’
<div n="lb"/>({%tṛtīya%})<sup>4</sup> referring to the threefold division of heaven, parallel
<div n="lb"/>to the threefold division of earth, atmosphere, and sky ({@Div@}).
<div n="lb"/>The Nāka is said to be on the third ridge ({%pṛṣṭha%}), above the
<div n="lb"/>luminous space ({%rocana%}) of the sky.<sup>5</sup> Elsewhere<sup>6</sup> the series
<div n="lb"/>earth, atmosphere, sky, and the firmament ({%nāka%}), heaven
<div n="lb"/>({%svar%}), the celestial light ({%jyotis%}), occurs. The word {%nāka%}
<div n="lb"/>is explained in the Brāhmaṇas<sup>7</sup> as derived from {%na,%} ‘not,’
<div n="lb"/>and {%aka,%} ‘pain,’ because those who go there are free from
<div n="lb"/>sorrow.
