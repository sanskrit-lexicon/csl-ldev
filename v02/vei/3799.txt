{@Hāyana@}¦ denotes a ‘year,’ usually in compounds.<sup>1</sup> In the
Kāṭhaka Saṃhitā<sup>2</sup> and the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa<sup>3</sup> the term
appears as a designation of a species of red rice. As an adjec-
tive in the sense of ‘lasting a year’ or ‘recurring every year,’
it is applied to fever in the Atharvaveda.<sup>4</sup>
<F>1) Av. viii. 2, 21; {%śata-hāyana,%} ‘a
hundred years old,’ viii. 2, 8; 7, 22;
{%hāyanī,%} xii. 1, 36 (probably corrupt).</F>
<F>2) xv. 5.</F>
<F>3) v. 3, 3, 6 (the Taittirīya Saṃhitā,
i. 8, 10, 1, has instead {%mahā-vrīhi,%}
‘great rice’).</F>
<F>4) xix. 39, 10.
