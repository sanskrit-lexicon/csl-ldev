<L>3227<pc>2-370<k1>शाट्यायन<k2>शाट्यायन
{@Śāṭyāyana,@}¦ ‘descendant of Śāṭya,’ is the patronymic of a
teacher mentioned twice in the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa<sup>1</sup> and
often in the Jaiminīya Upaniṣad Brāhmaṇa.<sup>2</sup> In a Vaṃśa
(list of teachers) in the latter work<sup>3</sup> he is called a pupil of
{@Jvālāyana,@} while in the Vaṃśa at the end of the Sāmavidhāna
Brāhmaṇa he appears as a pupil of {@Bādarāyaṇa.@} The Śāṭyā-
yanins, his followers, are frequently mentioned in the Sūtras,<sup>4</sup>
the Śāṭyāyani Brāhmaṇa<sup>5</sup> and the Śāṭyāyanaka<sup>6</sup> being also
referred to in them. It has been shown by Oertel<sup>7</sup> that this
Brāhmaṇa bore a close resemblance to, and probably belonged
to the same period as, the Jaiminīya Brāhmaṇa.
