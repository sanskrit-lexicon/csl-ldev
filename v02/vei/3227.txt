{@Śāṭyāyana,@}¦ ‘descendant of Śāṭya,’ is the patronymic of a
<div n="lb"/>teacher mentioned twice in the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa<sup>1</sup> and
<div n="lb"/>often in the Jaiminīya Upaniṣad Brāhmaṇa.<sup>2</sup> In a Vaṃśa
<div n="lb"/>(list of teachers) in the latter work<sup>3</sup> he is called a pupil of
<div n="lb"/>{@Jvālāyana,@} while in the Vaṃśa at the end of the Sāmavidhāna
<div n="lb"/>Brāhmaṇa he appears as a pupil of {@Bādarāyaṇa.@} The Śāṭyā-
<div n="lb"/>yanins, his followers, are frequently mentioned in the Sūtras,<sup>4</sup>
<div n="lb"/>the Śāṭyāyani Brāhmaṇa<sup>5</sup> and the Śāṭyāyanaka<sup>6</sup> being also
<div n="lb"/>referred to in them. It has been shown by Oertel<sup>7</sup> that this
<div n="lb"/>Brāhmaṇa bore a close resemblance to, and probably belonged
<div n="lb"/>to the same period as, the Jaiminīya Brāhmaṇa.
