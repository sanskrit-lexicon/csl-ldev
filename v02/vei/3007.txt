<L>3007<pc>2-315<k1>विसल्य<k2>विसल्य
{@Visalya@}¦<sup>1</sup> and {@Visalyaka@}<sup>2</sup> are names of a disease in the
Atharvaveda. Since Shaṅkar Paṇḍit's reciters<sup>3</sup> pronounced
the word as Visalpaka in all the passages, that should probably
be adopted as the right reading.<sup>4</sup> Some sort of pain is meant,
perhaps ‘neuralgia,’ in connexion with fever.
<F>1) ix. 8, 20.</F>
<F>2) vi. 127, 1 {%et seq.;%} ix. 8, 2. 5;
xix. 44, 2.</F>
<F>3) See Bloomfield, {%Hymns of the Athar-%}
{%vaveda,%} 601; Whitney, Translation of
the Atharvaveda, 376. {%Cf.%} Zimmer,
{%Altindisches Leben,%} 378, 384.</F>
<F>4) The commentator Sāyaṇa on vi. 127
reads {%visalpakaḥ,%} and on xix. 44, 2,
{%visarpakaḥ.%}</F>
