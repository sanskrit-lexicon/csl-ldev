<L>1051<pc>1-284<k1>जामातृ<k2>जामातृ
{@Jāmātṛ@}¦ is a rare word denoting ‘son-in-law’ in the Rigveda,<sup>1</sup>
where also occurs the word {@Vijāmātṛ,@} denoting an ‘unsatis-
factory son-in-law,’ as one who does not pay a sufficient price,
or one who, having other defects, must purchase a bride.
Friendly relations between son-in-law and father-in-law are
referred to in the Rigveda.<sup>2</sup>
<F>1) viii. 2, 20. Vāyu is called the
{%jāmātṛ%} of Tvaṣṭṛ in viii. 26, 21. 22.
