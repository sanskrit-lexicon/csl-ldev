<L>1830<pc>1-532<k1>पिप्पलाद<k2>पिप्पलाद
{@Pippalāda@}¦ (‘eater of berries’) is the name of a teacher
mentioned in the Praśna Upaniṣad.<sup>1</sup> In the plural the name
denotes a school of the Atharvaveda.<sup>2</sup> Their (Paippalāda)
recension of the text of the Saṃhitā has been reproduced in
facsimile by Garbe and Bloomfield,<sup>3</sup> and in part published.<sup>4</sup>
<F>1) i. 1.</F>
<F>2) Weber, {%Indische Studien,%} 3, 277;
{%Indian Literature,%} 153, 159, 160, 164.</F>
<F>3) Baltimore, 1901.</F>
<F>4) The variants of the Paippalāda are
given in part in Whitney's Translation
of the Atharvaveda, and the text of
books i. and ii. has been edited by
Barret in the {%Journal of the American%}
{%Oriental Society,%} 26, 197-295; 30, 187 {%et%}
{%seq. Cf.%} also Lanman in Whitney's
Translation, lxxix {%et seq.%}</F>
