<L>899<pc>1-237<k1>गोधूम<k2>गोधूम
{@Go-dhūma,@}¦ ‘wheat,’ is frequently referred to in the plural in
the Yajurveda Saṃhitās<sup>1</sup> and Brāhmaṇas,<sup>2</sup> and is expressly
distinguished from ‘rice’ ({@Vrīhi@}) or ‘barley’ ({@Yava@}).<sup>3</sup> ‘Groats’
({%saktavaḥ%}) made of this grain are also mentioned.<sup>4</sup> The word
occurs in the singular in the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa.<sup>5</sup>
<F>1) Maitrāyaṇī Saṃhitā, i. 2, 8; Vāja-
saneyi Saṃhitā, xviii. 12; xix. 22. 89;
xxi. 29, etc.</F>
<F>2) Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa, xii. 7, 1, 2; 2,
9; Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upaniṣad, vi. 3, 22
(Mādhyaṃdina = vi. 3, 13 Kāṇva), etc.</F>
<F>3) Taittirīya Brāhmaṇa, i. 3, 7, 2.</F>
<F>4) Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa, xii. 9, 1, 5.</F>
<F>5) v. 2, 1, 6.
