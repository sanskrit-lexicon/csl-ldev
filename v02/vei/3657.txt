{@Sṛjaya@}¦ is the name of one of the victims at the Aśvamedha
<div n="lb"/>(‘horse sacrifice’) in the Yajurveda.<sup>1</sup> What it was is unknown:
<div n="lb"/>Mahīdhara on the Vājasaneyi passage calls it a kind of bird;
<div n="lb"/>Sāyaṇa on the Taittirīya Saṃhitā gives the alternatives ‘black
<div n="lb"/>fly’ (when {%sṛjayā%} must be read), ‘white serpent,’ and ‘black
<div n="lb"/>buffalo.’
<F>1) Taittirīya Saṃhitā, v. 5, 14, 1;
<div n="lb"/>Maitrāyaṇī Saṃhitā, iii. 14, 14; Vāja-
<div n="lb"/>saneyi Saṃhitā, xxiv. 23. {%Cf.%} Zimmer,
<div n="lb"/>{%Altindisches Leben,%} 99.</F>
