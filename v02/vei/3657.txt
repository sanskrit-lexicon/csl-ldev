<L>3657<pc>2-469<k1>सृजय<k2>सृजय
{@Sṛjaya@}¦ is the name of one of the victims at the Aśvamedha
(‘horse sacrifice’) in the Yajurveda.<sup>1</sup> What it was is unknown:
Mahīdhara on the Vājasaneyi passage calls it a kind of bird;
Sāyaṇa on the Taittirīya Saṃhitā gives the alternatives ‘black
fly’ (when {%sṛjayā%} must be read), ‘white serpent,’ and ‘black
buffalo.’
<F>1) Taittirīya Saṃhitā, v. 5, 14, 1;
Maitrāyaṇī Saṃhitā, iii. 14, 14; Vāja-
saneyi Saṃhitā, xxiv. 23. {%Cf.%} Zimmer,
{%Altindisches Leben,%} 99.</F>
