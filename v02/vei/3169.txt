<L>3169<pc>2-354<k1>शमी<k2>शमी
{@Śamī@}¦ is the name of a tree in the Atharvaveda<sup>1</sup> and later.<sup>2</sup>
<div n="lb"/>It is described in the Atharvaveda<sup>3</sup> as destructive to the hair,<sup>4</sup> as
<div n="lb"/>producing intoxication, and as broad-leaved. These charac-
<div n="lb"/>teristics are totally wanting in the two trees, {%Prosopis spicigera%}
<div n="lb"/>or {%Mimosa suma,%} with which the Śamī is usually identified.<sup>5</sup>
[Page2-355+ 56]
<div n="lb"/>From the soft wood of the Śamī was formed the lower of the
<div n="lb"/>two sticks ({%araṇī%}) used for kindling the sacred fire,<sup>6</sup> the upper
<div n="lb"/>one (the drill) being of {@Aśvattha.@} The fruit of the tree is
<div n="lb"/>called Śamīdhānya.<sup>7</sup>
