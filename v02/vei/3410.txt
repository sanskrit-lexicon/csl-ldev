{@Śvaitreya@}¦ occurs in two passages of the Rigveda,<sup>1</sup> where
Sāyaṇa sees in the word the name of a man, a ‘descendant of
Śvitrā.’ The first passage is almost identical with one in the
sixth Maṇḍala of the Rigveda,<sup>2</sup> where, however, {@Daśadyu@}
appears alone without Śvaitreya. Ludwig<sup>3</sup> identifies Daśadyu
with Śvaitreya (‘son of Śvitrī’), and considers him a son of
{@Kutsa.@}<sup>4</sup> Bergaigne<sup>5</sup> and Baunack<sup>6</sup> think he is really {@Bhujyu.@}
Geldner<sup>7</sup> considers that he was a bull used for fighting, the son
[Page2-411+ 35]
of a Śvitrā cow,<sup>8</sup> but this is very doubtful, though the term
{%śvaitreya%} is elsewhere applied to a bull.<sup>9</sup> Śvitrya<sup>10</sup> seems to
have the same sense as Śvaitreya.
