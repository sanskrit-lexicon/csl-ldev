<L>2311<pc>2-115<k1>मकर<k2>मकर
{@Makara@}¦ is the name of an animal, probably the ‘crocodile,’<sup>1</sup>
which is included in the list of victims at the Aśvamedha
(‘horse sacrifice’) in the Yajurveda Saṃhitās.<sup>2</sup>
<F>1) The Makara, as a Hindu sculptural
ornament, originally represented a
crocodile. {%Cf.%} Cousen's article in the
{%Annual Report of the Archaological Survey%}
{%of India%} for 1903-4, pp. 227-231 (where
the Makara appears as the vehicle of
Varuṇa and of Gaṅgā). {%Cf.%} also {%op. cit.,%}
1904-5, pp. 80, 83, 84.</F>
<F>2) Taittirīya Saṃhitā, v. 5, 13, 1;
Maitrāyaṇī Saṃhitā, iii. 14, 16; Vāja
saneyi Saṃhitā, xxiv. 35. {%Cf.%} Zimmer,
{%Altindisches Leben,%} 97.</F>
