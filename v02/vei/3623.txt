<L>3623<pc>2-458<k1>सुरा<k2>सुरा
{@Surā@}¦ is the name of an intoxicating ‘spirituous liquor,’
<div n="lb"/>often mentioned in Vedic literature. In some passages<sup>1</sup> it is
<div n="lb"/>referred to favourably, in others with decided disapproval.<sup>2</sup> It
<div n="lb"/>is classed with the use of meat and with dicing as an evil in the
<div n="lb"/>Atharvaveda,<sup>3</sup> and often with dicing.<sup>4</sup> It was, as opposed to
<div n="lb"/>{@Soma,@} essentially a drink of ordinary life.<sup>5</sup> It was the drink of
<div n="lb"/>men in the {@Sabhā,@}<sup>6</sup> and gave rise to broils.<sup>7</sup>
<div n="P"/>Its exact nature is not certain. It may have been a strong
<div n="lb"/>spirit prepared from fermented grains and plants, as Eggeling<sup>8</sup>
[Page2-459+ 54]
<div n="lb"/>holds, or, as Whitney<sup>9</sup> thought, a kind of beer or ale.
<div n="lb"/>Geldner<sup>10</sup> renders it ‘brandy.’ It is sometimes mentioned in
<div n="lb"/>connexion with {@Madhu.@}<sup>11</sup> It was kept in skins.<sup>12</sup>
