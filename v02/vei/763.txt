<L>763<pc>1-194<k1>कौषीतकि<k2>कौषीतकि
{@Kauṣītaki@}¦ (‘descendant of Kuṣītaka’) is the patronymic of a
teacher, or series of teachers, to whom the doctrines set forth
in the Kauṣītaki Brāhmaṇa<sup>1</sup> and in the Śāṅkhāyana Āraṇyaka,<sup>2</sup>
and the Śrauta and Gṛhya Sūtras,<sup>3</sup> are referred. He is rarely
mentioned elsewhere.<sup>4</sup> The doctrine of Kauṣītaki is called the
Kauṣītaka.<sup>5</sup> The pupils of Kauṣītaki are known as the Kauṣī-
takis in the Nidāna Sūtra,<sup>6</sup> and in the Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa<sup>7</sup>
[Page1-195+ 43]
they with Kuṣītaka are stated to have been cursed by {@Luśākapi.@}
Elsewhere<sup>8</sup> they are called Kauṣītakins. If the Śāṅkhāyana
Āraṇyaka<sup>9</sup> can be trusted, there were among them at least two
leading teachers, {@Kahoḍa@} and {@Sarvajit,@} the former of whom is
mentioned elsewhere.<sup>10</sup>
