<L>1702<pc>1-497<k1>परिषद्<k2>परिषद्
{@Pari-ṣad@}¦ (lit., ‘sitting around’) denotes in the Upaniṣads<sup>1</sup> an
‘assemblage’ of advisers in questions of philosophy, and the
Gobhila Gṛhya Sūtra<sup>2</sup> refers to a teacher with his Pariṣad or
‘council.’ In the later literature the word denotes a body of
advisers on religious topics, but also the assessors of a judge,
or the council of ministers of a prince.<sup>3</sup> But in none of these
senses is the word found in the early literature, though the
institutions indicated by it must have existed at least in
embryo.

(Mādhyaṃdina = vi. 2, 1 Kāṇva); {%daivī%}

ii. 11, 13. 14.</F>
<F>2) iii. 2, 40.</F>
