{@Dakṣiṇā@}¦ appears repeatedly in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> and later<sup>2</sup> as the
<div n="lb"/>designation of the gift presented to priests at the sacrifice,
<div n="lb"/>apparently because a cow — a prolific ({%dakṣiṇā%}) one — was the
<div n="lb"/>usual ‘fee’<sup>3</sup> on such an occasion.<sup>4</sup> The later Dāna-stutis, or
<div n="lb"/>‘Praises of Gifts,’ in the Rigveda immensely exaggerate these
<div n="lb"/>donations, and the exaggeration grows in the Brāhmaṇas. It
<div n="lb"/>is important to notice that these enumerations of gifts in the
<div n="lb"/>main include nothing but articles of personal property, such as
<div n="lb"/>kine, horses, buffaloes, or camels ({%uṣṭra%}), ornaments, and so
<div n="lb"/>forth, but not land.<sup>5</sup> Reference is, however, made in the
<div n="lb"/>Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa<sup>6</sup> to land as a Dakṣiṇā, but with dis-
<div n="lb"/>approval, probably because the land came to be regarded as
<div n="lb"/>inalienable without the consent of the clansmen.<sup>7</sup>
<F>1) A whole hymn, Rv. x. 107, is
<div n="lb"/>devoted to its praise. {%Cf.%} i. 168, 7; vi.
<div n="lb"/>27, 8; viii. 24, 29; 39, 5; x. 62, 1, etc.</F>
<F>2) Av. iv. 11, 4; v. 7, 11; xi. 7, 9;
<div n="lb"/>8, 22; xiii. 1, 52; xviii. 4, 8, etc.;
<div n="lb"/>Taittirīya Saṃhitā, i. 7, 3, 1; 8, 1, 1;
<div n="lb"/>Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā, iv. 19, 23; xix. 30;
<div n="lb"/>Taittirīya Brāhmaṇa, i. 7, 3, 3 {%et seq.;%}
<div n="lb"/>Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa, i. 9, 3, 1 {%et seq.%}
<div n="lb"/>The verses ({%gāthā nārāśaṃsī,%} either as
<div n="lb"/>a single expression or as two separate
<div n="lb"/>terms) used to win these Dakṣiṇās were
<div n="lb"/>notoriously false. See Kāṭhaka Saṃ-
<div n="lb"/>hitā, xiv. 5; Taittirīya Brāhmaṇa, i. 3,
<div n="lb"/>2, 6. 7; Nirukta, i. 7; xi. 2.</F>

<div n="lb"/>in the use of the English word ‘fee’;
