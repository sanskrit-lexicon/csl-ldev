{@Kṣura@}¦ occurs three times in the Rigveda. The word appears
<div n="lb"/>to have the general sense of ‘blade’<sup>1</sup> in one passage,<sup>2</sup> possibly
<div n="lb"/>also in another,<sup>3</sup> where it is said that the hare swallowed a
[Page1-210+ 57]
<div n="lb"/>Kṣura, and where the sense ‘blade’ is adequate. In the third
<div n="lb"/>passage<sup>4</sup> there seems to be a reference to the sharpening of a
<div n="lb"/>razor on a grindstone<sup>5</sup> ({%bhurijos,%} the dual denoting precisely, as
<div n="lb"/>Pischel<sup>6</sup> points out, the two sides of the apparatus, between
<div n="lb"/>which the stone revolved like the modern grindstone). But
<div n="lb"/>Muir,<sup>7</sup> following another view of Roth,<sup>8</sup> adopts the sense ‘the
<div n="lb"/>edge of scissors,’ which, however, hardly suits the other
<div n="lb"/>passage, one in the Atharvaveda,<sup>9</sup> where a Kṣura is described
<div n="lb"/>as moving about on the {%bhurijos,%}<sup>10</sup> as the tongue on the lip. The
<div n="lb"/>meaning ‘razor’ is perfectly clear in the Atharvaveda,<sup>11</sup> where
<div n="lb"/>shaving by means of it is mentioned; in many other passages<sup>12</sup>
<div n="lb"/>either sense is adequate. A {%kṣuro bhṛjvān%} occurs in the Yajur-
<div n="lb"/>veda:<sup>13</sup> it seems to denote, as Bloomfield<sup>14</sup> suggests, a razor
<div n="lb"/>with a strop (in the shape of a small grinding apparatus).
<div n="lb"/>Kṣura-dhārā<sup>15</sup> denotes ‘the edge of a razor,’ like {%kṣurasya%}
<div n="lb"/>{%dhārā.%}<sup>16</sup> In the Upaniṣads<sup>17</sup> a razor-case (Kṣura-dhāna) is
<div n="lb"/>mentioned. See also {@Śmaśru.@}
