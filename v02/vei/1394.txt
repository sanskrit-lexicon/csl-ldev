{@Dyutāna Māruta@}¦ (‘descendant of the Maruts’) is the name
<div n="lb"/>of a divine being invoked in the Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā<sup>1</sup> and
<div n="lb"/>the Taittirīya Saṃhitā,<sup>2</sup> and also mentioned in the Kāṭhaka
<div n="lb"/>Saṃhitā.<sup>3</sup> In the Śatapatha<sup>4</sup> Brāhmaṇa the name is explained
<div n="lb"/>to mean Vāyu, while in the Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa<sup>5</sup> he seems
<div n="lb"/>to be regarded as the author of a Sāman (chant). He is
<div n="lb"/>treated as a Ṛṣi by the Anukramaṇī, which credits him with
<div n="lb"/>the authorship of a hymn of the Rigveda (viii. 96).

<F>3) xv. 7.</F>
<F>4) iii. 6, 1, 16.</F>
<F>5) xvii. 1, 7. {%Cf.%} vi. 4, 2.
