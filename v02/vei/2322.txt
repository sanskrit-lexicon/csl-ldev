<L>2322<pc>2-119<k1>मटची<k2>मटची
{@Maṭacī@}¦ occurs in a passage of the Chāndogya Upaniṣad.<sup>1</sup>
<div n="lb"/>where reference is made to the {@Kurus@} being overwhelmed by
<div n="lb"/>Maṭacīs.<sup>2</sup> Śaṅkara interprets the word by ‘thunderbolts’
<div n="lb"/>({%aśanayaḥ%}), while Ānandatīrtha in his commentary gives, as an
<div n="lb"/>alternative rendering, {%pāṣāṇa-vṛṣṭayaḥ — i.e.,%} ‘hailstones,’ which
<div n="lb"/>may be the sense. The Śabdakalpadruma,<sup>3</sup> agreeing with
<div n="lb"/>Ānandatīrtha,<sup>4</sup> says that Maṭacī means, ‘a kind of small red
<div n="lb"/>bird’ ({%rakta-varṇa-kṣudra-pakṣi-viśeṣa,%} reading {%-pakṣī-%}), and
<div n="lb"/>Jacob<sup>5</sup> suggests that the ‘locust’ is meant.
