{@Mayu@}¦ occurs in the list of victims at the Aśvamedha (‘horse
<div n="lb"/>sacrifice’) in the Yajurveda Saṃhitās.<sup>1</sup> The commentator on
<div n="lb"/>the Taittirīya Saṃhitā<sup>1</sup> explains the word as meaning either
<div n="lb"/>an ‘ape’ ({%kiṃpuruṣa%}) or a ‘forest peacock’ ({%āraṇya-mayūra%}).
[Page2-134+ 48]
<div n="lb"/>The former sense is supported by another passage of the
<div n="lb"/>Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā,<sup>2</sup> where the Mayu, being a substitute for
<div n="lb"/>the man, must be an ape. This sense also suits the word in
<div n="lb"/>the other passage<sup>3</sup> where it occurs.
