{@Pastyā@}¦ (fem. pl.) is a word occurring in several passages of
<div n="lb"/>the Rigveda.<sup>1</sup> Roth<sup>2</sup> ascribes to it the meaning of ‘house’ or
<div n="lb"/>‘dwelling,’ in the wide sense of the term, as well as that of the
<div n="lb"/>‘family’ living in the house; and this view is accepted by
<div n="lb"/>Zimmer.<sup>3</sup> On the other hand, Pischel<sup>4</sup> finds in two of the
<div n="lb"/>passages<sup>5</sup> usually referred to Pastyā the neuter Pastya, which
<div n="lb"/>appears in {@Pastya-sad@} and in {@Pastyā-vant@} (where the length
<div n="lb"/>of the second syllable is not primitive), and which is certainly
<div n="lb"/>found in the Rigveda<sup>6</sup> in the metaphorical sense of ‘dwelling,’
<div n="lb"/>ascribed to it in the Naighaṇṭuka.<sup>7</sup> In the other passages<sup>8</sup> he
<div n="lb"/>thinks the word means ‘rivers’ or ‘waters’; in particular,
<div n="lb"/>where Soma in the middle of the Pastyās<sup>9</sup> is spoken of, the
<div n="lb"/>sees a reference to {@Kurukṣetra,@} with its several rivers,<sup>10</sup> {@Āpayā,@}
<div n="lb"/>{@Dṛṣadvatī,@} and {@Sarasvatī@} ({%cf.%} 2. {@Pastyāvant@}). In some passages<sup>11</sup>
<div n="lb"/>he sees in Pastyā the proper name of a stream, just as Sindhu
<div n="lb"/>primarily means ‘river,’ then the ‘Indus.’

<div n="lb"/>11; vi, 49, 9; vii. 97, 5; ix, 65, 23;
<div n="lb"/>x. 46, 6. See also iv. 55, 3; viii. 27, 5,
