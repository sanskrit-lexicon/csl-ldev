<L>7<pc>1-005<k1>अक्षत<k2>अक्षत
{@Akṣata@}¦ or {@Akṣita.@} — In one passage of the Atharvaveda,<sup>1</sup>
<div n="lb"/>dealing with the {@Jāyānya,@} mention is made of a remedy for
<div n="lb"/>sores designated both Akṣita and Sukṣata, or, according to
<div n="lb"/>the reading of the Kauṣika Sūtra, Akṣata and Sukṣata, while
<div n="lb"/>Sāyaṇa has Akṣita and Sukṣita. Bloomfield<sup>2</sup> renders ‘not
<div n="lb"/>caused by cutting’ and ‘caused by cutting.’ Formerly<sup>3</sup> he
<div n="lb"/>suggested ‘tumour’ or ‘boil.’ Whitney<sup>4</sup> thinks that two
<div n="lb"/>varieties of Jāyānya are meant. Ludwig<sup>5</sup> reads with Sāyana
<div n="lb"/>{%akṣita,%} which he renders by ‘not firmly established’ in the
<div n="lb"/>invalid. Zimmer<sup>6</sup> finds in it a disease Kṣata.
