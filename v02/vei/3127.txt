<L>3127<pc>2-346<k1>शकंभर<k2>शकंभर
{@Śakaṃ-bhara,@}¦ ‘bearer of dung,’ is found in one passage of
the Atharvaveda,<sup>1</sup> where the sense is doubtful. Ludwig<sup>2</sup> and
Grill<sup>3</sup> see in it a tribal name, Bloomfield<sup>4</sup> the personification of
excessive evacuation (diarrhœa), while Whitney<sup>5</sup> considers
that it may refer to the {@Mahāvṛṣas,@} despised as having to
collect dung for fuel in the absence of wood in their country.
