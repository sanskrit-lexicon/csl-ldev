<L>3362<pc>2-399<k1>श्यामाक<k2>श्यामाक
{@Śyāmāka@}¦ is the name of a cultivated millet ({%Panicum frumen-%}
<div n="lb"/>{%taceum%}) in the later Saṃhitās<sup>1</sup> and the Brāhmaṇas.<sup>2</sup> The
<div n="lb"/>lightness of its seed is alluded to in the Atharvaveda,<sup>3</sup> where it
<div n="lb"/>is spoken of as blown away by the wind. There it is also
<div n="lb"/>mentioned as the food of pigeons.<sup>4</sup> The Śyāmāka and its seed
<div n="lb"/>({@Taṇḍula@}) are referred to as very small in the Chāndogya
<div n="lb"/>Upaniṣad,<sup>5</sup> where Max Müller<sup>6</sup> renders it as ‘canary seed.’
