{@Carṣaṇi,@}¦ used in the plural, denotes in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> ‘men’
<div n="lb"/>in general or ‘people,’ conceived either as active beings<sup>2</sup> or as
<div n="lb"/>cultivators<sup>3</sup> in opposition to nomads. The expression ‘king of
<div n="lb"/>men’ ({%rājā carṣaṇīnām%}) is frequently found.<sup>4</sup> The ‘people’ are
[Page1-258+ 52]
<div n="lb"/>also mentioned in connexion with war.<sup>5</sup> In the Atharvaveda<sup>6</sup>
<div n="lb"/>‘animals’ ({%paśu%}) and ‘men’ ({%carṣaṇi%}) are spoken of together.
<div n="P"/>For the five {%carṣaṇayaḥ,%}<sup>7</sup> see {@Pañca Janāsaḥ.@}
