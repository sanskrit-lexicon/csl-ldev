<L>1996<pc>2-032<k1>प्रतिसर<k2>प्रतिसर
{@Prati-sara@}¦ is used in several passages of the Atharvaveda<sup>1</sup>
and later<sup>2</sup> to denote an amulet, according to Roth,<sup>3</sup> because it
was a band, and so returned on itself ({%prati-sṛ,%} ‘go back’). The
sense is doubtful; perhaps ‘attacking’ may really be the root
idea.<sup>4</sup> {%Cf.%} {@Punaḥsara.@}
<F>1) ii. 11, 2; iv. 40, 1; viii. 5, 1. 4.</F>
<F>2) Satapatha Brāhmaṇa, v. 2, 4, 20;
Śāṅkhāyana Āraṇyaka, xii. 30, etc.</F>
<F>3) St. Petersburg Dictionary, {%s.v.,%}
