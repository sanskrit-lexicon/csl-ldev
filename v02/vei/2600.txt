<L>2600<pc>2-195<k1>यूषन्<k2>यूषन्
{@Yūṣan,@}¦ occurring in the description of the horse-sacrifice in
the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> and the Yajurveda Saṃhitās,<sup>2</sup> denotes the ‘broth’
which was made from the flesh of the sacrificial animal, and
was no doubt used as food. Vessels employed for holding it,
{@Pātra@} and {@Āsecana,@} are mentioned. Another form of the word,
found in the Taittirīya Saṃhitā,<sup>3</sup> is Yūs, which corresponds to
the Latin {%jus.%}
<F>1) i. 162, 13.</F>
<F>2) Taittirīya Saṃhitā, vi. 3, 11, 1. 4;
Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā, xxv. 9.</F>
<F>3) vi. 3, 11, 1. 4.
