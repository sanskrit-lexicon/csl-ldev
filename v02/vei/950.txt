{@Cakṣus,@}¦ ‘eye.’ The ‘evil eye’ ({%ghoraṃ cakṣus%}) was well
known in the Atharvaveda, which contains spells to counteract
its influence.<sup>1</sup> As remedies against it are mentioned salve from
Mount {@Trikakubh@}<sup>2</sup> and the {@Jaṅgiḍa@} plant.<sup>3</sup> In the wedding
ceremony the wife is entreated not to have the evil eye ({%aghora-%}
{%cakṣus%}).<sup>4</sup> The structure of the eye, and its division into white
({%śukla%}), dark ({%kṛṣṇa%}), and the pupil ({%kanīn ikā%}) are repeatedly
referred to in the later Brāhmaṇas.<sup>5</sup> The desease {@Alaji@} appears
to have been an affection of the eyes.
