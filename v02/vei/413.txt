{@Ūrṇā,@}¦ ‘wool,’ is very frequently mentioned from the Rigveda<sup>1</sup>
<div n="lb"/>onwards. The Paruṣṇī country was famous for its wool,<sup>2</sup> like
<div n="lb"/>Gandhāra<sup>3</sup> for its sheep. The term for the separate tufts was
<div n="lb"/>{%parvan%}<sup>4</sup> or {%parus.%}<sup>5</sup> ‘Soft as wool’ ({%ūrṇa-mradas%})<sup>6</sup> is not a rare
<div n="lb"/>epithet. The sheep is called ‘woolly’ ({%ūrṇāvatī%}).<sup>7</sup> ‘Woollen
<div n="lb"/>thread’ ({%ūrṇā-sūtra%}) is repeatedly referred to in the later
<div n="lb"/>Saṃhitās<sup>8</sup> and the Brāhmaṇas.<sup>9</sup> The word {%ūrṇā%} was not
<div n="lb"/>restricted to the sense of sheep's wool,<sup>10</sup> but might denote goat's
<div n="lb"/>hair also.<sup>11</sup>
