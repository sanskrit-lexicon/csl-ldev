<div n="lb"/>last act of the funeral rite included the removal of the bow from

<div n="lb"/>the right hand of the dead man.<sup>4</sup> The weapon was composed
<div n="lb"/>of a stout staff bent into a curved shape ({%vakra%}),<sup>5</sup> and of a bow-
<div n="lb"/>string ({@Jyā@}) made of a strip of cowhide<sup>6</sup> which joined the ends.
<div n="lb"/>The tips of the bow, when the string was fastened, were called
<div n="lb"/>{@Ārtnī.@} Relaxed when not in actual use, the bow was specially
<div n="lb"/>strung up when needed for shooting.<sup>7</sup> The stages of the

<div n="lb"/>stringing ({%ā-tan%}) of the bow, the placing ({%prati-dhā%}) of the
<div n="lb"/>arrow, the bending ({%ā-yam%}) of the bow, and the shooting ({%as%}).
