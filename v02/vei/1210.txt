<L>1210<pc>1-329<k1>त्रिककुद्<k2>त्रिककुद्
{@Tri-kakud@}¦<sup>1</sup> or {@Tri-kakubh,@}<sup>2</sup> ‘having three peaks,’ occurs in
the Atharvaveda and later as the name of a mountain in the
Himālaya, the modern Trikota. From it came the salve
(Āñjana),<sup>3</sup> which tradition made out to be derived from
Vṛtra's eye.<sup>4</sup>
<F>1) Av. iv. 9, 8; Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa,
iii. 1, 3, 12.</F>
<F>2) Maitrāyaṇī Saṃhitā, iii. 6, 3;
Kāṭhaka Saṃhitā, xxiii. 1; Vājasaneyi
Saṃhitā, xv. 4; Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa,
xxii. 14.</F>
<F>3) Hence called {%Traikakuda,%} Av. iv. 9,
9, 10; xix. 44, 6, etc.</F>
<F>4) Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa, {%loc. cit.;%}
Maitrāyaṇī and Kāṭhaka Saṃhitās,
{%loc. cit.%}
