<L>2239<pc>2-097<k1>भरद्वाज<k2>भरद्वाज
{@Bharad-vāja@}¦ is the name of the reputed<sup>1</sup> author of the sixth
<div n="lb"/>Maṇḍala of the Rigveda. The attribution is so far correct that
<div n="lb"/>Bharadvāja<sup>2</sup> and the Bharadvājas<sup>3</sup> are repeatedly mentioned as
<div n="lb"/>singers in that Maṇḍala. Judging by the tone of the references
<div n="lb"/>to Bharadvāja, he can hardly be deemed to have been a con-
<div n="lb"/>temporary of any of the hymns.<sup>4</sup> According to the Pañcaviṃśa
[Page2-098+ 52]
<div n="lb"/>Brāhmaṇa,<sup>5</sup> he was the Purohita of {@Divodāsa.@} This interpreta-
<div n="lb"/>tion is to be preferred to that of Roth,<sup>6</sup> who suggests that he
<div n="lb"/>and Divodāsa were identical. His connexion with the house of
<div n="lb"/>Divodāsa also appears from the statement of the Kāṭhaka
<div n="lb"/>Saṃhitā<sup>7</sup> that Bharadvāja gave {@Pratardana@} the kingdom. It
<div n="lb"/>is unnecessary to suppose that the same Bharadvāja was meant
<div n="lb"/>in both cases, and that Pratardana was a son of Divodāsa: the
<div n="lb"/>later Saṃhitās refer to Bharadvāja, like the other great sages,
<div n="lb"/>irrespective of chronology.
<div n="P"/>The Bharadvājas in their poems mention {@Bṛbu, Bṛsaya,@} and
<div n="lb"/>the {@Pārāvatas.@}<sup>8</sup> Hillebrandt<sup>9</sup> has pointed out that they are also
<div n="lb"/>connected with the {@Sṛñjayas.@} In particular, the Śāṅkhāyana
<div n="lb"/>Śrauta Sūtra<sup>10</sup> mentions that Bharadvāja gained largesse from
<div n="lb"/>{@Prastoka Sārñjaya@} and Bṛbu. But it is very doubtful if it is
<div n="lb"/>correct to place all these people and Divodāsa in Arachosia
<div n="lb"/>and Drangiana.
<div n="P"/>Bharadvāja as an author and a seer is frequently referred to
<div n="lb"/>in the later Saṃhitās<sup>11</sup> and the Brāhmaṇas.<sup>12</sup>
