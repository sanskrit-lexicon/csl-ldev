<L>630<pc>1-161<k1>कुण्डृणाची<k2>कुण्डृणाची
{@Kuṇḍṛṇācī@}¦ is the name of an animal of unknown character
<div n="lb"/>occurring in the lists of victims at the horse sacrifice (Aśva-
<div n="lb"/>medha) in the Yajurveda Saṃhitās.<sup>1</sup> The word also occurs in
<div n="lb"/>one passage of the Rigveda,<sup>2</sup> in which a bird would seem to be
<div n="lb"/>intended, though Sāyaṇa interprets it as meaning ‘with
<div n="lb"/>crooked flight’ ({%kuṭila-gatyā%}). In his commentary on the
<div n="lb"/>Taittirīya Saṃhitā<sup>3</sup> he takes the word to denote the house-
<div n="lb"/>lizard ({%gṛha-godhikā%}).
<F>1) Taittirīya Saṃhitā, v. 5, 16, 1;
<div n="lb"/>Maitrāyaṇī Saṃhitā, iii. 14, 18; Vāja-
<div n="lb"/>saneyi Saṃhitā, xxiv. 37.</F>
<F>2) i. 29, 6.</F>
<F>3) v. 5, 16, 1.
