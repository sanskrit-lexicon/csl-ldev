{@Dasyave vṛka,@}¦ ‘wolf to the {@Dasyu,@}’ is the name of a man
<div n="lb"/>mentioned four times in the Rigveda.<sup>1</sup> In one hymn<sup>2</sup> he is
<div n="lb"/>called a Ṛṣi, but in two others<sup>3</sup> he is clearly a prince victorious
<div n="lb"/>over the Dasyus, and a generous patron of the singer. It is
<div n="lb"/>hardly necessary to assume different persons,<sup>4</sup> for the term Ṛṣi
<div n="lb"/>is not altogether inconsistent with royalty. He was son of
<div n="lb"/>{@Pūtakratu@}<sup>5</sup> and {@Pūtakratā,@}<sup>6</sup> his wife.
<F>1) viii. 51, 2; 55, 1; 56, 1. 2.</F>
<F>2) viii. 51.</F>
<F>3) viii. 55. 56.</F>
<F>4) Especially as the name occurs only
<div n="lb"/>in the small collection of Kāṇva hymns
<div n="lb"/>forming the Vālakhilya group in the
<div n="lb"/>eighth Maṇḍala.</F>
<F>5) viii. 56, 2. {%Pautakrata. Cf.%} viii. 68,
<div n="lb"/>17.</F>
<F>6) viii. 56, 4.
