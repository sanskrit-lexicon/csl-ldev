{@Kusurubinda Auddālaki@}¦ appears as an authority on ritual
matters in the Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa,<sup>1</sup> the Taittirīya Saṃhitā,<sup>2</sup>
the Jaiminīya Brāhmaṇa,<sup>3</sup> and the Ṣaḍviṃśa Brāhmaṇa.<sup>4</sup> He
may have been the brother of {@Śvetaketu,@} as suggested by
Weber.<sup>5</sup>
<F>1) xxii. 15, 1. 10.</F>
<F>2) vii. 2, 2, 1.</F>
<F>3) i. 75 ({%Journal of the American Oriental%}
{%Society,%} 23, 327), where the reading
seems to be Asurbinda.</F>
<F>4) i. 16. See Weber, {%Indische Studien,%}
1, 39. The name is there read as Ku-
surubindu; in the Śāṅkhāyana Śrauta
Sūtra, xvi. 22, 14 it is Kusurabindu.</F>
<F>5) {%Indische Studien,%} 5, 61, n.</F>
