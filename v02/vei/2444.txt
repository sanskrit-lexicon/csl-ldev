{@Mātṛ@}¦ is the regular word for ‘mother’ from the Rigveda
<div n="lb"/>onwards,<sup>1</sup> being a formation probably developed under the
<div n="lb"/>influence of an onomatopœtic word {%mā,%}<sup>2</sup> used like Ambā<sup>3</sup>
<div n="lb"/>and {@Nanā.@}<sup>4</sup>
<div n="P"/>The relations of wife and husband, as well as of mother and
<div n="lb"/>children, are treated under {@Pati.@} It remains only to add that
<div n="lb"/>details are given in the Sūtras<sup>5</sup> of the respectful attention paid
<div n="lb"/>to a mother, and of the ceremonies in which she is concerned.
<div n="lb"/>The mother also appears interested in the fate of her children
<div n="lb"/>as in the story of the sale of {@Śunaḥśepa@} for adoption by
<div n="lb"/>{@Viśvāmitra@} in the Aitareya Brāhmaṇa.<sup>6</sup>
