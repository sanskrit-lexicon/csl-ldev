{@Kāla-kāñja.@}¦ — In the Atharvaveda<sup>1</sup> mention is made of the
<div n="lb"/>Kālakāñjas as being in the sky. Both Roth<sup>2</sup> and Zimmer<sup>3</sup>
<div n="lb"/>hold that some constellation is meant. But as the defeat of
<div n="lb"/>the Kālakāñjas is one of Indra's exploits,<sup>4</sup> it is doubtful whether
<div n="lb"/>any stress can be laid on that interpretation of the passage in
<div n="lb"/>the Atharvaveda. Whitney<sup>5</sup> suggests that the three stars of
<div n="lb"/>Orion are meant, Bloomfield<sup>6</sup> that the galaxy or the stars in
<div n="lb"/>general are intended.
