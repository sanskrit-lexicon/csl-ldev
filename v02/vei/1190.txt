<L>1190<pc>1-323<k1>तेजन<k2>तेजन
{@Tejana@}¦ denotes in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> a rod or staff of reed used
for measuring a field. In the Atharvaveda the sense of
‘bamboo’ is found twice,<sup>2</sup> the bamboo being specified in the
[Page1-324+ 53]
second passage as ‘of the spring’ ({%vāsantika%}); more particularly
it denotes the shaft of an arrow,<sup>3</sup> a sense often found in later
Vedic texts.<sup>4</sup>
<F>1) i. 110, 5.</F>
<F>2) i. 2, 4; xx. 136, 3 (= Khila, v. 22, 3).
{%Cf. taijana%} as an adjective in Kāṭhaka
Saṃhitā, xxi. 10.</F>
<F>3) Av. vi. 49, 1 (Whitney, Translation
of the Atharvaveda, 317); {%iṣu eka-tejanā,%}
‘an arrow with one shaft,’ vi. 57, 1.</F>
<F>4) Aitareya Brāhmaṇa, i. 25; iii. 26;
Kāṭhaka Saṃhitā, xxv. 1 (with {%śṛṅga%}
and {%śalya%} as the three parts of an
arrow; in Maitrāyaṇī Saṃhitā, iii. 8, 1,
{%kulmala%} takes the place of {%tejana; cf.%}
{%ibid.,%} 2). The Taittirīya Saṃhitā, vi. 3,
3, 1, has {%anīka, śalya,%} and {%tejana.%}
