<div n="lb"/>these are described<sup>2</sup> as being {%ghoṣavant,%} ‘sonant,’ and also as
<div n="lb"/>{%balavant,%} ‘uttered with force.’ The precise word for a mute is
<div n="lb"/>{%sparśa,%}<sup>3</sup> ‘contact,’ while {%ūṣman%} denotes a ‘sibilant,’ and {%svara%} a
<div n="lb"/>‘vowel,’ in the Aitareya<sup>3</sup> and Śāṅkhāyana Āraṇyakas.<sup>4</sup> The
<div n="lb"/>semivowels are there denoted by {%anta-sthā%} (‘intermediate’)<sup>5</sup> or
<div n="lb"/>{%akṣara.%}<sup>6</sup> Another division in the Aitareya Āraṇyaka<sup>7</sup> is into
<div n="lb"/>{%ghoṣa, ūṣman,%} and {%vyañjana,%} apparently ‘vowels,’ ‘sibilants,’
<div n="lb"/>and ‘consonants’ respectively. {%Ghoṣa%} elsewhere in that Āraṇ-
<div n="lb"/>yaka<sup>8</sup> seems to have the general sense of ‘sounds.’ The
<div n="lb"/>Taittirīya Upaniṣad<sup>9</sup> refers to {%mātrā,%} a ‘mora’;<sup>10</sup> {%bala,%} ‘force’
<div n="lb"/>of utterance, and {%varṇa,%} ‘letter,’ an expression found else-
<div n="lb"/>where<sup>11</sup> in the explanation of {%om,%} as compacted of {%a + u + m.%}

<div n="P"/>The Aitareya Āraṇyaka<sup>12</sup> and the Śāṅkhāyana Āraṇyaka<sup>13</sup>
<div n="lb"/>recognize the three forms of the Rigveda text as {%pratṛṇṇa,%}
<div n="lb"/>{%nirbhuja,%} and {%ubhayam-antareṇa,%} denoting respectively the Saṃ-
<div n="lb"/>hitā, Pada, and Krama Pāṭhas of the Rigveda.<sup>14</sup> The same
<div n="lb"/>authorities<sup>15</sup> recognize the importance of the distinction of the
<div n="lb"/>cerebral and dental {%n%} and {%s,%} and refer<sup>16</sup> to the {@Māṇḍūkeyas'@}
<div n="lb"/>mode of recitation. They also discuss<sup>17</sup> Sandhi, the euphonic
<div n="lb"/>‘combination’ of letters.
<div n="P"/>The Prātiśākhyas of the several Saṃhitās develop in detail
<div n="lb"/>the grammatical terminology, and Yāska's Nirukta<sup>18</sup> contains a
<div n="lb"/>good deal of grammatical material. The Śatapatha Brāh-
<div n="lb"/>maṇa<sup>19</sup> distinguishes the genders, and the Pañcaviṃśa Brāh-
<div n="lb"/>maṇa<sup>20</sup> the division of words in the Sāman recitation.
