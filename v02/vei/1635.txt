<L>1635<pc>1-464<k1>पक्ष<k2>पक्ष
{@Pakṣa@}¦ is, in the Atharvaveda,<sup>1</sup> applied to some part of a
house, either the ‘side posts,’ according to Roth,<sup>2</sup> Zimmer,<sup>3</sup>
and Grill,<sup>4</sup> or ‘sides,’ as understood by Whitney<sup>5</sup> and Bloom-
field.<sup>6</sup> The description of the roof ({@Chadis@}) in the Atharvaveda<sup>7</sup>
as {%catuṣ-pakṣa,%} ‘four-sided,’ tells in favour of the second
explanation. In the Taittirīya Brāhmaṇa<sup>8</sup> Pakṣa is used of
the sides of a chariot. For Pakṣa as the ‘half’ of a month, see
{@Māsa.@}
