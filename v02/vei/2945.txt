<L>2945<pc>2-298<k1>विदेह<k2>विदेह
{@Videha@}¦ is the name of a people who are not mentioned
<div n="lb"/>before the Brāhmaṇa period. In the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa<sup>1</sup>
<div n="lb"/>the legend of {@Videgha Māthava@} preserves clearly a tradition
<div n="lb"/>that in Videha culture came from the Brahmins of the West,
<div n="lb"/>and that {@Kosala@} was brahminized before Videha. The Videhas,
<div n="lb"/>however, derived some fame later from the culture of their king
<div n="lb"/>{@Janaka,@} who figures in the Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upaniṣad<sup>2</sup> as one of
<div n="lb"/>the leading patrons of the Brahman doctrine. In the Kauṣītaki
<div n="lb"/>Upaniṣad<sup>3</sup> the Videhas are joined with the {@Kāśis;@} in the list of
<div n="lb"/>peoples in the Aitareya Brāhmaṇa<sup>4</sup> the Videhas are passed
<div n="lb"/>over, probably because, with Kosala and Kāśi, they are included
<div n="lb"/>in the term {@Prācyas,@} ‘easterners.’ Again, in the Śāṅkhāyana
<div n="lb"/>Śrauta Sūtra<sup>5</sup> it is recorded that the Kāśi, Kosala, and Videha
<div n="lb"/>kingdoms had each the one Purohita, {@Jala Jātūkarṇya;@} and
<div n="lb"/>in another passage of the same text<sup>6</sup> the connexion between
<div n="lb"/>the Videha king, {@Para Āṭṇāra,@} and the Kosala king, {@Hiraṇya-@}
<div n="lb"/>{@nābha,@} is explained, while the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa<sup>7</sup> speaks of
<div n="lb"/>Para Āṭṇāra as the Kosala king, descendant of Hiraṇyanābha.
<div n="P"/>Another king of Videha was {@Namī Sāpya,@} mentioned in the
<div n="lb"/>Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa.<sup>8</sup> In the Saṃhitās of the Yajurveda<sup>9</sup>
<div n="lb"/>‘cows of Videha’ seem to be alluded to, though the com-
<div n="lb"/>mentator on the Taittirīya Saṃhitā merely takes the adjective
<div n="lb"/>{%vaidehī%} as ‘having a splendid body’ ({%viśiṣṭa-deha-sambandhinī%}),
<div n="lb"/>and the point of a place name in the expression is not very
<div n="lb"/>obvious. The Videhas also occur in the Baudhāyana Śrauta
<div n="lb"/>Sūtra<sup>10</sup> in Brāhmaṇa-like passages.
<div n="P"/>The boundary of Kosala and Videha was the {@Sadānīrā,@}
[Page2-299+ 52]
<div n="lb"/>probably the modern Gandak<sup>11</sup> (the Kondochates of the Greek
<div n="lb"/>geographers), which, rising in Nepal, flows into the Ganges
<div n="lb"/>opposite Patna. Videha itself corresponds roughly to the
<div n="lb"/>modern Tirhut.
