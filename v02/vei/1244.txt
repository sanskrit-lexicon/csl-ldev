{@Dakṣiṇā-patha@}¦ (lit., ‘the road to the south’), ‘the south
<div n="lb"/>country,’ is found, probably as a designation of the Deccan,
<div n="lb"/>as early as the Baudhāyana Dharma Sūtra,<sup>1</sup> coupled with
[Page1-337+ 45]
<div n="lb"/>Surāṣṭra. A similar expression is {%dakṣiṇā padā,%} ‘with south-
<div n="lb"/>ward foot,’ in the Rigveda,<sup>2</sup> referring to the place where the exile
<div n="lb"/>({%parā-vṛj%}) goes on being expelled. This no doubt simply means
<div n="lb"/>‘the south’ beyond the limits of the recognized Āryan world,
<div n="lb"/>which even as late as the Kauṣītaki Upaniṣad<sup>3</sup> appears as
<div n="lb"/>bounded by the Vindhyas on the south.
