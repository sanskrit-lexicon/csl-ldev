<L>3399<pc>2-407<k1>श्वश्रू<k2>श्वश्रू
{@Śvaśrū@}¦ denotes ‘mother-in-law’ of the husband<sup>1</sup> as well as
of the wife.<sup>2</sup> She, together with her husband, if he became
unable to manage the family,<sup>3</sup> fell under the daughter-in-law's
sway, but otherwise was entitled to regard.<sup>4</sup> The gambler in
the Rigveda<sup>5</sup> complains of his having lost the favour of his
wife's mother as one of the misfortunes brought upon him by
dicing.
<F>1) Rv. x. 85, 46; Av. xiv. 2, 26.</F>
<F>2) Rv. x. 34, 3.</F>
<F>3) Rv. x. 85, 46.</F>
<F>4) Av. xiv. 2, 26.</F>
<F>5) Rv. x. 34, 3.
