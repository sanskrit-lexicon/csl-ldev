1. {@Parvata@}¦ in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> and the Atharvaveda<sup>2</sup> is con-
joined with {%giri%} in the sense of ‘hill’ or ‘mountain.’ From
the Rigveda<sup>3</sup> onwards<sup>4</sup> it is common in this sense as connected
with the waters of rivers which flow in the hills.<sup>5</sup> The legend
of the mountains having wings is already found in the
Saṃhitās.<sup>6</sup> In the Kauṣītaki Upaniṣad<sup>7</sup> are mentioned the
southern ({%dakṣiṇa%}) and the northern ({%uttara%}) mountains, evidently
in allusion to the Himālaya and the Vindhya ranges. The
plants ({%oṣadhi%}) and aromatic products ({%añjana%}) of the mountains
are referred to in the Atharvaveda,<sup>8</sup> and their mineral treasures
in the Rigveda.<sup>9</sup>
