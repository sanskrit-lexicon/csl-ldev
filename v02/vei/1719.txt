<L>1719<pc>1-502<k1>पर्यङ्क<k2>पर्यङ्क
{@Pary-aṅka@}¦ is the name of the seat of the Brahman in the
Kauṣītaki Upaniṣad.<sup>1</sup> It seems to correspond to what is else-
where called {@Āsandi;@}<sup>2</sup> as used in the Upaniṣad, it can, how-
ever, hardly mean a long seat for reclining on, but rather a
throne.<sup>3</sup>

Aitareya Brāhmaṇa, viii. 5, 6, 12.</F>

401; Zimmer, {%Altindisches Leben,%} 155;
