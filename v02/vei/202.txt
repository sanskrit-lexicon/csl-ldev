<div n="lb"/>onwards to denote ‘snake.’ Reference is several times<sup>2</sup> made
<div n="lb"/>to its casting its slough. Mention is also made of the serpent's
<div n="lb"/>peculiar movement,<sup>3</sup> which earns for it the designation<sup>4</sup> of
<div n="lb"/>‘the toothed rope’ ({%datvatī rajjuḥ%}). The poisonous character
[Page1-051+ 39]
<div n="lb"/>of its bite is spoken of,<sup>5</sup> as well as the torpidity of the reptile
<div n="lb"/>in winter, when it creeps into the earth.<sup>6</sup> The cast skin is
<div n="lb"/>used as an amulet against highwaymen.<sup>7</sup> Mention is made of
<div n="lb"/>a mythical horse, Paidva, which the Aśvins gave to Pedu as
<div n="lb"/>a protection against snakes,<sup>8</sup> and which is invoked as a
<div n="lb"/>destroyer of serpents.<sup>9</sup> The ichneumon ({%nakula%}) is regarded
<div n="lb"/>as their deadly enemy, and as immune against their poison
<div n="lb"/>through the use of a healing plant,<sup>10</sup> while men kill them with
<div n="lb"/>sticks<sup>11</sup> or strike off their heads.<sup>12</sup>
