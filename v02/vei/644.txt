{@Kurīrin@}¦ (‘having a {@Kurīra@}’) is a word occurring in an
<div n="lb"/>ambiguous passage of the Atharvaveda,<sup>1</sup> in which it may be
<div n="lb"/>taken either as a noun meaning a ‘crested animal,’ perhaps as
<div n="lb"/>Zimmer<sup>2</sup> suggests the ‘peacock,’ or as an epithet of the word
<div n="lb"/>{@Aja,@} ‘goat,’ in which case it might mean ‘horned.’ But even
<div n="lb"/>in the latter alternative a metaphorical application of the word
<div n="lb"/>seems sufficient, just as in the Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa<sup>3</sup> {@Opaśa@}
<div n="lb"/>is used of the horns of cattle, and thus renders unnecessary
<div n="lb"/>the adoption of Geldner's<sup>4</sup> view that the original meaning of
<div n="lb"/>{@Kurīra@} is ‘horn.’
