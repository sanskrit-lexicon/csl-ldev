<L>16<pc>1-010<k1>अघा<k2>अघा
{@Aghā.@}¦ — In the wedding hymn of the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> it is said that
<div n="lb"/>cows are slain in the Aghās, and the wedding takes place at the
<div n="lb"/>{@Arjunīs@} (dual). The Atharvaveda<sup>2</sup> has the ordinary {@Maghās@}
<div n="lb"/>instead. It is impossible to resist the conclusion that the read-
<div n="lb"/>ing of the Rigveda was deliberately altered because of the con-
<div n="lb"/>nection of the slaughter of kine with sin ({%agha%}) — possibly, too,
<div n="lb"/>with a further desire to emphasize the contrast with {%aghnyā,%}
<div n="lb"/>a name for ‘cow.’ Moreover, in the Taittirīya Brāhmaṇa<sup>3</sup>
<div n="lb"/>occurs the formula ‘Svāhā to the Maghās, Svāhā to the
<div n="lb"/>Anaghās.’ See also {@Nakṣatra.@}
