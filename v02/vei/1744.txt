{@Pavīra,@}¦ according to the Nirukta,<sup>1</sup> denotes a ‘lance.’ The
epithet derived from this word, {%pavīravant%} or {%pavīrava,%} which is
found in the Atharvaveda<sup>2</sup> and the Yajurveda Saṃhitās,<sup>3</sup> is used
of the plough, apparently in the sense of ‘having a metal
share.’ The same epithet occurs in the Rigveda<sup>4</sup> applied to a
man, in the sense of ‘having a goad’ or ‘having a spear.’
