<L>228<pc>1-056<k1>आति<k2>आति
{@Āti,@}¦ an aquatic bird. The Apsarases in the legend of
Purūravas and Urvaśī appear to him like Ātis, probably swans.<sup>1</sup>
The birds appear also in the list of animals in the Aśvamedha
(‘horse sacrifice’),<sup>2</sup> where Mahīdhara<sup>3</sup> renders them as the
[Page1-057+ 40]
later Āḍī ({%Turdus ginginianus%}), and Sāyaṇa<sup>4</sup> quotes a view,
according to which the Āti was the Cāṣa, or blue jay ({%Coracias%}
{%indica%}).

maṇa, xi. 5, 1, 4.</F>
<F>2) Taittirīya Saṃhitā, v. 5, 13, 1;
Maītrāyaṇī Saṃhitā, iii. 14, 18; Vāja-
saneyi Saṃhitā, xxiv. 34.</F>
<F>3) On Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā, {%loc. cit.%}</F>
