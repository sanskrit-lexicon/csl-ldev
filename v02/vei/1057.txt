{@Jāyānya,@}¦<sup>1</sup> {@Jāyenya,@}<sup>2</sup> are variant forms of the name of a
disease mentioned in the Atharvaveda and the Taittirīya Saṃ-
hitā. In one passage of the former text<sup>3</sup> it is mentioned with
jaundice ({%harimā%}) and pains in the limbs ({%aṅga-bhedo visalpakaḥ%}).
Zimmer<sup>4</sup> thinks these are its symptoms, and identifies it with a
kind of Yakṣma, or disease of the lungs. Bloomfield<sup>5</sup> prefers
to identify it with syphilis, in accordance with certain indica-
tions in the ritual of the Kauśika Sūtra.<sup>6</sup> Roth conjectures
‘gout,’ but Whitney<sup>7</sup> leaves the nature of the disease doubtful.
