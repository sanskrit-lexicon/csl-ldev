<L>3612<pc>2-456<k1>सुब्रह्मण्य<k2>सुब्रह्मण्य
{@Su-brahmaṇya@}¦ in the Brāhmaṇas<sup>1</sup> denotes a priest who
officiates as one of the three assistants of the Udgātṛ (see {@Ṛtvij@}).
His office is Subrahmaṇyā.<sup>2</sup>
<F>1) Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa, xxv. 4, 6;
18, 4; Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa, iii. 3, 4, 9.
{%Cf.%} Weber, {%Indische Studien,%} 10, 362, 374.</F>
<F>2) Aitareya Brāhmaṇa, vi. 3, 1-7. 11,
12; Kauṣītaki Brāhmaṇa, xxvii. 6, etc.
The priest himself is so styled, Aitareya
Brāhmaṇa, vii. 1, 2; Pañcaviṃśa
Brāhmaṇa, xviii. 9, 19, etc.</F>
