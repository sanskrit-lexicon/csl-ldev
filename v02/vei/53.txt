<L>53<pc>1-019<k1>अदृष्ट<k2>अदृष्ट
{@A-dṛṣṭa.@}¦ — ‘The unseen’ is a term used in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup>
<div n="lb"/>and the Atharvaveda<sup>2</sup> to designate a species of vermin. The
<div n="lb"/>sun is also described as ‘the slayer of the unseen’ ({%adṛṣṭa-han%}),<sup>3</sup>
<div n="lb"/>and as a counterpart a ‘seen’ ({%dṛṣṭa%}) is mentioned.<sup>4</sup> In one
<div n="lb"/>passage<sup>5</sup> the epithets ‘seen’ and ‘unseen’ are applied to the
<div n="lb"/>worm ({@Kṛmi@}), their use being no doubt due to the widespread
<div n="lb"/>theory of diseases being due to worms, whether discernable by
<div n="lb"/>examination or not.<sup>6</sup>
