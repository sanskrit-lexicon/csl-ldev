<L>1780<pc>1-518<k1>पार<k2>पार
{@Pāra,@}¦ in accordance with its derivation ({%pṛ,%} ‘bring across’),
denotes the ‘farther bank’ of a river or stream, in which sense<sup>1</sup>
it occurs in the Rigveda<sup>2</sup> and later.<sup>3</sup>
<F>1) It also often has the generalized
sense of ‘extreme limit’ or ‘end,’ as
in i. 92, 6 ({%tamasas,%} ‘of darkness’);
v. 54, 10 ({%adhavanaḥ,%} ‘of a road’).</F>
<F>2) i. 121, 13 ({%nāvyānām,%} ‘of streams’);
viii. 96, 11 ({%nadīnām%}); i. 167, 2 ({%samud-%}
{%rasya%}); x. 155, 3 ({%sindhoḥ%}), etc.</F>
<F>3) Taittirīya Saṃhitā, vii. 5, 1, 2. 3;
Kāṭhaka Saṃhitā, xxxiii. 5; Śatapatha
Brāhmaṇa, iii. 6, 2, 4 ({%salilasya%});
Aitareya Brāhmaṇa, viii. 21 ({%pāra-kāma,%}
‘desiring the farther bank’), etc.</F>
