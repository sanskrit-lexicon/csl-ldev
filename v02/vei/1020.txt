{@Jambha@}¦ occurs twice in the Atharvaveda as the name of a
disease or a demon of disease. In one passage<sup>1</sup> it is said to be
cured by the {@Jaṅgiḍa@} plant; in the other<sup>2</sup> it is described as
{%samhanuḥ,%} ‘bringing the jaws together.’ Weber<sup>3</sup> argued from
[Page1-277+ 44]
the Kauśika Sūtra<sup>4</sup> that it was a child's ailment, especially
‘teething.’ Bloomfield<sup>5</sup> considers it to mean ‘convulsions,’
while Caland<sup>6</sup> thinks it denotes ‘tetanus.’ Whitney<sup>7</sup> decides
for ‘lockjaw’ or ‘convulsions.’
