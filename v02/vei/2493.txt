{@Muṇḍibha Audanya@}¦<sup>1</sup> or {@Audanyava@}<sup>2</sup> is the name of a man
in the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa<sup>1</sup> and the Taittirīya Brāhmaṇa.<sup>2</sup>
<F>1) Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa, xiii. 3, 5, 4.
Apparently the word is a patronymic,
‘son of Udanya’ (so Eggeling, {%Sacred%}
{%Books of the East,%} 44, 341, n. 1), or
‘son of Odana’ (so St. Petersburg
Dictionary, {%s.v.%}).</F>
<F>2) Taittirīya Brāhmaṇa, iii. 9, 15, 3
(‘descendant of Udanyu’).</F>
