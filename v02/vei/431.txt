{@Ṛśya@}¦ — This is the correct<sup>1</sup> spelling of a word that occurs in
the Rigveda<sup>2</sup> and the later literature<sup>3</sup> meaning ‘stag,’ the
feminine being {@Rohit.@}<sup>4</sup> Apparently deer were caught in pits
({%ṛśya-da%}).<sup>5</sup> The procreative power of the stag ({%ārśya vṛṣṇya%})
was celebrated.<sup>6</sup>
<F>1) It appears in Av. iv. 4. 7, as Ṛśa;
as Ṛṣya in Maitrāyaṇī Saṃhitā, iii. 14,
9. 18.</F>
<F>2) viii. 4, 10.</F>
<F>3) Av. iv. 4. 5. 7; v. 14, 3; i. 18. 4
({%ṛśya-pad%}); Vājasaneyi Samhitā, xxiv.
27.37; Aitareya Brāhmaṇa, iii. 33;
citation in Śāṅkhāyana Śrauta Sūtra,
viii. 25, 8, etc.</F>
<F>4) Av. iv. 4, 7.</F>
<F>5) Rv. x. 39, 8.</F>
<F>6) Av. iv. 4, 5.
