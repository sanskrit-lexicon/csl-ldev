{@Dīrghāyutva,@}¦ ‘longevity,’ is a constant object of the prayers
<div n="lb"/>of the Vedic Indians,<sup>1</sup> and length of life is never deprecated in
<div n="lb"/>the Saṃhitās and Brāhmaṇas, while the Atharvaveda<sup>2</sup> is full
<div n="lb"/>of spells intended to prolong existence ({%āyuṣyāṇi%}).
<F>1) Rv. x. 62, 2; Av. i. 22, 2, etc.;
<div n="lb"/>Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā, xviii. 6; Śata-
<div n="lb"/>patha Brāhmaṇa, i. 9, 1, 13, etc. So
<div n="lb"/>the adjective {%dīrghāyus,%} Rv. iv. 15, 9.
<div n="lb"/>10; x. 85, 39; Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā,
<div n="lb"/>xii. 100, etc. The Brāhmaṇas regularly
<div n="lb"/>express the reward for ritual actions
<div n="lb"/>by the phrase {%sarvam āyur eti,%} ‘he lives
<div n="lb"/>all his days.’ The ideal of life is 100
<div n="lb"/>years. See Weber, {%Indische Studien,%} 17,
<div n="lb"/>193; {%Festgruss an Roth,%} 137; Lanman,
<div n="lb"/>{%Sanskrit Reader,%} 384.</F>
<F>2) ii. 13; 28; 29; vii. 32, and many
<div n="lb"/>other hymns. See Bloomfield, {%Hymns%}
<div n="lb"/>{%of the Atharvaveda,%} 49 {%et seq.; Atharva-%}
<div n="lb"/>{%veda,%} 63-65.</F>
