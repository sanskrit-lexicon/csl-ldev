<L>1355<pc>1-374<k1>दृषद्वती<k2>दृषद्वती
{@Dṛṣadvatī,@}¦ ‘stony,’ is the name of a river which flows into
<div n="lb"/>the {@Sarasvatī@} after running for a time parallel to it. It is
<div n="lb"/>mentioned in the Rigveda,<sup>1</sup> along with the Sarasvatī and the
<div n="lb"/>{@Āpayā,@} as the scene of action of the Bharata princes. In the
<div n="lb"/>Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa<sup>2</sup> and later<sup>3</sup> the Dṛṣadvatī and the
<div n="lb"/>Sarasvatī are the scene of special sacrifices. In Manu<sup>4</sup> these
<div n="lb"/>two rivers form the western boundary of the Middle Country.

<F>2) xxv. 10, 13.</F>
