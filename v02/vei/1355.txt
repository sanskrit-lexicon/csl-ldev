<L>1355<pc>1-374<k1>दृषद्वती<k2>दृषद्वती
{@Dṛṣadvatī,@}¦ ‘stony,’ is the name of a river which flows into
the {@Sarasvatī@} after running for a time parallel to it. It is
mentioned in the Rigveda,<sup>1</sup> along with the Sarasvatī and the
{@Āpayā,@} as the scene of action of the Bharata princes. In the
Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa<sup>2</sup> and later<sup>3</sup> the Dṛṣadvatī and the
Sarasvatī are the scene of special sacrifices. In Manu<sup>4</sup> these
two rivers form the western boundary of the Middle Country.
<F>1) iii. 23, 4.</F>
<F>2) xxv. 10, 13.</F>
<F>3) Kātyāyana Srauta Sūtra, xxiv. 6, 6,
38; Lāṭyāyana Śrauta Sūtra, x. 19, 4.</F>
<F>4) ii. 17.
