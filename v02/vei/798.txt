<L>798<pc>1-208<k1>क्षिति<k2>क्षिति
{@Kṣiti@}¦ is in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> a regular word for ‘dwelling,’ and in
particular the {%kṣitir dhruvā,%} ‘the secure dwelling,’ is mentioned<sup>2</sup>
in a context that shows it, to be equivalent to the {@Vṛjana@} or
{@Grāma@} regarded as a stronghold. From this sense is developed
that of the peoples occupying the settlements,<sup>3</sup> and in particular
the five peoples<sup>4</sup> (for whom see {@Pañca Janāsaḥ@}).
<F>1) i. 65, 3; iii. 13, 4; v. 37. 4,
etc.</F>
<F>2) i. 73, 4 ({%cf.%} 2); vii. 88, 7. See
Zimmer, {%Altindisches Leben,%} 142.</F>
<F>3) Rv. iii. 38, 1; iv. 24, 4; 38, 5; v. 1,
10, etc.</F>
<F>4) i. 7, 9; 176, 3; v. 35, 2; vi. 46, 7;
vii. 75, 4; 79, 1.</F>
