<L>2955<pc>2-301<k1>विपाश्<k2>विपाश्
{@Vi-pāś@}¦ (‘fetterless’) is the name of a river mentioned twice in
<div n="lb"/>the Rigveda.<sup>1</sup> It is the modern Beās in the Panjab, the Hyphasis,
<div n="lb"/>Hypanis or Bipasis of the Greeks. Its small importance for the
<div n="lb"/>Vedic Indians is indicated by the fact that it is never mentioned
<div n="lb"/>in the earlier Vedic literature except in two hymns of the Rigveda.
<div n="lb"/>The Nirukta<sup>2</sup> preserves the notice that its earlier name was
<div n="lb"/>{@Uruñjirā,@} while the Gopatha Brāhmaṇa<sup>3</sup> places in the middle
<div n="lb"/>of it the {%Vasiṣṭha-śilāḥ.%} Pāṇinī<sup>4</sup> mentions the name, which
<div n="lb"/>otherwise in post-Vedic literature appears as Vipāśā. This
<div n="lb"/>river has changed its course considerably since ancient times.<sup>5</sup>
<F>1) iii. 33, 1. 3; iv. 30, 11. Yāska,
<div n="lb"/>Nirukta, xi. 48, sees in the latter
<div n="lb"/>passage an adjective {%vi-pāśin,%} but this
<div n="lb"/>is very improbable. See Oldenberg,
<div n="lb"/>{%Ṛgveda-Noten,%} 1, 294.</F>
<F>2) ix. 26. The Vipāś is also mentioned
