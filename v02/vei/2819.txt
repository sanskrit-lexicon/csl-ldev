<L>2819<pc>2-245<k1>वराह<k2>वराह
{@Varāha,@}¦ ‘boar,’ is found in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> and later.<sup>2</sup> The
god Rudra is described as the ‘boar of heaven.’<sup>3</sup> The use of
dogs to hunt the boar is once alluded to.<sup>4</sup> The variant form
of the word, Varāhu, is not used except metaphorically of
divinities.<sup>5</sup>
<F>1) i. 61, 7; viii. 77, 10; ix. 97, 7;
x. 28, 4 ({%cf.%} {@Kroṣṭṛ@}), etc.</F>
<F>2) Av. viii. 7, 23; xii. 1, 48; Kāṭhaka
Saṃhitā, viii. 2; xxv. 2, etc.; Maitrā-
yaṇi Saṃhitā, iii. 14. 19, etc.</F>
<F>3) Rv. i. 114, 5. {%Cf.%} Taittirīya Saṃ-
hitā, vi. 2, 4, 2; vii. 1, 5, 1. etc.</F>
<F>4) Rv. x. 86, 4, an obscure passage.</F>
<F>5) Rv. i. 88, 5; 121, 11; Taittirīya
Āraṇyaka, i. 9, 4.
