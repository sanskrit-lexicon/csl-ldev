{@Halikṣṇa@}¦<sup>1</sup> or {@Halīkṣṇa@}<sup>2</sup> is mentioned as one of the victims at
the Aśvamedha (‘horse sacrifice’) in the Yajurveda Saṃhitās.
The commentator Mahīdhara<sup>3</sup> thinks that it is a kind of lion,
Sāyaṇa<sup>4</sup> that a green Caṭaka bird or a lion ({%tṛṇa-hiṃsa%})<sup>5</sup> is
meant. In the Atharvaveda<sup>6</sup> Halīkṣṇa seems to be some par-
ticular intestine, but Weber<sup>7</sup> thinks it may mean ‘gall.’

7, 23, 1.</F>
