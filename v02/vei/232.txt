{@Ātharvaṇa,@}¦ a patronymic formed from the name of the
<div n="lb"/>mythic Atharvan, is found normally in the plural neuter as a
<div n="lb"/>designation of the hymns of the Atharvans. This use appears
<div n="lb"/>in the late nineteenth book of the Atharvaveda,<sup>1</sup> and in the
<div n="lb"/>Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa.<sup>2</sup> In the singular the expression
<div n="lb"/>Ātharvaṇa (Veda), though not occurring till the Chāndogya
<div n="lb"/>Upaniṣad,<sup>3</sup> is earlier than the term ‘Atharvaveda,’ which is
<div n="lb"/>first found in the Sūtras.<sup>4</sup> In the Nidāna Sūtra<sup>5</sup> Ātharvaṇikas,
<div n="lb"/>or ‘followers of the Atharvaveda,’ appear.
<div n="P"/>Specific but mainly mythical Ātharvaṇas are {@Kabandha,@}
