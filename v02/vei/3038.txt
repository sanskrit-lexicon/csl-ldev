{@Vṛṣa-daṃśa,@}¦ ‘strong-toothed,’ is the name of the cat in the
<div n="lb"/>Yajurveda Saṃhitās,<sup>1</sup> where it figures as a victim at the Aśva-
<div n="lb"/>medha (‘horse sacrifice’). It also appears in the Pañcaviṃśa
<div n="lb"/>Brāhmaṇa;<sup>2</sup> the fact that the sneeze of the cat is here referred
<div n="lb"/>to renders it likely that the animal was already tamed. Geldner<sup>3</sup>
<div n="lb"/>sees a house cat in the animal alluded to in a hymn of the
<div n="lb"/>Atharvaveda<sup>4</sup> by a set of curious epithets, including {%vṛṣadatī,%}
<div n="lb"/>‘strong-toothed,’ but Whitney<sup>5</sup> decisively rejects the idea that
<div n="lb"/>the hymn refers to the domestic cat.
