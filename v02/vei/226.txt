<L>226<pc>1-056<k1>आण्डीक<k2>आण्डीक
{@Āṇḍīka@}¦ (‘bearing eggs’) is a term found in the Atharvaveda<sup>1</sup>
denoting an edible plant, apparently with fruit or leaves of egg
shape ({%āṇḍa%}), akin to the lotus.
<F>1) iv. 34, 5; v. 17, 16. In the first
passage the Paippalāda version has
{%pauṇḍarīka%} in its place; Whitney,
Translation of the Atharvaveda, 207.
{%Cf.%} Zimmer, {%Altindisches Leben,%} 70;
Weber, {%Indische Studien,%} 18, 138.</F>
