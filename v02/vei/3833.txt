<L>3833<pc>2-509<k1>ह्रूडु<k2>ह्रूडु
{@Hrūḍu@}¦ is a word of unknown meaning applied to {@Takman@} in
the Atharvaveda.<sup>1</sup> It is variously spelled in the manuscript as
{%hruḍa, hūḍu, rūḍu,%} and so forth; the Paippalāda recension
reads {%huḍu,%} ‘ram.’ Henry<sup>2</sup> has conjectured that the word is
the equivalent of a proto-Semitic {%ḥarāḍu,%} ‘gold’ (Assyrian
{%huraçu%} and Hebrew {%ḥarāç%}), while Halévy<sup>3</sup> suggests that it may
be the Greek <lang n="greek">χλωρός,</lang> ‘greenish-yellow’; but both conjectures
are highly improbable.<sup>4</sup> Weber<sup>5</sup> thinks ‘cramp’ is meant.
