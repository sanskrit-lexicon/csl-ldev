{@Kṣattṛ@}¦ is a word of frequent occurrence in the later Saṃhitās
<div n="lb"/>and Brāhmaṇas, denoting a member of the royal entourage,
<div n="lb"/>but the sense is somewhat uncertain. In the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> it is
<div n="lb"/>used of a god as the ‘distributor’ of good things to his
<div n="lb"/>worshippers; the same sense seems to be found in the Athar-
<div n="lb"/>vaveda<sup>2</sup> and elsewhere.<sup>3</sup> In one passage of the Vājasaneyi
<div n="lb"/>Saṃhitā<sup>4</sup> the interpretation ‘doorkeeper’ is given by the com-
<div n="lb"/>mentator Mahīdhara, a sense which seems possible in other
<div n="lb"/>passages,<sup>5</sup> while Sāyaṇa ascribes to it in one passage of the
<div n="lb"/>Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa<sup>6</sup> the more dignified meaning of {%antaḥ-%}
<div n="lb"/>{%purādhyakṣa,%} ‘a chamberlain.’ In other passages,<sup>7</sup> again, the
<div n="lb"/>sense of ‘charioteer’ is not unlikely. Later the Kṣattṛ was
<div n="lb"/>regarded as a man of mixed caste.<sup>8</sup>
