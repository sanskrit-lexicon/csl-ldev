<L>1698<pc>1-496<k1>परिवित्त<k2>परिवित्त
{@Pari-vitta@}¦ denotes an ‘elder brother who is not married
when his younger brother is.’ The term occurs in the list of
sinful persons in the Yajurveda Saṃhitās<sup>1</sup> as well as in the
Atharvaveda,<sup>2</sup> where Ludwig<sup>3</sup> needlessly proposes to read
{%parivettā,%} the ‘younger brother who marries before his eldest
brother.’ The name for the younger brother in the older texts
is Parivividāna.<sup>4</sup>
