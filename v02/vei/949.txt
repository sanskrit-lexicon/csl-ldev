<L>949<pc>1-252<k1>चक्रवाक<k2>चक्रवाक
{@Cakra-vāka@}¦ is the name, apparently derived from the nature
of its cry, of a species of gander ({%Anas casarca%}), the modern
Chakwā, as it is called in Hindī, or Brahmany duck in English.<sup>1</sup>
It is mentioned in the Rigveda<sup>2</sup> and in the list of victims at the
[Page1-253+ 58]
Aśvamedha, or horse sacrifice, in the Yajurveda,<sup>3</sup> while in the
Atharvaveda<sup>4</sup> it already appears as the type of conjugal fidelity,
its characteristic in the classical literature.
