{@Madhyaṃ-dina,@}¦ ‘mid-day,’ is a frequent designation of time
in the Rigveda,<sup>1</sup> the later Saṃhitās,<sup>2</sup> and the Brāhmaṇas.<sup>3</sup>
{%Cf.%} {@Ahan.@}
<F>1) iv. 28, 3; viii. 1, 29; 13, 13; 27,
19; x. 151, 5, etc.</F>
<F>2) Av. ix. 6, 46; Taittirīya Saṃhitā,
vi. 2, 5, 4, etc.</F>
<F>3) Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa, xv. 9, 16;
Taittirīya Brāhmaṇa, i. 5, 3, 2; Śata-
patha Brāhmaṇa, ii. 2, 3, 9; Chāndogya
Upaniṣad, ii. 9, 6; 14, 1, etc. The
word is sometimes used as an abbrevia-
tion for the ‘midday libation’ (like
{%mittag%} in German for ‘midday meal’)
in Aitareya Brāhmaṇa, iii. 10, 2, 5;
Kauṣītaki Brāhmaṇa, xxix. 8.</F>
