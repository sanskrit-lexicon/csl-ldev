<L>2055<pc>2-045<k1>प्राचीनयोग्य<k2>प्राचीनयोग्य
{@Prācīna-yogya,@}¦ ‘descendant of Prācīnayoga,’ is the name
of a teacher, a pupil of {@Pārāśarya,@} in the first Vaṃśa (list of
teachers) in the Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upaniṣad.<sup>1</sup> A Prācīnayogya
is mentioned also in the Chāndogya<sup>2</sup> and the Taittirīya<sup>3</sup>
Upaniṣads, and the same patronymic is found in the Śatapatha
Brāhmaṇa<sup>4</sup> and in the Jaiminīya Upaniṣad Brāhmaṇa (see
{@Puluṣa, Satyayajña,@}<sup>5</sup> {@Somaśuṣma@}).
<F>1) ii. 6, 2 (Kāṇva) .</F>
<F>2) v. 13, 1.</F>
<F>3) i. 6, 2.</F>
<F>4) (Of {@Satyayajña Pauluṣi@}) x. 6, 1, 5;
(of {@Śauceya@}) xi. 5, 3, 1. 8 ({%cf.%} Gopatha
Brāhmaṇa, i. 3, 11). {%Cf.%} Weber, {%Indische%}
{%Studien,%} 1, 61; 2, 213; 3, 274.</F>
<F>5) Called Prācīnayoga in i. 39, 1, but
this is probably merely a blunder of
the manuscript.</F>
