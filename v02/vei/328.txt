<L>328<pc>1-082<k1>ईषा<k2>ईषा
{@Īṣā@}¦ denotes the ‘pole of a chariot.’ Normally the chariot had
one pole ({%ekeṣaḥ%}),<sup>1</sup> but sometimes two poles are referred to.<sup>2</sup>
The word is often<sup>3</sup> compounded with {@Yuga,@} ‘yoke,’ into which
it was fastened (see {@Kha@}), and tied with ropes.<sup>4</sup> Exactly how it
was attached to the chariot we do not know.<sup>5</sup> See also {@Ratha.@}
<F>1) Rv. x. 135, 3; iii. 53, 17; viii. 5,
29; Av. viii. 8, 23.</F>
<F>2) {%Cf.%} Av. ii. 8, 4; Satapatha Brāh-
maṇa, i. 1, 2, 12 ({%īṣā-yugāni,%} but in
iii. 9, 4, 3 dual); Kātyāyana Śrauta
Sūtra, vii. 9, 14, etc.</F>
<F>3) Av., {%loc. cit.,%} etc.</F>
<F>4) Rv. x. 60, 8. {%Cf.%} iii. 6, 6.</F>
<F>5) {%Vāṇī%} in Rv. i. 119, 5 is taken by
Zimmer, {%Altindisches Leben,%} 249, to refer
to the front part of the chariot, but it
seems simply to be ‘voice.’</F>
