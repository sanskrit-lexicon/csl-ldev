<L>33<pc>1-013<k1>अज्ञातयक्ष्म<k2>अज्ञातयक्ष्म
{@Ajñāta-yakṣma,@}¦ the ‘unknown sickness,’ is mentioned in
<div n="lb"/>the Rigveda,<sup>1</sup> Atharvaveda,<sup>2</sup> and Kāṭhaka Saṃhitā.<sup>3</sup> It is
<div n="lb"/>referred to in connection with Rājayakṣma. Grohmann<sup>4</sup> thinks
<div n="lb"/>that the two are different forms of disease, hypertrophy and
<div n="lb"/>atrophy, the purpose of the spell in the Rigveda being thus the
<div n="lb"/>removal of all disease. From the Atharvaveda<sup>5</sup> he deduces its
<div n="lb"/>identity with {@Balāsa.@} Zimmer,<sup>6</sup> however, points out that this
[Page1-014+ 42]
<div n="lb"/>conclusion is unjustified, leaving the disease unidentified, which
<div n="lb"/>seems to accord with its name.
