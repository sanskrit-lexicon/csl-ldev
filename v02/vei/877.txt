{@Giri,@}¦ ‘mountain’ or ‘height,’ is a word that occurs repeatedly
<div n="lb"/>in the Rigveda.<sup>1</sup> Thus reference is made to the trees on the
<div n="lb"/>hills, hence called ‘tree-haired’ ({%vṛkṣa-keśāḥ%}),<sup>2</sup> and to the
<div n="lb"/>streams proceeding from the hills to the sea ({%samudra%}).<sup>3</sup> The
<div n="lb"/>term is frequently coupled with the adjectival {%parvata.%}<sup>4</sup> The
<div n="lb"/>Rigveda mentions the waters from the hills,<sup>5</sup> and the Athar-
<div n="lb"/>vaveda<sup>6</sup> refers to the snowy mountains. Actual names of
<div n="lb"/>mountains, as {@Mūjavant, Trikakud, Himavant,@} are very rare.
<div n="lb"/>References to {@Krauñca, Mahāmeru,@} and {@Maināga,@} are confined
<div n="lb"/>to the Taittirīya Āraṇyaka, while {@Nāvaprabhraṃśana@} can no
<div n="lb"/>longer be considered a proper name.<sup>7</sup>
