{@Vāraṇa@}¦ in two passages of the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> is taken by Roth<sup>2</sup>
as an adjective with {@Mṛga,@} meaning ‘wild beast.’ But the
sense intended must have been ‘elephant,’ the usual sense of
Vāraṇa in the classical literature. Probably the feminine
Vāraṇī in the Atharvaveda<sup>3</sup> likewise denotes a ‘female
elephant.’
