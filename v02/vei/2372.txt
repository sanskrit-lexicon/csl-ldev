{@Marud-vṛdhā@}¦<sup>1</sup> is the name of a stream mentioned in the
<div n="lb"/>Nadīstuti (‘Praise of Rivers’) in the Rigveda<sup>2</sup> along with the
<div n="lb"/>{@Asiknī@} (Akesines) and the {@Vitastā@} (Hydaspes). Roth<sup>3</sup> con-
<div n="lb"/>siders that the Marudvṛdhā denotes the stream formed by the
<div n="lb"/>combined waters of these two rivers down to its junction with
<div n="lb"/>the {@Paruṣṇī@} (Ravi), a view accepted by Zimmer.<sup>4</sup> On the
<div n="lb"/>other hand, Ludwig<sup>5</sup> thinks that the Marudvṛdhā designates
[Page2-136+ 57]
<div n="lb"/>the stream formed by the junction of the Paruṣṇī with the
<div n="lb"/>combined waters of the Asiknī and Vitastā, a view which
<div n="lb"/>seems less likely.
<F>1) Literally, ‘rejoicing in the Maruts’
<div n="lb"/>-{%i.e.,%} ‘swollen by the rainy winds.’
