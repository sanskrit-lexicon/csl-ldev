{@Pauta-krata,@}¦ ‘descendant of {@Pūtakratā,@}’ is the metronymic
<div n="lb"/>of a man, apparently {@Dasyave Vṛka,@} in the Rigveda.<sup>1</sup> Schefte-
<div n="lb"/>lowitz<sup>2</sup> proposes to read Pūtakratu with the Kashmir MS. of
<div n="lb"/>the Rigveda, arguing that in the same hymn Pūtakratāyī, the
<div n="lb"/>wife of Pūtakratu, is referred to, and that therefore Pūtakratu
<div n="lb"/>is appropriate, Pūtakratāyī<sup>3</sup> being the feminine, like Manāyī,<sup>4</sup>
<div n="lb"/>for Manāvī. But the ordinary reading in the sense of descendant
<div n="lb"/>is perfectly legitimate, as Oldenberg<sup>5</sup> has pointed out.
