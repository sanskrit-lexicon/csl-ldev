2. {@Yuga@}¦ in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> frequently denotes a ‘generation’;
<div n="lb"/>but the expression {%daśame yuge%} applied to {@Dīrghatamas@} in one
<div n="lb"/>passage<sup>2</sup> must mean ‘tenth decade’ of life.
<div n="P"/>There is no reference in the older Vedic texts to the five-year
<div n="lb"/>cycle (see {@Saṃvatsara@}). The quotation from the Pañcaviṃśa
<div n="lb"/>Brāhmaṇa<sup>3</sup> given in the St. Petersburg Dictionary, and by
<div n="lb"/>Zimmer<sup>4</sup> and others, is merely a citation from a modern text
<div n="lb"/>in the commentary on that work.
<div n="P"/>Nor do the older Vedic texts know of any series of Yugas or
<div n="lb"/>ages such as are usual later. In the Atharvaveda<sup>5</sup> there are
<div n="lb"/>mentioned in order a hundred years, an {%ayuta%} (10,000?), and
<div n="lb"/>then two, three, or four Yugas: the inference from this seems
<div n="lb"/>to be that a Yuga means more than an {%ayuta,%} but is not very
[Page2-193+ 67]
<div n="lb"/>certain. Zimmer<sup>6</sup> adduces a passage from the Rigveda,<sup>7</sup>
<div n="lb"/>but the reference there, whatever it may be,<sup>8</sup> is certainly
<div n="lb"/>not to the four ages ({%cf.%} also {@Triyuga@}).<sup>9</sup> The Taittirīya
<div n="lb"/>Brāhmaṇa<sup>10</sup> recognizes long periods of time — {%e.g.,%} one of
<div n="lb"/>100,000 years.
<div n="P"/>To the four ages, Kali, Dvāpara, Tretā, and Kṛta, there is no
<div n="lb"/>certain reference in Vedic literature, though the names occur as
<div n="lb"/>the designations of throws at dice (see {@Akṣa@}). In the Aitareya
<div n="lb"/>Brāhmaṇa<sup>11</sup> the names occur, but it is not clear that the ages
<div n="lb"/>are really meant. Haug<sup>12</sup> thought that the dice were meant:
<div n="lb"/>this view is at least as probable as the alternative explanation,
<div n="lb"/>which is accepted by Weber,<sup>13</sup> Roth,<sup>14</sup> Wilson,<sup>15</sup> Max Müller,<sup>16</sup>
<div n="lb"/>and Muir.<sup>17</sup> Roth, indeed, believes that the verse is an inter-
<div n="lb"/>polation; but in any case it must be remembered that the
<div n="lb"/>passage is from a late book of the Aitareya Brāhmaṇa. Four
<div n="lb"/>ages — Puṣya, Dvāpara, Khārvā, and Kṛta — are mentioned in
<div n="lb"/>the late Ṣaḍviṃśa Brāhmaṇa,<sup>18</sup> and the Dvāpara in the Gopatha
<div n="lb"/>Brāhmaṇa.<sup>19</sup>
