<L>365<pc>1-092<k1>उपबर्हण<k2>उपबर्हण
{@Upa-barhaṇa@}¦ denotes a ‘pillow’ or ‘cushion,’ especially of a
seat ({%āsandī%}), occurring in the Rigveda,<sup>1</sup> the Atharvaveda,<sup>2</sup> and
the Brāhmaṇas.<sup>3</sup> The feminine Upabarhaṇī is found in the
Rigveda with the same sense, but used metaphorically of the
earth.
<F>1) x. 85, 7.</F>
<F>2) ix. 5, 28; xii. 2, 19. 20; xv. 3, 7.</F>
<F>3) Aitareya Brāhmaṇa, viii. 12; Śata-
patha Brāhmaṇa, xiii. 8, 4, 10; Kauṣī-
taki Upaniṣad, i. 5; Taittirīya Brāh-
maṇa, i. 1, 6, 10; 6, 8, 9; Kāṭhaka
Saṃhitā, xxviii. 4, etc.</F>
