<L>1285<pc>1-350<k1>दान<k2>दान<h>1
1. {@Dāna,@}¦ ‘giving,’ ‘gift,’ is a word of frequent occurrence in
<div n="lb"/>the Rigveda, especially in the Dāna-stutis<sup>1</sup> (‘Praises of Gifts’)
<div n="lb"/>of generous patrons (see {@Dakṣiṇā@}). One of the characteristics
<div n="lb"/>of the {@Brāhmaṇa@} is his right to receive gifts, which it is
<div n="lb"/>obligatory on the other castes to present.<sup>2</sup> The gift of a
<div n="lb"/>daughter ({%kanyāyā dānam%}) was a form of marriage<sup>3</sup> (see {@Vivāha@}),
<div n="lb"/>because in it the girl was ‘given’ away by her father or
<div n="lb"/>brother.
<F>1) The term seems first to occur in
<div n="lb"/>the Bṛhaddevatā, vi. 45, 92, and in
<div n="lb"/>similar works.</F>
<F>2) Satapatha Brāhmaṇa, xi. 5, 7, 1;
<div n="lb"/>Weber, {%Indische Studien,%} 10, 47-61.</F>
<F>3) Nirukta, iii. 4.</F>
