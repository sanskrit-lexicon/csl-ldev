{@Āsaṅga Plāyogi@}¦ is a king who appears in a Dānastuti
<div n="lb"/>(‘Praise of Gifts’) in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> as a generous patron.
<div n="lb"/>Owing, however, to the addition of a curious phallic verse to
<div n="lb"/>the hymn, and its early misunderstanding,<sup>2</sup> a legend was
<div n="lb"/>invented that he lost his manhood and became a woman, but
<div n="lb"/>by the intercession of {@Medhyātithi@} was transformed into a man,
<div n="lb"/>much to the delight of his wife, {@Śaśvatī,@} whose existence is
<div n="lb"/>based on a misunderstanding of the phrase ‘every woman’
<div n="lb"/>({%śaśvatī nārī%}) in the added verse.<sup>3</sup> Another misunderstanding

<div n="lb"/>of the Dānastuti<sup>4</sup> gives him a son Svanad-ratha, really a mere
<div n="lb"/>epithet, and makes him a descendant of Yadu.
