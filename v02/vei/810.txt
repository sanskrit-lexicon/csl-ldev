<L>810<pc>1-212<k1>क्षोणी<k2>क्षोणी
{@Kṣoṇī.@}¦ — This word, when used in the plural, denotes,
according to the St. Petersburg Dictionary and Ludwig,<sup>1</sup> in
several passages of the Rigveda,<sup>2</sup> the free retainers of the king.
Geldner<sup>3</sup> at one time thought it referred to the wives of the
king, pointing to polygamy; but later<sup>4</sup> he concluded that it
means certain divine wives.
<F>1)Translation of the Rigveda, 3, 247.</F>
<F>2) i. 57, 4; 173, 7; viii. 3, 10; 13, 17;
x. 95. 19. In ii. 34, 13; x. 22, 9, the
sense is doubtful.</F>
<F>3) Bezzenberger, {%Beiträge,%} 11, 327.</F>
<F>4) {%Vedische Studien,%} 1, 279, 283.</F>
