<L>87<pc>1-025<k1>अपामार्ग<k2>अपामार्ग
{@Apā-mārga.@}¦ — A plant ({%Achyranthes aspera%}) used frequently<sup>1</sup> in
witchcraft practices, and for medical purposes, especially against
{@Kṣetriya.@} It is described in the Atharvaveda<sup>2</sup> as ‘revertive’
({%punaḥ-sara%}), either, as Roth<sup>3</sup> and Zimmer<sup>4</sup> think, because of
its having reverted leaves (a view also accepted by Whitney<sup>5</sup> ),
[Page1-026+ 46]
or because, as Bloomfield<sup>6</sup> holds, it wards off a spell by
causing it to recoil on its user.
