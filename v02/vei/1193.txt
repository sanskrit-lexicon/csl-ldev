<L>1193<pc>1-324<k1>तैत्तिरीय<k2>तैत्तिरीय
{@Taittirīya@}¦ is the name of one of the divisions of the Black
Yajurveda, which is, however, not found thus described until
the Sūtra period.<sup>1</sup> The school is represented by a Saṃhitā,<sup>2</sup>
a Brāhmaṇa,<sup>3</sup> and an Āraṇyaka,<sup>4</sup> besides an Upaniṣad,<sup>5</sup> which
forms a part of the Āraṇyaka.
<F>1) Anupada Sūtra, ii. 6; vii. 7. 10,
etc. See Weber {%Indian Literature,%} 87
{%et seq.;%} Macdonell, {%Sanskrit Literature,%}
175 {%et seq.;%} von Schroeder, Maitrāyaṇī
Saṃhitā, 1, x {%et seq.%}</F>
<F>2) Edited by Weber, {%Indische Studien,%}
xi, xii, and in the {%Bibliotheca Indica,%}
1854-1899.</F>
<F>3) Edited in the {%Bibliotheca Indica,%}
1855-1870, and in the Ānandāśrama
Series, 1898.</F>
<F>4) Edited in the {%Bibliotheca Indica,%} 1864-
1872, and in the Ānandāśrama Series,
1898.</F>
<F>5) Edited by Roer, 1850, and in the
Ānandāśrama Series, 1889.</F>
