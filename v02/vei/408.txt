<L>408<pc>1-105<k1>उस्र<k2>उस्र
{@Usra,@}¦ m.; {@Usrā,@} f.; {@Usrika,@} m.; {@Usriya,@} m.; {@Usriyā,@} f. All
these words denote a ‘bull’ or a ‘cow,’ occurring frequently in
the Rigveda,<sup>1</sup> and sometimes later,<sup>2</sup> but usually with some
reference to the morning light. In some passages the sense is
doubtful. See {@Go.@}
<F>1) {%Usra,%} Rv. vi. 12, 4; {%usrā,%} i. 3, 8;
viii. 75, 8; 96, 8; ix. 58, 2, etc.;
{%usrika,%} i. 190, 5; {%usriya,%} v. 58, 6 (with
{%vṛṣabhaḥ%}); ix. 74. 3; {%usriyā,%} i. 153, 4;
180, 3; ii. 40, 2, etc. In ix. 70, 6,
{%usriya%} is applied to a calf; and in
ix. 68, 1; 93, 2, {%usriyā%} means ‘milk.’</F>
<F>2) {%Usrau dhūrṣāhau,%} Vājasaneyi Saṃ-
hitā, iv. 33; {%usrā,%} Av. xii. 3, 37; {%usriya,%}
Av. i. 12, 1; {%usriyā,%} Av. ix. 4, 1;
Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā, xxxv. 2. 3. In
Av. v. 20, 1; 28, 3, it means ‘cow-
hide,’ or perhaps in v. 28, 3, ‘milk.’</F>
