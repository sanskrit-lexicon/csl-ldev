<L>348<pc>1-086<k1>उदान<k2>उदान
{@Udāna@}¦ is usually the fifth of the vital breaths ({@Prāṇa@}) when
<div n="lb"/>five are enumerated.<sup>1</sup> Sometimes<sup>2</sup> it appears as the second,
<div n="lb"/>coming after Prāṇa, and followed by Vyāna or Samāna.
<div n="lb"/>Again,<sup>3</sup> it is found simply opposed to Prāṇa, or it simply follows
<div n="lb"/>Prāṇa and Apāna.<sup>4</sup> In the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa<sup>5</sup> it is treated
<div n="lb"/>as the breath that consumes food, a notion traceable in
<div n="lb"/>the later Upaniṣads,<sup>6</sup> while it is also regarded as the wind
<div n="lb"/>that rises up by the throat,<sup>7</sup> and leads the spirit forth at
<div n="lb"/>death.<sup>8</sup>
