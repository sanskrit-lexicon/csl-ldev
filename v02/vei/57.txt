{@Adhi-rāja.@}¦ — The word occurs fairly often throughout the
<div n="lb"/>early literature,<sup>1</sup> denoting ‘overlord’ among kings or princes.
[Page1-020+ 47]
<div n="lb"/>In no passage is it clear that a real over-king is meant, as
<div n="lb"/>the word {%rājan%} may mean king or merely prince, a person of
<div n="lb"/>royal blood. On the whole it seems most probable that the
<div n="lb"/>word connotes no more than ‘king’ as opposed to ‘prince.’
