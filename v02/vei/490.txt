{@Auśija,@}¦ ‘descendant of Uśij,’ is a patronymic clearly applied
<div n="lb"/>to {@Kakṣīvant@} in the Rigveda.<sup>1</sup> It is also applied to {@Ṛjiśvan,@}<sup>2</sup>
<div n="lb"/>but Ludwig<sup>3</sup> thinks that the correct reading of the passage in
<div n="lb"/>question is {%auśijasyarjiśvā,%} ‘Ṛjiśvan, son of Auśija.’ In one
<div n="lb"/>verse<sup>4</sup> Auśija and Kakṣīvant are both mentioned, but in such a
<div n="lb"/>way that two different persons must apparently be meant. In
<div n="lb"/>other passages where the patronymic occurs alone, it is doubtful
<div n="lb"/>who is meant, or whether a proper name at all is intended.<sup>5</sup>
<div n="lb"/>Kakṣīvant Auśija appears also in the Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa<sup>6</sup>
<div n="lb"/>and elsewhere.
