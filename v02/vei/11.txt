<L>11<pc>1-006<k1>अगस्त्य<k2>अगस्त्य
{@Agastya.@}¦ — This is the name of a sage, of mythical character,
who plays a great part in the later literature. He was a {@Māna,@}<sup>1</sup>
and therefore is called {@Mānya@}<sup>2</sup> and son of Māna, and only once
is there a reference<sup>3</sup> to the legend prevalent in latter times that
he was a son of Mitra and Varuṇa.
<div n="P"/>His greatest feat was the reconciliation of Indra and the
Maruts after Indra had been annoyed at his proposing to give
the Maruts an offering to the exclusion of Indra. This feat is
the subject of three hymns of the Rigveda,<sup>4</sup> and is often referred
[Page1-007+ 62]
to in the Brāhmaṇas,<sup>5</sup> though the exact details and significance
of the legend are variously treated by Oldenberg,<sup>6</sup> Sieg,<sup>7</sup> Hertel,<sup>8</sup>
and von Schroeder.<sup>9</sup>
<div n="P"/>He also appears in a strange dialogue with Lopāmudrā in
the Rigveda,<sup>10</sup> which appears to show him as an ascetic who
finally yields to temptation. Von {@Schroeder@}<sup>11</sup> regards it as
a ritual drama of vegetation magic.
<div n="P"/>In another passage of the Rigveda<sup>12</sup> he appears as helping
in the Aśvins’ gift of a leg to Viśpalā. Sāyaṇa holds that be
was the Purohita of {@Khela,@} and Sieg<sup>13</sup> accepts this view, while
Pischel<sup>14</sup> thinks that Khela is a deity, Vivasvant.
<div n="P"/>Geldner<sup>15</sup> shows from the Rigveda<sup>16</sup> that Agastya, as brother
of Vasiṣṭha — both being miraculous sons of Mitra and Varuṇa
-introduces {@Vasiṣṭha@} to the {@Tṛtsus.@} There are two other
references to Agastya in the Rigveda, the one<sup>17</sup> including him
in a long list of persons, the other alluding to his sister's sons
({%nadbhyaḥ%}),<sup>18</sup> apparently {@Bandhu,@} etc. In the Atharvaveda<sup>19</sup>
he appears as connected with witchcraft, and in a long list of
sages.<sup>20</sup> In the Maitrāyaṇī Saṃhitā<sup>21</sup> cows, with a peculiar
mark on their ears ({%viṣṭya-karṇyaḥ%}), are associated with him.
