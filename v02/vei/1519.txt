{@Nara, Nṛ.@}¦ — The general name for ‘man’ in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup>
and later<sup>2</sup> is Nṛ, while Nara<sup>3</sup> is found occasionally in the later
Saṃhitās and the Brāhmaṇas.<sup>4</sup>
<F>1) i. 25, 5; 167, 20; 178, 3; ii. 34, 6;
iii. 16, 4, etc.</F>
<F>2) Av. ii. 9, 2; ix. 1, 3; xiv. 2, 9;
Aitareya Brāhmaṇa, iii. 34; vi. 27. 32,
etc.</F>
<F>3) This form of the word, common in
the post-Vedic language, is secondary,
having originated from cases like {%nar-%}
{%am,%} understood as {%nara-m;%} but its origin
goes back to the Indo-Iranian period.
See Brugmann, {%Grundriss,%} 2, 106. {%Cf.%}
Macdonell, {%Vedic Grammar,%} 318, {%a%} 5.</F>
<F>4) Taittirīya Saṃhitā, vii. 1, 12, 1;
Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa, ix. 3, 1, 3;
Nirukta, v. 1, etc.</F>
