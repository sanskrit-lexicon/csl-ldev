<L>288<pc>1-070<k1>आश्विन<k2>आश्विन
{@Āśvina,@}¦ or {@Āśvīna,@} designates, in the Atharvaveda<sup>1</sup> and two
Brāhmaṇas,<sup>2</sup> the length of journey made in a day by a horseman
({%aśvin%}). The exact distance is not defined. In the Atharva it
appears to exceed five leagues, being mentioned immediately
after a distance of three or five {@Yojanas;@} in the Aitareya
Brāhmaṇa the heavenly world is placed at a distance of a
thousand {@Āśvinas.@}
<F>1) vi. 131, 3.</F>
<F>2) Aitareya Brāhmaṇa, ii. 17; Pañ-
caviṃśa Brāhmaṇa in {%Indische Studien,%}
1, 34.</F>
