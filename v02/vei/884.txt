<L>884<pc>1-228<k1>गृत्समद<k2>गृत्समद
{@Gṛtsa-mada@}¦ is the name of a seer to whom the Sarvānu-
kramaṇī attributes the authorship of the second Maṇḍala of the
Rigveda. This tradition is supported by the Aitareya Brāh-
[Page1-229+ 57]
maṇa<sup>1</sup> and the Aitareya Araṇyaka.<sup>2</sup> The Kauṣītaki Brāhmaṇa<sup>3</sup>
speaks of him as a {@Bhārgava,@} ‘descendant of Bhṛgu,’ with a
variant {@Bābhrava,@} ‘descendant of Babhru,’ but the later
tradition keeps to the former patronymic.<sup>4</sup> The Gṛtsamadas
are often mentioned in the second Maṇḍala of the Rigveda,<sup>5</sup>
and are also called Śunahotras,<sup>6</sup> but never Gārtsamadas or
Śaunahotras, and Gṛtsamada himself never occurs there.<sup>7</sup>
