<L>1678<pc>1-492<k1>परस्वन्त्<k2>परस्वन्त्
{@Parasvant@}¦ denotes a large wild animal which Roth<sup>1</sup> con-
jectures to be the wild ass. It is mentioned in the Vṛṣākapi
hymn<sup>2</sup> of the Rigveda, twice in the Atharvaveda,<sup>3</sup> and in the
list of victims at the Aśvamedha (‘horse sacrifice’) in the
Yajurveda Saṃhitās,<sup>4</sup> in all of which passages the sense of
‘wild ass’ is satisfactory. More doubtful is the meaning of the
word {%paraśvā(n)%} in the Kauṣītaki Upaniṣad,<sup>5</sup> where the com-
mentary explains it as ‘serpent.’ It is, of course, quite possible
that the word has nothing to do with {%parasvant.%} Bühler<sup>6</sup>
suggests connexion with the Pāli {%palāsāda,%} ‘rhinoceros.’
