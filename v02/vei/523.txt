<L>523<pc>1-138<k1>करम्भ<k2>करम्भ
{@Karambha@}¦ is the name, from the Rigveda onwards,<sup>1</sup> of a kind
of porridge made of grain ({@Yava@}), which was unhusked, parched
slightly, and kneaded.<sup>2</sup> It was the especial sacrificial portion of
Pūṣan, no doubt in his capacity of an agricultural deity.
Karmabha was also made of barley ({@Upavāka@})<sup>3</sup> or of sesame
({@Tirya@}).<sup>4</sup>
<F>1) Rv. i. 187, 16; iii. 52, 7; vi. 56, 1;
57, 2; viii. 102, 2; Av. iv. 7, 2. 3;
vi. 16, 1; Taittirīya Saṃhitā, iii. 1, 10,
2; vi. 5, 11, 4, etc.</F>
<F>2) Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa, ii. 5, 2, 14;
iv. 2, 4, 18. {%Cf.%} Schrader, {%Prehistoric%}
{%Antiquities,%} 317; Eggeling, {%Sacred Books%}
{%of the East,%} 12, 395, n. 1.</F>
<F>3) Vājasaneyi Saṃhitā, xix. 22.</F>
<F>4) Av. iv. 7, 3, but see Bloomfield,
{%Hymns of the Atharvaveda,%} 377; Whit-
ney, Translation of the Atharvaveda,
155.
