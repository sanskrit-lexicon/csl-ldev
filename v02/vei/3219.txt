{@Śākalya,@}¦ ‘descendant of Śakala,’ is the patronymic of
{@Vidagdha@} in the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa,<sup>1</sup> and of {@Sthavira@} in the
Aitareya<sup>2</sup> and Śāṅkhāyana Āraṇyakas.<sup>3</sup> An undefined Śākalya
[Page2-369+ 35]
is mentioned in the same Āraṇyakas,<sup>4</sup> in the Nirukta,<sup>5</sup> and often
later, as a teacher dealing with the text of the Rigveda.
Weber<sup>6</sup> is inclined to identify Vidagdha with the Śākalya who
is known as the maker of the Pada Pāṭha of the Rigveda, but
Oldenberg<sup>7</sup> thinks that the latter was later than the Brāhmaṇa
period. Geldner<sup>8</sup> identifies the two; this view, however, is
not very probable.<sup>9</sup>
