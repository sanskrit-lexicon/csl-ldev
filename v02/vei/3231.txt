{@Śāṇḍilya,@}¦ ‘descendant of Śaṇḍila,’ is the patronymic of
<div n="lb"/>several teachers (see {@Udara@} and {@Suyajña@}). The most important
<div n="lb"/>Śāṇḍilya is the one cited several times as an authority in the
<div n="lb"/>Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa,<sup>1</sup> where his Agni, or ‘sacrificial fire,’ is
<div n="lb"/>called Śāṇḍila.<sup>2</sup> From this it appears clearly that he was one
<div n="lb"/>of the great teachers of the fire ritual which occupies the fifth
<div n="lb"/>and following books of the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa. In the
<div n="lb"/>Vaṃśa (list of teachers) at the end of the tenth book<sup>3</sup> he is
<div n="lb"/>given as a pupil of {@Kuśri@} and a teacher of {@Vātsya;@} another list
<div n="lb"/>at the end of the last book in the Kāṇva recension<sup>4</sup> gives him
<div n="lb"/>as a pupil of Vātsya, and the latter as a pupil of Kuśri. In the
