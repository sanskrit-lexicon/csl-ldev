<L>100<pc>1-027<k1>अभिप्रतारिन्काक्षसेनि<k2>अभिप्रतारिन्काक्षसेनि
{@Abhi-pratārin Kākṣa-seni@}¦ is mentioned in the Jaiminīya
Upaniṣad Brāhmaṇa,<sup>1</sup> the Chāndogya Upaniṣad,<sup>2</sup> and the
Pañcaviṃśa Brāhmaṇa,<sup>3</sup> as engaged in discussions on philo-
sophy. The Jaiminīya Brāhmaṇa<sup>4</sup> further reports that his
sons divided the property amongst themselves while he was
yet alive. He was a Kuru and a prince.
