<L>2996<pc>2-312<k1>विषाणका<k2>विषाणका
{@Viṣāṇakā@}¦ is the name of a plant in the Atharvaveda.<sup>1</sup> Bloom-
field,<sup>2</sup> however, thinks that the word may merely mean ‘horn.’
It is used as a remedy against the disease Vātīkāra.<sup>3</sup> That
[Page2-313+ 50]
disease is of doubtful character: Zimmer<sup>4</sup> thinks that it is one
‘caused by wounds,’ comparing the adjective {%a-vāta,%} ‘uninjured,’
in the Rigveda,<sup>5</sup> but Bloomfield<sup>6</sup> shows that ‘wind’ in the
body is meant as causing the disease.
