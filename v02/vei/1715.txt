<L>1715<pc>1-500<k1>पर्ण<k2>पर्ण<h>2
2. {@Parṇa@}¦ denotes the tree {%Butea frondosa,%} later usually called
{@Palāśa.@} It occurs in the Rigveda<sup>1</sup> in connexion with the
{@Aśvattha,@} and with that tree as well as the {@Nyagrodha@} in the
Atharvaveda,<sup>2</sup> which mentions both amulets<sup>3</sup> and the cover of
sacrificial dishes<sup>4</sup> as made from its wood. Its use for the
[Page1-501+ 55]
making of sacrificial implements like the ladle ({%juhū%}),<sup>5</sup> or
sacrificial posts,<sup>6</sup> or the small ladle called {%sruva,%}<sup>7</sup> is mentioned.
The Taittirīya Saṃhitā<sup>8</sup> ascribes its origin to the loss of a
feather by the Gāyatrī when winning the Soma. The tree is
also often mentioned elsewhere.<sup>9</sup> Reference too is sometimes
made to its bark ({%parṇa-valka%}).<sup>10</sup>
