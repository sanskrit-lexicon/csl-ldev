{%vidalāmalaka%}¦
<div n="P"/>This term occurs Kauṭ. 2, 15, 18 (text p. 74; transl. Kangle p. 142;
transl. <bot>J. J.</bot> Meyer p. 141; transl. Sham. p. 101); (see App. 3: {%amla%}).
Sham. is of the opinion that it is a synonym of {%āmalaka;%} <bot>J. J.</bot> Meyer
(p. 141, footnote 7) is unsure about the meaning of the word; he seems
to prefer to take the word as a compound and remarks that {%vidala%}
(acc. to the dictionaries) designates <bot>BAUHINIA VARIEGATA</bot>, but acc. to
Gaṇ. the pomegranate ({%dāḍima%}). In my opinion the word {%vidalāmalaka%}
is a compound and the first member is {%vidala%} or {%vidalā.%}
<div n="HI"/>{%vidala:%} (1) <bot>BAUHINIA VARIEGATA LINN.</bot> (MW; Vśs: = {%raktakāñcana%});
(2) <bot>B. PURPUREA LINN.</bot> (Vśs: = {%raktakāñcana%});
(3) the seeds of <bot>PUNICA GRANATUM LINN.</bot> (Vśs).
<div n="HI"/>{%vidalā:%} (1) <bot>IPOMOEA TURPETHUM R. BR.</bot> (MW; Vśs);
(2) = {%sātalā%} (Vśs; variously identified, but acc. to Vśs.
<bot>EUPHORBIA NERIFOLIA LINN.</bot>);
(3) the bark of <bot>PUNICA GRANATUM LINN.</bot> (MW).
<div n="P"/>The best solution is to read {%vidala%} and to take it in the sense of
{%dāḍima%} (<bot>PUNICA GRANATUM LINN.</bot>) because the pomegranate is usually
[Page-601+ 34]
enumerated as one of the fruits called {%phalāmla.%} (Kangle also trans-
lates: pomegranate).
