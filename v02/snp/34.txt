<div n="P"/>This term occurs Kauṭ. 2, 25, 27 (text p. 79; transl. Kangle p. 178;
transl. <bot>J. J.</bot> Meyer p. 187; transl. Sham. p. 133). Sham. renders it by
<bot>SOLANUM MELONGENA</bot>, without any comment. <bot>J. J.</bot> Meyer takes it as
the fragrant bark of <bot>FERONIA ELEPHANTUM</bot> ({%elavāluka%}), but remarks
that the reading {%elāvāluka%} makes it probable that one should translate:
{%elā%} and {%vāluka%} (i. e. {%harivāluka,%} the fragrant bark of {%kapittha%}); Kangle
translates: cardamum, {%vāluka.%} Acc. to Vśs {%elāvāluka%} is a synonym of
{%elavāluka.%}
