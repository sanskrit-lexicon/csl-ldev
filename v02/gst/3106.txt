{#अनुपातक#}¦ Tatpur. n. ({#-कम्#}) A heinous offence, one similar
<div n="lb">to the four great crimes ({#महापातक#}) which are: <sup>a.</sup> Causing
<div n="lb">the death of a Brāhmaṇa (directly or indirectly). <sup>b.</sup> Drink-
<div n="lb">ing spirits. <sup>c.</sup> Stealing (acc. to the comm. ‘gold from a
<div n="lb">Brāhmaṇa’). <sup>d.</sup> Committing incest with the wife of a
<div n="lb">father, natural or spiritual. The {#अनुपातक#} mentioned by
<div n="lb">Manu are the following: 1. False boasting of superiority
<div n="lb">(as pretending to be a Brāhmaṇa or conversant with the
<div n="lb">four Vedas, if one is not); 2. Malignant report, before a
<div n="lb">king, of crimes (comm.: punishable by death)--omitted
<div n="lb">by Yājnavalkya--; 3. Falsely accusing a spiritual preceptor;
<div n="lb">4. Forgetting the Veda; 5. Showing contempt of the Veda
<div n="lb">(comm.: by resorting to heretical writings); 6. Giving false
<div n="lb">evidence (omitted by Yājnav.); 7. Killing a friend (comm.:
<div n="lb">not a Brāhmaṇa, this case being the first {#महापातक#}); 8.
<div n="lb">(Knowingly) eating things prohibited (i. e. food fit only for
<div n="lb">a Śūdra, as garlick &c.) or unfit to be tasted (as excrements
<div n="lb">&c.); [9. (According to Yājnavalkya) Kissing a woman
[Page094-b+ 56]
<div n="lb">while in menstruation; 10. (Acc. to Yājn.) Deceitfulness
<div n="lb">(i. e. saying one thing and doing another)]; 11. Appro-
<div n="lb">priating a deposit (comm.: not the gold of a Brāhmaṇa
<div n="lb">(this case being the third {#महापातक#}) or acc. to another
<div n="lb">author. a deposit not of gold, if it belongs to a Brāhmaṇa);
<div n="lb">12--17. Stealing a man, a horse, silver, a piece of land, a
<div n="lb">diamond or a gem [acc. to Yājnavalkya, 12--17. a horse,
<div n="lb">a gem, a man, a woman, a piece of land, and a cow];
<div n="lb">18--22. Sexual intercourse with one's own sister, with little
<div n="lb">girls before they have attained to puberty, with women of
<div n="lb">the lowest tribe (comm.: a Chāṇdālī), with the wives of a
<div n="lb">friend or of a son [to which Yājnavalkya adds: 23--30. with
<div n="lb">a woman of the same gotra or family, the sister of a father
<div n="lb">or mother, the wife of a maternal uncle, the wife of one's
<div n="lb">father, the daughter or wife of a spiritual teacher or one's
<div n="lb">own daughter; Nārada adds moreover: 31--40. with a mother
<div n="lb">in law, the wife of a paternal uncle or of a pupil, the friend
<div n="lb">of a sister, one who comes for protection, the wife of one
<div n="lb">who carries on the government of the country--comm.:
<div n="lb">if he is not a Kshatriya--, a nurse, a female mendicant,
<div n="lb">a female devotee and a woman of the Brāhmaṇa class.]--
<div n="lb">Similar to the first Mahāpātaka are the Anupātakas, acc.
<div n="lb">to Manu, 1--3, acc. to Yājnavalkya, 3--5 and 7. Similar to
<div n="lb">the second Mahāpātaka are the Anup., acc. to Manu, 4--8;
<div n="lb">acc. to Yājnav., 1. 8. 9. 10. Similar to the third Mahāp.
<div n="lb">are the Anup., acc. to Manu and Yājnav., 11--17. Similar
<div n="lb">to the fourth Mahāp. are, according to Manu, the Anupāt.
<div n="lb">18--22, acc. to Yājnav. (and Nārada, Yama &c.), 18-40. E.
