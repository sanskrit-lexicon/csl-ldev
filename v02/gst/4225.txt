{#अन्यत्व#}¦ n. ({#-त्वम्#}) The being other, otherness, difference
(the same as {#अन्यता,#} but more frequently used as this
word); e. g. {#अन्यत्वेऽप्यभ्यासस्योपचारात्#} ‘even if there
were different (sounds, sound must be permanent) because
it is possible to repeat it (in teaching)’; also with a noun
in the ablative, e. g. {#वचनात्तु ततोऽन्यत्वम्#} ‘(if it is main-
tained that {#इतिकर्तव्यता#} refers to profane things, the answer
is:) but on account of a (distinct vaidik) passage its dif-
ference from that (is established), i. e. it refers only to sacred
or vaidik objects’; or {#तयोरन्यत्वे तुच्छत्वम्#} (comm. {#अका-#}
{#र्यस्य प्रकृतिपुरुषभिन्नत्वे तुच्छत्वम्#} &c.).--The word occurs
[Page142-a+ 56]
for the most part, however, as the latter part of a com-
pound--e. g. {#स्फटिकान्यत्वाभिमानवत्तदन्यत्वाभिमानः#}--
and especially in scientific writings, e. g. in the Vārttikas
of Pāṇini in compounds as {#अङ्गान्यत्व, कर्त्रन्यत्व, कारकान्यत्व,#}
{#कालान्यत्व,#} in the Vedānta Sūtras as in {#तदनन्यत्व,#} in the
Mīmānsā S. as in {#प्रयोजनान्यत्व, वेदान्यत्व, रूपान्यत्व#} &c.,
in the Nyāya S. as in {#स्थानान्यत्व, कर्मान्यत्व,#} in the Sāṅkhya
S. {#उभयान्यत्व,#} in the Yoga S. as in {#क्रमान्यत्व#} &c. &c. E.
{#अन्य,#} taddh. aff. {#त्व#}.
