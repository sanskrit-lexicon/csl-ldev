{#अन्तःपात#}¦ Tatpur. m. ({#-तः#}) <sup>1</sup> (In ritual literature.) A pale
which is fixed three steps eastward from a large post that
stands before the {#आहवनीय#} (q. v.) fire in the fore part of
the {#प्राचीनवंश#} (q. v.), and which thus comes to be placed
in the middle between the {#ऐष्टिकवेदि#} q. v. and the {#महावेदि#}.
<sup>2</sup> (In vaidik grammar.) A technical name for that Sandhi,
which, according to some grammarians requires, the in-
sertion of a {#क्#} between a final {#ङ्#} of one word and an initial
sibilant of a following one (as in {#अर्वाङ्क्छश्वत्तमम्#} instead
of {#अर्वाङ् शश्वत्तमम्#} or {#प्रत्यङ्क्स#} instead of {#प्रत्यङ् स#}), of a
{#त्#} between a final {#ट्#} or {#न्#} and an initial {#स्#} (as in the {#अप्राट्त्स#}
instead of {#अप्राट् स#} or in {#तान्त्सम्#} instead of {#तान्सम्#}), and of
a {#च्#} between a final {#ञ्#} and an initial {#श्#} (as in {#वज्रिञ्च्छ्न-#}
{#थिहि#} instead of {#वज्रिञ्श्नथिहि#}). E. {#अन्तर्#} and {#पात#}.
