{#अदद्र्यञ्च्#}¦ Tatpur. m. f. n. ({#-द्र्यङ्-द्रीची-द्र्यक्#}) Going to that.
(This word is probably one of a mere grammatical coinage,
to illustrate a rule concerning the pronom. theme {#अदस्#}.
According to some it would have the same meaning as {#अ-#}
{#दमुयञ्च्, अमुद्र्यञ्च्#} and {#अमुमुयञ्च्#} to which others add {#अद-#}
{#मुद्र्यञ्च्, अमुमूयञ्च्, अदमूयञ्च्#} qq. vv., but it might also be
that {#अदद्र्यञ्च्#} (as well as {#अदमुयञ्च्, अदमुद्र्यञ्च्, अदमूयञ्च्#})
is formed to show that its first part represents not the
whole theme, but merely the neuter of {#अदस्,#} while the
first part of {#अमुद्र्यञ्च्, अमुमुयञ्च्#} and {#अमुमूयञ्च्#} refer to its
masc. and femin. For the declension of this word, see
{#अञ्च्#} II. E. {#अदस्#} and {#अञ्च्#} (II. 1.), with {#अद्रि#} instead of
the final {#अस्#} of {#अदस्#}.
