{#अङ्ग#}¦ I. Ind. (see {#निपात#}.) <sup>1</sup> A vocative particle implying
<div n="lb"><sup>a.</sup> solicitation, equivalent to the English ‘do’, in phrases as
<div n="lb">‘do give’ &c. <sup>b.</sup> abuse, <sup>c.</sup> joy. <sup>2</sup> A particle implying con-
<div n="lb">firmation, as ‘indeed, certainly’, or putting stress on the
<div n="lb">word with which it is connected. <sup>3</sup> Again, further, the
<div n="lb">rather. <sup>4</sup> Quick. <sup>5</sup> A particle of assent used in composition
<div n="lb">with {#कृ#} and its derivatives, {#ई#} being substituted for the ter-
<div n="lb">minating vowel as in {#अङ्गीकरण#} &c. (See {#गति#}.)
<div n="P">II. n. ({#-ङ्गम्#}) <sup>1</sup> A limb or member. <sup>2</sup> The body. <sup>3</sup> A part
<div n="lb">or division of a whole. <sup>4</sup> A division of Hindu learning, com-
<div n="lb">prehending especially such science as is considered depend-
<div n="lb">ant upon the Vedas, hence also called Vedāṅga; works
<div n="lb">on six subjects come under this description, viz. {#शिक्षा#} or
<div n="lb">pronunciation, {#व्याकरण#} or grammar, {#छन्दस्#} or prosody,
<div n="lb">{#निरुक्त#} or explanation of obscure terms, {#कल्प#} or description
<div n="lb">of religious rites and {#ज्योतिष#} or astronomy. Also the Jainas
<div n="lb">call the twelve divisions of their holy work Gaṇipiṭaka,
<div n="lb">Angas. <sup>5</sup> An inferior or secondary part of a whole. <sup>6</sup> (In
<div n="lb">rhetoric.) An incidental passion or figure, illustrative of,
<div n="lb">but subordinate to the main subject of the description. <sup>7</sup> All
<div n="lb">the dramatis personae of a play, except the hero and he-
<div n="lb">roine, the body of the characters. <sup>8</sup> (In grammar.) The
<div n="lb">theme or that part of a word in general, to which the affix
[Page018-a+ 56]
<div n="lb">is attached, whether the affix be one of inflexion or deri-
<div n="lb">vation. <sup>9</sup> An expedient, a means of success. <sup>10</sup> Mind, un-
<div n="lb">derstanding. <sup>11</sup> A denomination of the numeral six (this
<div n="lb">being the number of the Vedāṅgas). E. {#अङ्ग्,#} kṛt aff.
<div n="lb">{#अच्#}.
<div n="P">III. m. ({#-ङ्गः#}) <sup>1</sup> A country, Bengal proper, including
<div n="lb">Bhāgalpur, and its inhabitants. (In this sense the word is
<div n="lb">mostly used as a plural.) <sup>2</sup> A proper name: the son of Ūru
<div n="lb">by Āgneyī and grandson to Chākṣuṣa or the sixth Manu,
<div n="lb">a descendant of Atri; the eldest son of the king Bali. E. {#अङ्ग्,#}
<div n="lb">kṛt aff. {#घञ्#}.
<div n="P">IV. m. f. n. ({#-ङ्गः-ङ्गा-ङ्गम्#}) <sup>1</sup> Having limbs, a body &c.
<div n="lb"><sup>2</sup> Near, proximate. E. {#अङ्ग#} (see II.), taddh. aff. {#अच्#}.
