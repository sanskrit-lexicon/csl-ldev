{#अङ्गुल#}¦ I. m. (and according to one authority also n.) ({#-लः#}
<div n="lb">{#-लम्#}) <sup>1</sup> A finger. <sup>2</sup> The thumb. <sup>3</sup> A finger's breadth, as a
<div n="lb">linear measure, viz. a measure of eight barley corns joined
<div n="lb">side by side in breadth or of three grains of rice in length.
<div n="lb">Twelve angulas make a vitasti or span, and twenty-four
<div n="lb">a hasta or cubit. <sup>4</sup> (In astronomy.) A digit or one twelfth
<div n="lb">part of any dimension, subdivided into sixty vyangulas.
<div n="lb">E. {#अङ्गुल#} is considered by the gramm. authorities as a sub-
<div n="lb">stitute of {#अङ्गुलि,#} as which it ought to occur only at the end
<div n="lb">of certain Tatpur., derived then with samāsānta aff. {#अच्#} and
<div n="lb">at the end of certain Bahuvr., derived with samāsānta aff. {#षच्#}.
<div n="lb">In both cases it is a neutre. The native dictionaries how-
<div n="lb">ever state as above.
<div n="P">II. m. ({#-लः#}) A proper name of the sage Chāṇakya. E.
