<L>3988<pc>129-a<k1>अन्त्यज<k2>अन्त्यज
{#अन्त्यज#}¦ Tatpur. 1. m. f. n. ({#-जः-जा-जम्#}) Latest born,
youngest (? comp. {#अन्तज#}).
<div n="P">2. m. ({#-जः#}) A man of the lowest tribe; the same
as {#अन्त्य#}.
<div n="P">3. f. ({#-जा#}) A woman of the lowest tribe; the same as
{#अन्त्या#}. [This word (2. and 3.) is explained in various ways.
{%Vijnāneśvara%} identifies it sometimes with {#चाण्डाल,#} in other
[Page129-b+ 56]
places he and {%Kullūka%} explain it {#चाण्डालादि#} ‘Chāṇḍāla
and so on’. Of more distinct definitions the following may
be noticed: that of {%Yama,%} who comprises under the term
{#अन्त्यजाः#} a washerman, a worker in leather, a dancer, a
Varuḍa, a fisherman, a Meda or attendant on women, and
a Bhilla ({#रजकश्चर्मकारश्च नटो वरुड एव च । कैवर्तमेद-#}
{#भिल्लाश्च सप्तैते ह्यन्त्यजाः स्मृताः#}); of {%Samvarta,%} who com-
prises under it a washerman, a hunter, an actor, a flute-
player and a worker in leather ({#रजकव्याधशैलूषवेणुचर्मोप-#}
{#जीविनाम्#} &c.); of {%Āpastamba,%} who enumerates a Chāṇḍāla,
a Meda, a Śvapacha or Śvapāka (the son of an Ugrā woman
by a Kshattriya male) and a man who belongs to the Kāpā-
lika worshippers of Śiva ({#चाण्डालमेदश्वपचकपालव्रतचारि-#}
{#णाम्#} &c.); of another lawbook which names, a Chāṇḍāla, a
Pukkaśa, a Mlechchha, a Śvapāka and a man who has be-
come an outcast ({#चाण्डालं पुष्कसं म्लेच्छं श्वपाकं पतितं तथा#}).
In a similar manner {%Śātātapa%} speaks of {#अन्त्यजा#} women as
of {#कैवर्ती रजकीं चैव वेणुचर्मोपजीविनीम्;#} {%Āpastamba%} as of
{#म्लेच्छी नटी चर्मकारी रजकी वरुडी तथा,#} and {%Uśanas',%} ‘wife
of a man who eats the food of a Kāpālika’ ({#कापालिकान्न-#}
{#भोक्तॄणां तन्नारी°#}) refers probably also to the {#अन्त्यजा#}.--
Halayudha who calls the Śūdra {#अन्त्यवर्ण,#} enumerates the
following under the head of {#अन्तजाति#} (the same as {#अन्त्यज#}):
{#‘अन्तावसायी चण्डालो निषादश्च जलङ्गमः । श्वपचः पुक्कश-#}
{#श्चैव मातङ्गः प्लवगः स्मृतः ॥ किराताः शबरा निष्ठ्याः पुलिन्दा#}
{#नाहला भटाः माला म्लेच्छादयो भिल्लाः कथ्यन्ते ह्यन्तजातयः’#}.]
Comp. {#अन्त्यजन्मन्#} and {#अन्त्यजाति#}. E. {#अन्त्य#} and {#ज#}.
