{#अनन्तरज#}¦ Tatpur. m. ({#-जः#}) The son of a man of the three
<div n="lb">first castes, married to a woman of a caste inferior in order
[Page068-b+ 56]
<div n="lb">to his own, but not to a Sūdra woman viz. of a Brāhmaṇa
<div n="lb">by a Kṣatriyā or Vaiśyā and of a Kṣatriyā by a Vaiśyā.
<div n="lb">The three kinds of sons got by parents of the same caste
<div n="lb">are called {#सजातिज#} and both, comprising six kinds, have
<div n="lb">the privileges of a Dvija or twice-born man. (This is
<div n="lb">the explanation given by a commentator of Manu and,
<div n="lb">according to him, {#अनन्तरज#} would have a different mean-
<div n="lb">ing than {#अनन्तरजात,#} though both words appear to be
<div n="lb">synonymes.) E. {#अनन्तर#} and {#ज#}.
